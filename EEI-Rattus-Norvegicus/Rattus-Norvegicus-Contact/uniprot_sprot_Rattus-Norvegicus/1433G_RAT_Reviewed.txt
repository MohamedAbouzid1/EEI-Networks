CC   -!- FUNCTION: Adapter protein implicated in the regulation of a large
CC       spectrum of both general and specialized signaling pathways. Binds to a
CC       large number of partners, usually by recognition of a phosphoserine or
CC       phosphothreonine motif. Binding generally results in the modulation of
CC       the activity of the binding partner. Promotes inactivation of WDR24
CC       component of the GATOR2 complex by binding to phosphorylated WDR24.
CC       Participates in the positive regulation of NMDA glutamate receptor
CC       activity by promoting the L-glutamate secretion through interaction
CC       with BEST1. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P61981}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer (By similarity). Interacts with SAMSN1 (By
CC       similarity). Interacts with RAF1, SSH1 and CRTC2/TORC2 (By similarity).
CC       Interacts with ABL1 (phosphorylated form); the interaction retains it
CC       in the cytoplasm (By similarity). Interacts with GAB2 (By similarity).
CC       Interacts with MDM4 (phosphorylated); negatively regulates MDM4
CC       activity toward TP53 (By similarity). Interacts with PKA-phosphorylated
CC       AANAT and SIRT2 (PubMed:11427721). Interacts with the 'Thr-369'
CC       phosphorylated form of DAPK2 (By similarity). Interacts with PI4KB,
CC       TBC1D22A and TBC1D22B (By similarity). Interacts with SLITRK1 (By
CC       similarity). Interacts with LRRK2; this interaction is dependent on
CC       LRRK2 phosphorylation (By similarity). Interacts with MARK2 and MARK3
CC       (By similarity). Interacts with MEFV (By similarity). Interacts with
CC       ENDOG, TSC2 and PIK3C3; interaction with ENDOG weakens its interaction
CC       with TSC2 and PIK3C3 (By similarity). Interacts with (phosphorylated)
CC       WDR24 (By similarity). Interacts with BEST1; this interaction promotes
CC       L-glutamate channel activity leading to the positive regulation of NMDA
CC       glutamate receptor activity through the L-glutamate secretion (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P61981,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P61982, ECO:0000269|PubMed:11427721}.
