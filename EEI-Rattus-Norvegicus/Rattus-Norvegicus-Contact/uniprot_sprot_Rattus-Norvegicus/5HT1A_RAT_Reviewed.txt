CC   -!- FUNCTION: G-protein coupled receptor for 5-hydroxytryptamine
CC       (serotonin) (PubMed:2140514, PubMed:2156831). Also functions as a
CC       receptor for various drugs and psychoactive substances (By similarity).
CC       Ligand binding causes a conformation change that triggers signaling via
CC       guanine nucleotide-binding proteins (G proteins) and modulates the
CC       activity of downstream effectors, such as adenylate cyclase (By
CC       similarity). HTR1A is coupled to G(i)/G(o) G alpha proteins and
CC       mediates inhibitory neurotransmission: signaling inhibits adenylate
CC       cyclase activity and activates a phosphatidylinositol-calcium second
CC       messenger system that regulates the release of Ca(2+) ions from
CC       intracellular stores (By similarity). Beta-arrestin family members
CC       regulate signaling by mediating both receptor desensitization and
CC       resensitization processes (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P08908, ECO:0000269|PubMed:2140514,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:2156831}.
CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: G-protein coupled receptor activity is regulated
CC       by lipids: phosphatidylinositol 4-phosphate increases HTR1A-mediated
CC       activity. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P08908}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Heterodimer; heterodimerizes with GPER1 (By similarity).
CC       Interacts with YIF1B (PubMed:18685031). Interacts with GPR39 and GALR1
CC       (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P08908,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:18685031}.
CC   -!- INTERACTION:
CC       P19327; Q04589: Fgfr1; NbExp=5; IntAct=EBI-6570156, EBI-2480918;
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000269|PubMed:2156831};
CC       Multi-pass membrane protein {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P08908}. Cell
CC       projection, dendrite {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18685031}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Detected in hypothalamus, mesencephalon, amygdala,
CC       medulla, thalamus, septum and hippocampus.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18685031, ECO:0000269|PubMed:2156831}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the G-protein coupled receptor 1 family. 5-
CC       hydroxytryptamine receptor subfamily. HTR1A sub-subfamily.
