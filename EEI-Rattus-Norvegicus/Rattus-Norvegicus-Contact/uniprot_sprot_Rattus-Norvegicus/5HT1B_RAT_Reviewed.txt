CC   -!- FUNCTION: G-protein coupled receptor for 5-hydroxytryptamine
CC       (serotonin) (PubMed:1836757, Ref.1). Also functions as a receptor for
CC       ergot alkaloid derivatives, various anxiolytic and antidepressant drugs
CC       and other psychoactive substances, such as lysergic acid diethylamide
CC       (LSD) (By similarity). Ligand binding causes a conformation change that
CC       triggers signaling via guanine nucleotide-binding proteins (G proteins)
CC       and modulates the activity of downstream effectors, such as adenylate
CC       cyclase (By similarity). HTR1B is coupled to G(i)/G(o) G alpha proteins
CC       and mediates inhibitory neurotransmission by inhibiting adenylate
CC       cyclase activity (By similarity). Arrestin family members inhibit
CC       signaling via G proteins and mediate activation of alternative
CC       signaling pathways (By similarity). Regulates the release of 5-
CC       hydroxytryptamine, dopamine and acetylcholine in the brain, and thereby
CC       affects neural activity, nociceptive processing, pain perception, mood
CC       and behavior (By similarity). Besides, plays a role in vasoconstriction
CC       of cerebral arteries (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P28222,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:1836757, ECO:0000269|Ref.1}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer. Heterodimer with HTR1D.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P28222}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000269|PubMed:1836757,
CC       ECO:0000269|Ref.1}; Multi-pass membrane protein {ECO:0000255}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: Ligands are bound in a hydrophobic pocket formed by the
CC       transmembrane helices. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P28222}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: A residue in the 7th transmembrane region ('Thr-355' in human,
CC       'Asn-351' in mouse and rat) is important for species-specific
CC       sensitivity to various agonists. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P28222}.
CC   -!- PTM: Phosphorylated. Desensitization of the receptor may be mediated by
CC       its phosphorylation. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P28222}.
CC   -!- PTM: Palmitoylated. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P28222}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the G-protein coupled receptor 1 family.
CC       {ECO:0000255|PROSITE-ProRule:PRU00521}.
