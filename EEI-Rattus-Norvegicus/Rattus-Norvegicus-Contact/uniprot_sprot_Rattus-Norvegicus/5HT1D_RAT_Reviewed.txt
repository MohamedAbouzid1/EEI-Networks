CC   -!- FUNCTION: G-protein coupled receptor for 5-hydroxytryptamine
CC       (serotonin) (PubMed:19912901, PubMed:8978753). Also functions as a
CC       receptor for ergot alkaloid derivatives, various anxiolytic and
CC       antidepressant drugs and other psychoactive substances (By similarity).
CC       Ligand binding causes a conformation change that triggers signaling via
CC       guanine nucleotide-binding proteins (G proteins) and modulates the
CC       activity of downstream effectors, such as adenylate cyclase (By
CC       similarity). HTR1D is coupled to G(i)/G(o) G alpha proteins and
CC       mediates inhibitory neurotransmission by inhibiting adenylate cyclase
CC       activity (By similarity). Regulates the release of 5-hydroxytryptamine
CC       in the brain, and thereby affects neural activity (By similarity). May
CC       also play a role in regulating the release of other neurotransmitters
CC       (By similarity). May play a role in vasoconstriction (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P28221, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19912901,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:8978753}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer. Heterodimer with HTR1B.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P28221}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19912901,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:8978753}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P28221}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Detected in dorsal raphe.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19912901}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the G-protein coupled receptor 1 family.
