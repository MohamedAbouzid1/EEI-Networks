CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Forms homopentameric as well as heteropentameric serotonin-
CC       activated cation-selective channel complexes with HTR3B or HTR3C or
CC       HTR3D or HTR3E. The homomeric complex is functional but exhibits low
CC       conductance with modified voltage dependence, and decreased agonist and
CC       antagonist affinity. Heteropentameric complexes display properties
CC       which resemble that of neuronal serotonin-activated channels in vivo
CC       (PubMed:10854267, PubMed:12151552). Interacts with RIC3 (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P46098,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:10854267, ECO:0000269|PubMed:12151552}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Postsynaptic cell membrane
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P46098}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P23979}. Cell membrane
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P46098}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P23979}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed in central and peripheral neurons.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12151552}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: By nerve growth factor in PC12 cells.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10854267}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The HA-stretch region of HTR3A seems to be responsible for the
CC       low conductance of HTR3A homomers compared to that of HTR3A/HTR3B
CC       heteromers. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P46098}.
