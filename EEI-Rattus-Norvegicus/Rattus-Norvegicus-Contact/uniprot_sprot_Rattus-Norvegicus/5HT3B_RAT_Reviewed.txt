CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Forms homopentameric as well as heteropentameric serotonin-
CC       activated cation-selective channel complexes with HTR3A. The homomeric
CC       complex is not functional. Heteropentameric complexes display
CC       properties which resemble that of neuronal serotonin-activated channels
CC       in vivo. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10854267, ECO:0000269|PubMed:12151552}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Postsynaptic cell membrane
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O95264}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000255}. Cell membrane {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O95264}; Multi-pass
CC       membrane protein {ECO:0000255}. Note=Presumably retained within the
CC       endoplasmic reticulum unless complexed with HTR3A.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O95264}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed in peripheral neurons, but not in neurons
CC       of the central nervous system. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12151552}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: By nerve growth factor. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10854267}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The HA-stretch region of HTR3B seems to confer increased
CC       conductance to HTR3A/HTR3B heteromers compared to that of HTR3A
CC       homomers. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O95264}.
CC   -!- PTM: N-glycosylation is required for membrane localization.
CC       {ECO:0000250}.

CC       5-hydroxytryptamine receptor (TC 1.A.9.2) subfamily. HTR3B sub-
CC       subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
