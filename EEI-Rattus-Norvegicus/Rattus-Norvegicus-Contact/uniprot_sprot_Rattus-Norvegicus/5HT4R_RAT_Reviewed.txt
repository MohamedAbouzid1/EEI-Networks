CC   -!- FUNCTION: G-protein coupled receptor for 5-hydroxytryptamine
CC       (serotonin), a biogenic hormone that functions as a neurotransmitter, a
CC       hormone and a mitogen (PubMed:10220570, PubMed:7796807). Ligand binding
CC       causes a conformation change that triggers signaling via guanine
CC       nucleotide-binding proteins (G proteins) and modulates the activity of
CC       downstream effectors (By similarity). HTR4 is coupled to G(s) G alpha
CC       proteins and mediates activation of adenylate cyclase activity (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q13639,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:10220570, ECO:0000269|PubMed:7796807}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts (via C-terminus 330-346 AA) with GRK5; this
CC       interaction is promoted by 5-HT (serotonin).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q13639}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q13639};
CC       Multi-pass membrane protein {ECO:0000255}. Endosome membrane
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P97288}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000255}. Note=Interaction with SNX27 mediates recruitment to
CC       early endosomes, while interaction with NHERF1 and EZR might target the
CC       protein to specialized subcellular regions, such as microvilli.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P97288}.
