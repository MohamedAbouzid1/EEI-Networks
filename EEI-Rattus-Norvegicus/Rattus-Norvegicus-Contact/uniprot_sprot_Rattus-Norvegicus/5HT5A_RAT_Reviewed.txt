CC   -!- FUNCTION: G-protein coupled receptor for 5-hydroxytryptamine
CC       (serotonin), a biogenic hormone that functions as a neurotransmitter, a
CC       hormone and a mitogen (PubMed:7682702). Also functions as a receptor
CC       for ergot alkaloid derivatives and other psychoactive substances (By
CC       similarity). Ligand binding causes a conformation change that triggers
CC       signaling via guanine nucleotide-binding proteins (G proteins) and
CC       modulates the activity of downstream effectors (By similarity). Htr5a
CC       is coupled to G(i)/G(o) G alpha proteins and mediates inhibitory
CC       neurotransmission: signaling inhibits adenylate cyclase activity and
CC       activates a phosphatidylinositol-calcium second messenger system that
CC       regulates the release of Ca(2+) ions from intracellular stores (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P47898,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:7682702}.
