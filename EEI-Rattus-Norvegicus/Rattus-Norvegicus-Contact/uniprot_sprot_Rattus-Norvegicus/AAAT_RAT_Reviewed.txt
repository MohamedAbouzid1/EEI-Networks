CC   -!- FUNCTION: Sodium-coupled antiporter of neutral amino acids. In a tri-
CC       substrate transport cycle, exchanges neutral amino acids between the
CC       extracellular and intracellular compartments, coupled to the inward
CC       cotransport of at least one sodium ion (By similarity)
CC       (PubMed:10698697). The preferred substrate is the essential amino acid
CC       L-glutamine, a precursor for biosynthesis of proteins, nucleotides and
CC       amine sugars as well as an alternative fuel for mitochondrial oxidative
CC       phosphorylation. Exchanges L-glutamine with other neutral amino acids
CC       such as L-serine, L-threonine and L-asparagine in a bidirectional way.
CC       Provides L-glutamine to proliferating stem and activated cells driving
CC       the metabolic switch toward cell differentiation (By similarity). The
CC       transport cycle is usually pH-independent, with the exception of L-
CC       glutamate. Transports extracellular L-glutamate coupled to the
CC       cotransport of one proton and one sodium ion in exchange for
CC       intracellular L-glutamine counter-ion. May provide for L-glutamate
CC       uptake in glial cells regulating glutamine/glutamate cycle in the
CC       nervous system (By similarity). Can transport D-amino acids. Mediates
CC       D-serine release from the retinal glia potentially affecting NMDA
CC       receptor function in retinal neurons (By similarity). Displays
CC       sodium- and amino acid-dependent but uncoupled channel-like anion
CC       conductance with a preference SCN(-) >> NO3(-) > I(-) > Cl(-)
CC       (PubMed:10698697). Through binding of the fusogenic protein syncytin-
CC       1/ERVW-1 may mediate trophoblasts syncytialization, the spontaneous
CC       fusion of their plasma membranes, an essential process in placental
CC       development (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q15758,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:10698697}.
