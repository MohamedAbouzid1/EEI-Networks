CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalyzes the hydrolysis of acyl-CoAs into free fatty acids
CC       and coenzyme A (CoASH), regulating their respective intracellular
CC       levels (PubMed:11785945). Displays no strong substrate specificity with
CC       respect to the carboxylic acid moiety of Acyl-CoAs (By similarity).
CC       Hydrolyzes medium length (C2 to C20) straight-chain, saturated and
CC       unsaturated acyl-CoAS but is inactive towards substrates with longer
CC       aliphatic chains (PubMed:11785945). Moreover, it catalyzes the
CC       hydrolysis of CoA esters of bile acids, such as choloyl-CoA and
CC       chenodeoxycholoyl-CoA and competes with bile acid CoA:amino acid N-
CC       acyltransferase (BAAT) (By similarity). Is also able to hydrolyze CoA
CC       esters of dicarboxylic acids (By similarity). It is involved in the
CC       metabolic regulation of peroxisome proliferation (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O14734, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P58137,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:11785945}.
