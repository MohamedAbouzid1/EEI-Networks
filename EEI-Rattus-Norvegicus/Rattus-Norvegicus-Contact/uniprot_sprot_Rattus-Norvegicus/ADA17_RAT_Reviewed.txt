CC   -!- FUNCTION: Transmembrane metalloprotease which mediates the ectodomain
CC       shedding of a myriad of transmembrane proteins including adhesion
CC       proteins, growth factor precursors and cytokines important for
CC       inflammation and immunity (By similarity). Cleaves the membrane-bound
CC       precursor of TNF-alpha to its mature soluble form. Responsible for the
CC       proteolytical release of soluble JAM3 from endothelial cells surface.
CC       Responsible for the proteolytic release of several other cell-surface
CC       proteins, including p75 TNF-receptor, interleukin 1 receptor type II,
CC       p55 TNF-receptor, transforming growth factor-alpha, L-selectin, growth
CC       hormone receptor, MUC1 and the amyloid precursor protein. Acts as an
CC       activator of Notch pathway by mediating cleavage of Notch, generating
CC       the membrane-associated intermediate fragment called Notch
CC       extracellular truncation (NEXT). Plays a role in the proteolytic
CC       processing of ACE2 (By similarity). Plays a role in hemostasis through
CC       shedding of GP1BA, the platelet glycoprotein Ib alpha chain (By
CC       similarity). Mediates the proteolytic cleavage of LAG3, leading to
CC       release the secreted form of LAG3 (By similarity). Mediates the
CC       proteolytic cleavage of IL6R, leading to the release of secreted form
CC       of IL6R. Mediates the proteolytic cleavage and shedding of FCGR3A upon
CC       NK cell stimulation, a mechanism that allows for increased NK cell
CC       motility and detachment from opsonized target cells (By similarity).
CC       Cleaves TREM2, resulting in shedding of the TREM2 ectodomain (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P78536,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Z0F8}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY:
CC       Reaction=Narrow endopeptidase specificity. Cleaves Pro-Leu-Ala-Gln-
CC         Ala-|-Val-Arg-Ser-Ser-Ser in the membrane-bound, 26-kDa form of tumor
CC         necrosis factor alpha (TNFalpha). Similarly cleaves other membrane-
CC         anchored, cell-surface proteins to 'shed' the extracellular domains.;
CC         EC=3.4.24.86; Evidence={ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P78536};
CC   -!- COFACTOR:
CC       Name=Zn(2+); Xref=ChEBI:CHEBI:29105;
CC         Evidence={ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P78536};
CC       Note=Binds 1 zinc ion per subunit. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P78536};
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts with MAD2L1, MAPK14 and MUC1. Interacts with
CC       iRhom1/RHBDF1 and iRhom2/RHBDF2. Interacts with FRMD8 via its
CC       interaction with iRhom1/RHBDF1 and iRhom2/RHBDF2. Interacts with
CC       TSPAN8. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P78536}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Membrane {ECO:0000255}; Single-pass type I
CC       membrane protein {ECO:0000255}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: Must be membrane anchored to cleave the different substrates.
CC       The cytoplasmic domain is not required for the this activity. Only the
CC       catalytic domain is essential to shed TNF and p75 TNFR (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The conserved cysteine present in the cysteine-switch motif
CC       binds the catalytic zinc ion, thus inhibiting the enzyme. The
CC       dissociation of the cysteine from the zinc ion upon the activation-
CC       peptide release activates the enzyme.
CC   -!- PTM: The precursor is cleaved by a furin endopeptidase. {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- PTM: Phosphorylated. Stimulation by growth factor or phorbol 12-
CC       myristate 13-acetate induces phosphorylation of Ser-822 but decreases
CC       phosphorylation of Ser-794. Phosphorylation at Thr-735 by MAPK14 is
CC       required for ADAM17-mediated ectodomain shedding (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P78536}.
