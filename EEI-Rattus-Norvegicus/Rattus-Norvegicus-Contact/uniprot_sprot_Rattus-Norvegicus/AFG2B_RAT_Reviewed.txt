CC   -!- FUNCTION: ATP-dependent chaperone part of the 55LCC heterohexameric
CC       ATPase complex which is chromatin-associated and promotes replisome
CC       proteostasis to maintain replication fork progression and genome
CC       stability. Required for replication fork progression, sister chromatid
CC       cohesion, and chromosome stability. The ATPase activity is specifically
CC       enhanced by replication fork DNA and is coupled to cysteine protease-
CC       dependent cleavage of replisome substrates in response to replication
CC       fork damage. Uses ATPase activity to process replisome substrates in S-
CC       phase, facilitating their proteolytic turnover from chromatin to ensure
CC       DNA replication and mitotic fidelity. Plays an essential role in the
CC       cytoplasmic maturation steps of pre-60S ribosomal particles by
CC       promoting the release of shuttling protein RSL24D1/RLP24 from the pre-
CC       ribosomal particles. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9BVQ7}.
