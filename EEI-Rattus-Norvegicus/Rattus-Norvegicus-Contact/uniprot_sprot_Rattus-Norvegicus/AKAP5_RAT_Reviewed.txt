CC   -!- FUNCTION: Multivalent scaffold protein that anchors the cAMP-dependent
CC       protein kinase/PKA to cytoskeletal and/or organelle-associated
CC       proteins, targeting the signal carried by cAMP to specific
CC       intracellular effectors (By similarity). Association with the beta2-
CC       adrenergic receptor (beta2-AR) not only regulates beta2-AR signaling
CC       pathway, but also the activation by PKA by switching off the beta2-AR
CC       signaling cascade. Plays a role in long term synaptic potentiation by
CC       regulating protein trafficking from the dendritic recycling endosomes
CC       to the plasma membrane and controlling both structural and functional
CC       plasticity at excitatory synapses (By similarity). In hippocampal
CC       pyramidal neurons, recruits KCNK2/TREK-1 channel at postsynaptic dense
CC       bodies microdomains and converts it to a leak channel no longer
CC       sensitive to stimulation by arachidonic acid, acidic pH or mechanical
CC       stress, nor inhibited by Gq-coupled receptors but still under the
CC       negative control of Gs-coupled receptors (By similarity). Associates
CC       with ORAI1 pore-forming subunit of CRAC channels in Ca(2+) signaling
CC       microdomains where it recruits NFATC2/NFAT1 and couples store-operated
CC       Ca(2+) influx to calmodulin and calcineurin signaling and activation of
CC       NFAT-dependent transcriptional responses.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:D3YVF0, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P24588}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Binding protein for dimer of the RII-beta regulatory subunit
CC       of cAMP-dependent protein kinase (PKA) and also for the protein kinase
CC       C (PKC) and the phosphatase calcineurin (PP2B). Each enzyme is
CC       inhibited when bound to the anchoring protein. Also binds the beta2-
CC       adrenergic receptor. Part of a complex containing AKAP5, ADCY5, ADCY6
CC       and PDE4C (By similarity). Interacts with ADCY8, and enhances its
CC       phosphorylation at lipid rafts (By similarity). Interacts with ORAI1
CC       (isoform alpha) (via N-terminus) upon store depletion and in response
CC       to LTC4. Does not interact with ORAI2 and ORAI3 paralogs. Interacts
CC       (via leucine zipper domain) with NFATC2/NFAT1 (By similarity).
CC       Interacts with calmodulin; the interaction is calcium-independent (By
CC       similarity). Interacts with KCNQ2; the interaction may help KCNQ2
CC       channel complex to retain calcium-bound calmodulin (PubMed:24349250).
CC       Interacts with KCNK2; the channel is recruited to postsynaptic
CC       microdomains by AKAP5 where it can integrate neurotransmitter receptor
CC       signals. Part of a complex composed of AKAP5 and ADRB2. {ECO:0000250,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:D3YVF0, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P24588,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:24349250}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Postsynaptic recycling endosome membrane
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P24588}; Lipid-anchor
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P24588}. Cell projection, dendrite
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:D3YVF0}. Postsynaptic cell membrane
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:D3YVF0}; Lipid-anchor. Note=Associates with
CC       lipid rafts. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P24588}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: RII-alpha binding site, predicted to form an amphipathic helix,
CC       could participate in protein-protein interactions with a complementary
CC       surface on the R-subunit dimer. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P24588}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The N-terminal region, which is highly basic, is required for
CC       interaction with calmodulin. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P24588}.
CC   -!- PTM: Palmitoylated. Palmitoylation at Cys-36 and Cys-123 plays a key
CC       role in the targeting of AKAP5 to lipid rafts. Palmitoylation by ZDHHC2
CC       is required for AKAP5 function in LTP-stimulated recycling endosome
CC       exocytosis. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P24588}.
