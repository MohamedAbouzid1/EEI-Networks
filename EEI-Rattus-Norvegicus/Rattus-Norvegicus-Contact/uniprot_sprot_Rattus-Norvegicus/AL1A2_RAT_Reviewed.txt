CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalyzes the NAD-dependent oxidation of aldehyde substrates,
CC       such as all-trans-retinal and all-trans-13,14-dihydroretinal, to their
CC       corresponding carboxylic acids, all-trans-retinoate and all-trans-
CC       13,14-dihydroretinoate, respectively (By similarity) (PubMed:8663198).
CC       Retinoate signaling is critical for the transcriptional control of many
CC       genes, for instance it is crucial for initiation of meiosis in both
CC       male and female (By similarity). Recognizes retinal as substrate, both
CC       in its free form and when bound to cellular retinol-binding protein
CC       (PubMed:8663198). Lacks activity with benzaldehyde, acetaldehyde and
CC       octanal (PubMed:8663198). Displays complete lack of activity with
CC       citral (PubMed:8663198). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q62148,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:8663198}.
