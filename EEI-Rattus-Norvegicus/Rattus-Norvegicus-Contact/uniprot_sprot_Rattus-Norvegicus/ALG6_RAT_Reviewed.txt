CC   -!- FUNCTION: Dolichyl pyrophosphate Man9GlcNAc2 alpha-1,3-
CC       glucosyltransferase that operates in the biosynthetic pathway of
CC       dolichol-linked oligosaccharides, the glycan precursors employed in
CC       protein asparagine (N)-glycosylation. The assembly of dolichol-linked
CC       oligosaccharides begins on the cytosolic side of the endoplasmic
CC       reticulum membrane and finishes in its lumen. The sequential addition
CC       of sugars to dolichol pyrophosphate produces dolichol-linked
CC       oligosaccharides containing fourteen sugars, including two GlcNAcs,
CC       nine mannoses and three glucoses. Once assembled, the oligosaccharide
CC       is transferred from the lipid to nascent proteins by
CC       oligosaccharyltransferases. In the lumen of the endoplasmic reticulum,
CC       adds the first glucose residue from dolichyl phosphate glucose (Dol-P-
CC       Glc) onto the lipid-linked oligosaccharide intermediate
CC       Man(9)GlcNAc(2)-PP-Dol to produce Glc(1)Man(9)GlcNAc(2)-PP-Dol.
CC       Glc(1)Man(9)GlcNAc(2)-PP-Dol is a substrate for ALG8, the following
CC       enzyme in the biosynthetic pathway. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Y672}.
