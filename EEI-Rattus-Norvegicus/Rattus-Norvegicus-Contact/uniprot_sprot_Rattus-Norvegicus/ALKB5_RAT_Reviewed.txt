CC   -!- FUNCTION: Dioxygenase that specifically demethylates N(6)-
CC       methyladenosine (m6A) RNA, the most prevalent internal modification of
CC       messenger RNA (mRNA) in higher eukaryotes (By similarity). Demethylates
CC       RNA by oxidative demethylation, which requires molecular oxygen, alpha-
CC       ketoglutarate and iron (By similarity). Demethylation of m6A mRNA
CC       affects mRNA processing, translation and export (By similarity). Can
CC       also demethylate N(6)-methyladenosine in single-stranded DNA (in vitro)
CC       (By similarity). Required for the late meiotic and haploid phases of
CC       spermatogenesis by mediating m6A demethylation in spermatocytes and
CC       round spermatids: m6A demethylation of target transcripts is required
CC       for correct splicing and the production of longer 3'-UTR mRNAs in male
CC       germ cells (By similarity). Involved in paraspeckle assembly, a nuclear
CC       membraneless organelle, by undergoing liquid-liquid phase separation
CC       (By similarity). Paraspeckle assembly is coupled with m6A demethylation
CC       of RNAs, such as NEAT1 non-coding RNA (By similarity). Also acts as a
CC       negative regulator of T-cell development: inhibits gamma-delta T-cell
CC       proliferation via demethylation of JAG1 and NOTCH2 transcripts (By
CC       similarity). Inhibits regulatory T-cell (Treg) recruitment by mediating
CC       demethylation and destabilization of CCL28 mRNAs (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q3TSG4, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q6P6C2}.
