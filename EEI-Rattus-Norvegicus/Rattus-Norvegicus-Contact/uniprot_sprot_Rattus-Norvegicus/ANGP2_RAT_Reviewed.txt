CC   -!- FUNCTION: Binds to TEK/TIE2, competing for the ANGPT1 binding site, and
CC       modulating ANGPT1 signaling (By similarity). Can induce tyrosine
CC       phosphorylation of TEK/TIE2 in the absence of ANGPT1. In the absence of
CC       angiogenic inducers, such as VEGF, ANGPT2-mediated loosening of cell-
CC       matrix contacts may induce endothelial cell apoptosis with consequent
CC       vascular regression (By similarity). In concert with VEGF, it may
CC       facilitate endothelial cell migration and proliferation, thus serving
CC       as a permissive angiogenic signal (By similarity). Involved in the
CC       regulation of lymphangiogenesis (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O15123}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts with TEK/TIE2, competing for the same binding site
CC       as ANGPT1 (By similarity). Interacts with ITGA5 (By similarity).
CC       Interacts with SVEP1/polydom (By similarity). Interacts with THBD; this
CC       interaction significantly inhibits the generation of activated PC and
CC       TAFIa/CPB2 by the thrombin/thrombomodulin complex (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O15123, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O35608}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Secreted {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O15123}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The Fibrinogen C-terminal domain mediates interaction with the
CC       TEK/TIE2 receptor. {ECO:0000250}.
