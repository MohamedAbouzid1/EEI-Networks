CC   -!- FUNCTION: Transports adenosine triphosphate (ATP) and possibly other
CC       nucleoside triphosphates (NTPs) from cytosol to the extracellular
CC       space. Mainly regulates their levels locally in peripheral tissues
CC       while playing a minor systemic role. Prevents abnormal ectopic
CC       mineralization of the joints by regulating the extracellular levels of
CC       the calcification inhibitor inorganic pyrophosphate (PPi), which
CC       originates from the conversion of extracellular NTPs to NMPs and PPis
CC       by ENPP1. Regulates the release of the TCA cycle intermediates to the
CC       extracellular space, in particular citrate, succinate and malate.
CC       Extracellular citrate mostly present in bone tissue is required for
CC       osteogenic differentiation of mesenchymal stem cells, stabilization of
CC       hydroxyapatite structure and overall bone strength. The transport
CC       mechanism remains to be elucidated. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9HCJ1}.
