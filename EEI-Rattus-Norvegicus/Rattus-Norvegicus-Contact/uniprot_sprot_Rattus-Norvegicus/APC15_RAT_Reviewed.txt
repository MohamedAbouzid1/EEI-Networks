CC   -!- FUNCTION: Component of the anaphase promoting complex/cyclosome
CC       (APC/C), a cell cycle-regulated E3 ubiquitin ligase that controls
CC       progression through mitosis and the G1 phase of the cell cycle. The
CC       APC/C complex catalyzes assembly of branched 'Lys-11'-/'Lys-48'-linked
CC       branched ubiquitin chains on target proteins. In the complex, plays a
CC       role in the release of the mitotic checkpoint complex (MCC) from the
CC       APC/C: not required for APC/C activity itself, but promotes the
CC       turnover of CDC20 and MCC on the APC/C, thereby participating in the
CC       responsiveness of the spindle assembly checkpoint. Also required for
CC       degradation of CDC20. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P60006}.
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Protein modification; protein ubiquitination.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P60006}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: The mammalian APC/C is composed at least of 14 distinct
CC       subunits ANAPC1, ANAPC2, CDC27/APC3, ANAPC4, ANAPC5, CDC16/APC6,
CC       ANAPC7, CDC23/APC8, ANAPC10, ANAPC11, CDC26/APC12, ANAPC13, ANAPC15 and
CC       ANAPC16 that assemble into a complex of at least 19 chains with a
CC       combined molecular mass of around 1.2 MDa; APC/C interacts with FZR1
CC       and FBXO5. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P60006}.
