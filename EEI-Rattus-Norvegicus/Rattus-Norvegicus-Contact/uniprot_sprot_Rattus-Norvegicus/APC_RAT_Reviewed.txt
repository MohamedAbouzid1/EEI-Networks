CC   -!- FUNCTION: Tumor suppressor. Promotes rapid degradation of CTNNB1 and
CC       participates in Wnt signaling as a negative regulator. APC activity is
CC       correlated with its phosphorylation state. Activates the GEF activity
CC       of SPATA13 and ARHGEF4. Plays a role in hepatocyte growth factor (HGF)-
CC       induced cell migration (By similarity). Required for MMP9 up-regulation
CC       via the JNK signaling pathway in colorectal tumor cells (By
CC       similarity). Associates with both microtubules and actin filaments,
CC       components of the cytoskeleton (By similarity). Plays a role in
CC       mediating the organization of F-actin into ordered bundles (By
CC       similarity). Functions downstream of Rho GTPases and DIAPH1 to
CC       selectively stabilize microtubules (By similarity). Acts as a mediator
CC       of ERBB2-dependent stabilization of microtubules at the cell cortex. It
CC       is required for the localization of MACF1 to the cell membrane and this
CC       localization of MACF1 is critical for its function in microtubule
CC       stabilization (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P25054,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q61315}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Forms homooligomers (Probable). Found in a complex consisting
CC       of ARHGEF4, APC and CTNNB1 (By similarity). Found in a complex composed
CC       of MACF1, APC, AXIN1, CTNNB1 and GSK3B (PubMed:16815997). The complex
CC       composed, at least, of APC, CTNNB1 and GSK3B interacts with JPT1; the
CC       interaction requires the inactive form of GSK3B (phosphorylated at
CC       'Ser-9') (By similarity). Interacts with APC2 (By similarity).
CC       Interacts with DLG1 (via PDZ domains) and DLG3 (via PDZ domains) (By
CC       similarity). Interacts with alpha- and beta-catenins (By similarity).
CC       Interacts with AXIN1 (via RGS domain) (By similarity). Interacts with
CC       ARHGEF4 (via N-terminus) (By similarity). Interacts (via C-terminal
CC       residues 2674-2843) with MAPRE1 (via C-terminal residues 206-211); the
CC       interaction inhibits association with and bundling of F-actin (By
CC       similarity). Interacts with MAPRE2 and MAPRE3 (via C-terminus) (By
CC       similarity). Interacts with DIAPH1; DIAPH1 acts as a scaffold protein
CC       for MAPRE1 and APC to stabilize microtubules and promote cell migration
CC       (By similarity). Interacts with DIAPH2 (By similarity). Interacts with
CC       SCRIB; may mediate APC targeting to adherens junctions of epithelial
CC       cells (By similarity). Interacts with SPATA13 (via N-terminus and SH3
CC       domain) (By similarity). Interacts with ASAP1 (via SH3 domain) (By
CC       similarity). Interacts (at the cell membrane) with AMER1 and AMER2 (via
CC       ARM repeats) (By similarity). Interacts with KHDRBS1 (By similarity).
CC       Interacts with actin; binds both to F-actin and actin filament bundles
CC       (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P25054,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q61315, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16815997,
CC       ECO:0000305}.
