CC   -!- FUNCTION: Forms a water-specific channel (PubMed:19800950,
CC       PubMed:7509789, PubMed:7528931). Plays an important role in brain water
CC       homeostasis and in glymphatic solute transport. Required for a normal
CC       rate of water exchange across the blood brain interface. Required for
CC       normal levels of cerebrospinal fluid influx into the brain cortex and
CC       parenchyma along paravascular spaces that surround penetrating
CC       arteries, and for normal drainage of interstitial fluid along
CC       paravenous drainage pathways. Thereby, it is required for normal
CC       clearance of solutes from the brain interstitial fluid, including
CC       soluble beta-amyloid peptides derived from APP. Plays a redundant role
CC       in urinary water homeostasis and urinary concentrating ability (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P55088,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19800950, ECO:0000269|PubMed:7509789,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:7528931}.
