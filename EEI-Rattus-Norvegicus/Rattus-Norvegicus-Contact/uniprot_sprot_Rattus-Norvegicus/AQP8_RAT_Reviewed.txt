CC   -!- FUNCTION: Channel that allows the facilitated permeation of water and
CC       uncharged molecules, such as hydrogen peroxide and the neutral form of
CC       ammonia (NH3), through cellular membranes such as plasma membrane,
CC       inner mitochondrial membrane and endoplasmic reticulum membrane of
CC       several tissues (PubMed:12774023, PubMed:15749715, PubMed:15948717,
CC       PubMed:15988592, PubMed:16177191, PubMed:16624991, PubMed:17189259,
CC       PubMed:18059526, PubMed:20132793, PubMed:23299935, PubMed:31372390,
CC       PubMed:33892285, PubMed:9299432, PubMed:9374520). The transport of
CC       ammonia neutral form induces a parallel transport of proton, at
CC       alkaline pH when the concentration of ammonia is high
CC       (PubMed:15988592). However, it is unclear whether the transport of
CC       proton takes place via the aquaporin or via an endogenous pathway
CC       (PubMed:15988592). Also, may transport ammonia analogs such as
CC       formamide and methylamine, a transport favourited at basic pH due to
CC       the increase of unprotonated (neutral) form, which is expected to favor
CC       diffusion (PubMed:15749715, PubMed:15948717, PubMed:15988592). Does not
CC       transport urea or glycerol (PubMed:15948717). The water transport
CC       mechanism is mercury- and copper-sensitive and passive in response to
CC       osmotic driving forces (PubMed:18059526, PubMed:20132793,
CC       PubMed:9299432, PubMed:9374520). At the canicular plasma membrane,
CC       mediates the osmotic transport of water toward the bile canaliculus and
CC       facilitates the cAMP-induced bile canalicular water secretion, a
CC       process involved in bile formation (By similarity). In addition,
CC       mediates the hydrogen peroxide release from hepatocyte mitochondria
CC       that modulates the SREBF2-mediated cholesterol synthesis and
CC       facilitates the mitochondrial ammonia uptake which is metabolized into
CC       urea, mainly under glucagon stimulation (PubMed:20132793,
CC       PubMed:23299935, PubMed:31372390). In B cells, transports the CYBB-
CC       generated hydrogen peroxide from the external leaflet of the plasma
CC       membrane to the cytosol to promote B cell activation and
CC       differentiation for signal amplification (By similarity). In the small
CC       intestine and colon system, mediates water transport through
CC       mitochondria and apical membrane of epithelial cells (PubMed:15749715,
CC       PubMed:16177191, PubMed:18059526). May play an important role in the
CC       adaptive response of proximal tubule cells to acidosis possibly
CC       facilitating mitochondrial ammonia transport (By similarity).
