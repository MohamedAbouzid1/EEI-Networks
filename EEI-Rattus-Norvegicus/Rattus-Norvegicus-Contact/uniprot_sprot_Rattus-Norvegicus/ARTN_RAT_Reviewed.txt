CC   -!- FUNCTION: Growth factor that supports the survival of sensory and
CC       sympathetic peripheral neurons in culture and also supports the
CC       survival of dopaminergic neurons of the ventral mid-brain. Acts by
CC       binding to its coreceptor, GFRA3, leading to autophosphorylation and
CC       activation of the RET receptor (By similarity). Strong attractant of
CC       gut hematopoietic cells thus promoting the formation Peyer's patch-like
CC       structures, a major component of the gut-associated lymphoid tissue (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q5T4W7,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Z0L2}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer; disulfide-linked. Interacts with GFRA3 coreceptor
CC       and RET: forms a 2:2:2 ternary complex composed of ARTN ligand, GFRA3
CC       and RET receptor. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q5T4W7}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Secreted {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Z0L2}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Cochlea. Expressed at higher level in sesorineural
CC       epithelium than in the modiolus region or substantia nigra.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10719212, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9883723}.
CC   -!- DEVELOPMENTAL STAGE: Expressed during embryogenesis. At 14 dpc, not
CC       detected in the brain or spinal cord. A striking expression seen in the
CC       nerve roots but not in the developing neurons of the dorsal root
CC       ganglia. A significant expression also seen around the superior
CC       mesentric artery. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9883723}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the TGF-beta family. GDNF subfamily.
CC       {ECO:0000305}.
