CC   -!- FUNCTION: Transcription factor that plays a key role in neuronal
CC       differentiation: acts as a pioneer transcription factor, accessing
CC       closed chromatin to allow other factors to bind and activate neural
CC       pathways (PubMed:10648228). Directly binds the E box motif (5'-CANNTG-
CC       3') on promoters and promotes transcription of neuronal genes. The
CC       combination of three transcription factors, ASCL1, POU3F2/BRN2 and
CC       MYT1L, is sufficient to reprogram fibroblasts and other somatic cells
CC       into induced neuronal (iN) cells in vitro. Plays a role at early stages
CC       of development of specific neural lineages in most regions of the CNS,
CC       and of several lineages in the PNS. Essential for the generation of
CC       olfactory and autonomic neurons. Acts synergistically with FOXN4 to
CC       specify the identity of V2b neurons rather than V2a from bipotential p2
CC       progenitors during spinal cord neurogenesis, probably through DLL4-
CC       NOTCH signaling activation. Involved in the regulation of
CC       neuroendocrine cell development in the glandular stomach (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q02067,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:10648228}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Efficient DNA binding requires dimerization with another bHLH
CC       protein. Forms a heterodimer with TCF3. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P50553}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Nucleus {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q02067}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Developing CNS and PNS at embryonic and postnatal
CC       stages. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:2392153}.
CC   -!- DEVELOPMENTAL STAGE: It is first observed after neurulation, in 10.5
CC       dpc rat embryos, and is restricted to subsets of neuroepithelial cells
CC       in the spinal cord and the brain, between 10.5 dpc and 13.5 dpc. In the
CC       periphery, its expression is restricted to some lineages of neural
CC       crest-derived cells, namely in sympathetic and enteric neural
CC       precursors. In the PNS its expression is extinguished at or before
CC       differentiation. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:2392153}.
