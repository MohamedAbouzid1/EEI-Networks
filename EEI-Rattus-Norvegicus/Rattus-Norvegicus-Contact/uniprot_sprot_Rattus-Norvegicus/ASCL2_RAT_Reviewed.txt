CC   -!- FUNCTION: Transcription factor (PubMed:1314394). Binds to E-box motifs
CC       5'-CANNTG-3' in the regulatory elements of target genes, probably as a
CC       heterodimer with another basic helix-loop-helix (bHLH) protein such as
CC       the transcription factor TCF3. May bind both open and closed chromatin,
CC       acting as a pioneer transcription factor to allow other factors to bind
CC       and activate lineage-specific genes. Required during post-implantation
CC       development for the generation of some differentiated trophoblast cell
CC       types. Transcriptional activity of ASCL2 may be antagonised in a subset
CC       of trophoblast cells by bHLH transcription factor HAND1, perhaps by
CC       competing for dimerization with other bHLH proteins. Involved in
CC       differentiation and function of follicular T-helper (Tfh) cells,
CC       thereby playing a role in germinal center responses; probably modulates
CC       expression of genes involved in Tfh cell function, such as BCL6. May
CC       also act as a suppressor of Th1-, Th2- and Th17-cell differentiation.
CC       Induces the formation of stem cells in intestinal crypts in vitro,
CC       synergistically activating transcription of target genes, such as SOX9,
CC       together with TCF4/beta-catenin. May form a bistable transcriptional
CC       switch, controlling expression of its own gene together with Wnt/R-
CC       spondin signaling, and thereby maintaining stem cell characteristics
CC       (By similarity). Modulates expression of target genes, including
CC       perhaps down-regulating EGR1/Krox24 and chemokine CXCL10/Mob-1 and up-
CC       regulating CXCR4 and CDKN1C/p57kip2, in Schwann cells
CC       (PubMed:12196582). May play a role in reducing proliferation of Schwann
CC       cells, perhaps acting via modulation of expression of CDKN1C
CC       (PubMed:12196582). May be dispensable for blastocyst formation and
CC       later embryonic function (By similarity). May be involved in the
CC       determination of neuronal precursors (PubMed:2392153).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O35885, ECO:0000269|PubMed:12196582,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:1314394, ECO:0000269|PubMed:2392153}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Efficient DNA binding requires dimerization with another bHLH
CC       protein (PubMed:1314394). Forms heterodimers with bHLH transcription
CC       factor TCF3 (PubMed:1314394). May not heterodimerise with bHLH protein
CC       HAND1 (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O35885,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:1314394}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Nucleus {ECO:0000255|PROSITE-ProRule:PRU00981,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:12196582}. Cytoplasm {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12196582}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed in Schwann cells in the peripheral nerve
CC       (at protein level) (PubMed:12196582). Also expressed by endothelial
CC       cells (at protein level) (PubMed:12196582). May be expressed in
CC       neuronal precursor cells (PubMed:2392153).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12196582, ECO:0000269|PubMed:2392153}.
CC   -!- DEVELOPMENTAL STAGE: Expressed at embryonic day 15.5 (dpc) in placenta
CC       (at protein level). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12196582}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Transiently repressed after nerve injury.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12196582}.
