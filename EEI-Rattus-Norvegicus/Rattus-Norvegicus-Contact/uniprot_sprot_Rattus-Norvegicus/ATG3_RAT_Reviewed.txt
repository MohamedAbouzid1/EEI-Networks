CC   -!- FUNCTION: E2 conjugating enzyme that catalyzes the covalent conjugation
CC       of the C-terminal Gly of ATG8-like proteins (GABARAP, GABARAPL1,
CC       GABARAPL2 or MAP1LC3A) to the amino group of phosphatidylethanolamine
CC       (PE)-containing lipids in the membrane resulting in membrane-bound
CC       ATG8-like proteins which is one of the key steps in the development of
CC       autophagic isolation membranes during autophagosome formation. Cycles
CC       back and forth between binding to ATG7 for loading with the ATG8-like
CC       proteins and binding to E3 enzyme, composed of ATG12, ATG5 and ATG16L1
CC       to promote ATG8-like proteins lipidation (By similarity). Also plays a
CC       role as a membrane curvature sensor that facilitates LC3/GABARAP
CC       lipidation by sensing local membrane stress associated with lipid-
CC       packing defects as occurs with high molar proportions of conical lipids
CC       or strident membrane curvature (By similarity). Interacts with
CC       negatively-charged membranes promoting membrane tethering and enhancing
CC       LC3/GABARAP lipidation (By similarity). Also acts as an autocatalytic
CC       E2-like enzyme by catalyzing the conjugation of ATG12 to itself in an
CC       ATG7-dependent manner, this complex thus formed, plays a role in
CC       mitochondrial homeostasis but not in autophagy. ATG12-ATG3 conjugation
CC       promotes late endosome to lysosome trafficking and basal autophagosome
CC       maturation via its interaction with PDCD6IP. ATG12-ATG3 conjugate is
CC       also formed upon viccina virus infection, leading to the disruption the
CC       cellular autophagy which is not necessary for vaccinia survival and
CC       proliferation. Promotes primary ciliogenesis by removing OFD1 from
CC       centriolar satellites via the autophagic pathway (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9CPX6, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9NT62}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homdimer. Interacts with ATG7; this interaction forms an E1-E2
CC       complex that is essential for the transfer of GABARAP thioester from
CC       ATG7 to ATG3 and disrupts interaction with the E3 enzyme complex.
CC       Interacts with ATG12; this interaction is ATG7-dependent, essential for
CC       phosphatidylethanolamine (PE)-conjugated ATG8-like proteins formation
CC       and also mediates the autoconjugation of ATG12 on ATG3. Interacts with
CC       FNBP1L. Interacts with the E3 enzyme complex composed of 4 sets of
CC       ATG12-ATG5 and ATG16L1 (400 kDa); this interaction disrupts interaction
CC       with ATG7 and promotes ATG8-like proteins lipidation. Interacts with
CC       GABARAP and MAP1LC3A. Interacts with the ATG12-ATG5 conjugate; this
CC       interaction inhibits ATG8-like proteins lipidation (By similarity).
CC       Interacts (ATG12-ATG3 conjugate form) with PDCD6IP (via the BRO1
CC       domain); this interaction is bridged by ATG12 and promotes multiple
CC       PDCD6IP-mediated functions such as endolysosomal trafficking,
CC       macroautophagy and exosome biogenesis (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9CPX6, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9NT62}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9NT62}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The membrane-curvature-sensing motif targets curved membranes.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9CPX6}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The N-terminal region works in concert with its geometry-
CC       selective amphipathic helix (AH) to promote LC3-PE conjugation activity
CC       only on the target membrane. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9NT62}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The LC3 interacting regions (LIR) motif mediates interaction
CC       with GABARAP and MAP1LC3A in a beta-sheet conformation-dependent
CC       manner. The LIR motif is required for LC3 lipidation and ATG3~LC3
CC       thioester formation. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9NT62}.
CC   -!- PTM: Conjugated to ATG12 at Lys-243. ATG12-conjugation plays a role in
CC       regulation of mitochondrial homeostasis and cell death, while it is not
CC       involved in PE-conjugation to ATG8-like proteins and autophagy.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9CPX6}.
CC   -!- PTM: Cleaved by CASP8 upon death ligand binding such as tumor necrosis
CC       factor-alpha. CASP8 cleavage blocks survival-related autophagy and
CC       favors apoptosis. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9NT62}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the ATG3 family. {ECO:0000305}.
