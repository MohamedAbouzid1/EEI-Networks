CC   -!- FUNCTION: Deubiquitinating enzyme that plays a key role in chromatin by
CC       mediating deubiquitination of histone H2A and HCFC1. Catalytic
CC       component of the polycomb repressive deubiquitinase (PR-DUB) complex, a
CC       complex that specifically mediates deubiquitination of histone H2A
CC       monoubiquitinated at 'Lys-120' (H2AK119ub1). Does not deubiquitinate
CC       monoubiquitinated histone H2B. The PR-DUB complex is an epigenetic
CC       regulator of gene expression and acts as a transcriptional coactivator,
CC       affecting genes involved in development, cell communication, signaling,
CC       cell proliferation and cell viability. Antagonizes PRC1 mediated
CC       H2AK119ub1 monoubiquitination. As part of the PR-DUB complex,
CC       associates with chromatin enriched in histone marks H3K4me1, H3K4me3,
CC       and H3K27Ac, but not in H3K27me3. Acts as a regulator of cell growth by
CC       mediating deubiquitination of HCFC1 N-terminal and C-terminal chains,
CC       with some specificity toward 'Lys-48'-linked polyubiquitin chains
CC       compared to 'Lys-63'-linked polyubiquitin chains. Deubiquitination of
CC       HCFC1 does not lead to increase stability of HCFC1. Interferes with the
CC       BRCA1 and BARD1 heterodimer activity by inhibiting their ability to
CC       mediate ubiquitination and autoubiquitination. It however does not
CC       mediate deubiquitination of BRCA1 and BARD1. Able to mediate
CC       autodeubiquitination via intramolecular interactions to counteract
CC       monoubiquitination at the nuclear localization signal (NLS), thereby
CC       protecting it from cytoplasmic sequestration. Acts as a tumor
CC       suppressor. Negatively regulates epithelial-mesenchymal transition
CC       (EMT) of trophoblast stem cells during placental development by
CC       regulating genes involved in epithelial cell integrity, cell adhesion
CC       and cytoskeletal organization. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q92560}.

CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Core component of the polycomb repressive deubiquitinase (PR-
CC       DUB) complex, at least composed of BAP1, one of ASXL1, ASXL2 or
CC       (probably) ASXL3, and one of MBD5 or MBD6. The PR-DUB core associates
CC       with a number of accessory proteins, including FOXK1, FOXK2, KDM1B,
CC       HCFC1, YY1 and OGT; KDM1B specifically associates with ASXL2 PR-DUB
CC       complexes. The BAP1 deubiquitinase activity is not required for PR-DUB
CC       assembly. Homodimerize (via coiled-coil hinge-region between the UCH
CC       and UCH37-like C-terminal domains) to mediate assembly of 2 copies of
CC       the BAP1-ASXL heterodimer into a bisymmetric tetramer; dimerization
CC       enhances association with nucleosomes. The PR-DUB complex associates
CC       with nucleosomes to mediate deubiquitination of 'lys-120' of histone
CC       H2AK118ub1 substrates; the association requires the positively charged
CC       C-terminal tail of BAP1. Interacts (via UCH37-like C-terminal domain)
CC       with ASXL1 (via DEUBAD domain); the interaction is direct and forms a
CC       ubiquitin binding cleft. The interaction with ASXL1 stabilzes BAP1 but
CC       is not required for nucleosome binding. Associates (via C-terminus)
CC       with nucleosome and chromatosome complexes through direct interaction
CC       with DNA and the histone3/4 dimer; this association displaces the
CC       histone-2A C-terminal tail, extending and orienting the H2AK118ub1
CC       substrate towards the BAP1 deubiquitinase active site. Also interacts
CC       (via arginine finger) directly with the histone H2A-H2B acidic patch;
CC       this interaction is not critical for nucleosome-chromatosome
CC       association but may play a role in orienting the H2AK118ub1 substrate
CC       towards the PR-DUB complex active site. Interacts with BRCA1 (via the
CC       RING finger). Interacts (via HBM-like motif) with HCFC1. Interacts (via
CC       UCH37-like C-terminal domain) with YY1; the interaction is direct and
CC       requires the interaction with HCFC1. Interacts (when phosphorylated at
CC       Thr-491) with FOXK1. Interacts (when phosphorylated at Thr-491) with
CC       FOXK2; leading to recruitment of the PR-DUB complex and repression of
CC       FOXK2 target genes. Interacts (via non-classical PY-NLS) with
CC       TNPO1/transportin-1 (via HEAT repeats 8-12); the interaction is direct,
CC       mediates BAP1 nuclear localization and disrupts BAP1 homodimerization.
CC       Interacts (via C-terminus) with KPNA1/importin alpha5 and
CC       KPNA2/importin alpha1; these interactions can contribute to BAP1
CC       nuclear localization but are less important than the interaction with
CC       TNPO1/transportin-1. The interaction with TNPO1/transportin-1 disrupts
CC       homodimerization and blocks ubiquitination by UBE2O.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q92560}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q92560}. Nucleus
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q92560}. Chromosome
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q92560}. Note=Mainly nuclear. Binds to
CC       chromatin. Localizes to the cytoplasm when monoubiquitinated by the
CC       E2/E3 hybrid ubiquitin-protein ligase UBE2O (By similarity).
CC       Recruitment to chromatin is dependent on ASXL1/2/3 and recruitment to
CC       specific genes on FOXK1/2 (By similarity). Nuclear localization is
CC       redundantly mediated by the importin and transportin systems;
CC       TNPO1/transportin-1 is the major mediator of nuclear localization (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q92560,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q99PU7}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: Possesses 2 overlapping nuclear localization sequences (NLS), a
CC       classic bipartite NLS and a non-classical PY-NLS. The classical NLS
CC       probably mediates import via the importin alpha/beta system while the
CC       PY-NLS mediates nuclear import via the transportin system.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q92560}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The positively charged C-terminal tail stabilizes the
CC       interaction with nucleosomes/chromatosomes through interaction with the
CC       DNA backbone. Binding of ASXL1 just upstream of the positively charged
CC       C-terminal tail may stabilize its orientation to align the PR-DUB with
CC       its H2AK118ub1 substrate. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q92560}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The ubiquitin C-terminal hydrolase (UCH) domain, together with
CC       the DEUBAD domain of ASXL1, forms the ubiquitin binding cleft of the
CC       PR-DUB complex. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q92560}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The positively charged Arg-finger motif mediates interaction
CC       with the histone H2A-H2B acidic patch; this interaction is critical for
CC       nucleosomal H2AK119ub1 deubiquitination activity but not nucleosomal
CC       binding. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q92560}.
CC   -!- PTM: Ubiquitinated: monoubiquitinated at multiple sites within its
CC       nuclear localization signal (NLS) BY UBE2O, leading to cytoplasmic
CC       retention. Able to mediate autodeubiquitination via intramolecular
CC       interactions to counteract cytoplasmic retention. Monoubiquitinated on
CC       at least 4 sites near or within its PY-NLS.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q92560}.
