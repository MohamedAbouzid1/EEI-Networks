CC   -!- FUNCTION: Component of the BLOC-1 complex, a complex that is required
CC       for normal biogenesis of lysosome-related organelles (LRO), such as
CC       platelet dense granules and melanosomes. In concert with the AP-3
CC       complex, the BLOC-1 complex is required to target membrane protein
CC       cargos into vesicles assembled at cell bodies for delivery into
CC       neurites and nerve terminals. The BLOC-1 complex, in association with
CC       SNARE proteins, is also proposed to be involved in neurite extension.
CC       As part of the BORC complex may play a role in lysosomes movement and
CC       localization at the cell periphery. Associated with the cytosolic face
CC       of lysosomes, the BORC complex may recruit ARL8B and couple lysosomes
CC       to microtubule plus-end-directed kinesin motor. May play a role in cell
CC       proliferation. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q6QNY1,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9CWG9}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Component of the biogenesis of lysosome-related organelles
CC       complex 1 (BLOC-1) composed of BLOC1S1, BLOC1S2, BLOC1S3, BLOC1S4,
CC       BLOC1S5, BLOC1S6, DTNBP1/BLOC1S7 and SNAPIN/BLOC1S8. Octamer composed
CC       of one copy each BLOC1S1, BLOC1S2, BLOC1S3, BLOC1S4, BLOC1S5, BLOC1S6,
CC       DTNBP1/BLOC1S7 and SNAPIN/BLOC1S8. Interacts directly with BLOC1S1,
CC       BLOC1S3, BLOC1S4, BLOC1S5 and SNAPIN. The BLOC-1 complex associates
CC       with the AP-3 protein complex and membrane protein cargos. Component of
CC       the BLOC-one-related complex (BORC) which is composed of BLOC1S1,
CC       BLOC1S2, BORCS5, BORCS6, BORCS7, BORCS8, KXD1 and SNAPIN. Interacts
CC       with gamma-tubulin. Interacts with IFT57.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q6QNY1, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9CWG9}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm, cytoskeleton, microtubule organizing
CC       center, centrosome {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q6QNY1}. Lysosome membrane
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q6QNY1}. Note=Localizes to the centrosomes in a
CC       microtubule-dependent manner. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q6QNY1}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Widely expressed. Highly expressed in most brain
CC       regions, spinal cord, kidney and ovary. In the brain, expressed in the
CC       frontal cortex and the superior thalamic radiation, with the strongest
CC       expression in the granule cells of hippocampal CA1, CA3 and dentate
CC       gyrus regions. In the spinal cord, mainly expressed in the gray matter.
CC       Expression level is high in laminae I-II and V-VI small sensory neurons
CC       in the dorsal horn, as well as in the large motor neurons of the
CC       ventral horn. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16176350}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Overexpressed in injured nerves.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16176350}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the BLOC1S2 family. {ECO:0000305}.
