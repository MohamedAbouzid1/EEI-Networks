CC   -!- FUNCTION: Chromatin reader protein that specifically recognizes and
CC       binds histone H4 acetylated at 'Lys-5' and 'Lys-12' (H4K5ac and
CC       H4K12ac, respectively), thereby controlling gene expression and
CC       remodeling chromatin structures. Recruits transcription factors and
CC       coactivators to target gene sites, and activates RNA polymerase II
CC       machinery for transcriptional elongation. Plays a key role in genome
CC       compartmentalization via its association with CTCF and cohesin:
CC       recruited to chromatin by CTCF and promotes formation of topologically
CC       associating domains (TADs) via its ability to bind acetylated histones,
CC       contributing to CTCF boundary formation and enhancer insulation. Also
CC       recognizes and binds acetylated non-histone proteins, such as STAT3.
CC       Involved in inflammatory response by regulating differentiation of
CC       naive CD4(+) T-cells into T-helper Th17: recognizes and binds STAT3
CC       acetylated at 'Lys-87', promoting STAT3 recruitment to chromatin. In
CC       addition to acetylated lysines, also recognizes and binds lysine
CC       residues on histones that are both methylated and acetylated on the
CC       same side chain to form N6-acetyl-N6-methyllysine (Kacme), an
CC       epigenetic mark of active chromatin associated with increased
CC       transcriptional initiation. Specifically binds histone H4 acetyl-
CC       methylated at 'Lys-5' and 'Lys-12' (H4K5acme and H4K12acme,
CC       respectively). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P25440}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer. Interacts with E2F1. Interacts with (acetylated)
CC       STAT3; promoting STAT3 recruitment to chromatin (By similarity).
CC       Interacts with CTCF; promoting BRD2 recruitment to chromatin (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P25440,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q7JJ13}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Nucleus {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P25440}.
CC       Chromosome {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P25440}. Note=Detected on chromatin
CC       and nucleosomes. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P25440}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The first bromo domain specifically recognizes histone H4
CC       acetylated at 'Lys-12' (H4K12ac). It also specifically binds histone H4
CC       acetyl-methylated at 'Lys-5' and 'Lys-12' (H4K5acme and H4K12acme,
CC       respectively). The second bromo domain recognizes and binds histone H4
CC       acetylated at 'Lys-5' and 'Lys-12' (H4K5ac and H4K12ac, respectively).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P25440}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the BET family. {ECO:0000305}.
