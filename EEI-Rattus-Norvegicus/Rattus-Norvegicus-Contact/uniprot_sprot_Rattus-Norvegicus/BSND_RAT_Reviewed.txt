CC   -!- FUNCTION: Regulatory subunit of anion-selective CLCNKA:BSND and
CC       CLCNKB:BSND heteromeric channels involved in basolateral chloride
CC       conductance along the nephron to achieve urine concentration and
CC       maintain systemic acid-base homeostasis, and in the stria vascularis of
CC       the inner ear to establish the endocochlear potential necessary for
CC       normal hearing (By similarity). Most likely acts as a chaperone that
CC       allosterically regulates proper sorting of CLCNKA:BSND and CLCNKB:BSND
CC       channels at the basolateral plasma membrane domain and functional
CC       switch to ion conducting state. Mediates constitutive opening of
CC       channel common gates (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8WZ55}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts with CLCNK channels. Forms heteromers with CLCNKA in
CC       the thin ascending limb of Henle and with CLCNKB in the thick ascending
CC       limb and more distal segments. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8WZ55}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Basolateral cell membrane
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8WZ55}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000255}. Note=Mostly sorted at the basolateral membrane. A
CC       significant amount also observed intracellularly. Staining in membranes
CC       of the renal tubule and of potassium-secreting epithelia of the inner
CC       ear is basolateral (By similarity). {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed along the distal nephron.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12111250}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Regulated in parallel with CLCNKA under furosemide
CC       treatment. A significant decrease occurred after furosemide treatment
CC       in inner medulla (0.5 fold), whereas cortical and outer medulla levels
CC       remained unaffected. Regulation with CLCNKA in inner medulla is limited
CC       to the thin limb; levels in collecting ducts were not affected by
CC       furosemide treatment. During furosemide treatment selective down-
CC       regulation with CLCNKA in thin limb plays a role in maintaining salt
CC       and water homeostasis. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12759757}.
CC   -!- PTM: Palmitoylation is necessary for activation of plasma membrane-
CC       inserted CLC-K/barttin channels. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8WZ55}.
