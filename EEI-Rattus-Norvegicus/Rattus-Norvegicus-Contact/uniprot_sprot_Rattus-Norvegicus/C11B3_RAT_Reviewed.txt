CC   -!- FUNCTION: A cytochrome P450 monooxygenase involved in the biosynthesis
CC       of adrenal corticoids (PubMed:8674838). Catalyzes the hydroxylation of
CC       steroids at 11beta, 18- or 19-positions, with preferred
CC       regioselectivity at 11beta and 18 (PubMed:8674838). Converts 11-
CC       deoxycorticosterone into corticosterone, 18-hydroxy-11-
CC       deoxycorticosterone, and/or 19-hydroxy-11-deoxycorticosterone, but not
CC       to 18-hydroxycorticosterone or aldosterone (PubMed:8674838).
CC       Mechanistically, uses molecular oxygen inserting one oxygen atom into a
CC       substrate for hydroxylation and reducing the second into a water
CC       molecule. Two electrons are provided by NADPH via a two-protein
CC       mitochondrial transfer system comprising flavoprotein FDXR
CC       (adrenodoxin/ferredoxin reductase) and nonheme iron-sulfur protein FDX1
CC       or FDX2 (adrenodoxin/ferredoxin) (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P15538, ECO:0000269|PubMed:8674838}.
