CC   -!- FUNCTION: F-actin-capping proteins bind in a Ca(2+)-independent manner
CC       to the fast growing ends of actin filaments (barbed end) thereby
CC       blocking the exchange of subunits at these ends. Unlike other capping
CC       proteins (such as gelsolin and severin), these proteins do not sever
CC       actin filaments. May play a role in the formation of epithelial cell
CC       junctions (By similarity). Forms, with CAPZB, the barbed end of the
CC       fast growing ends of actin filaments in the dynactin complex and
CC       stabilizes dynactin structure. The dynactin multiprotein complex
CC       activates the molecular motor dynein for ultra-processive transport
CC       along microtubules (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:A0PFK5,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P52907}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Component of the F-actin capping complex, composed of a
CC       heterodimer of an alpha and a beta subunit. Subunit of dynactin, a
CC       multiprotein complex part of a tripartite complex with dynein and a
CC       adapter, such as BICDL1, BICD2 or HOOK3. The dynactin complex is built
CC       around ACTR1A/ACTB filament and consists of an actin-related filament
CC       composed of a shoulder domain, a pointed end and a barbed end. Its
CC       length is defined by its flexible shoulder domain. The soulder is
CC       composed of 2 DCTN1 subunits, 4 DCTN2 and 2 DCTN3. The 4 DCNT2 (via N-
CC       terminus) bind the ACTR1A filament and act as molecular rulers to
CC       determine the length. The pointed end is important for binding dynein-
CC       dynactin cargo adapters. Consists of 4 subunits: ACTR10, DCNT4, DCTN5
CC       and DCTN6. The barbed end is composed of a CAPZA1:CAPZB heterodimers,
CC       which binds ACTR1A/ACTB filament and dynactin and stabilizes dynactin
CC       (By similarity). Component of the WASH complex, composed of F-actin-
CC       capping protein subunit alpha (CAPZA1, CAPZA2 or CAPZA3), F-actin-
CC       capping protein subunit beta (CAPZB), WASHC1, WASHC2, WASHC3, WASHC4
CC       and WASHC5. Interacts with S100B (By similarity). Interacts with
CC       S100A1. Interacts with SH3BP1; recruits CAPZA1 to forming cell
CC       junctions. Interacts with CD2AP (By similarity). Directly interacts
CC       with CRACD; this interaction decreases binding to actin (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:A0PFK5,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P52907, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19452629}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm, cytoskeleton
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19343716}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the F-actin-capping protein alpha subunit
CC       family. {ECO:0000255}.
