CC   -!- FUNCTION: Plays a critical role in cortical radial and GnRH neurons
CC       migration during brain development. Regulates cortical radial migration
CC       by negatively controlling the activity of histone deacetylase 6 (HDAC6)
CC       and promotes centrosome maturation. CAMDI is required for dilation
CC       formation of cortical neurons during radial migration. Plays a critical
CC       role in learning and memory performance through regulation of AMPA-
CC       selective glutamate receptors (AMPARs) cell surface expression in
CC       competition with KIBRA. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:E9Q8Q6}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts with DISC1. Interacts preferentially with
CC       phosphorylated forms of myosin regulatory light chain (MRLC) (By
CC       similarity). Interacts (via the N-terminal region) with HDAC6; inhibits
CC       the deacetylase activity of HDAC6 (PubMed:27737934). Interacts with
CC       KIBRA (via the C-terminal region); retains AMPAR in the cytosol after
CC       internalization (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:E9Q8Q6,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:27737934}.
CC   -!- INTERACTION:
CC       D3ZEY0; Q9Z2V5: Hdac6; Xeno; NbExp=3; IntAct=EBI-21448776, EBI-1009256;
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:E9Q8Q6}.
CC       Cytoplasm, cytoskeleton, microtubule organizing center, centrosome
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:E9Q8Q6}. Note=Co-localized with DISC1 at/around
CC       the centrosome. Localizes to the centrosome, at least in part, in a
CC       DISC1-dependent manner. Accumulates and oscillates at the dilation in
CC       cortical neurons during migration. CAMDI protein level is stabilized at
CC       the G1 phase and destabilized at the G2 /M phase.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:E9Q8Q6}.
CC   -!- PTM: Ubiquitinated and degradated by the CDC20-APC/C pathway. During
CC       brain development, CDC20-APC/C complex degrades CCDC141 after
CC       centrosome translocation into the dilated area. CCDC141 is restabilized
CC       in the dilation until the centrosome enters the dilation, at which
CC       point it is once again immediately destabilized by CDC20-APC/C complex.
CC       The oscillatory regulation of CCDC141 protein is needed for proper
CC       cortical migration. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:E9Q8Q6}.
CC   -!- PTM: Phosphorylation at Thr-91 by PLK1 affects CCDC141 degradation.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:E9Q8Q6}.
