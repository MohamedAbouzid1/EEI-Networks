CC   -!- FUNCTION: Component of the multi-pass translocon (MPT) complex that
CC       mediates insertion of multi-pass membrane proteins into the lipid
CC       bilayer of membranes. The MPT complex takes over after the SEC61
CC       complex: following membrane insertion of the first few transmembrane
CC       segments of proteins by the SEC61 complex, the MPT complex occludes the
CC       lateral gate of the SEC61 complex to promote insertion of subsequent
CC       transmembrane regions (By similarity). Within the MPT complex, the PAT
CC       subcomplex sequesters any highly polar regions in the transmembrane
CC       domains away from the non-polar membrane environment until they can be
CC       buried in the interior of the fully assembled protein. Within the PAT
CC       subcomplex, CCDC47 occludes the lateral gate of the SEC61 complex (By
CC       similarity). Involved in the regulation of calcium ion homeostasis in
CC       the ER. Required for proper protein degradation via the ERAD (ER-
CC       associated degradation) pathway (By similarity). Has an essential role
CC       in the maintenance of ER organization during embryogenesis (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:A0A8I3P7X4,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q96A33, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9D024}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Component of the PAT complex, composed of WDR83OS/Asterix and
CC       CCDC47. The PAT complex is part of the multi-pass translocon (MPT)
CC       complex, composed of three subcomplexes, the GEL complex (composed of
CC       RAB5IF/OPTI and TMCO1), the BOS complex (composed of NCLN/Nicalin,
CC       NOMO1 and TMEM147) and the PAT complex (composed of WDR83OS/Asterix and
CC       CCDC47). The MPT complex associates with the SEC61 complex (By
CC       similarity). Interacts with VCP, HSPA5, DERL1, DERL2 and SELENOS (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q96A33,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9D024}.

CC       {ECO:0000255}. Rough endoplasmic reticulum membrane
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9D024}; Single-pass type I membrane protein
