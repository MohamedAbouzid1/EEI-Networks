CC   -!- FUNCTION: Regulates the gating properties of AMPA-selective glutamate
CC       receptors (AMPARs). Modulates their gating properties by accelerating
CC       their rates of activation, deactivation and desensitization. Displays
CC       subunit-specific AMPA receptor regulation. Shows specificity for GRIA1,
CC       GRIA4 and the long isoform of GRIA2. According to PubMed:18817736,
CC       shows only specificity for GRIA2 and specifically to the form of GRIA2
CC       for which a single amino acid in the pore region has been edited from a
CC       glutamine to an arginine residue. Thought to stabilize the calcium
CC       channel in an inactivated (closed) state. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18817736,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19234459}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: The L-type calcium channel is composed of five subunits:
CC       alpha-1, alpha-2/delta, beta and gamma. Acts as an auxiliary subunit
CC       for AMPA-selective glutamate receptors (AMPARs). Found in a complex
CC       with GRIA1, GRIA2, GRIA3, GRIA4, CNIH2, CNIH3, CACNG2, CACNG3, CACNG4,
CC       CACNG7 and CACNG8. Interacts with GRIA1, GRIA2, GRIA3 and GRIA4.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18817736, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19234459,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19265014}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Membrane {ECO:0000250}; Multi-pass membrane
CC       protein {ECO:0000250}. Postsynaptic density membrane
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18817736}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the PMP-22/EMP/MP20 family. CACNG subfamily.
CC       {ECO:0000305}.
