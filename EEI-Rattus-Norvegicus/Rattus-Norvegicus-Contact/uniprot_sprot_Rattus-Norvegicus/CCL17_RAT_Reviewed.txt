CC   -!- FUNCTION: Chemokine, which displays chemotactic activity for T
CC       lymphocytes, preferentially Th2 cells, but not monocytes or
CC       granulocytes. Therefore plays an important role in a wide range of
CC       inflammatory and immunological processes. Acts by binding to CCR4 at T-
CC       cell surface. Mediates GM-CSF/CSF2-driven pain and inflammation (By
CC       similarity). In the brain, required to maintain the typical, highly
CC       branched morphology of hippocampal microglia under homeostatic
CC       conditions. May be important for the appropriate adaptation of
CC       microglial morphology and synaptic plasticity to acute
CC       lipopolysaccharide (LPS)-induced neuroinflammation. Plays a role in
CC       wound healing, mainly by inducing fibroblast migration into the wound
CC       (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q92583,
