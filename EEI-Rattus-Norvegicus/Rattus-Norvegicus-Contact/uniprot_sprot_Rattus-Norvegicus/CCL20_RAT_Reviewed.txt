CC   -!- FUNCTION: Acts as a ligand for C-C chemokine receptor CCR6. Signals
CC       through binding and activation of CCR6 and induces a strong chemotactic
CC       response and mobilization of intracellular calcium ions. The ligand-
CC       receptor pair CCL20-CCR6 is responsible for the chemotaxis of dendritic
CC       cells (DC), effector/memory T-cells and B-cells and plays an important
CC       role at skin and mucosal surfaces under homeostatic and inflammatory
CC       conditions, as well as in pathology, including cancer and autoimmune
CC       diseases. CCL20 acts as a chemotactic factor that attracts lymphocytes
CC       and, slightly, neutrophils, but not monocytes. Involved in the
CC       recruitment of both the pro-inflammatory IL17 producing helper T-cells
CC       (Th17) and the regulatory T-cells (Treg) to sites of inflammation.
CC       Required for optimal migration of thymic natural regulatory T cells
CC       (nTregs) and DN1 early thymocyte progenitor cells. Positively regulates
CC       sperm motility and chemotaxis via its binding to CCR6 which triggers
CC       Ca2+ mobilization in the sperm which is important for its motility. May
CC       be involved in formation and function of the mucosal lymphoid tissues
CC       by attracting lymphocytes and dendritic cells towards epithelial cells.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O89093, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P78556}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Secreted {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P78556}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Low levels in thymus and lung.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: By TNF in experimental allergic panencaphalomyelitis (EAP)
CC       and by TNF and IL-1 in primary astrocytes.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the intercrine beta (chemokine CC) family.
CC       {ECO:0000305}.
