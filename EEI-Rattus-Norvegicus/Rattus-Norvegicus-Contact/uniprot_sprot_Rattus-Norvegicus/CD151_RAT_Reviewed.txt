CC   -!- FUNCTION: Structural component of specialized membrane microdomains
CC       known as tetraspanin-enriched microdomains (TERMs), which act as
CC       platforms for receptor clustering and signaling. Plays a role in
CC       various cellular and molecular mechanism through its association with
CC       both integrin and non-integrin proteins. These interactions facilitate
CC       critical cellular functions, including cell-to-cell communication,
CC       wound healing, platelet aggregation, trafficking, cell motility, and
CC       angiogenesis. Via interaction with JAM-A/F11R and integrin ITGA3:ITGB1,
CC       promotes the recruitment of signaling molecules such as RAC1, CDC42 and
CC       RhoGTPases to facilitate the polarization of epithelial cells and the
CC       reorganization of the actin cytoskeleton, which are critical steps in
CC       cell migration process. Regulates the glycosylation pattern of
CC       ITGA3:ITGB1 thereby modulating its activity. Plays an essential role in
CC       the maintenance of central laminin-binding integrin ITGA6:ITGB4-
CC       containing adhesion complexes. Essential for the proper assembly of the
CC       glomerular and tubular basement membranes in kidney. Contributes to T-
CC       cell activation by modulating integrin signaling leading to activation
CC       of downstream targets PTK2 and MAPK1/MAPK3.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P48509}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts with integrins ITGA3:ITGB1, ITGA5:ITGB1, ITGA3:ITGB1
CC       and ITGA6:ITGB4 and with CD9 and CD181. Interacts (via the second
CC       extracellular domain) with integrin ITGAV:ITGB3. Interacts with ITGA3;
CC       this interaction modulates ITGA3 glycosylation pattern. Interacts with
CC       F11R. Interacts with RAC1 and CDC42; these interactions mediate
CC       physical association of RAC1 and CDC42 with integrin adhesion receptor
CC       complexes. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P48509}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P48509};
CC       Multi-pass membrane protein {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P48509}.
CC       Note=Relocalizes to the immune synapse in T-cells upon activation.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P48509}.
CC   -!- PTM: Palmitoylated. Palmitoylation by ZDHHC2 regulates CD151
CC       expression, association with other tetraspanin family proteins and
CC       function in cell adhesion. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P48509}.
CC   -!- PTM: Ubiquitinated by RNF128 on lysine residues present in the
CC       tetraspanin amino terminus via 'Lys-48'-linked ubiquitin leading to
CC       proteasomal degradation. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P48509}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the tetraspanin (TM4SF) family. {ECO:0000305}.
