CC   -!- FUNCTION: Receptor that plays a role in T-cell activation,
CC       proliferation, survival and the maintenance of immune homeostasis.
CC       Functions not only as an amplifier of TCR signals but delivers unique
CC       signals that control intracellular biochemical events that alter the
CC       gene expression program of T-cells. Stimulation upon engagement of its
CC       cognate ligands CD80 or CD86 increases proliferation and expression of
CC       various cytokines in particular IL2 production in both CD4(+) and
CC       CD8(+) T-cell subsets. Mechanistically, ligation induces recruitment of
CC       protein kinase C-theta/PRKCQ and GRB2 leading to NF-kappa-B activation
CC       via both PI3K/Akt-dependent and -independent pathways. In conjunction
CC       with TCR/CD3 ligation and CD40L costimulation, enhances the production
CC       of IL4 and IL10 in T-cells. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P10747}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer; disulfide-linked. Interacts with DUSP14. Binds to
CC       CD80/B7-1 and CD86/B7-2/B70. Interacts with GRB2. Interacts with
CC       PIK3R1. Interacts with PRKCQ. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P10747}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P10747};
CC       Single-pass type I membrane protein {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P10747}.
CC   -!- PTM: Phosphorylated by LCK. Dephosphorylated by PTPN11.
