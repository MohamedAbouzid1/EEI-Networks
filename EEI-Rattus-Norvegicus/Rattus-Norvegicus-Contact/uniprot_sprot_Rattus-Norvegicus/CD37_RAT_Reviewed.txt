CC   -!- FUNCTION: Structural component of specialized membrane microdomains
CC       known as tetraspanin-enriched microdomains (TERMs), which act as
CC       platforms for receptor clustering and signaling. Participates thereby
CC       in diverse biological functions such as cell signal transduction,
CC       adhesion, migration and protein trafficking. Upon ligand binding, two
CC       signaling pathways are activated, one acting through phosphorylation by
CC       LYN leading to cell death or a survival pathway with activation of
CC       GSK3B (By similarity). Plays an essential role essential for clustering
CC       of integrin ITGA4/ITGB1 and promotes its mobility in the plasma
CC       membrane of B-cells. In turn, participates in ITGA4/ITGB1 integrin-
CC       mediated antiapoptotic signaling through AKT (By similarity). Plays
CC       also a role in the migration of dendritic cells and neutrophils to
CC       draining lymph nodes, as well as in their integrin-mediated adhesion
CC       (By similarity). Negatively regulates IL-6 responses through direct
CC       interaction with SOCS3 thereby preventing constitutive IL-6 signaling.
CC       Alternatively, inhibition of IL-6 signaling can also occur via
CC       interaction and stabilization of DECTIN1/CLEC7A at the cell membrane to
CC       inhibit its ability to promote the production of IL-6 (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P11049, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q61451}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts with SCIMP. Interacts with SOCS3. Interacts with
CC       DECTIN1/CLEC7A. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P11049}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P11049};
CC       Multi-pass membrane protein {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P11049}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: B-lymphocytes.
CC   -!- PTM: Tyrosine phosphorylated; leading to activation of downstream
CC       signaling pathways. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P11049}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the tetraspanin (TM4SF) family. {ECO:0000305}.
