CC   -!- FUNCTION: Structural component of specialized membrane microdomains
CC       known as tetraspanin-enriched microdomains (TERMs), which act as
CC       platforms for receptor clustering and signaling. Participates thereby
CC       in diverse biological functions such as cell signal transduction,
CC       adhesion, migration and protein trafficking. Acts as a attenuator of
CC       EGF signaling, facilitating ligand-induced endocytosis of the receptor
CC       and its subsequent desensitization. Mechanistically, modulates ligand-
CC       induced ubiquitination and trafficking of EGFR via E3 ligase CBL
CC       phosphorylation by PKC. Increases cell-matrix adhesion by regulating
CC       the membrane organization of integrin alpha4/ITA4. Modulates adhesion
CC       and suppresses cell migration through other integrins such as the
CC       alpha6/ITGA6 and beta1/ITGB1. Decreases cell-associated plasminogen
CC       activation by interfering with the interaction between urokinase-type
CC       plasminogen activator/PLAU and its receptor PLAUR (By similarity).
CC       Associates with CD4 or CD8 and delivers costimulatory signals for the
CC       TCR/CD3 pathway. Plays a role in the restrains phagocyte migration but
CC       supports macrophage activation (By similarity). Plays a role in TLR9
CC       trafficking to acidified CpG-containing compartments by controlling
CC       interaction between TLR9 and VAMP3 and subsequent myddosome assembly
CC       (By similarity). Inhibits LPS-induced inflammatory response by
CC       preventing binding of LPS to TLR4 on the cell surface (By similarity).
CC       Plays a role in the activation of macrophages into anti-inflammatory
CC       phenotypes (By similarity). Independently of Toll-like receptor (TLR)
CC       signaling, is recruited to pathogen-containing phagosomes prior to
CC       fusion with lysosomes and participates in antigen presentation (By
CC       similarity). Acts also to control angiogenesis and switch angiogenic
CC       milieu to quiescent state by binding and sequestering VEGFA and PDGFA
CC       to inhibit the signaling they trigger via their respective cell surface
CC       receptor (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P27701,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P40237}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Forms homooligomers. Interacts directly with IGSF8. Interacts
CC       with EGFR (By similarity). Interacts with VEGFA and PDGFA (By
CC       similarity). Interacts with ITGA4. Interacts with ITGA6; this
CC       interaction reduces ITGA6 cell surface expression. Interacts with
CC       ITGB1. Interacts with TLR4; this interaction inhibits TLR4-mediated
CC       signaling pathway (By similarity). Interacts with TLR9 (By similarity).
CC       Interacts with PLAUR (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P27701,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P40237}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P27701};
CC       Multi-pass membrane protein {ECO:0000255}.
CC   -!- PTM: Palmitoylated. Palmitoylation contributes to oligomerization and
CC       surface expression. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P27701}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the tetraspanin (TM4SF) family. {ECO:0000305}.
