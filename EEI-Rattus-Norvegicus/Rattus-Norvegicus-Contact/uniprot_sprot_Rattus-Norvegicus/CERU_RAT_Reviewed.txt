CC   -!- FUNCTION: Multifunctional blue, copper-binding (6-7 atoms per molecule)
CC       glycoprotein. It has ferroxidase activity oxidizing Fe(2+) to Fe(3+)
CC       without releasing radical oxygen species. It is involved in iron
CC       transport across the cell membrane. Copper ions provide a large number
CC       of enzymatic activites. Oxidizes highly toxic ferrous ions to the
CC       ferric state for further incorporation onto apo-transferrins, catalyzes
CC       Cu(+) oxidation and promotes the oxidation of biogenic amines such as
CC       norepinephrin and serotonin (By similarity). Provides Cu(2+) ions for
CC       the ascorbate-mediated deaminase degradation of the heparan sulfate
CC       chains of GPC1 (PubMed:14707133). Has glutathione peroxidase-like
CC       activity, can remove both hydrogen peroxide and lipid hydroperoxide in
CC       the presence of thiols. Also shows NO-oxidase and NO2 synthase
CC       activities that determine endocrine NO homeostasis (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P00450, ECO:0000269|PubMed:14707133}.
