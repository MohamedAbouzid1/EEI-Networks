CC   -!- FUNCTION: Lipid transferase specifically expressed in white adipose
CC       tissue, which promotes unilocular lipid droplet formation by mediating
CC       lipid droplet fusion (By similarity). Lipid droplet fusion promotes
CC       their enlargement, restricting lipolysis and favoring lipid storage (By
CC       similarity). Localizes on the lipid droplet surface, at focal contact
CC       sites between lipid droplets, and mediates atypical lipid droplet
CC       fusion by undergoing liquid-liquid phase separation (LLPS) and
CC       promoting directional net neutral lipid transfer from the smaller to
CC       larger lipid droplets (By similarity). The transfer direction may be
CC       driven by the internal pressure difference between the contacting lipid
CC       droplet pair (By similarity). Its role in neutral lipid transfer and
CC       lipid droplet enlargement is activated by the interaction with PLIN1
CC       (By similarity). May also act as a CEBPB coactivator in the white
CC       adipose tissue to control the expression of a subset of CEBPB
CC       downstream target genes, including SOCS1, SOCS3, TGFB1, TGFBR1, ID2 and
CC       XDH (PubMed:23233732). When overexpressed in preadipocytes, induces
CC       apoptosis or increases cell susceptibility to apoptosis induced by
CC       serum deprivation or TGFB treatment (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P56198, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q96AQ7,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:23233732}.

CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer (By similarity). Interacts with CIDEA (By
CC       similarity). Homooligomer; undergoes liquid-liquid phase separation
CC       (LLPS) via its N-terminus, facilitating lipid droplet fusion, occurs at
CC       the lipid droplet contact sites (By similarity). Interacts with PLIN1
CC       (By similarity). Interacts with NFAT5; this interaction is direct and
CC       retains NFAT5 in the cytoplasm (PubMed:23233732). Interacts with CEBPB
CC       (By similarity). Interacts with isoform CLSTN3beta of CLSTN3;
CC       inhibiting the lipid transferase activity of CIDEC (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P56198, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q96AQ7,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:23233732}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Lipid droplet {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P56198}.
CC       Endoplasmic reticulum {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P56198}. Nucleus
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P56198}. Note=Diffuses quickly on lipid droplet
CC       surface, but becomes trapped and clustered at lipid droplet contact
CC       sites, thereby enabling its rapid enrichment at lipid droplet contact
CC       sites. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P56198}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Down-regulated upon hypertonic conditions.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:23233732}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The CIDE-N domain is involved in homodimerization which is
CC       crucial for its function in promoting lipid exchange and transfer.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P56198}.
CC   -!- PTM: Ubiquitinated and targeted to proteasomal degradation, resulting
CC       in a short half-life (about 15 minutes in 3T3-L1 cells). Protein
CC       stability depends on triaclyglycerol synthesis, fatty acid availability
CC       and lipid droplet formation. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P56198}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the CIDE family. {ECO:0000305}.
