CC   -!- FUNCTION: L-cysteine transaminase that catalyzes the reversible
CC       transfer of the amino group from L-cysteine to the alpha-keto acid 2-
CC       oxoglutarate to respectively form 2-oxo-3-sulfanylpropanoate and L-
CC       glutamate (By similarity). The catalytic cycle occurs in the presence
CC       of pyridoxal 5'-phosphate (PLP) cofactor that facilitates
CC       transamination by initially forming an internal aldimine with the
CC       epsilon-amino group of active site Lys-55 residue on the enzyme (PLP-
CC       enzyme aldimine), subsequently displaced by formation of an external
CC       aldimine with the substrate amino group (PLP-L-cysteine aldimine). The
CC       external aldimine is further deprotonated to form a carbanion
CC       intermediate, which in the presence of 2-oxoglutarate regenerates PLP
CC       yielding final products 2-oxo-3-sulfanylpropanoate and L-glutamate. The
CC       proton transfer in carbanion intermediate is suggested to be controlled
CC       by the active site lysine residue, whereas PLP stabilizes carbanion
CC       structure through electron delocalization, also known as the electron
CC       sink effect (By similarity). Plays a key role in regulating maximal
CC       capacity for electron transport and oxidative phosphorylation (By
CC       similarity). May be involved in iron-sulfur cluster shuttling and/or in
CC       redox reactions. Can transfer the [2Fe-2S] cluster to an apo-acceptor
CC       protein only when in the oxidation state, likely serving as a redox
CC       sensor that regulates mitochondrial iron-sulfur cluster assembly and
CC       iron trafficking upon oxidative stress (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q91WS0, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9NZ45}.
