CC   -!- FUNCTION: Substrate adapter of E3 ligase complexes mediating
CC       ufmylation, the covalent attachment of the ubiquitin-like modifier UFM1
CC       to substrate proteins, and which is involved in various processes, such
CC       as ribosome recycling and reticulophagy (also called ER-phagy) (By
CC       similarity). As part of the UREL complex, plays a key role in ribosome
CC       recycling by promoting mono-ufmylation of RPL26/uL24 subunit of the 60S
CC       ribosome (By similarity). Ufmylation of RPL26/uL24 occurs on free 60S
CC       ribosomes following ribosome dissociation: it weakens the junction
CC       between post-termination 60S subunits and SEC61 translocons, promoting
CC       release and recycling of the large ribosomal subunit from the
CC       endoplasmic reticulum membrane (By similarity). Ufmylation of
CC       RPL26/uL24 and subsequent 60S ribosome recycling either take place
CC       after normal termination of translation or after ribosome stalling
CC       during cotranslational translocation at the endoplasmic reticulum (By
CC       similarity). Within the UREL complex, CDK5RAP3 acts as a substrate
CC       adapter that constrains UFL1 ligase activity to mono-ufmylate
CC       RPL26/uL24 at 'Lys-134' (By similarity). The UREL complex is also
CC       involved in reticulophagy in response to endoplasmic reticulum stress
CC       by promoting ufmylation of proteins such as CYB5R3, thereby promoting
CC       lysosomal degradation of ufmylated proteins (By similarity). Also acts
CC       as a regulator of transcription: negatively regulates NF-kappa-B-
CC       mediated gene transcription through the control of RELA phosphorylation
CC       (By similarity). Also regulates mitotic G2/M transition checkpoint and
CC       mitotic G2 DNA damage checkpoint (By similarity). Through its
CC       interaction with CDKN2A/ARF and MDM2 may induce MDM2-dependent p53/TP53
CC       ubiquitination, stabilization and activation in the nucleus, thereby
CC       promoting G1 cell cycle arrest and inhibition of cell proliferation (By
CC       similarity). May also play a role in the rupture of the nuclear
CC       envelope during apoptosis (By similarity). May regulate MAPK14 activity
CC       by regulating its dephosphorylation by PPM1D/WIP1 (By similarity).
CC       Required for liver development (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q96JB5, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q99LM2}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Substrate adapter component of the UFM1 ribosome E3 ligase
CC       (UREL) complex, composed of UFL1, DDRGK1 and CDK5RAP3 (By similarity).
CC       Interaction with UFL1 anchors CDK5RAP3 in the cytoplasm, preventing its
CC       translocation to the nucleus which allows expression of the CCND1
CC       cyclin and progression of cells through the G1/S transition
CC       (PubMed:20531390). Interacts with ATG8 family proteins MAP1LC3A,
CC       MAP1LC3B, GABARAP, GABARAPL1 and GABARAPL2 (By similarity). Interacts
CC       with CDK5R1; competes with CDK5RAP1 and CDK5RAP2 (PubMed:10721722).
CC       Interacts with RELA (By similarity). Interacts with CHEK1; may
CC       negatively regulate CHEK1 and thereby stimulate entry into mitosis (By
CC       similarity). Interacts with CDKN2A/ARF and MDM2; forms a ternary
CC       complex involved in regulation of p53/TP53 (By similarity). Interacts
CC       with MAPK14 (By similarity). Interacts with CCNB1 (By similarity).
CC       Interacts with TUBG1; may regulate CDK5RAP3 in mitotic G2/M transition
CC       checkpoint (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q96JB5,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:10721722, ECO:0000269|PubMed:20531390}.

CC       failing heart. Highly expressed in the ventricular section in subacute
CC       and chronic ischemic heart failure. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12054757}.
