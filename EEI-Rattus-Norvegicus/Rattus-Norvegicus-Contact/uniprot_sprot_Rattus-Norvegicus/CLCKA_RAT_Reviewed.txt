CC   -!- FUNCTION: Anion-selective channel permeable to small monovalent anions
CC       with ion selectivity for chloride > bromide > nitrate > iodide (By
CC       similarity). Forms a homodimeric channel where each subunit has its own
CC       ion conduction pathway. Conducts double-barreled currents controlled by
CC       two types of gates, two fast gates that control each subunit
CC       independently and a slow common gate that opens and shuts off both
CC       subunits simultaneously (PubMed:12111250, PubMed:16849430,
CC       PubMed:20538786). Assembles with the regulatory subunit BSND/Barttin
CC       for sorting at the basolateral plasma membrane domain. CLCNKA:BSND
CC       channels are activated upon membrane hyperpolarization mostly
CC       controlled by fast gating (PubMed:12111250, PubMed:16849430,
CC       PubMed:20538786). Mediates transepithelial chloride transport from the
CC       lumen to interstitial compartment along the thin ascending limb of
CC       Henle's loop, contributing to generation of hypertonic medullary
CC       interstitium as a countercurrent system to achieve urine concentration
CC       (By similarity). Conducts chloride currents in the stria vascularis of
CC       the inner ear to establish the endocochlear potential necessary for
CC       normal hearing (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P51800,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9WUB7, ECO:0000269|PubMed:12111250,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:16849430, ECO:0000269|PubMed:20538786}.

CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: Activated by extracellular Ca(2+) and inhibited by
CC       extracellular acidic pH. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12111250}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer. Interacts with BSND.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P51800}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Basolateral cell membrane
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9WUB7}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000255}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed predominantly in the kidney. Expressed
CC       strongly in the cortical thick ascending limb and the distal convoluted
CC       tubule, with minor expression in the S3 segment of the proximal tubule
CC       and the cortical collecting tubule. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:8041726}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Regulated in parallel with BSND under furosemide treatment.
CC       Decreased to half in the inner medulla under furosemide treatment. In
CC       the renal cortex and outer medulla levels were weak and did not change.
CC       Regulation with BSND in inner medulla is limited to the thin limb;
CC       levels in collecting ducts were not affected by furosemide treatment.
CC       During furosemide treatment selective down-regulation with BSND in thin
CC       limb plays a role in maintaining salt and water homeostasis.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12759757}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the chloride channel (TC 2.A.49) family. CLCNKA
CC       subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
