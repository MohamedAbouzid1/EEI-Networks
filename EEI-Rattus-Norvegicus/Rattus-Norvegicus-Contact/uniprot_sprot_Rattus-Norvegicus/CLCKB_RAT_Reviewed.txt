CC   -!- FUNCTION: Anion-selective channel permeable to small monovalent anions
CC       with ion selectivity for chloride > bromide > nitrate > iodide (By
CC       similarity). Forms a homodimeric channel where each subunit has its own
CC       ion conduction pathway. May conduct double-barreled currents controlled
CC       by two types of gates, two fast gates that control each subunit
CC       independently and a slow common gate that opens and shuts off both
CC       subunits simultaneously (By similarity). Assembles with the regulatory
CC       subunit BSND/Barttin for sorting at the basolateral plasma membrane
CC       domain and functional switch to the ion conducting state. CLCNKB:BSND
CC       channels display mostly a linear current-voltage relationship
CC       controlled by common gate (By similarity). Mediates chloride
CC       conductance along nephron segments, namely the thick ascending limb of
CC       Henle's loop, convoluted tubule and the collecting duct, contributing
CC       to the maintenance of systemic acid-base and electrolyte homeostasis
CC       (By similarity). Conducts chloride currents in the stria vascularis of
CC       the inner ear to establish the endocochlear potential necessary for
CC       normal hearing (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P51801,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9WUB6}.
