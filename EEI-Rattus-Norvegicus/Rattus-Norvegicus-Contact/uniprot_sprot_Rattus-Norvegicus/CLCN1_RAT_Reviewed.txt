CC   -!- FUNCTION: Voltage-gated chloride channel involved in skeletal muscle
CC       excitability. Generates most of the plasma membrane chloride
CC       conductance in skeletal muscle fibers, stabilizes the resting membrane
CC       potential and contributes to the repolarization phase during action
CC       potential firing (By similarity) (PubMed:1659664, PubMed:9565403).
CC       Forms a homodimeric channel where each subunit has its own ion
CC       conduction pathway. Conducts double-barreled currents controlled by two
CC       types of gates, two fast glutamate gates that control each subunit
CC       independently and a slow common gate that opens and shuts off both
CC       subunits simultaneously. Has a significant open probability at muscle
CC       resting potential and is further activated upon membrane depolarization
CC       (By similarity). Permeable to small monovalent anions with ion
CC       selectivity for chloride > thiocyanate > bromide > nitrate > iodide
CC       (PubMed:1659664, PubMed:9565403). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P35523,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:1659664, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9565403}.

CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: Modulated by membrane voltage with depolarization
CC       favouring channel opening and hyperpolarization favouring channel
CC       closure. Inhibited by acidic pH and ATP binding due to a shift of
CC       voltage dependence of common gating to more positive voltages.
CC       Inhibited by 9-anthracene-carboxylic acid.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P35523, ECO:0000269|PubMed:1659664}.
