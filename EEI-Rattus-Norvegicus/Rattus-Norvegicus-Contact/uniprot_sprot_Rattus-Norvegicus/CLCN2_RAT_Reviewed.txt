CC   -!- FUNCTION: Voltage-gated and osmosensitive chloride channel. Forms a
CC       homodimeric channel where each subunit has its own ion conduction
CC       pathway. Conducts double-barreled currents controlled by two types of
CC       gates, two fast glutamate gates that control each subunit independently
CC       and a slow common gate that opens and shuts off both subunits
CC       simultaneously. Displays inward rectification currents activated upon
CC       membrane hyperpolarization and extracellular hypotonicity
CC       (PubMed:12163466, PubMed:1311421, PubMed:1334533, PubMed:22405205).
CC       Contributes to chloride conductance involved in neuron excitability. In
CC       hippocampal neurons, generates a significant part of resting membrane
CC       conductance and provides an additional chloride efflux pathway to
CC       prevent chloride accumulation in dendrites upon GABA receptor
CC       activation. In glia, associates with the auxiliary subunit
CC       HEPACAM/GlialCAM at astrocytic processes and myelinated fiber tracts
CC       where it may regulate transcellular chloride flux buffering
CC       extracellular chloride and potassium concentrations (By similarity)
CC       (PubMed:8816717). Regulates aldosterone production in adrenal glands.
CC       The opening of CLCN2 channels at hyperpolarized membrane potentials in
CC       the glomerulosa causes cell membrane depolarization, activation of
CC       voltage-gated calcium channels and increased expression of aldosterone
CC       synthase, the rate-limiting enzyme for aldosterone biosynthesis (By
CC       similarity). Contributes to chloride conductance in retinal pigment
CC       epithelium involved in phagocytosis of shed photoreceptor outer
CC       segments and photoreceptor renewal (By similarity). Conducts chloride
CC       currents at the basolateral membrane of epithelial cells with a role in
CC       chloride reabsorption rather than secretion (By similarity). Permeable
CC       to small monovalent anions with chloride > thiocyanate > bromide >
CC       nitrate > iodide ion selectivity (By similarity) (PubMed:1311421,
CC       PubMed:22405205, PubMed:8816717). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P51788,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9R0A1, ECO:0000269|PubMed:12163466,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:1311421, ECO:0000269|PubMed:1334533,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:22405205, ECO:0000269|PubMed:8816717}.
