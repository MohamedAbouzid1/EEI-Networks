CC   -!- FUNCTION: Forms paracellular channels: coassembles with CLDN16 into
CC       tight junction strands with cation-selective channels through the
CC       strands, conveying epithelial permeability in a process known as
CC       paracellular tight junction permeability (By similarity). Involved in
CC       the maintenance of ion gradients along the nephron. In the thick
CC       ascending limb (TAL) of Henle's loop, facilitates sodium paracellular
CC       permeability from the interstitial compartment to the lumen,
CC       contributing to the lumen-positive transepithelial potential that
CC       drives paracellular magnesium and calcium reabsorption (By similarity).
CC       Forms paracellular barriers on its own. In the peripheral nervous
CC       system, represents a major constituent of the tight junctions in
CC       Schwann cells and contributes to electrical sealing. During retinal
CC       neurogenesis, may regulate the barrier properties of tight junctions in
CC       retinal pigment epithelium, required for proper retinal tissue
CC       differentiation and vision (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8N6F1, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9ET38}.
