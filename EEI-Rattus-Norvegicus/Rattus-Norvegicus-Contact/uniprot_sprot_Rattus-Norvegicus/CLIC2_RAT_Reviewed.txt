CC       insertion seems to be redox-regulated and may occur only under
CC       oxidizing conditions. Modulates the activity of RYR2 and inhibits
CC       calcium influx (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O15247}.

CC   -!- DOMAIN: Members of this family may change from a globular, soluble
CC       state to a state where the N-terminal domain is inserted into the
CC       membrane and functions as a chloride channel. The redox status of the
CC       active cysteine in Cys-X-X-Cys motif likely determines the capacity to
CC       adopt a soluble or membrane-inserted state. A conformation change of
CC       the N-terminal domain is thought to expose hydrophobic surfaces that
CC       trigger membrane insertion. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O15247}.
