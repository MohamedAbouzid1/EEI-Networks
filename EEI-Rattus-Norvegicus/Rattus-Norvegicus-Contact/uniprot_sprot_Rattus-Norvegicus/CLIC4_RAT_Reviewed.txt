CC   -!- FUNCTION: In the soluble state, catalyzes glutaredoxin-like thiol
CC       disulfide exchange reactions with reduced glutathione as electron donor
CC       (By similarity). Can insert into membranes and form voltage-dependent
CC       multi-ion conductive channels. Membrane insertion seems to be redox-
CC       regulated and may occur only under oxidizing conditions
CC       (PubMed:17453412, PubMed:9295337). Has alternate cellular functions
CC       like a potential role in angiogenesis or in maintaining apical-
CC       basolateral membrane polarity during mitosis and cytokinesis. Could
CC       also promote endothelial cell proliferation and regulate endothelial
CC       morphogenesis (tubulogenesis). Promotes cell-surface expression of
CC       HRH3. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Y696, ECO:0000269|PubMed:17453412,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:18302930, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9295337}.

CC       as membrane protein with probably a single transmembrane domain.
CC       Present in an intracellular vesicular compartment that likely represent

CC       cardiac cells. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Y696,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:26777142}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Detected in brain, in cell bodies and dendrites of
CC       Purkinje cells in cerebellar neurons (at protein level)
CC       (PubMed:18302930). Expressed neonatal and adult cardiomyocytes (at
CC       protein level) (PubMed:26777142). Marked expression was found in
CC       hippocampus and cerebellum, and in many other tissues (PubMed:9295337,
CC       PubMed:18302930). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18302930,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:26777142, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9295337}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The active G-site contains a monothiol Cys-X-X-Ser motif which
CC       mediates glutathione-dependent redox catalysis.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Y696}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: Members of this family may change from a globular, soluble
CC       state to a state where the N-terminal domain is inserted into the
CC       membrane and functions as a chloride channel. The redox status of the
CC       active cysteine in Cys-X-X-Cys/Ser motif likely determines the capacity
CC       to adopt a soluble or membrane-inserted state. A conformation change of
CC       the N-terminal domain is thought to expose hydrophobic surfaces that
CC       trigger membrane insertion. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Y696}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the chloride channel CLIC family. {ECO:0000305}.
