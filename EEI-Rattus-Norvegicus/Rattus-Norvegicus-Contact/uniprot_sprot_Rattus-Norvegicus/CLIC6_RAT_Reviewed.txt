CC   -!- FUNCTION: In the soluble state, catalyzes glutaredoxin-like thiol
CC       disulfide exchange reactions with reduced glutathione as electron donor
CC       (By similarity). Can insert into membranes and form voltage-dependent
CC       chloride-selective channels. The channel opens upon membrane
CC       depolarization at positive voltages and closes at negative membrane
CC       voltages (By similarity). May play a critical role in water-secreting
CC       cells, possibly through the regulation of chloride ion transport (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q96NY7,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9N2G5, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Y696}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY:
CC       Reaction=chloride(in) = chloride(out); Xref=Rhea:RHEA:29823,
CC         ChEBI:CHEBI:17996; Evidence={ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q96NY7};
CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: Channel activity is redox- and pH-regulated.
CC       Inhibited by IAA-94. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q96NY7}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Monomer (soluble state) (By similarity). Interacts with
CC       dopamine receptors DRD2, DRD3 and DRD4. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8BHB9,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:14499480}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm {ECO:0000250}. Cell membrane
CC       {ECO:0000305|PubMed:14499480}; Single-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000305|PubMed:14499480}. Note=Predominantly cytoplasmic. Upon
CC       chloride ion efflux from the cell, it is translocated to the plasma
CC       membrane (By similarity). Colocalizes with DRD3 at the plasma membrane.
CC       {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Predominantly expressed in brain, pituitary and
CC       stomach. In adult brain, it is restricted to the choroid plexus, the
CC       striatal proliferative subventricular zone and the cerebellum where it
CC       colocalizes with the D(3)R in the Purkinje cells of the lobules IX and
CC       X. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:14499480}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The active G-site contains a monothiol Cys-X-X-Ser motif which
CC       mediates glutathione-dependent redox catalysis.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Y696}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: Members of this family may change from a globular, soluble
CC       state to a state where the N-terminal domain is inserted into the
CC       membrane and functions as a chloride channel. The redox status of the
CC       active cysteine in Cys-X-X-Cys/Ser motif likely determines the capacity
CC       to adopt a soluble or membrane-inserted state. A conformation change of
CC       the N-terminal domain is thought to expose hydrophobic surfaces that
CC       trigger membrane insertion (By similarity). {ECO:0000250,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Y696}.
CC   -!- PTM: Phosphorylated. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9N2G5}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the chloride channel CLIC family. {ECO:0000305}.
