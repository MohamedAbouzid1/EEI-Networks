CC   -!- FUNCTION: Pore-forming subunit of the olfactory cyclic nucleotide-gated
CC       channel. Operates in the cilia of olfactory sensory neurons where
CC       chemical stimulation of the odorant is converted to an electrical
CC       signal. Mediates odorant-induced cAMP-dependent Ca(2+) influx
CC       triggering neuron depolarization. The rise of intracellular Ca(2+)
CC       levels potentiates the olfactory response by activating Ca(2+)-
CC       dependent Cl(-) channels, but it also serves as a negative feedback
CC       signal to desensitize the channel for rapid adaptation to odorants.
CC       Conducts cAMP- and cGMP-gated ion currents, with permeability for
CC       monovalent and divalent cations. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10377344,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:1697649, ECO:0000269|PubMed:27405959,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:7522325, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9878057}.

CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell projection, cilium membrane

CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Olfactory neurons. Widely expressed in brain,
