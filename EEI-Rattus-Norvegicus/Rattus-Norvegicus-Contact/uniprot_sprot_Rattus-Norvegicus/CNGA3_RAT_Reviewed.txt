CC   -!- FUNCTION: Pore-forming subunit of the cone cyclic nucleotide-gated
CC       channel. Mediates cone photoresponses at bright light converting
CC       transient changes in intracellular cGMP levels into electrical signals.
CC       In the dark, cGMP levels are high and keep the channel open enabling a
CC       steady inward current carried by Na(+) and Ca(2+) ions that leads to
CC       membrane depolarization and neurotransmitter release from synaptic
CC       terminals. Upon photon absorption cGMP levels decline leading to
CC       channel closure and membrane hyperpolarization that ultimately slows
CC       neurotransmitter release and signals the presence of light, the end
CC       point of the phototransduction cascade. Conducts cGMP- and cAMP-gated
CC       ion currents, with permeability for monovalent and divalent cations.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q16281, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q29441}.
CC   -!- FUNCTION: [Isoform 2]: Pore-forming subunit of the gustatory cyclic
CC       nucleotide-gated channel. In the taste buds, may sense oral
CC       extracellular pH and conduct ion currents that modulate the
CC       excitability of taste cells. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11387380,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15178415, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9278419}.
