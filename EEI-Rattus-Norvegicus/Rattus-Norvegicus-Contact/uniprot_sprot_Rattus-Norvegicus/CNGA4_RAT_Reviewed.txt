CC   -!- FUNCTION: Pore-forming subunit of the olfactory cyclic nucleotide-gated
CC       channel. Operates in the cilia of olfactory sensory neurons where
CC       chemical stimulation of the odorant is converted to an electrical
CC       signal. Mediates odorant-induced cAMP-dependent Ca(2+) influx
CC       triggering neuron depolarization. The rise of intracellular Ca(2+)
CC       levels potentiates the olfactory response by activating Ca(2+)-
CC       dependent Cl(-) channels, but it also serves as a negative feedback
CC       signal to desensitize the channel for rapid adaptation to odorants.
CC       Conducts cAMP- and cGMP-gated ion currents, with permeability for
CC       monovalent and divalent cations (PubMed:10377344, PubMed:15134638,
CC       PubMed:27405959, PubMed:7522325, PubMed:7522482, PubMed:9878057). May
CC       conduct nitric oxide-gated Ca(2+) currents relevant to neurons of
CC       vomeronasal organ, a system involved in the perception of pheromones
CC       (PubMed:9208862). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10377344,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15134638, ECO:0000269|PubMed:27405959,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:7522325, ECO:0000269|PubMed:7522482,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9208862, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9878057}.

CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: Ca(2+)-calmodulin exerts its inhibitory effect in
CC       cAMP sensitivity by binding to IQ-like motif of CNGA4 and preferably
CC       binds to the channel in the closed state. Inhibition by PIP3 of the CNG
CC       channel probably occurs via CGNA2 binding. Ca(2+) currents are
CC       inhibited by pimozide, an L-type Ca(2+) channel blocker.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15134638, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15195096,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9208862}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: The olfactory cyclic nucleotide-gated channel is an
CC       heterotetramer composed of CNGA2, CNGA4 and CNGB1b subunits with 2:1:1
CC       stoichiometry (PubMed:15134638, PubMed:27405959). May form homomeric
CC       channels gated by nitric oxide (PubMed:9208862).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15134638, ECO:0000269|PubMed:27405959,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9208862}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell projection, cilium membrane
CC       {ECO:0000305|PubMed:10377344}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000255}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Olfactory neurons. Expressed in olfactory sensory
CC       cilia (at protein level). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10377344,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:7522325, ECO:0000269|PubMed:7522482}.
