CC   -!- FUNCTION: [High affinity copper uptake protein 1]: Uniporter that
CC       mediates the transport of copper(1+) from the extracellular space to
CC       the cytoplasm, across the plasma membrane (PubMed:23123662). Then,
CC       delivers directly copper(1+) to specific chaperone such as ATOX1, via a
CC       copper(1+)- mediated transient interaction between the C-terminal
CC       domain and a copper(1+) chaperone, thus controlling intracellular
CC       copper(1+) levels (By similarity). May function in copper(1+) import
CC       from the apical membrane thus may drive intestinal copper absorption
CC       (By similarity). The copper(1+) transport mechanism is sodium-
CC       independent, saturable and of high-affinity (PubMed:23123662). Also
CC       mediates the uptake of silver(1+) (By similarity). May function in the
CC       influx of the platinum-containing chemotherapeutic agents
CC       (PubMed:23123662). The platinum-containing chemotherapeutic agents
CC       uptake is saturable (PubMed:23123662). Also participates in the first
CC       step of copper(2+) acquisition by cells through a direct transfer of
CC       copper(2+) from copper(2+) carriers in blood, such as ALB to the N-
CC       terminal domain of SLC31A1, leading to copper(2+) reduction and
CC       probably followed by copper(1+) stabilization (By similarity). In
CC       addition, functions as a redox sensor to promote angiogenesis in
CC       endothelial cells, in a copper(1+) transport independent manner, by
CC       transmitting the VEGF-induced ROS signal through a sulfenylation at
CC       Cys-189 leading to a subsequent disulfide bond formation between
CC       SLC31A1 and KDR (By similarity). The SLC31A1-KDR complex is then co-
CC       internalized to early endosomes, driving a sustained VEGFR2 signaling
CC       (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O15431,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8K211, ECO:0000269|PubMed:23123662}.
CC   -!- FUNCTION: [Truncated CTR1 form]: Mobilizes copper(1+) out of the
CC       endosomal compartment, making copper(1+) available for export out of
CC       the cells. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8K211}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY:
CC       Reaction=Cu(+)(out) = Cu(+)(in); Xref=Rhea:RHEA:75211,

CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: Copper uptake is inhibited by cold temperature,
CC       silver and zinc ions (PubMed:23123662). Platinum-containing
CC       chemotherapeutic agents uptake is inhibited by cold temperature and
CC       copper (PubMed:23123662). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:23123662}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homotrimer; is stabilized by cisplatin via interactions
CC       between cisplatin and the methionine-rich clusters, and could be
CC       crucial for the copper(2+) reduction process and copper(1+)
CC       stabilization. Heterotrimer between SLC31A1, CCS and SOD1; this
CC       heterotrimer is copper(1+)-mediated and its maintenance is regulated
CC       through SOD1 activation. Interacts with KDR; this interaction is
CC       induced upon VEGFA stimulation leading to SLC31A1 and KDR subsequent
CC       co-internalization to early endosomes, thereby activating KDR
CC       downstream signaling in endothelial cells. Interacts (via C-terminal
CC       domain) with ATOX1 (via dimer form); this interaction improves ATOX1
CC       stability and controls intracellular copper(1+) levels. Interacts with
CC       SLC31A2; this interaction stabilizes SLC31A2 and protects its from
CC       ubiquitination and degradation. Interacts (via C-terminal domain) with
CC       CCS; this interaction is copper(1+)-mediated.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O15431}.

CC       Multi-pass membrane protein {ECO:0000255}. Early endosome membrane
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O15431}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000255}. Recycling endosome membrane
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O15431}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000255}. Apical cell membrane {ECO:0000269|PubMed:20699218};
CC       Multi-pass membrane protein {ECO:0000255}. Late endosome membrane
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8K211}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000255}. Basolateral cell membrane
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8K211}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000255}. Note=The localization is controlled by the intra and
CC       extra-cellular copper concentration. Under conditions of elevated
CC       extracellular copper concentrations, it is rapidly internalized by
CC       endocytosis from the plasma membrane by a clathrin- and dynamin-
CC       mediated process and degradated in order to prevent intracellular
CC       copper accumulation and to reduce the transport of the copper across
CC       the membrane. The internalized SLC31A1 is then localized in early
CC       endosomes, and, upon a low extracellular copper concentrations, it is
CC       transported back to the plasma membrane in a RAB11A-dependent recycling
CC       pathway (By similarity). Localizes to the apical membrane in intestinal
CC       epithelial cells (PubMed:20699218). Localizes to the neuronal cell body
CC       plasma membranes (PubMed:23123662). Mainly localized on the basolateral
CC       side of renal tubular cells (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O15431, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8K211,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:20699218, ECO:0000269|PubMed:23123662}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The C-terminal domain mediates copper(1+) binding and is
CC       involved in the copper(1+)-dependent-ATOX1 interaction. The C-terminal
CC       domain appears to act to limit transport through the pore by regulating
CC       the rate of exit of copper ions at the intracellular side. The N-
CC       terminal domain can collect copper(2+) from copper(2+) carriers in
CC       blood. The N-terminal domain, in the trimeric arrangement, tunes its
CC       reactivity with copper, promoting copper(2+) reduction and copper(1+)
CC       stabilization. The bis-His motif directly coordinate to copper(2+).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O15431}.
CC   -!- PTM: O-Glycosylation at Thr-30 protects from proteolytic cleavage in
CC       the N-terminal extracellular domain. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O15431}.
CC   -!- PTM: Proteolytic cleavage, leading to a truncated form, is facilitated
CC       by SLC31A2 and initiated preferentially by CTSL and to a minor extend
CC       by CTSB in endolysosomal compartments. A post-CTSL/cathepsin L
CC       processing occurs to yield to the fully truncated form.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O15431}.
CC   -!- PTM: Sulfenylated at Cys-186 after stimulation with VEGFA, which
CC       induces SLC31A1-KDR disulfide bond formation and their co-
CC       internalization to early endosomes, driving to a sustained VEGFR2
CC       signaling. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O15431}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the copper transporter (Ctr) (TC 1.A.56) family.
CC       SLC31A subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
