CC   -!- FUNCTION: A cytochrome P450 monooxygenase involved in the metabolism of
CC       retinoates (RAs), the active metabolites of vitamin A, and critical
CC       signaling molecules in animals (By similarity). RAs exist as at least
CC       four different isomers: all-trans-RA (atRA), 9-cis-RA, 13-cis-RA, and
CC       9,13-dicis-RA, where atRA is considered to be the biologically active
CC       isomer, although 9-cis-RA and 13-cis-RA also have activity (By
CC       similarity). Catalyzes the hydroxylation of atRA primarily at C-4 and
CC       C-18, thereby contributing to the regulation of atRA homeostasis and
CC       signaling (By similarity). Hydroxylation of atRA limits its biological
CC       activity and initiates a degradative process leading to its eventual
CC       elimination (By similarity). Involved in the convertion of atRA to all-
CC       trans-4-oxo-RA. Can oxidize all-trans-13,14-dihydroretinoate (DRA) to
CC       metabolites which could include all-trans-4-oxo-DRA, all-trans-4-
CC       hydroxy-DRA, all-trans-5,8-epoxy-DRA, and all-trans-18-hydroxy-DRA (By
CC       similarity). Shows preference for the following substrates: atRA > 9-
CC       cis-RA > 13-cis-RA (By similarity). Plays a central role in germ cell
CC       development: acts by degrading RAs in the developing testis, preventing
CC       STRA8 expression, thereby leading to delay of meiosis. Required for the
CC       maintenance of the undifferentiated state of male germ cells during
CC       embryonic development in Sertoli cells, inducing arrest in G0 phase of
CC       the cell cycle and preventing meiotic entry. Plays a role in skeletal
CC       development, both at the level of patterning and in the ossification of
CC       bone and the establishment of some synovial joints. Essential for
CC       postnatal survival (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q811W2,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9NR63}.
CC   -!- FUNCTION: Has also a significant activity in oxidation of tazarotenic
CC       acid and may therefore metabolize that xenobiotic in vivo.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9NR63}.
