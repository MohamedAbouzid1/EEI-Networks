CC   -!- FUNCTION: Substrate recognition component of a DCX (DDB1-CUL4-X-box) E3
CC       protein ligase complex that mediates the ubiquitination and subsequent
CC       proteasomal degradation of target proteins, such as MEIS2, ILF2 or
CC       GLUL. Normal degradation of key regulatory proteins is required for
CC       normal limb outgrowth and expression of the fibroblast growth factor
CC       FGF8. Maintains presynaptic glutamate release and consequently
CC       cognitive functions, such as memory and learning, by negatively
CC       regulating large-conductance calcium-activated potassium (BK) channels
CC       in excitatory neurons. Likely to function by regulating the assembly
CC       and neuronal surface expression of BK channels via its interaction with
CC       KCNT1 (By similarity). May also be involved in regulating anxiety-like
CC       behaviors via a BK channel-independent mechanism (By similarity). Plays
CC       a negative role in TLR4 signaling by interacting with TRAF6 and ECSIT,
CC       leading to inhibition of ECSIT ubiquitination, an important step of the
CC       signaling (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8C7D2,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q96SW2, ECO:0000305}.
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Protein modification; protein ubiquitination.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q96SW2}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Component of a DCX (DDB1-CUL4-X-box) protein ligase complex,
CC       at least composed of CRBN, CUL4A, DDB1 and RBX1. Interacts directly
CC       with DDB1 (By similarity). Interacts with KCNT1 (PubMed:16045448).
CC       Interacts with TRAF6 and ECSIT (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q96SW2, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16045448}.
