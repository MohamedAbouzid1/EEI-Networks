CC   -!- FUNCTION: Postsynaptic adhesion molecule that binds to presynaptic
CC       neurexins to mediate both excitatory and inhibitory synapse formation
CC       (By similarity). Promotes synapse development by acting as a cell
CC       adhesion molecule at the postsynaptic membrane, which associates with
CC       neurexin-alpha at the presynaptic membrane (By similarity). Also
CC       functions as a cargo in axonal anterograde transport by acting as a
CC       molecular adapter that promotes KLC1 association with vesicles. Complex
CC       formation with APBA2 and APP, stabilizes APP metabolism and enhances
CC       APBA2-mediated suppression of beta-APP40 secretion, due to the
CC       retardation of intracellular APP maturation (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O94985, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q99JH7,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9EPL2}.
CC   -!- FUNCTION: [Soluble Alc-alpha]: As intracellular fragment AlcICD,
CC       suppresses APBB1-dependent transactivation stimulated by APP C-terminal
CC       intracellular fragment (AICD), most probably by competing with AICD for
CC       APBB1-binding. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O94985}.
CC   -!- FUNCTION: [CTF1-alpha]: In complex with APBA2 and C99, a C-terminal APP
CC       fragment, abolishes C99 interaction with PSEN1 and thus APP C99
CC       cleavage by gamma-secretase, most probably through stabilization of the
CC       direct interaction between APBA2 and APP.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O94985}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Directly interacts with APBA2 (By similarity). Forms a
CC       tripartite complex with APBA2 and APP (By similarity). Interacts with
CC       KLC1 (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O94985,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9EPL2}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: [Soluble Alc-alpha]: Interacts with APBB1; this interaction
CC       stabilizes AlcICD metabolism. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O94985}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: [CTF1-alpha]: Interacts with PSEN1.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O94985}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Postsynaptic cell membrane
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11161476}; Single-pass type I membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000255}. Endoplasmic reticulum membrane
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O94985}; Single-pass type I membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000255}. Golgi apparatus membrane {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O94985};
CC       Single-pass type I membrane protein {ECO:0000255}. Cell projection,
CC       neuron projection {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O94985}. Note=Localized in the
CC       postsynaptic membrane of both excitatory and inhibitory synapses.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9EPL2}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: [Soluble Alc-alpha]: Nucleus
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O94985}. Note=The AlcICD fragment is
CC       translocated to the nucleus upon interaction with APBB1.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O94985}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Preferentially expressed in the retina and brain.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15326124}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The cytoplasmic domain binds synaptic Ca(2+).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9EPL2}.
CC   -!- PTM: Proteolytically processed under normal cellular conditions. A
CC       primary zeta-cleavage generates a large extracellular (soluble) N-
CC       terminal domain (sAlc) and a short C-terminal transmembrane fragment
CC       (CTF1). A secondary cleavage catalyzed by presenilin gamma-secretase
CC       within the transmembrane domain releases the beta-Alc-alpha chain in
CC       the extracellular milieu and produces an intracellular fragment
CC       (AlcICD). This processing is strongly suppressed in the tripartite
CC       complex formed with APBA2 and APP, which seems to prevent the
CC       association with PSEN1. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O94985}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the calsyntenin family. {ECO:0000305}.
