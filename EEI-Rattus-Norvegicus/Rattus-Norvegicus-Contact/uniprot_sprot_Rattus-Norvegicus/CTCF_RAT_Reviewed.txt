CC   -!- FUNCTION: Chromatin binding factor that binds to DNA sequence specific
CC       sites and regulates the 3D structure of chromatin. Binds together
CC       strands of DNA, thus forming chromatin loops, and anchors DNA to
CC       cellular structures, such as the nuclear lamina. Defines the boundaries
CC       between active and heterochromatic DNA via binding to chromatin
CC       insulators, thereby preventing interaction between promoter and nearby
CC       enhancers and silencers. Plays a critical role in the epigenetic
CC       regulation. Participates in the allele-specific gene expression at the
CC       imprinted IGF2/H19 gene locus (By similarity). On the maternal allele,
CC       binding within the H19 imprinting control region (ICR) mediates
CC       maternally inherited higher-order chromatin conformation to restrict
CC       enhancer access to IGF2. Mediates interchromosomal association between
CC       IGF2/H19 and WSB1/NF1 and may direct distant DNA segments to a common
CC       transcription factory. Regulates asynchronous replication of IGF2/H19.
CC       Plays a critical role in gene silencing over considerable distances in
CC       the genome (By similarity). Preferentially interacts with unmethylated
CC       DNA, preventing spreading of CpG methylation and maintaining
CC       methylation-free zones. Inversely, binding to target sites is prevented
CC       by CpG methylation. Plays an important role in chromatin remodeling.
CC       Can dimerize when it is bound to different DNA sequences, mediating
CC       long-range chromatin looping. Causes local loss of histone acetylation
CC       and gain of histone methylation in the beta-globin locus, without
CC       affecting transcription. When bound to chromatin, it provides an anchor
CC       point for nucleosomes positioning (By similarity). Seems to be
CC       essential for homologous X-chromosome pairing (By similarity). May
CC       participate with Tsix in establishing a regulatable epigenetic switch
CC       for X chromosome inactivation. May play a role in preventing the
CC       propagation of stable methylation at the escape genes from X-
CC       inactivation. Involved in sister chromatid cohesion. Associates with
CC       both centromeres and chromosomal arms during metaphase and required for
CC       cohesin localization to CTCF sites. Plays a role in the recruitment of
CC       CENPE to the pericentromeric/centromeric regions of the chromosome
CC       during mitosis. Acts as a transcriptional repressor binding to
CC       promoters of vertebrate MYC gene and BAG1 gene. Also binds to the PLK
CC       and PIM1 promoters. Acts as a transcriptional activator of APP.
CC       Regulates APOA1/C3/A4/A5 gene cluster and controls MHC class II gene
CC       expression (By similarity). Plays an essential role in oocyte and
CC       preimplantation embryo development by activating or repressing
CC       transcription (By similarity). Seems to act as tumor suppressor (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P49711,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q61164}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts with CHD8 (By similarity). Interacts with LLPH (By
CC       similarity). Interacts with CENPE (By similarity). Interacts with BRD2;
CC       promoting BRD2 recruitment to chromatin (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P49711, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q61164}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Nucleus, nucleoplasm
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q61164}. Chromosome
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P49711}. Chromosome, centromere
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P49711}. Note=May translocate to the nucleolus
CC       upon cell differentiation. Associates with both centromeres and
CC       chromosomal arms during metaphase. Associates with the H19 ICR in
CC       mitotic chromosomes. May be preferentially excluded from
CC       heterochromatin during interphase. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P49711}.
CC   -!- PTM: Sumoylated on Lys-74 and Lys-699; sumoylation of CTCF contributes
CC       to the repressive function of CTCF on the MYC P2 promoter.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q61164}.
