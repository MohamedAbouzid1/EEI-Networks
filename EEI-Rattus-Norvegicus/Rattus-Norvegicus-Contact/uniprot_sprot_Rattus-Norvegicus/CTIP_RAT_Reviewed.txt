CC   -!- FUNCTION: Endonuclease that cooperates with the MRE11-RAD50-NBN (MRN)
CC       complex in DNA-end resection, the first step of double-strand break
CC       (DSB) repair through the homologous recombination (HR) pathway. HR is
CC       restricted to S and G2 phases of the cell cycle and preferentially
CC       repairs DSBs resulting from replication fork collapse. Key determinant
CC       of DSB repair pathway choice, as it commits cells to HR by preventing
CC       classical non-homologous end-joining (NHEJ). Specifically promotes the
CC       endonuclease activity of the MRN complex to clear DNA ends containing
CC       protein adducts: recruited to DSBs by NBN following phosphorylation by
CC       CDK1, and promotes the endonuclease activity of MRE11 to clear protein-
CC       DNA adducts and generate clean double-strand break ends. Functions
CC       downstream of the MRN complex and ATM, promotes ATR activation and its
CC       recruitment to DSBs in the S/G2 phase facilitating the generation of
CC       ssDNA. Component of the BRCA1-RBBP8 complex that regulates CHEK1
CC       activation and controls cell cycle G2/M checkpoints on DNA damage (By
CC       similarity). During immunoglobulin heavy chain class-switch
CC       recombination, promotes microhomology-mediated alternative end joining
CC       (A-NHEJ) and plays an essential role in chromosomal translocations (By
CC       similarity). Binds preferentially to DNA Y-junctions and to DNA
CC       substrates with blocked ends and promotes intermolecular DNA bridging
CC       (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q80YR6,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q99708}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homotetramer; formed by antiparallel association of helical
CC       extensions protruding from the N-termini of two parallel coiled-coil
CC       dimers (By similarity). Forms a dumbbell-shaped particle in which polar
CC       globular domains are held about 30 nm apart by a central rod (By
CC       similarity). Homotetramerization is required for DNA-end resection and
CC       repair (By similarity). Interacts (via the PXDLS motif) with CTBP1; the
CC       interaction is disrupted via binding of the adenovirus E1A to CTBP1.
CC       Component of the BRCA1-RBBP8 complex. Interacts (the Ser-326
CC       phosphorylated form) with BRCA1 (via the C-terminal BRCT domains): the
CC       interaction occurs in the G2 phase, ubiquitinates RBBP8 and involves
CC       RBBP8 in BRCA1-dependent G2/M checkpoint control on DNA damage.
CC       Interacts with RB1. Interacts with the MRN complex. Interacts directly
CC       with MRE11; the interaction is required for efficient homologous
CC       recombination (HR) and regulation of the MRN complex. Interacts
CC       directly with RAD50. Interacts (when phosphorylated by CDK1) with NBN;
CC       promoting association with the MRN complex. Interacts with LMO4 (via
CC       the LIM zinc-binding 1 domain). Interacts with SIAH1. Interacts with
CC       RNF138. Interacts with EXD2. Interacts with CUL3 and KLHL15; this
CC       interaction leads to RBBP8 proteasomal degradation. Directly interacts
CC       with PIN1; this interaction depends upon RBBP8 phosphorylation.
CC       Interacts with FZR1; this interaction leads to APC/C-mediated RBBP8
CC       proteasomal degradation. Interacts with AUNIP; leading to recruit RBBP8
CC       to sites of DNA damage. Interacts with SAMHD1 (By similarity).
CC       Interacts with HDGFL2 (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q99708}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Nucleus {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q99708}.
CC       Chromosome {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q99708}. Note=Associates with sites
CC       of DNA damage in S/G2 phase. Recruited to DSBs by the MRE11-RAD50-NBN
CC       (MRN) complex following phosphorylation by CDK1, which promotes
CC       interaction with NBN. Ubiquitinated RBBP8 binds to chromatin following
CC       DNA damage. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q99708}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The PXDLS motif binds to a cleft in CtBP proteins.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q99708}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The damage-recruitment motif is required for DNA binding and
CC       translocation to sites of DNA damage. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q99708}.
CC   -!- PTM: Hyperphosphorylation upon ionizing radiation results in
CC       dissociation from BRCA1. Phosphorylation at Thr-843 by CDK1 is
CC       essential for the recruitment to DNA and the DNA repair function.
CC       Phosphorylation at Thr-843 and Thr-855 promote interaction with NBN and
CC       recruitment to double-strand breaks (DSBs). Phosphorylated on Ser-326
CC       as cells enter G2 phase. Phosphorylated on Ser-326 as cells enter G2
CC       phase. This phosphorylation is required for binding BRCA1 and for the
CC       G2/M DNA damage transition checkpoint control (By similarity).
CC       Phosphorylation at Ser-276 may serve as a PIN1 isomerization site (By
CC       similarity). Phosphorylation is not required for tetramerization (By
CC       similarity). Binds to DNA more strongly when dephosphorylated (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q99708}.
CC   -!- PTM: Ubiquitinated. Ubiquitination at multiple sites by BRCA1 (via its
CC       N-terminal RING domain) does not lead to its proteasomal degradation
CC       but instead the ubiquitinated RBBP8 binds to chromatin following DNA
CC       damage and may play a role in G2/M checkpoint control. Ubiquitinated by
CC       RNF138 at its N-terminus. Ubiquitinated through 'Lys-48' by the E3
CC       CUL3-KLHL15 complex; this modification leads to proteasomal degradation
CC       (By similarity). Ubiquitinated by the E3 FZR1/APC/C complex; this
CC       modification leads to proteasomal degradation (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q99708}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: Binds one Zn(2+) atom per dimer. Zn(2+)-binding is not
CC       required for homotetramerization. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q99708}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the COM1/SAE2/CtIP family. {ECO:0000305}.
