CC   -!- FUNCTION: Core component of multiple cullin-5-RING E3 ubiquitin-protein
CC       ligase complexes (ECS complexes, also named CRL5 complexes), which
CC       mediate the ubiquitination and subsequent proteasomal degradation of
CC       target proteins. Acts a scaffold protein that contributes to catalysis
CC       through positioning of the substrate and the ubiquitin-conjugating
CC       enzyme. The functional specificity of the E3 ubiquitin-protein ligase
CC       complex depends on the variable SOCS box-containing substrate
CC       recognition component (By similarity). Acts as a key regulator of
CC       neuron positioning during cortex development: component of various
CC       SOCS-containing ECS complexes, such as the ECS(SOCS7) complex, that
CC       regulate reelin signaling by mediating ubiquitination and degradation
CC       of DAB1 (By similarity). ECS(SOCS1) seems to direct ubiquitination of
CC       JAK2. The ECS(SOCS2) complex mediates the ubiquitination and subsequent
CC       proteasomal degradation of phosphorylated EPOR and GHR. The ECS(SPSB3)
CC       complex catalyzes ubiquitination of nuclear CGAS. ECS(KLHDC1) complex
CC       is part of the DesCEND (destruction via C-end degrons) pathway and
CC       mediates ubiquitination and degradation of truncated SELENOS
CC       selenoprotein produced by failed UGA/Sec decoding, which ends with a
CC       glycine. The ECS(ASB9) complex mediates ubiquitination and degradation
CC       of CKB. As part of some ECS complex, promotes 'Lys-11'-linked
CC       ubiquitination and degradation of BTRC. As part of a multisubunit ECS
CC       complex, polyubiquitinates monoubiquitinated POLR2A. May form a cell
CC       surface vasopressin receptor (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q93034, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9D5V5}.
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Protein modification; protein ubiquitination.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q93034}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Component of multiple cullin-5-RING E3 ubiquitin-protein
CC       ligase complexes (ECS complexes, also named CRL5 complexes) formed of
CC       CUL5, Elongin BC (ELOB and ELOC), RNF7/RBX2 and a variable SOCS box
CC       domain-containing protein as substrate-specific recognition component.
CC       CUL5-containing ECS complexes specifically contain RNF7/RBX2, and not
CC       RBX1, as catalytic subunit. Component of the ECS(ASB2) complex with the
CC       substrate recognition component ASB2. Component of the ECS(ASB6)
CC       complex with the substrate recognition component ASB6. Component of the
CC       ECS(ASB7) complex with the substrate recognition component ASB7.
CC       Component of the ECS(ASB9) complex with the substrate recognition
CC       component ASB9. Component of the ECS(ASB11) complex with the substrate
CC       recognition component ASB11. Component of the ECS(ASB12) complex with
CC       the substrate recognition component ASB12. Component of the ECS(LRRC41)
CC       complex with the substrate recognition component LRRC41. Component of
CC       the ECS(SOCS1) complex with the substrate recognition component SOCS1.
CC       Component of the ECS(SOCS2) complex with the substrate recognition
CC       component SOCS2. Component of the ECS(WSB1) complex with the substrate
CC       recognition subunit WSB1. Component of the ECS(SOCS3) complex with the
CC       substrate recognition component SOCS3 (By similarity). Component of the
CC       ECS(SOCS7) complex with the substrate recognition component SOCS7 (By
CC       similarity). Component of the ECS(SPSB1) complex with the substrate
CC       recognition component SPSB1. Component of the ECS(SPSB3) complex with
CC       the substrate recognition component SPSB3. Component of the ECS(SPSB2)
CC       complex with the substrate recognition component SPSB2. Component of
CC       the ECS(SPSB4) complex with the substrate recognition component SPSB4.
CC       Component of the ECS(RAB40C) complex with the substrate recognition
CC       subunit RAB40C. Component of the ECS(KLHDC1) complex with the substrate
CC       recognition component KLHDC1. Component of the ECS(PCMTD1) complex with
CC       the substrate recognition subunit PCMTD1. May also form complexes
CC       containing RBX1 and ELOA or VHL; additional evidence is however
CC       required to confirm this result in vivo. Interacts (when neddylated)
CC       with ARIH2; leading to activate the E3 ligase activity of ARIH2.
CC       Interacts with ERCC6; the interaction is induced by DNA damaging agents
CC       or inhibitors of RNA polymerase II elongation. Interacts with ELOA (via
CC       the BC-box). Interacts (unneddylated form) with DCUN1D1, DCUN1D2,
CC       DCUN1D3, DCUN1D4 and DCUN1D5; these interactions promote the cullin
CC       neddylation (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q93034,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9D5V5}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Nucleus {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q93034}.
CC       Note=Localizes to sites of DNA damage in a UBAP2 and UBAP2L-dependent
CC       manner. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q93034}.
CC   -!- PTM: Neddylated; which enhances the ubiquitination activity of ECS
CC       complexes and prevents binding of the inhibitor CAND1. Deneddylated via
CC       its interaction with the COP9 signalosome (CSN).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q93034}.
