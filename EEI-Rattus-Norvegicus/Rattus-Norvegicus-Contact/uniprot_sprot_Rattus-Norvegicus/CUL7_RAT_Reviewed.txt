CC   -!- FUNCTION: Core component of the 3M and Cul7-RING(FBXW8) complexes,
CC       which mediate the ubiquitination and subsequent proteasomal degradation
CC       of target proteins (By similarity). Core component of the 3M complex, a
CC       complex required to regulate microtubule dynamics and genome integrity
CC       (By similarity). It is unclear how the 3M complex regulates
CC       microtubules, it could act by controlling the level of a microtubule
CC       stabilizer (By similarity). The Cul7-RING(FBXW8) complex alone lacks
CC       ubiquitination activity and does not promote polyubiquitination and
CC       proteasomal degradation of p53/TP53 (By similarity). However it
CC       mediates recruitment of p53/TP53 for ubiquitination by neddylated CUL1-
CC       RBX1 (By similarity). Interaction with CUL9 is required to inhibit CUL9
CC       activity and ubiquitination of BIRC5 (By similarity). The Cul7-
CC       RING(FBXW8) complex also mediates ubiquitination and consequent
CC       degradation of target proteins such as GORASP1, IRS1 and MAP4K1/HPK1
CC       (By similarity). Ubiquitination of GORASP1 regulates Golgi
CC       morphogenesis and dendrite patterning in brain (PubMed:21572988).
CC       Mediates ubiquitination and degradation of IRS1 in a mTOR-dependent
CC       manner: the Cul7-RING(FBXW8) complex recognizes and binds IRS1
CC       previously phosphorylated by S6 kinase (RPS6KB1 or RPS6KB2) (By
CC       similarity). The Cul7-RING(FBXW8) complex also mediates ubiquitination
CC       of MAP4K1/HPK1: recognizes and binds autophosphorylated MAP4K1/HPK1,
CC       leading to its degradation, thereby affecting cell proliferation and
CC       differentiation (By similarity). Acts as a regulator in trophoblast
CC       cell epithelial-mesenchymal transition and placental development (By
CC       similarity). While the Cul7-RING(FBXW8) and the 3M complexes are
CC       associated and involved in common processes, CUL7 and the Cul7-
CC       RING(FBXW8) complex may have additional functions (By similarity).
CC       Probably plays a role in the degradation of proteins involved in
CC       endothelial proliferation and/or differentiation (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q14999, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8VE73,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:21572988}.
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Protein modification; protein ubiquitination.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:21572988}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Component of the 3M complex, composed of core components CUL7,
CC       CCDC8 and OBSL1 (PubMed:21572988). Component of the Cul7-RING(FBXW8)
CC       complex consisting of CUL7, RBX1, SKP1 and FBXW8 (PubMed:21572988).
CC       Within the Cul7-RING(FBXW8) complex interacts with FBXW8 and RBX1, but
CC       not with SKP1 (By similarity). Interacts with CUL1 (via the C-terminal
CC       domain); the interaction seems to be mediated by FBXW8; it is likely
CC       specific to FBXW8, but not other F-box proteins (By similarity).
CC       Interacts (via the CPH domain) with p53/TP53; the interaction
CC       preferentially involves tetrameric and dimeric p53/TP53; this
CC       interaction recruits p53/TP53 for ubiquitination by neddylated CUL1-
CC       RBX1 (By similarity). The CUL7-CUL9 heterodimer seems to interact
CC       specifically with p53/TP53 (By similarity). Interacts with FBXW8;
CC       interaction is mutually exclusive of binding to CUL9 or p53/TP53 (By
CC       similarity). Interacts with CUL9; leading to inhibited CUL9 activity
CC       (By similarity). Interacts with OBSL1 (By similarity). Interacts (as
CC       part of the 3M complex) with HDAC4 and HDAC5; it is negatively
CC       regulated by ANKRA2 (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q14999,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:21572988}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm {ECO:0000269|PubMed:21572988}.
CC       Cytoplasm, cytoskeleton, microtubule organizing center, centrosome
CC       {ECO:0000250}. Cytoplasm, perinuclear region
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:21572988}. Golgi apparatus
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:21572988}. Note=During mitosis, localizes to the
CC       mitotic apparatus. CCDC8 is required for centrosomal location (By
CC       similarity). Colocalizes with FBXW8 at the Golgi apparatus in neurons;
CC       localization to Golgi is mediated by OBSL1. {ECO:0000250}.
