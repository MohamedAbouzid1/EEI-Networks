CC   -!- FUNCTION: Pro-inflammatory cytokine that is involved in a wide variety
CC       of processes such as chemotaxis, differentiation, and activation of
CC       peripheral immune cells, regulation of cell growth, apoptosis and
CC       modulation of angiostatic effects (By similarity). Plays thereby an
CC       important role during viral infections by stimulating the activation
CC       and migration of immune cells to the infected sites (By similarity).
CC       Mechanistically, binding of CXCL10 to the CXCR3 receptor activates G
CC       protein-mediated signaling and results in downstream activation of
CC       phospholipase C-dependent pathway, an increase in intracellular calcium
CC       production and actin reorganization. In turn, recruitment of activated
CC       Th1 lymphocytes occurs at sites of inflammation (By similarity).
CC       Activation of the CXCL10/CXCR3 axis also plays an important role in
CC       neurons in response to brain injury for activating microglia, the
CC       resident macrophage population of the central nervous system, and
CC       directing them to the lesion site. This recruitment is an essential
CC       element for neuronal reorganization (By similarity) (PubMed:30257241,
CC       PubMed:30448292). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P02778,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P17515, ECO:0000269|PubMed:30257241,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:30448292}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Monomer, dimer, and tetramer. Interacts with CXCR3 (via N-
CC       terminus). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P02778}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Secreted {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P02778}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: In the central nervous system, CXCL10 is
CC       predominantly localized to activated neurons (PubMed:30448292).
CC       Expressed in both microglia and astrocytes (PubMed:30257241).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:30257241, ECO:0000269|PubMed:30448292}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the intercrine alpha (chemokine CxC) family.
CC       {ECO:0000305}.
