CC   -!- FUNCTION: Probable multifunctional globin with a hexacoordinated heme
CC       iron required for the catalysis of various reactions depending on redox
CC       condition of the cell as well as oxygen availability (PubMed:28393874).
CC       Has a nitric oxide dioxygenase (NOD) activity and is most probably
CC       involved in cell-mediated and oxygen-dependent nitric oxide consumption
CC       (PubMed:28393874). By scavenging this second messenger may regulate
CC       several biological processes including endothelium-mediated
CC       vasodilation and vascular tone (PubMed:28393874). Under normoxic
CC       conditions functions as a nitric oxide dioxygenase (NOD) but under
CC       hypoxic conditions the globin may switch its function to that of a
CC       nitrite (NO2) reductase (NiR), generating nitric oxide. Could also have
CC       peroxidase and superoxide dismutase activities, detoxifying reactive
CC       oxygen species and protecting cells against oxidative stress. Also
CC       binds dioxygen with low affinity and could function as an oxygen sensor
CC       but has probably no function as a respiratory oxygen carrier (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8WWM9,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:28393874}.
