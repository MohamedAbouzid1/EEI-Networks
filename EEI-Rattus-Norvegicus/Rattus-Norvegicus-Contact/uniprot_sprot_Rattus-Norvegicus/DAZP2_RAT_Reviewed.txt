CC   -!- FUNCTION: In unstressed cells, promotes SIAH1-mediated
CC       polyubiquitination and degradation of the serine/threonine-protein
CC       kinase HIPK2, probably by acting as a loading factor that potentiates
CC       complex formation between HIPK2 and ubiquitin ligase SIAH1 (By
CC       similarity). In response to DNA damage, localizes to the nucleus
CC       following phosphorylation by HIPK2 and modulates the expression of a
CC       subset of TP53/p53 target genes by binding to TP53 at target gene
CC       promoters (By similarity). This limits the expression of a number of
CC       cell death-mediating TP53 target genes, reducing DNA damage-induced
CC       cell death (By similarity). Enhances the binding of transcription
CC       factor TCF7L2/TCF4, a Wnt signaling pathway effector, to the promoters
CC       of target genes (By similarity). Plays a role in stress granule
CC       formation (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q15038,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9DCP9}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts with SOX6. Interacts with DAZ1 and DAZL. Interacts
CC       with IL17RB. May interact with FAM168B. Interacts with INCA1. Interacts
CC       with EIF4G1 and EIF4G2 (By similarity). Interacts (via PPAY motif) with
CC       NEDD4 (via WW domains) (By similarity). Interacts with transcription
CC       factor TCF4; the interaction results in localization of DAZAP2 to the
CC       nucleus (By similarity). Interacts with transcription factors TCF7 and
CC       TCF7L1 (By similarity). Interacts with transcription factor LEF1 (By
CC       similarity). Interacts with serine/threonine-protein kinase HIPK2; the
CC       interaction results in phosphorylation of DAZAP2 which causes
CC       localization of DAZAP2 to the nucleus, reduces interaction of DAZAP2
CC       with HIPK2 and prevents DAZAP2-dependent degradation of HIPK2 (By
CC       similarity). Interacts with ubiquitin ligase SIAH1; the interaction is
CC       decreased following phosphorylation of DAZAP2 by HIPK2 (By similarity).
CC       Interacts with TP53; the interaction is triggered by DNA damage (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q15038,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9DCP9}.
