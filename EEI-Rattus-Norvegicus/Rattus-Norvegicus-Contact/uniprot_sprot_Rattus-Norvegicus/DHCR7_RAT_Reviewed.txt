CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalyzes the last step of the cholesterol synthesis pathway,
CC       which transforms cholesta-5,7-dien-3beta-ol (7-dehydrocholesterol,7-
CC       DHC) into cholesterol by reducing the C7-C8 double bond of its sterol
CC       core (PubMed:10329655). Can also metabolize cholesta-5,7,24-trien-
CC       3beta-ol (7-dehydrodemosterol, 7-DHD) to desmosterol, which is then
CC       metabolized by the Delta(24)-sterol reductase (DHCR24) to cholesterol
CC       (By similarity). Modulates ferroptosis (a form of regulated cell death
CC       driven by iron-dependent lipid peroxidation) through the metabolic
CC       breakdown of the anti-ferroptotic metabolites 7-DHC and 7-DHD which,
CC       when accumulated, divert the propagation of peroxyl radical-mediated
CC       damage from phospholipid components to its sterol core, protecting
CC       plasma and mitochondrial membranes from phospholipid autoxidation (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O88455,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9UBM7, ECO:0000269|PubMed:10329655}.
