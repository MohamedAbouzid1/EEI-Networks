CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalytic subunit of DNA polymerase gamma solely responsible
CC       for replication of mitochondrial DNA (mtDNA). Replicates both heavy and
CC       light strands of the circular mtDNA genome using a single-stranded DNA
CC       template, RNA primers and the four deoxyribonucleoside triphosphates as
CC       substrates (By similarity). Has 5' -> 3' polymerase activity.
CC       Functionally interacts with TWNK and SSBP1 at the replication fork to
CC       form a highly processive replisome, where TWNK unwinds the double-
CC       stranded DNA template prior to replication and SSBP1 covers the
CC       parental heavy strand to enable continuous replication of the entire
CC       mitochondrial genome. A single nucleotide incorporation cycle includes
CC       binding of the incoming nucleotide at the insertion site, a
CC       phosphodiester bond formation reaction that extends the 3'-end of the
CC       primer DNA, and translocation of the primer terminus to the post-
CC       insertion site. After completing replication of a mtDNA strand,
CC       mediates 3' -> 5' exonucleolytic degradation at the nick to enable
CC       proper ligation (By similarity). Highly accurate due to high nucleotide
CC       selectivity and 3' -> 5' exonucleolytic proofreading. Proficiently
CC       corrects base substitutions, single-base additions and deletions in
CC       non-repetitive sequences and short repeats, but displays lower
CC       proofreading activity when replicating longer homopolymeric stretches.
CC       Exerts exonuclease activity toward single-stranded DNA and double-
CC       stranded DNA containing 3'-terminal mispairs. When a misincorporation
CC       occurs, transitions from replication to a pro-nucleolytic editing mode
CC       and removes the missincorporated nucleoside in the exonuclease active
CC       site. Proceeds via an SN2 nucleolytic mechanism in which Asp-198
CC       catalyzes phosphodiester bond hydrolysis and Glu-200 stabilizes the
CC       leaving group. As a result the primer strand becomes one nucleotide
CC       shorter and is positioned in the post-insertion site, ready to resume
CC       DNA synthesis (By similarity). Exerts 5'-deoxyribose phosphate (dRP)
CC       lyase activity and mediates repair-associated mtDNA synthesis (gap
CC       filling) in base-excision repair pathway. Catalyzes the release of the
CC       5'-terminal 2-deoxyribose-5-phosphate sugar moiety from incised
CC       apurinic/apyrimidinic (AP) sites to produce a substrate for DNA ligase.
CC       The dRP lyase reaction does not require divalent metal ions and likely
CC       proceeds via a Schiff base intermediate in a beta-elimination reaction
CC       mechanism (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P54098}.
