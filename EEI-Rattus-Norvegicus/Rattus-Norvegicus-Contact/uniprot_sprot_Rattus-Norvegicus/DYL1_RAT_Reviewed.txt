CC   -!- FUNCTION: Acts as one of several non-catalytic accessory components of
CC       the cytoplasmic dynein 1 complex that are thought to be involved in
CC       linking dynein to cargos and to adapter proteins that regulate dynein
CC       function (PubMed:11746667, PubMed:8702622). Cytoplasmic dynein 1 acts
CC       as a motor for the intracellular retrograde motility of vesicles and
CC       organelles along microtubules (PubMed:11746667, PubMed:8702622). May
CC       play a role in changing or maintaining the spatial distribution of
CC       cytoskeletal structures (PubMed:11746667, PubMed:8702622). In addition
CC       to its role in cytoskeleton and transport, acts as a protein-protein
CC       adapter, which inhibits and/or sequesters target proteins (By
CC       similarity). Involved in the response to DNA damage by acting as a key
CC       regulator of DNA end resection: when phosphorylated at Ser-88,
CC       recruited to DNA double-strand breaks (DSBs) by TP53BP1 and acts by
CC       disrupting MRE11 dimerization, thereby inhibiting DNA end resection (By
CC       similarity). In a subset of DSBs, DYNLL1 remains unphosphorylated and
CC       promotes the recruitment of the Shieldin complex (By similarity). Binds
CC       and inhibits the catalytic activity of neuronal nitric oxide
CC       synthase/NOS1 (PubMed:8864115). Promotes transactivation functions of
CC       ESR1 and plays a role in the nuclear localization of ESR1 (By
CC       similarity). Regulates apoptotic activities of BCL2L11 by sequestering
CC       it to microtubules (By similarity). Upon apoptotic stimuli the BCL2L11-
CC       DYNLL1 complex dissociates from cytoplasmic dynein and translocates to
CC       mitochondria and sequesters BCL2 thus neutralizing its antiapoptotic
CC       activity (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P63167,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:11746667, ECO:0000269|PubMed:8702622,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:8864115}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer. Monomer; the monomeric form is incapable of binding
CC       to target proteins (By similarity). The cytoplasmic dynein 1 complex
CC       consists of two catalytic heavy chains (HCs) and a number of non-
CC       catalytic subunits presented by intermediate chains (ICs), light
CC       intermediate chains (LICs) and light chains (LCs); the composition
CC       seems to vary in respect to the IC, LIC and LC composition
CC       (PubMed:11746667, PubMed:8702622). The heavy chain homodimer serves as
CC       a scaffold for the probable homodimeric assembly of the respective non-
CC       catalytic subunits (PubMed:11746667, PubMed:8702622). The ICs and LICs
CC       bind directly to the HC dimer and the LCs assemble on the IC dimer
CC       (PubMed:11746667, PubMed:8702622). Interacts with TXNDC17. Interacts
CC       with WWC1 and ESR1. The WWC1-DYNLL1 interaction is mandatory for the
CC       recruitment and transactivation functions of ESR1 or DYNLL1 to the
CC       target chromatin. Interacts with BCL2L11 (PubMed:21478148). Interacts
CC       with BCL2; the interaction is greatly enhanced in the nucleus and in
CC       mitochondria upon induction of apoptosis. Interacts with PAK1; the
CC       interaction requires dimeric DYNLL1. Interacts with MYZAP. Part of an
CC       astrin (SPAG5)-kinastrin (SKAP) complex containing KNSTRN, SPAG5, PLK1,
CC       DYNLL1 and SGO2. Interacts with ATMIN; this interaction inhibits ATMIN
CC       transcriptional activity and hence may play a role in a feedback loop
CC       whereby DYNLL1 inhibits transactivation of its own promoter by ATMIN.
CC       Interacts with NEK9 (not phosphorylated at 'Ser-944') (By similarity).
CC       Interacts with BICD2 (By similarity). Interacts with BCAS1
CC       (PubMed:16133941). Interacts with Basson/BSN. Interacts with HDAC6.
CC       Interacts with TPPP. Interacts with AMBRA1 (via TQT motifs); tethering
CC       AMBRA1 to the cytoskeleton. Interacts with FAM83D/CHICA (via C-
CC       terminus). Interacts with HMMR, SPAG5/Astrin and KNSTRN/Kinastrin.
CC       Interacts with TLK2 (By similarity). Interacts with NOS1
CC       (PubMed:8864115). Interacts with WWC1, WWC2 and WWC3. Interacts with
CC       MRE11; inhibiting MRE11 homodimerization and activity (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P63167, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P63168,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:11746667, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16133941,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:21478148, ECO:0000269|PubMed:8702622,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:8864115}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: (Microbial infection) Interacts with rabies virus
CC       phosphoprotein. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11602781}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm, cytoskeleton, microtubule organizing
CC       center, centrosome {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P63167}. Chromosome
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P63167}. Cytoplasm, cytoskeleton
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:8702622}. Nucleus {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P63167}.
CC       Mitochondrion {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P63167}. Note=Upon induction of
CC       apoptosis translocates together with BCL2L11 to mitochondria. Recruited
CC       to DNA double-strand breaks (DSBs) by TP53BP1 when phosphorylated at
CC       Ser-88. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P63167}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Weaker expression in the cerebellum and spinal cord
CC       compared with other brain regions.
CC   -!- PTM: Phosphorylation at Ser-88 promotes recruitment to DNA double-
