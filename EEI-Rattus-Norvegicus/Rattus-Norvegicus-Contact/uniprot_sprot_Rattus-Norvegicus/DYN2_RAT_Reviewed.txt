CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalyzes the hydrolysis of GTP and utilizes this energy to
CC       mediate vesicle scission at plasma membrane during endocytosis and
CC       filament remodeling at many actin structures during organization of the
CC       actin cytoskeleton (PubMed:19605363, PubMed:19995918, PubMed:35620056).
CC       Plays an important role in vesicular trafficking processes, namely
CC       clathrin-mediated endocytosis (CME), exocytic and clathrin-coated
CC       vesicle from the trans-Golgi network, and PDGF stimulated
CC       macropinocytosis (PubMed:19995918, PubMed:21195118, PubMed:9438853).
CC       During vesicular trafficking process, associates to the membrane,
CC       through lipid binding, and self-assembles into ring-like structure
CC       through oligomerization to form a helical polymer around the vesicle
CC       membrane and leading to vesicle scission (PubMed:35620056). Plays a
CC       role in organization of the actin cytoskeleton by mediating arrangement
CC       of stress fibers and actin bundles in podocytes (PubMed:35620056).
CC       During organization of the actin cytoskeleton, self-assembles into
CC       ring-like structure that directly bundles actin filaments to form
CC       typical membrane tubules decorated with dynamin spiral polymers
CC       (PubMed:35620056). Self-assembly increases GTPase activity and the GTP
CC       hydrolysis causes the rapid depolymerization of dynamin spiral
CC       polymers, and results in dispersion of actin bundles (PubMed:35620056).
CC       Remodels, through its interaction with CTTN, bundled actin filaments in
CC       a GTPase-dependent manner and plays a role in orchestrating the global
CC       actomyosin cytoskeleton (PubMed:19605363). The interaction with CTTN
CC       stabilizes the interaction of DNM2 and actin filaments and stimulates
CC       the intrinsic GTPase activity that results in actin filament-barbed
CC       ends and increases the sensitivity of filaments in bundles to the actin
CC       depolymerizing factor, CFL1 (PubMed:19605363). Plays a role in the
CC       autophagy process, by participating in the formation of ATG9A vesicles
CC       destined for the autophagosomes through its interaction with SNX18, by
CC       mediating recycling endosome scission leading to autophagosome release
CC       through MAP1LC3B interaction and by regulating maturation of apoptotic
CC       cell corpse-containing phagosomes by recruiting PIK3C3 to the phagosome
CC       membrane (By similarity). Also plays a role in cytokinesis
CC       (PubMed:21195118). May participate in centrosome cohesion through its
CC       interaction with TUBG1 (PubMed:15048127). Plays a role in the
CC       regulation of neuron morphology, axon growth and formation of neuronal
CC       growth cones (PubMed:21210813). Involved in membrane tubulation (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P39054,
