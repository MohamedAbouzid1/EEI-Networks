CC   -!- FUNCTION: Hydrolase that metabolizes extracellular nucleotides,
CC       including ATP, GTP, UTP and CTP (By similarity). Limits mast cells and
CC       basophils response during inflammation and during the chronic phases of
CC       allergic responses by eliminating extracellular ATP, a signaling
CC       molecule activating these cells in an autocrine manner. Metabolizes
CC       extracellular ATP in the lumen of the small intestine, and thereby
CC       prevents ATP-induced apoptosis of intestinal plasmacytoid dendritic
CC       cells (By similarity). Has a broad specificity and can also hydrolyze
CC       UDP-GlcNAc into UMP and GlcNAc-1-phosphate and potentially several
CC       other intracellular nucleotide sugars, including UDP-GalNAc, CMP-NeuAc,
CC       GDP-Fuc, and UDP-GlcA. Thereby, could modulate glycan biosynthesis and
CC       protein glycosylation (By similarity). Can hydrolyze extracellular
CC       dinucleoside polyphosphates, including the vasoactive adenosine
CC       polyphosphates as well. In addition, displays an alkaline
CC       phosphodiesterase activity in vitro (PubMed:9096610).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O14638, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q6DYE8,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9096610}.
