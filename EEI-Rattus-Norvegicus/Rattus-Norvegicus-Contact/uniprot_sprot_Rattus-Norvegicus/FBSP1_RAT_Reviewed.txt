CC   -!- FUNCTION: Component of E3 ubiquitin ligase complex consisting of
CC       FBXO45, MYCBP2 and SKP1. Functions in substrate recognition but plays
CC       also an important role in assembly of the complex (By similarity).
CC       Required for normal neuromuscular synaptogenesis, axon pathfinding and
CC       neuronal migration (By similarity). Regulates neuron migration during
CC       brain development through interaction with N-cadherin/CDH2 after
CC       secretion via a non-classical mechanism (By similarity). Plays a role
CC       in the regulation of neurotransmission at mature neurons
CC       (PubMed:19996097). May control synaptic activity by controlling UNC13A
CC       via ubiquitin dependent pathway (By similarity). Specifically
CC       recognizes TP73, promoting its ubiquitination and degradation.
CC       Polyubiquitinates NMNAT2, an adenylyltransferase that acts as an axon
CC       maintenance factor, and regulates its stability and degradation by the
CC       proteasome. Acts also by ubiquitinating FBXW7 during prolonged mitotic
CC       arrest and promotes FBXW7 proteasomal degradation. Induces subsequently
CC       an increase in mitotic slippage and prevents mitotic cell death. In
CC       response to influenza infection, mediates interferon-lambda receptor
CC       IFNLR1 polyubiquitination and degradation through the ubiquitin-
CC       proteasome system by docking with its intracellular receptor domain (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P0C2W1,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19996097}.
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Protein modification; protein ubiquitination.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P0C2W1}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Forms a complex with MYCBP2 and SKP1. Interacts with HEY1;
CC       leading to FBXO45 nuclear translocation. Interacts (via SPRY domain)
CC       with CDH2. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P0C2W1}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Secreted {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P0C2W1}.
CC       Postsynaptic cell membrane {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19996097}. Presynaptic
CC       cell membrane {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19996097}. Nucleus
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P0C2W1}. Note=Secreted by a non-classical
CC       mechanism. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P0C2W1}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the FBXO45/Fsn family. {ECO:0000305}.
