CC   -!- FUNCTION: Substrate recognition component of the SCF (SKP1-CUL1-F-box
CC       protein)-type E3 ubiquitin ligase complex, SCF(FBXO3), which mediates
CC       the ubiquitination and subsequent proteasomal degradation of target
CC       proteins. Mediates the ubiquitination of HIPK2 and probably that of
CC       EP300, leading to rapid degradation by the proteasome. In the presence
CC       of PML, HIPK2 ubiquitination still occurs, but degradation is
CC       prevented. PML, HIPK2 and FBXO3 may act synergically to activate
CC       p53/TP53-dependent transactivation. The SCF(FBXO3) also acts as a
CC       regulator of inflammation by mediating ubiquitination and degradation
CC       of FBXL2 in response to lipopolysaccharide (LPS) (By similarity). The
CC       SCF(FBXO3) complex specifically recognizes FBXL2 phosphorylated at
CC       'Thr-404' and promotes its ubiquitination (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9DC63, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9UK99}.
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Protein modification; protein ubiquitination.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9UK99}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Part of a SCF (SKP1-cullin-F-box) protein ligase complex
CC       SCF(FBXO3) consisting of FBXO3, SKP1, CUL1 and RBX1. Interacts with
CC       PML, interaction is direct and takes place either alone or within the
CC       SCF complex. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9UK99}.
