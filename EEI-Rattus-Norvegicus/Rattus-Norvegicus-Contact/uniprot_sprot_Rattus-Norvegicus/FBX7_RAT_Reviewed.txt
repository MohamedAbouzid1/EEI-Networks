CC   -!- FUNCTION: Substrate recognition component of a SCF (SKP1-CUL1-F-box
CC       protein) E3 ubiquitin-protein ligase complex which mediates the
CC       ubiquitination and subsequent proteasomal degradation of target
CC       proteins and plays a role in several biological processes such as cell
CC       cycle, cell proliferation, or maintenance of chromosome stability.
CC       Recognizes and ubiquitinates BIRC2 and the cell cycle regulator DLGAP5.
CC       Plays a role downstream of PINK1 in the clearance of damaged
CC       mitochondria via selective autophagy (mitophagy) by targeting PRKN to
CC       dysfunctional depolarized mitochondria. Promotes MFN1 ubiquitination.
CC       Mediates the ubiquitination and proteasomal degradation of UXT isoform
CC       2, thereby impairing the NF-kappa-B signaling pathway. Inhibits NF-
CC       kappa-B pathway also by promoting the ubiquitinatioin of TRAF2 (By
CC       similarity). Affects the assembly state and activity of the proteasome
CC       in the cells including neurons by ubiquitinating the proteasomal
CC       subunit PSMA2 via 'Lys-63'-linked polyubiquitin chains (By similarity).
CC       Promotes 'Lys-48'-linked polyubiquitination SIRT7, leading to the
CC       hydrogen peroxide-induced cell death (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q3U7U3, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Y3I1}.
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Protein modification; protein ubiquitination.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Part of the SCF (SKP1-CUL1-F-box) E3 ubiquitin-protein ligase
CC       complex SCF(FBXO7) formed of CUL1, SKP1, RBX1 and FBXO7. Interacts via
CC       its C-terminal proline-rich region with DLGAP5. Interacts with BIRC2.
CC       Interacts with CDK6 and promotes its interaction with D-type cyclin.
CC       Interacts (via the N-terminal Ubl domain) with PRKN. Interacts (via N-
CC       terminal region) with PINK1. Interacts with PSMF1 (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm {ECO:0000250}. Nucleus {ECO:0000250}.
CC       Mitochondrion {ECO:0000250}. Cytoplasm, cytosol {ECO:0000250}.
CC       Note=Predominantly cytoplasmic. A minor proportion is detected in the
CC       nucleus. Relocates from the cytosol to depolarized mitochondria (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The ubiquitin-like region mediates interaction with PRKN.
CC       {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The proline-rich region is important for protein-protein
CC       interactions. {ECO:0000250}.
