CC   -!- FUNCTION: Substrate recognition component of a SCF (SKP1-CUL1-F-box
CC       protein) E3 ubiquitin-protein ligase complex which mediates the
CC       ubiquitination and subsequent proteasomal degradation of target
CC       proteins and plays a role in several biological processes such as cell
CC       cycle, cell proliferation, or maintenance of chromosome stability.
CC       Ubiquitinates mTORC1-bound TTI1 and TELO2 when they are phosphorylated
CC       by CK2 following growth factor deprivation, leading to their
CC       degradation. In contrast, does not mediate ubiquitination of TTI1 and
CC       TELO2 when they are part of the mTORC2 complex. As a consequence,
CC       mTORC1 is inactivated to restrain cell growth and protein translation,
CC       while mTORC2 is the activated due to the relief of feedback inhibition
CC       by mTORC1. Plays a role in maintaining epithelial cell survival by
CC       regulating the turn-over of chromatin modulator PRMT4 through
CC       ubiquitination and degradation by the proteasomal pathway (By
CC       similarity). Regulates also PPARgamma stability by facilitating
CC       PPARgamma/PPARG ubiquitination and thereby plays a role in adipocyte
CC       differentiation (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8BK06,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9UK97}.
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Protein modification; protein ubiquitination.

CC       complex SCF(FBXO9) composed of CUL1, SKP1, RBX1 and FBXO9. Interacts
CC       with TTI1 and TELO2; when TTI1 and TELO2 are phosphorylated by CK2.
