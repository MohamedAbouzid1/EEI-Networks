CC   -!- FUNCTION: Involved in the fragmentation of the mitochondrial network
CC       and its perinuclear clustering (PubMed:15979461). Plays a minor role in
CC       the recruitment and association of the fission mediator dynamin-related
CC       protein 1 (DNM1L) to the mitochondrial surface and mitochondrial
CC       fission (By similarity). May not be essential for the assembly of
CC       functional fission complexes and the subsequent membrane scission event
CC       (By similarity). Also mediates peroxisomal fission (By similarity). May
CC       act when the products of fission are directed toward mitochondrial
CC       homeostasis, mitophagy, or apoptosis (By similarity). Can induce
CC       cytochrome c release from the mitochondrion to the cytosol, ultimately
CC       leading to apoptosis (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Y3D6,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15979461}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts with DNM1L/DLP1 through the TPR region; may form
CC       part of a larger protein complex at the endoplasmic reticulum-
CC       mitochondrial interface during mitochondrial fission. Interacts with
CC       MARCHF5. Interacts with MIEF1. Interacts with PEX11A, PEX11B and
CC       PEX11G. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Y3D6}.
