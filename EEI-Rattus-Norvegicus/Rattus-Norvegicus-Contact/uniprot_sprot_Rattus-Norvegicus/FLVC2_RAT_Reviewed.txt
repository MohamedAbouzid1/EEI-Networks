CC   -!- FUNCTION: Choline uniporter that specifically mediates choline uptake
CC       at the blood-brain-barrier. Responsible for the majority of choline
CC       uptake across the blood-brain-barrier from the circulation into the
CC       brain (By similarity). Choline, a nutrient critical for brain
CC       development, is a precursor of phosphatidylcholine, as well as betaine
CC       (By similarity). Also mediates transport of ethanolamine (By
CC       similarity). Choline and ethanolamine transport is not coupled with
CC       proton transport and is exclusively driven by the choline gradient
CC       across the plasma membrane (By similarity). However, the presence of an
CC       inwardly directed proton gradient enhances choline uptake (By
CC       similarity). Also acts as a heme b transporter (By similarity).
CC       Required to regulate mitochondrial respiration processes, ATP synthesis
CC       and thermogenesis (By similarity). At low heme levels, interacts with
CC       components of electron transfer chain (ETC) complexes and ATP2A2,
CC       leading to ubiquitin-mediated degradation of ATP2A2 and inhibition of
CC       thermogenesis (By similarity). Upon heme binding, dissociates from ETC
CC       complexes to allow switching from mitochondrial ATP synthesis to
CC       thermogenesis (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q91X85,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9UPI3}.

CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts with components of electron transfer chain complexes
CC       III, IV and V including CYC1, NDUFA4, COX4I1, ATP5PD and ATP5F1C; these
CC       interactions occur in the absence of heme and are disrupted upon heme
CC       binding. Interacts with ATP2A2; this interaction occurs in the absence
CC       of heme and promotes ATP2A2 proteasomal degradation; the complex is
CC       dissociated upon heme binding. Interacts with HMOX1; this interaction
CC       is potentiated in the presence of heme. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q91X85}.

CC       {ECO:0000255}. Endoplasmic reticulum membrane
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9UPI3}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000255}. Note=Present on both luminal (blood-facing) and
CC       abluminal (brain-facing) sides of brain endothelial cell plasma
CC       membranes, with higher luminal membrane expression (By similarity).
CC       Also localizes in mitochondria where it interacts with components of
CC       the electron transfer complexes III, IV and V. Colocalizes with ATP2A2
CC       at the mitochondrial-ER contact junction (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q91X85, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9UPI3}.

CC       canal of the spinal cord at 17 dpc (at protein level).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:14729055}.
