CC   -!- FUNCTION: Associates with and regulates the activity of the
CC       sodium/potassium-transporting ATPase (NKA) which catalyzes the
CC       hydrolysis of ATP coupled with the exchange of Na(+) and K(+) ions
CC       across the plasma membrane. Increases the apparent affinity of the
CC       transporter for Na(+) and increases NKA activity.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11483503}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Regulatory subunit of the sodium/potassium-transporting ATPase
CC       which is composed of a catalytic alpha subunit, a non-catalytic beta
CC       subunit and a regulatory subunit. The regulatory subunit, a member of
CC       the FXYD protein family, modulates the enzymatic activity in a
CC       tissue- and isoform-specific way by changing affinities of the Na+/K+-
CC       ATPase toward Na(+), K(+) or ATP. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11483503}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000305|PubMed:17567018};
CC       Single-pass type I membrane protein {ECO:0000305|PubMed:17567018}.
CC       Basolateral cell membrane {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11181413}; Single-pass
CC       type I membrane protein {ECO:0000305|PubMed:17567018}. Note=Detected in
CC       the basolateral membrane of collecting duct principal cells.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11181413}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Selectively present in the distal parts of the
CC       nephron (medullary and papillary collecting ducts and end portions of
CC       cortical collecting tubule) and in the epithelial cells of the distal
CC       colon. No expression is found in renal proximal tubule, loop of henle
CC       and distal tubule, proximal colon, small intestine, lung, choroid
CC       plexus, salivary glands, or brain. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:8843704}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: By corticosteroids. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:7597086}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the FXYD family. {ECO:0000305}.
