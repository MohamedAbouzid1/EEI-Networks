CC   -!- FUNCTION: Associates with and regulates the activity of the
CC       sodium/potassium-transporting ATPase (NKA) which catalyzes the
CC       hydrolysis of ATP coupled with the exchange of Na(+) and K(+) ions
CC       across the plasma membrane (By similarity). May increase NKA activity
CC       by increasing the apparent affinity for Na(+). Involved in down-
CC       regulation of E-cadherin which results in reduced cell adhesion.
CC       Promotes metastasis (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P97808,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q96DB9}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Regulatory subunit of the sodium/potassium-transporting ATPase
CC       which is composed of a catalytic alpha subunit, a non-catalytic beta
CC       subunit and an additional regulatory subunit. The regulatory subunit, a
CC       member of the FXYD protein family, modulates the enzymatic activity in
CC       a tissue- and isoform-specific way by changing affinities of the
CC       Na+/K+-ATPase toward Na(+), K(+) or ATP.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P97808}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P97808};
CC       Single-pass type I membrane protein {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P97808}.
CC       Basolateral cell membrane {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P97808}; Single-pass
CC       type I membrane protein {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P97808}. Note=In kidneys
CC       localizes to the basolateral membrane of the connecting tubule.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P97808}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Spleen, lung, skeletal muscle, and testis.
