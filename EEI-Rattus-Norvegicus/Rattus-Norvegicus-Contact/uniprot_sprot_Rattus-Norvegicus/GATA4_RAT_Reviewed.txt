CC   -!- FUNCTION: Transcriptional activator that binds to the consensus
CC       sequence 5'-AGATAG-3' and plays a key role in cardiac development (By
CC       similarity). In cooperation with TBX5, it binds to cardiac super-
CC       enhancers and promotes cardiomyocyte gene expression, while it down-
CC       regulates endocardial and endothelial gene expression (By similarity).
CC       Acts as a transcriptional activator of ANF in cooperation with NKX2-5
CC       (By similarity). Promotes cardiac myocyte enlargement (By similarity).
CC       Required during testicular development (By similarity). Involved in
CC       bone morphogenetic protein (BMP)-mediated induction of cardiac-specific
CC       gene expression (PubMed:15329343). Binds to BMP response element
CC       (BMPRE) DNA sequences within cardiac activating regions
CC       (PubMed:15329343). May play a role in sphingolipid signaling by
CC       regulating the expression of sphingosine-1-phosphate degrading enzyme,
CC       sphingosine-1-phosphate lyase (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P43694, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q08369,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15329343}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts with the homeobox domain of NKX2-5 through its C-
CC       terminal zinc finger. Also interacts with JARID2 which represses its
CC       ability to activate transcription of ANF. Interacts (via the second Zn
CC       finger) with NFATC4 (By similarity). Interacts with LMCD1 (By
CC       similarity). Forms a complex made of CDK9, CCNT1/cyclin-T1, EP300 and
CC       GATA4 that stimulates hypertrophy in cardiomyocytes. Interacts with
CC       NR5A1, ZFPM2 and TBX5. Interacts with ZNF260. Interacts with TBX18.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P43694, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q08369,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:16166646}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Nucleus {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P43694}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed predominantly in gastric mucosa and at
CC       much lower levels in the intestine. Also expressed in testis.
CC   -!- PTM: Methylation at Lys-299 attenuates transcriptional activity.
CC       {ECO:0000250}.
