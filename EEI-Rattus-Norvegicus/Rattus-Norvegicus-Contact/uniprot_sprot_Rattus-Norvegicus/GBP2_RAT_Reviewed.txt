CC   -!- FUNCTION: Interferon (IFN)-inducible GTPase that plays important roles
CC       in innate immunity against a diverse range of bacterial, viral and
CC       protozoan pathogens (By similarity). Hydrolyzes GTP to GMP in 2
CC       consecutive cleavage reactions, but the major reaction product is GDP
CC       (By similarity). Following infection, recruited to the pathogen-
CC       containing vacuoles or vacuole-escaped bacteria and acts as a positive
CC       regulator of inflammasome assembly by promoting the release of
CC       inflammasome ligands from bacteria. Acts by promoting lysis of
CC       pathogen-containing vacuoles, releasing pathogens into the cytosol.
CC       Following pathogen release in the cytosol, promotes recruitment of
CC       proteins that mediate bacterial cytolysis: this liberates ligands that
CC       are detected by inflammasomes, such as lipopolysaccharide (LPS) that
CC       activates the non-canonical CASP4/CASP11 inflammasome or double-
CC       stranded DNA (dsDNA) that activates the AIM2 inflammasome. Confers
CC       protection to the protozoan pathogen Toxoplasma gondii (By similarity).
CC       Independently of its GTPase activity, acts as an inhibitor of various
CC       viruses infectivity by inhibiting FURIN-mediated maturation of viral
CC       envelope proteins (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P32456,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Z0E6}.
