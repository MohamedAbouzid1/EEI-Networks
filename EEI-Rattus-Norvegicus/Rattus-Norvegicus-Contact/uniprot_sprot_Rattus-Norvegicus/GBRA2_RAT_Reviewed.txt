CC   -!- FUNCTION: Alpha subunit of the heteropentameric ligand-gated chloride
CC       channel gated by gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA), a major inhibitory
CC       neurotransmitter in the brain (By similarity). GABA-gated chloride
CC       channels, also named GABA(A) receptors (GABAAR), consist of five
CC       subunits arranged around a central pore and contain GABA active binding
CC       site(s) located at the alpha and beta subunit interface(s) (By
CC       similarity). When activated by GABA, GABAARs selectively allow the flow
CC       of chloride anions across the cell membrane down their electrochemical
CC       gradient (By similarity). Chloride influx into the postsynaptic neuron
CC       following GABAAR opening decreases the neuron ability to generate a new
CC       action potential, thereby reducing nerve transmission (By similarity).
CC       The alpha-2 subunit exhibits synaptogenic activity together with beta-2
CC       and very little to no activity together with beta-3, the gamma-2
CC       subunit being necessary but not sufficient to induce rapid synaptic
CC       contacts formation (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P10063,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P14867, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P26048,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P47869}.

CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: Activated by pentobarbital (By similarity).
CC       Inhibited by the antagonist bicuculline (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P10063}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Heteropentamer, formed by a combination of alpha (GABRA1-6),
CC       beta (GABRB1-3), gamma (GABRG1-3), delta (GABRD), epsilon (GABRE), rho
CC       (GABRR1-3), pi (GABRP) and theta (GABRQ) subunits, each subunit
CC       exhibiting distinct physiological and pharmacological properties (By
CC       similarity). Binds UBQLN1 (By similarity). Interacts with KIF21B
CC       (PubMed:25172774). Interacts with LHFPL4 (By similarity). Interacts
CC       with SHISA7; interaction leads to the regulation of GABA(A) receptor
CC       trafficking, channel deactivation kinetics and pharmacology (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P26048,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:25172774}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Postsynaptic cell membrane
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P26048}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000255}. Cell membrane {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P26048}; Multi-pass
CC       membrane protein {ECO:0000255}. Cytoplasmic vesicle membrane
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:25172774}. Cell projection, dendrite
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P26048}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed in brain (at protein level).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:25172774}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The extracellular domain contributes to synaptic contact
CC       formation. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P26048}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The GABA-binding pockets are located at the interface between
CC       neighboring alpha and beta subunits. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P14867}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: GABAARs subunits share a common topological structure: a
CC       peptide sequence made up of a long extracellular N-terminal, four
CC       transmembrane domains, intracellular or cytoplasmic domain located
CC       between the third and the fourth transmembrane domains.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P14867}.
