CC   -!- FUNCTION: Alpha subunit of the heteropentameric ligand-gated chloride
CC       channel gated by gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA), a major inhibitory
CC       neurotransmitter in the brain (By similarity). GABA-gated chloride
CC       channels, also named GABA(A) receptors (GABAAR), consist of five
CC       subunits arranged around a central pore and contain GABA active binding
CC       site(s) located at the alpha and beta subunit interface(s) (By
CC       similarity). Alpha-4/GABRA4 subunit often assembles with delta or
CC       gamma-2 subunits, in combination with beta subunits (By similarity).
CC       When activated by GABA, GABAARs selectively allow the flow of chloride
CC       anions across the cell membrane down their electrochemical gradient (By
CC       similarity). GABAARs containing alpha-4 are predominantly
CC       extrasynaptic, contributing to tonic inhibition in dentate granule
CC       cells and thalamic relay neurons (By similarity). Extrasynaptic alpha-
CC       4-containing GABAARs control levels of excitability and network
CC       activity (By similarity). GABAAR containing alpha-4-beta-3-delta
CC       subunits can simultaneously bind GABA and histamine where histamine
CC       binds at the interface of two neighboring beta subunits, which may be
CC       involved in the regulation of sleep and wakefulness (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P48169, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9D6F4}.
CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: Potentiated by histamine.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P48169}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Heteropentamer, formed by a combination of alpha (GABRA1-6),
CC       beta (GABRB1-3), gamma (GABRG1-3), delta (GABRD), epsilon (GABRE), rho
CC       (GABRR1-3), pi (GABRP) and theta (GABRQ) chains, each subunit
CC       exhibiting distinct physiological and pharmacological properties.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P48169}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9D6F4};
CC       Multi-pass membrane protein {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P48169}.
CC       Postsynaptic cell membrane; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P48169}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed in the brain.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:1655526}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The GABA-binding pockets are located at the interface between
CC       neighboring alpha and beta subunits. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P48169}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: GABAARs subunits share a common topological structure: a
CC       peptide sequence made up of a long extracellular N-terminal, four
CC       transmembrane domains, intracellular or cytoplasmic domain located
CC       between the third and the fourth transmembrane domains.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P14867}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the ligand-gated ion channel (TC 1.A.9) family.
CC       Gamma-aminobutyric acid receptor (TC 1.A.9.5) subfamily. GABRA4 sub-
CC       subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
