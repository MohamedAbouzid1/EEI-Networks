CC   -!- FUNCTION: Alpha subunit of the heteropentameric ligand-gated chloride
CC       channel gated by gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA), a major inhibitory
CC       neurotransmitter in the brain (PubMed:10462548, PubMed:1660002,
CC       PubMed:2153588). GABA-gated chloride channels, also named GABA(A)
CC       receptors (GABAAR), consist of five subunits arranged around a central
CC       pore and contain GABA active binding site(s) located at the alpha and
CC       beta subunit interface(s) (By similarity). When activated by GABA,
CC       GABAARs selectively allow the flow of chloride anions across the cell
CC       membrane down their electrochemical gradient (PubMed:10462548,
CC       PubMed:1660002, PubMed:2153588). GABAARs containing alpha-5/GABRA5
CC       subunits are mainly extrasynaptic and contribute to the tonic GABAergic
CC       inhibition in the hippocampus (By similarity). Extrasynaptic alpha-5-
CC       containing GABAARs in CA1 pyramidal neurons play a role in learning and
CC       memory processes (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P31644,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8BHJ7, ECO:0000269|PubMed:10462548,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:1660002, ECO:0000269|PubMed:2153588}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY:
CC       Reaction=chloride(in) = chloride(out); Xref=Rhea:RHEA:29823,
CC         ChEBI:CHEBI:17996; Evidence={ECO:0000269|PubMed:10462548,
CC         ECO:0000269|PubMed:1660002, ECO:0000269|PubMed:2153588};
CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: Allosterically potentiated by alphaxalone.
CC       Allosterically inhibited by pregnenolone sulfate. Inhibited by zinc and
CC       lanthanum. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10462548}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Heteropentamer, formed by a combination of alpha (GABRA1-6),
CC       beta (GABRB1-3), gamma (GABRG1-3), delta (GABRD), epsilon (GABRE), rho
CC       (GABRR1-3), pi (GABRP) and theta (GABRQ) chains, each subunit
CC       exhibiting distinct physiological and pharmacological properties.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10462548, ECO:0000269|PubMed:1660002}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Postsynaptic cell membrane
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P31644}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P31644}. Cell membrane
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P31644}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P31644}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed in brain areas such as cerebral cortex,
CC       hippocampal formation and olfactory bulb granular layer.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:2153588}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: GABAARs subunits share a common topological structure: a
CC       peptide sequence made up of a long extracellular N-terminal, four
CC       transmembrane domains, intracellular or cytoplasmic domain located
CC       between the third and the fourth transmembrane domains.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P31644}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the ligand-gated ion channel (TC 1.A.9) family.
CC       Gamma-aminobutyric acid receptor (TC 1.A.9.5) subfamily. GABRA5 sub-
CC       subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
