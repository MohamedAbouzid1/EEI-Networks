CC   -!- FUNCTION: Alpha subunit of the heteropentameric ligand-gated chloride
CC       channel gated by gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA), a major inhibitory
CC       neurotransmitter in the brain (PubMed:2166916, PubMed:9464994). GABA-
CC       gated chloride channels, also named GABA(A) receptors (GABAAR), consist
CC       of five subunits arranged around a central pore and contain GABA active
CC       binding site(s) located at the alpha and beta subunit interface(s) (By
CC       similarity). When activated by GABA, GABAARs selectively allow the flow
CC       of chloride anions across the cell membrane down their electrochemical
CC       gradient (By similarity). Alpha-6/GABRA6 subunits are found at both
CC       synaptic and extrasynaptic sites (PubMed:2166916, PubMed:9464994).
CC       Chloride influx into the postsynaptic neuron following GABAAR opening
CC       decreases the neuron ability to generate a new action potential,
CC       thereby reducing nerve transmission (By similarity). Extrasynaptic
CC       alpha-6-containing receptors contribute to the tonic GABAergic
CC       inhibition (PubMed:9464994). Alpha-6 subunits are also present on
CC       glutamatergic synapses (PubMed:9464994). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P08219,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P14867, ECO:0000269|PubMed:2166916,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9464994}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY:
CC       Reaction=chloride(in) = chloride(out); Xref=Rhea:RHEA:29823,
CC         ChEBI:CHEBI:17996; Evidence={ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P08219};
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Heteropentamer, formed by a combination of alpha (GABRA1-6),
CC       beta (GABRB1-3), gamma (GABRG1-3), delta (GABRD), epsilon (GABRE), rho
CC       (GABRR1-3), pi (GABRP) and theta (GABRQ) chains, each subunit
CC       exhibiting distinct physiological and pharmacological properties (By
CC       similarity). Binds UBQLN1 (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P14867, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P16305}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Postsynaptic cell membrane
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9464994}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000255}. Cell membrane {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9464994}; Multi-pass
CC       membrane protein {ECO:0000255}. Note=Present in Golgi synapses, on the
CC       extrasynaptic membranes, and in some of the mossy fiber to granule cell
CC       synapses (PubMed:9464994). Also found on the extrasynaptic somatic and
CC       dendritic membranes (PubMed:9464994). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9464994}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed in brain, in cerebellar granule cells.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:2166916, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9464994}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: GABAARs subunits share a common topological structure: a
CC       peptide sequence made up of a long extracellular N-terminal, four
CC       transmembrane domains, intracellular or cytoplasmic domain located
CC       between the third and the fourth transmembrane domains.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P14867}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the ligand-gated ion channel (TC 1.A.9) family.
CC       Gamma-aminobutyric acid receptor (TC 1.A.9.5) subfamily. GABRA6 sub-
CC       subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
