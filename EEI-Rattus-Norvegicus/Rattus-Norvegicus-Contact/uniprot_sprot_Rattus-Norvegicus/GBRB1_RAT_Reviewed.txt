CC   -!- FUNCTION: Beta subunit of the heteropentameric ligand-gated chloride
CC       channel gated by gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA), a major inhibitory
CC       neurotransmitter in the brain (PubMed:1977069). GABA-gated chloride
CC       channels, also named GABA(A) receptors (GABAAR), consist of five
CC       subunits arranged around a central pore and contain GABA active binding
CC       site(s) located at the alpha and beta subunit interface(s)
CC       (PubMed:30044221). When activated by GABA, GABAARs selectively allow
CC       the flow of chloride anions across the cell membrane down their
CC       electrochemical gradient (PubMed:1977069). Chloride influx into the
CC       postsynaptic neuron following GABAAR opening decreases the neuron
CC       ability to generate a new action potential, thereby reducing nerve
CC       transmission (PubMed:1977069). Beta-containing GABAARs can
CC       simultaneously bind GABA and histamine where histamine binds at the
CC       interface of two neighboring beta subunits, which may be involved in
CC       the regulation of sleep and wakefulness (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P28472, ECO:0000269|PubMed:1977069,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:30044221}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY:
CC       Reaction=chloride(in) = chloride(out); Xref=Rhea:RHEA:29823,
CC         ChEBI:CHEBI:17996; Evidence={ECO:0000269|PubMed:1977069};
CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: Potentiated by histamine.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P63138}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Heteropentamer, formed by a combination of alpha (GABRA1-6),
CC       beta (GABRB1-3), gamma (GABRG1-3), delta (GABRD), epsilon (GABRE), rho
CC       (GABRR1-3), pi (GABRP) and theta (GABRQ) chains, each subunit
CC       exhibiting distinct physiological and pharmacological properties
CC       (PubMed:30044221). Binds UBQLN1 (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P50571, ECO:0000269|PubMed:30044221}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Postsynaptic cell membrane
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P08220}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P08220}. Cell membrane
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P08220}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P08220}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: GABAARs subunits share a common topological structure: a
CC       peptide sequence made up of a long extracellular N-terminal, four
CC       transmembrane domains, intracellular or cytoplasmic domain located
CC       between the third and the fourth transmembrane domains.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:30044221}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the ligand-gated ion channel (TC 1.A.9) family.
CC       Gamma-aminobutyric acid receptor (TC 1.A.9.5) subfamily. GABRB1 sub-
CC       subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
