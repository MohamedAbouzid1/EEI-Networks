CC   -!- FUNCTION: Beta subunit of the heteropentameric ligand-gated chloride
CC       channel gated by gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA), a major inhibitory
CC       neurotransmitter in the brain (PubMed:1660002, PubMed:2548852). GABA-
CC       gated chloride channels, also named GABA(A) receptors (GABAAR), consist
CC       of five subunits arranged around a central pore and contain GABA active
CC       binding site(s) located at the alpha and beta subunit interface(s) (By
CC       similarity). When activated by GABA, GABAARs selectively allow the flow
CC       of chloride anions across the cell membrane down their electrochemical
CC       gradient (PubMed:1660002). Chloride influx into the postsynaptic neuron
CC       following GABAAR opening decreases the neuron ability to generate a new
CC       action potential, thereby reducing nerve transmission (By similarity).
CC       GABAARs containing alpha-1 and beta-2 or -3 subunits exhibit
CC       synaptogenic activity; the gamma-2 subunit being necessary but not
CC       sufficient to induce rapid synaptic contacts formation (By similarity).
CC       Extrasynaptic beta-2 receptors contribute to the tonic GABAergic
CC       inhibition (PubMed:9464994). Beta-containing GABAARs can simultaneously
CC       bind GABA and histamine where histamine binds at the interface of two
CC       neighboring beta subunits, which may be involved in the regulation of
CC       sleep and wakefulness (PubMed:18281286). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P08219,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P47870, ECO:0000269|PubMed:1660002,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:18281286, ECO:0000269|PubMed:2548852,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9464994}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY:
CC       Reaction=chloride(in) = chloride(out); Xref=Rhea:RHEA:29823,
CC         ChEBI:CHEBI:17996; Evidence={ECO:0000269|PubMed:1660002};
CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: Allosterically activated by benzodiazepines and
CC       the anesthetic etomidate (By similarity). Inhibited by the antagonist
CC       bicuculline (By similarity). Potentiated by histamine
CC       (PubMed:18281286). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P0C2W5,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P47870, ECO:0000269|PubMed:18281286}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Heteropentamer, formed by a combination of alpha (GABRA1-6),
CC       beta (GABRB1-3), gamma (GABRG1-3), delta (GABRD), epsilon (GABRE), rho
CC       (GABRR1-3), pi (GABRP) and theta (GABRQ) chains, each subunit
CC       exhibiting distinct physiological and pharmacological properties
CC       (PubMed:1660002, PubMed:18281286). Interacts with UBQLN1
CC       (PubMed:11528422). May interact with KIF21B (PubMed:25172774).
CC       Identified in a complex of 720 kDa composed of LHFPL4, NLGN2, GABRA1,
CC       GABRB2, GABRG2 and GABRB3 (PubMed:28279354).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11528422, ECO:0000269|PubMed:1660002,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:18281286, ECO:0000269|PubMed:25172774,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:28279354}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Postsynaptic cell membrane
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18281286, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9464994}; Multi-pass
CC       membrane protein {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P47870}. Cell membrane
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18281286, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9464994}; Multi-pass
CC       membrane protein {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P47870}. Cytoplasmic vesicle
CC       membrane {ECO:0000269|PubMed:25172774}. Note=The beta-2 subunits are
CC       present in Golgi synapses and on the extrasynaptic membranes (alpha-
CC       1/6-beta-2/3-gamma-2 receptors), and in some of the mossy fiber to
CC       granule cell synapses (alpha-6-beta-2/3-gamma-2 receptors). Present in
CC       GABAergic and glutamatergic synapses on granule cells (PubMed:9464994).
CC       The beta-2 subunit (alpha-6-beta-2/3-delta receptors) are found only on
CC       the extrasynaptic somatic and dendritic membranes (PubMed:9464994).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9464994}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed in brain (at protein level), in
CC       cerebellar granule cells. Expressed in lungs, in alveolar epithelium
CC       (PubMed:17003036). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17003036,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:25172774, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9464994}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The extracellular domain contributes to synaptic contact
CC       formation. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P63137}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: GABAARs subunits share a common topological structure: a
CC       peptide sequence made up of a long extracellular N-terminal, four
CC       transmembrane domains, intracellular or cytoplasmic domain located
CC       between the third and the fourth transmembrane domains.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P47870}.
