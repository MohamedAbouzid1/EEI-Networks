CC   -!- FUNCTION: Delta subunit of the heteropentameric ligand-gated chloride
CC       channel gated by gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA), a major inhibitory
CC       neurotransmitter in the brain (By similarity). GABA-gated chloride
CC       channels, also named GABA(A) receptors (GABAAR), consist of five
CC       subunits arranged around a central pore and contain GABA active binding
CC       site(s) located at the alpha and beta subunit interface(s) (By
CC       similarity). When activated by GABA, GABAARs selectively allow the flow
CC       of chloride anions across the cell membrane down their electrochemical
CC       gradient (By similarity). GABAARs containing delta/GABRD subunits are
CC       predominantly expressed and located in extrasynaptic or perisynaptic
CC       positions on hippocampus and cerebellar granule cells, and contribute
CC       to the tonic GABAergic inhibition (PubMed:9464994). GABAAR containing
CC       alpha-4-beta-3-delta subunits can simultaneously bind GABA and
CC       histamine where histamine binds at the interface of two neighboring
CC       beta subunits, which may be involved in the regulation of sleep and
CC       wakefulness (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O14764,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9464994}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY:
CC       Reaction=chloride(in) = chloride(out); Xref=Rhea:RHEA:29823,
CC         ChEBI:CHEBI:17996; Evidence={ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O14764};
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Heteropentamer, formed by a combination of alpha (GABRA1-6),
CC       beta (GABRB1-3), gamma (GABRG1-3), delta (GABRD), epsilon (GABRE), rho
CC       (GABRR1-3), pi (GABRP) and theta (GABRQ) chains, each subunit
CC       exhibiting distinct physiological and pharmacological properties.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O14764}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9464994};
CC       Multi-pass membrane protein {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O14764}. Note=Delta
CC       subunits are found only on the extrasynaptic somatic and dendritic
CC       membranes. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9464994}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Found in the brain, in cerebellar granule cells
CC       (PubMed:9464994). Expressed in lungs, in alveolar epithelium
CC       (PubMed:17003036). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17003036,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9464994}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: GABAARs subunits share a common topological structure: a
CC       peptide sequence made up of a long extracellular N-terminal, four
CC       transmembrane domains, intracellular or cytoplasmic domain located
CC       between the third and the fourth transmembrane domains.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O14764}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the ligand-gated ion channel (TC 1.A.9) family.
CC       Gamma-aminobutyric acid receptor (TC 1.A.9.5) subfamily. GABRD sub-
CC       subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
