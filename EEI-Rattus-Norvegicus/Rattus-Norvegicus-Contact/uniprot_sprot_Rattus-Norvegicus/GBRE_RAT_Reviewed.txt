CC   -!- FUNCTION: Epsilon subunit of the heteropentameric ligand-gated chloride
CC       channel gated by gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA), a major inhibitory
CC       neurotransmitter in the brain (By similarity). GABA-gated chloride
CC       channels, also named GABA(A) receptors (GABAAR), consist of five
CC       subunits arranged around a central pore and contain GABA active binding
CC       site(s) located at the alpha and beta subunit interfaces (By
CC       similarity). When activated by GABA, GABAARs selectively allow the flow
CC       of chloride anions across the cell membrane down their electrochemical
CC       gradient (By similarity). GABARs containing epsilon subunit may also
CC       permit spontaneous chloride channel activity while preserving the
CC       structural information required for GABA-gated openings (By
CC       similarity). GABARs containing epsilon subunit may regulate cardiac
CC       function (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P08219,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P47870, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P78334}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY:
CC       Reaction=chloride(in) = chloride(out); Xref=Rhea:RHEA:29823,
CC         ChEBI:CHEBI:17996; Evidence={ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P78334};
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Heteropentamer, formed by a combination of alpha (GABRA1-6),
CC       beta (GABRB1-3), gamma (GABRG1-3), delta (GABRD), epsilon (GABRE), rho
CC       (GABRR1-3), pi (GABRP) and theta (GABRQ) chains, each subunit
CC       exhibiting distinct physiological and pharmacological properties.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P18507}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000255}. Postsynaptic cell membrane; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000255}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed in brain and heart (PubMed:10804200).
CC       Strongly expressed in locus ceruleus from the first postnatal day
CC       (PubMed:10804200). Weakly expressed in other brainstem nuclei and in
CC       the hypothalamus (PubMed:10804200). Found in the cerebral cortex of
CC       pups (PubMed:10804200). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10804200}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: GABAARs subunits share a common topological structure: a
CC       peptide sequence made up of a long extracellular N-terminal, four
CC       transmembrane domains, intracellular or cytoplasmic domain located
CC       between the third and the fourth transmembrane domains.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P18507}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the ligand-gated ion channel (TC 1.A.9) family.
CC       Gamma-aminobutyric acid receptor (TC 1.A.9.5) subfamily. GABRE sub-
CC       subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
