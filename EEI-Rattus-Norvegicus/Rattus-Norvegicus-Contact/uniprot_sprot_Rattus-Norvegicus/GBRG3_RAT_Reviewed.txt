CC   -!- FUNCTION: Gamma subunit of the heteropentameric ligand-gated chloride
CC       channel gated by gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA), a major inhibitory
CC       neurotransmitter in the brain (PubMed:10462548, PubMed:1311098,
CC       PubMed:1660002). GABA-gated chloride channels, also named GABA(A)
CC       receptors (GABAAR), consist of five subunits arranged around a central
CC       pore and contain GABA active binding site(s) located at the alpha and
CC       beta subunit interface(s) (By similarity). When activated by GABA,
CC       GABAARs selectively allow the flow of chloride across the cell membrane
CC       down their electrochemical gradient (PubMed:10462548, PubMed:1311098,
CC       PubMed:1660002). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P18507,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:10462548, ECO:0000269|PubMed:1311098,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:1660002}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY:
CC       Reaction=chloride(in) = chloride(out); Xref=Rhea:RHEA:29823,
CC         ChEBI:CHEBI:17996; Evidence={ECO:0000269|PubMed:10462548,
CC         ECO:0000269|PubMed:1311098, ECO:0000269|PubMed:1660002};
CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: Allosterically potentiated by alphaxalone.
CC       Allosterically inhibited by pregnenolone sulfate. Inhibited by zinc and
CC       lanthanum. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10462548}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Heteropentamer, formed by a combination of alpha (GABRA1-6),
CC       beta (GABRB1-3), gamma (GABRG1-3), delta (GABRD), epsilon (GABRE), rho
CC       (GABRR1-3), pi (GABRP) and theta (GABRQ) chains, each subunit
CC       exhibiting distinct physiological and pharmacological properties.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10462548, ECO:0000269|PubMed:1660002}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Postsynaptic cell membrane; Multi-pass membrane
CC       protein {ECO:0000255}. Cell membrane; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000255}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed in brain. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:1311098}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: GABAARs subunits share a common topological structure: a
CC       peptide sequence made up of a long extracellular N-terminal, four
CC       transmembrane domains, intracellular or cytoplasmic domain located
CC       between the third and the fourth transmembrane domains.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P18507}.
CC   -!- PTM: May be palmitoylated. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P22723}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the ligand-gated ion channel (TC 1.A.9) family.
CC       Gamma-aminobutyric acid receptor (TC 1.A.9.5) subfamily. GABRG3 sub-
CC       subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
