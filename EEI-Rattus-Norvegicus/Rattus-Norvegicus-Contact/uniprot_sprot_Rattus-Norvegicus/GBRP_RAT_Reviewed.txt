CC   -!- FUNCTION: Pi subunit of the heteropentameric ligand-gated chloride
CC       channel gated by gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA) (PubMed:17003036).
CC       GABA-gated chloride channels, also named GABA(A) receptors (GABAAR),
CC       consist of five subunits arranged around a central pore and contain
CC       GABA active binding site(s) located at the alpha and beta subunit
CC       interfaces (By similarity). When activated by GABA, GABAARs selectively
CC       allow the flow of chloride anions across the cell membrane down their
CC       electrochemical gradient (PubMed:17003036). Pi-containing GABAARs are
CC       mostly located in peripheral tissues (PubMed:17003036). In the uterus,
CC       pi subunits modulate uterus contraction by altering the sensitivity of
CC       GABAARs to pregnanolone (By similarity). In the lungs, pi-containing
CC       GABAARs contribute to pulmonary fluid transport via luminal secretion
CC       of chloride (PubMed:17003036). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O00591,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P47870, ECO:0000269|PubMed:17003036}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY:
CC       Reaction=chloride(in) = chloride(out); Xref=Rhea:RHEA:29823,
CC         ChEBI:CHEBI:17996; Evidence={ECO:0000269|PubMed:17003036};
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Heteropentamer, formed by a combination of alpha (GABRA1-6),
CC       beta (GABRB1-3), gamma (GABRG1-3), delta (GABRD), epsilon (GABRE), rho
CC       (GABRR1-3), pi (GABRP) and theta (GABRQ) chains, each subunit
CC       exhibiting distinct physiological and pharmacological properties.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O00591}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000255}. Apical cell membrane {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17003036};
CC       Multi-pass membrane protein {ECO:0000255}. Note=Located on the apical
CC       plasma membrane of alveolar epithelial type II cells.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17003036}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed in lungs, in alveolar epithelium.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17003036}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: GABAARs subunits share a common topological structure: a
CC       peptide sequence made up of a long extracellular N-terminal, four
CC       transmembrane domains, intracellular or cytoplasmic domain located
CC       between the third and the fourth transmembrane domains.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P18507}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the ligand-gated ion channel (TC 1.A.9) family.
CC       Gamma-aminobutyric acid receptor (TC 1.A.9.5) subfamily. GABRP sub-
CC       subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
