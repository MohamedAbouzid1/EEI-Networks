CC   -!- FUNCTION: Rho subunit of the pentameric ligand-gated chloride channels
CC       responsible for mediating the effects of gamma-aminobutyric acid
CC       (GABA), the major inhibitory neurotransmitter in the brain
CC       (PubMed:8524843, PubMed:8905713). Rho-containing GABA-gated chloride
CC       channels are a subclass of GABA(A) receptors (GABAARs) entirely
CC       composed of rho subunits, where GABA molecules bind at the rho
CC       intersubunit interfaces (By similarity). When activated by GABA, rho-
CC       GABAARs selectively allow the flow of chloride anions across the cell
CC       membrane down their electrochemical gradient (PubMed:8524843,
CC       PubMed:8905713). Rho-1 subunits are primarily expressed in retina where
CC       rho-1-containing GABAARs may play a role in retinal neurotransmission
CC       (By similarity). Rho-1 GABAARs are also involved in neuronal tonic
CC       (extrasynaptic) and phasic (synaptic) transmission in the Purkinje
CC       neurons of the cerebellum (By similarity). Rho-1 GABAARs may also
CC       contribute to the regulation of glial development in the cerebellum by
CC       controlling extrasynaptic transmission (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P24046, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P56475,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:8524843, ECO:0000269|PubMed:8905713}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY:
CC       Reaction=chloride(in) = chloride(out); Xref=Rhea:RHEA:29823,
CC         ChEBI:CHEBI:17996; Evidence={ECO:0000269|PubMed:8524843,
CC         ECO:0000269|PubMed:8905713};
CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: Inhibited by TPMPA, a rho-specific antagonist (By
CC       similarity). Inhibited by picrotoxin, when forming a homopentamer
CC       (PubMed:8524843, PubMed:8905713). In contrast with other GABAARs, rho-1
CC       GABAAR is not inhibited by bicuculline, when forming a homopentamer
CC       (PubMed:8905713). Down-regulated by external protons when forming a
CC       homopentamer (PubMed:8905713). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P24046,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:8524843, ECO:0000269|PubMed:8905713}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Three rho subunits (rho-1/GBRR1, rho-2/GBRR2 and rho-3/GBRR3)
CC       coassemble either to form functional homopentamers or heteropentamers
CC       (PubMed:8524843, PubMed:8905713). Rho-1/GBRR1 subunits can also
CC       associate with alpha-1/GBRA1 subunits to form a functional GABAAR (By
CC       similarity). Interacts with SQSTM1 (PubMed:12431995).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P56475, ECO:0000269|PubMed:12431995,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:8524843, ECO:0000269|PubMed:8905713}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Postsynaptic cell membrane
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P56475}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P24046}. Cell membrane
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P24046}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P24046}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: GABAARs subunits share a common topological structure: a
CC       peptide sequence made up of a long extracellular N-terminal, four
CC       transmembrane domains, intracellular or cytoplasmic domain located
CC       between the third and the fourth transmembrane domains.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P24046}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the ligand-gated ion channel (TC 1.A.9) family.
CC       Gamma-aminobutyric acid receptor (TC 1.A.9.5) subfamily. GABRR1 sub-
CC       subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
