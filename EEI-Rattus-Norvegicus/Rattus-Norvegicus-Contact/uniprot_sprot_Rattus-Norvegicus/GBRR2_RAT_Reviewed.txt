CC   -!- FUNCTION: Rho subunit of the pentameric ligand-gated chloride channels
CC       responsible for mediating the effects of gamma-aminobutyric acid
CC       (GABA), the major inhibitory neurotransmitter in the brain
CC       (PubMed:8524843). Rho-containing GABA-gated chloride channels are a
CC       subclass of GABA(A) receptors (GABAARs) entirely composed of rho
CC       subunits, where GABA molecules bind at the rho intersubunit interfaces
CC       (By similarity). When activated by GABA, rho-GABAARs selectively allow
CC       the flow of chloride anions across the cell membrane down their
CC       electrochemical gradient (PubMed:8524843). Rho-2 GABAARs may contribute
CC       to the regulation of glial development in the cerebellum by controlling
CC       extrasynaptic transmission (By similarity). Rho-2 GABAARs are also
CC       involved in neuronal tonic (extrasynaptic) and phasic (synaptic)
CC       transmission in the Purkinje neurons of the cerebellum (By similarity).
CC       Rho-2 GABAARs expressed in retina may play a role in retinal
CC       neurotransmission (PubMed:7643126). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P24046,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P56476, ECO:0000269|PubMed:7643126,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:8524843}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY:
CC       Reaction=chloride(in) = chloride(out); Xref=Rhea:RHEA:29823,
CC         ChEBI:CHEBI:17996; Evidence={ECO:0000269|PubMed:8524843};
CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: In contrast with rho-1 and rho-3 homopentamers,
CC       rho-2 GABAARs are not inhibited by picrotoxin.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:8524843}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Three rho subunits (rho-1/GBRR1, rho-2/GBRR2 and rho-3/GBRR3)
CC       coassemble either to form functional homopentamers or heteropentamers
CC       (PubMed:8524843). Rho-2 is unable to form a functional homopentamer
CC       (PubMed:8524843). Interacts with SQSTM1 (PubMed:12431995).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12431995, ECO:0000269|PubMed:8524843}.
