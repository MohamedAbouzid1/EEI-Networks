CC   -!- FUNCTION: Rho subunit of the pentameric ligand-gated chloride channels
CC       responsible for mediating the effects of gamma-aminobutyric acid
CC       (GABA), the major inhibitory neurotransmitter in the brain
CC       (PubMed:11861315). Rho-containing GABA-gated chloride channels are a
CC       subclass of GABA(A) receptors (GABAARs) entirely composed of rho
CC       subunits, where GABA molecules bind at the rho intersubunit interfaces
CC       (By similarity). When activated by GABA, rho-GABAARs selectively allow
CC       the flow of chloride anions across the cell membrane down their
CC       electrochemical gradient (PubMed:11861315).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P24046, ECO:0000269|PubMed:11861315}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY:
CC       Reaction=chloride(in) = chloride(out); Xref=Rhea:RHEA:29823,
CC         ChEBI:CHEBI:17996; Evidence={ECO:0000269|PubMed:11861315};
CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: Activated by agonists in the following the potency
CC       order: muscimol > TACP > TACA > thiomuscimol > CAMP > CACA, when
CC       forming a homopentamer (PubMed:11861315). Inhibited by TPMPA, a rho-
CC       specific antagonist, when forming a homopentamer (PubMed:11861315).
CC       Inhibited antagonists in the following the potency order: TAMP = TPMPA
CC       > P4MPA = THIP > 14AA > 3-APA, when forming a homopentamer
CC       (PubMed:11861315). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11861315}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Three rho subunits (rho-1/GBRR1, rho-2/GBRR2 and rho-3/GBRR3)
CC       coassemble either to form functional homopentamers or heteropentamers
CC       (PubMed:11861315). Forms a ternary complex with SQSTM1 and PRKCZ
CC       (PubMed:12431995). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11861315,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:12431995}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Postsynaptic cell membrane; Multi-pass membrane
CC       protein {ECO:0000255}. Cell membrane; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000255}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed in retina. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:8605242}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: GABAARs subunits share a common topological structure: a
CC       peptide sequence made up of a long extracellular N-terminal, four
CC       transmembrane domains, intracellular or cytoplasmic domain located
CC       between the third and the fourth transmembrane domains.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P24046}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the ligand-gated ion channel (TC 1.A.9) family.
CC       Gamma-aminobutyric acid receptor (TC 1.A.9.5) subfamily. GABRR3 sub-
CC       subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
