CC   -!- FUNCTION: Hormone produced in response to various stresses to confer
CC       information about those stresses to the brain, and trigger an aversive
CC       response, characterized by nausea and/or loss of appetite
CC       (PubMed:28846099, PubMed:28953886, PubMed:31928886, PubMed:34187898,
CC       PubMed:37060902). The aversive response is both required to reduce
CC       continuing exposure to those stresses at the time of exposure and to
CC       promote avoidance behavior in the future (By similarity). Acts by
CC       binding to its receptor, GFRAL, activating GFRAL-expressing neurons
CC       localized in the area postrema and nucleus tractus solitarius of the
CC       brainstem (PubMed:28846099, PubMed:28953886, PubMed:37060902). It then
CC       triggers the activation of neurons localized within the parabrachial
CC       nucleus and central amygdala, which constitutes part of the 'emergency
CC       circuit' that shapes responses to stressful conditions
CC       (PubMed:37060902). The GDF15-GFRAL signal induces expression of genes
CC       involved in metabolism, such as lipid metabolism in adipose tissues (By
CC       similarity). Contributes to the effect of metformin, an anti-diabetic
CC       drug, on appetite reduction and weight loss: produced in the kidney in
CC       response to metformin treatment, thereby activating the GDF15-GFRAL
CC       response, leading to reduced appetite and weight (PubMed:37060902).
CC       Required for avoidance behavior in response to food allergens: induced
CC       downstream of mast cell activation to promote aversion and minimize
CC       harmful effects of exposure to noxious substances (By similarity).
CC       Produced in response to anticancer drugs, such as camptothecin or
CC       cisplatin, promoting nausea and contributing to malnutrition (By
CC       similarity). Overproduced in many cancers, promoting anorexia in cancer
CC       (cachexia) (By similarity). Responsible for the risk of nausea during
CC       pregnancy: high levels of GDF15 during pregnancy, mostly originating
CC       from embryos, are associated with increased nausea (By similarity).
CC       Maternal sensitivity to nausea is probably determined by pre-pregnancy
CC       exposure to GDF15, females with naturally high level of GDF15 being
CC       less susceptible to nausea than female rats with low levels of GDF15
CC       before pregnancy (By similarity). Promotes metabolic adaptation in
CC       response to systemic inflammation caused by bacterial and viral
CC       infections in order to promote tissue tolerance and prevent tissue
CC       damage (By similarity). Required for tissue tolerance in response to
CC       myocardial infarction by acting as an inhibitor of leukocyte integring
CC       activation, thereby protecting against cardiac rupture (By similarity).
CC       Inhibits growth hormone signaling on hepatocytes (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q99988, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Z0J7,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:28846099, ECO:0000269|PubMed:28953886,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:31928886, ECO:0000269|PubMed:34187898,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:37060902}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer; disulfide-linked (By similarity). Interacts with
CC       GFRAL and RET; ligand of GFRAL, which mediates GDF15 internalization
CC       and cellular signaling through interaction with RET via the formation
CC       of a 2:2:2 ternary complex composed of GDF15, GFRAL and RET (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q99988,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Z0J7}.
