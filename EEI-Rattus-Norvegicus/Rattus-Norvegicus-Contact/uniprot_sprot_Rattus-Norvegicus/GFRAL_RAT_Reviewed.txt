CC   -!- FUNCTION: Brainstem-restricted receptor for GDF15 hormone, which
CC       triggers an aversive response, characterized by nausea, vomiting,
CC       and/or loss of appetite in response to various stresses
CC       (PubMed:28846098, PubMed:28846099, PubMed:31928886, PubMed:37060902).
CC       The aversive response is both required to reduce continuing exposure to
CC       those stresses at the time of exposure and to promote avoidance
CC       behavior in the future (By similarity). The GDF15-GFRAL aversive
CC       response is triggered by stresses, such as anticancer drugs
CC       (camptothecin or cisplatin), cancers or drugs such as metformin
CC       (PubMed:37060902). Upon interaction with its ligand, GDF15, mediates
CC       the GDF15-induced autophosphorylation and activation of the RET
CC       tyrosine kinase receptor, leading to activation of MAPK- and
CC       AKT- signaling pathways (By similarity). Ligand-binding activates
CC       GFRAL-expressing neurons localized in the area postrema and nucleus
CC       tractus solitarius of the brainstem (By similarity). The GDF15-GFRAL
CC       signal induces expression of genes involved in metabolism, such as
CC       lipid metabolism in adipose tissues (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q6SJE0, ECO:0000269|PubMed:28846098,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:28846099, ECO:0000269|PubMed:31928886,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:37060902}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts (via the extracellular domain) with GDF15 and RET;
CC       receptor of GDF15, mediates cellular signaling through interaction with
CC       RET after GDF15-binding. Interaction with RET requires previous GDF15-
CC       binding. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q6SJE0}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000305|PubMed:28846098};
CC       Single-pass membrane protein {ECO:0000255}; Extracellular side
CC       {ECO:0000305|PubMed:28846098}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed in the brainstem, restricted to cells in
CC       the area postrema and the immediately adjacent region of the nucleus
CC       tractus solitarius (PubMed:28846097, PubMed:28846099). Detected at low
CC       levels in testis (PubMed:28846099). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:28846097,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:28846099}.
CC   -!- PTM: Cleaved and inactivated by MMP14, inhibiting the GDF15-GFRAL
CC       aversive response. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q6UXV0}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the GDNFR family. {ECO:0000305}.
