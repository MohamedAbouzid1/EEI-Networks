CC   -!- FUNCTION: Plays an important role in maintenance of mitochondrial
CC       morphology and in mediating either calcium or potassium/proton antiport
CC       (By similarity). Mediates proton-dependent calcium efflux from
CC       mitochondrion (By similarity). Functions also as an electroneutral
CC       mitochondrial proton/potassium exchanger (By similarity). Required for
CC       the mitochondrial tubular network and cristae organization (By
CC       similarity). Involved in apoptotic release of cytochrome c (By
CC       similarity). Inhibits AFG3L2 proteolytic activity, stimulating
CC       respiration and stabilizing respiratory enzymes in actively respiring
CC       mitochondria (By similarity). However, when mitochondria become
CC       hyperpolarized, GHITM loses its inhibitory activity toward AFG3L2 and
CC       the now active AFG3L2 turns first on GHITM and, if hyperpolarization
CC       persists, on other proteins of the mitochondria, leading to a broad
CC       remodeling of the proteome (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9H3K2}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts with LETM1 and AFG3L2.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9H3K2}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Mitochondrion inner membrane
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9H3K2}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000255}.
CC   -!- PTM: Undergoes AFG3L2-mediated proteolytic degradation, upon
CC       hyperpolarization of mitochondria. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9H3K2}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the BI1 family. {ECO:0000305}.
