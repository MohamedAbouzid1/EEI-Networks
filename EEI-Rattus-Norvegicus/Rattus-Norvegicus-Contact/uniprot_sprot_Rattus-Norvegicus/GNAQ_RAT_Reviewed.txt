CC   -!- FUNCTION: Guanine nucleotide-binding proteins (G proteins) function as
CC       transducers downstream of G protein-coupled receptors (GPCRs) in
CC       numerous signaling cascades. The alpha chain contains the guanine
CC       nucleotide binding site and alternates between an active, GTP-bound
CC       state and an inactive, GDP-bound state. Signaling by an activated GPCR
CC       promotes GDP release and GTP binding. The alpha subunit has a low
CC       GTPase activity that converts bound GTP to GDP, thereby terminating the
CC       signal. Both GDP release and GTP hydrolysis are modulated by numerous
CC       regulatory proteins. Signaling is mediated via phospholipase C-beta-
CC       dependent inositol lipid hydrolysis for signal propagation: activates
CC       phospholipase C-beta: following GPCR activation, GNAQ activates PLC-
CC       beta (PLCB1, PLCB2, PLCB3 or PLCB4), leading to production of
CC       diacylglycerol (DAG) and inositol 1,4,5-trisphosphate (IP3). Required
CC       for platelet activation. Regulates B-cell selection and survival and is
CC       required to prevent B-cell-dependent autoimmunity. Regulates chemotaxis
CC       of BM-derived neutrophils and dendritic cells (in vitro) (By
CC       similarity). Transduces FFAR4 signaling in response to long-chain fatty
CC       acids (LCFAs) (By similarity). Together with GNA11, required for heart
CC       development (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P21279,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P50148}.
