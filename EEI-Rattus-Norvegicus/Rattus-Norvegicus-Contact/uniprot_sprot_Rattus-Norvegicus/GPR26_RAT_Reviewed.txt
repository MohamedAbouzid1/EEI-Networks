CC   -!- FUNCTION: Orphan receptor. Displays a significant level of constitutive
CC       activity. Its effect is mediated by G(s)-alpha protein that stimulate
CC       adenylate cyclase, resulting in an elevation of intracellular cAMP (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane; Multi-pass membrane protein.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Detected in extracts of several brain regions
CC       including striatum, pons, cerebellum and cortex. Not detected in
CC       numerous peripheral tissue extracts, except in testis. In the brain,
CC       detected in cortical structures including the anterior cingulate area,
CC       posterior cingulate and the frontoparietal, somatosensory and piriform
CC       cortices. Prominent also in the olfactory tubercle, the islands of
CC       Calleja, ventromedial and posterior nuclei of the hypothalamus, the
CC       medial septal nucleus, nucleus of the diagonal band and the ventral
CC       tegmental area. Localized also to hippocampal structures, with signals
CC       strongest over the CA2 and CA3 regions of Ammon's horn and less so over
CC       the dentate gyrus. Expressed in the caudate putamen only in its most
CC       caudal portion, with a decreasing gradient of signal from the dorsal to
CC       ventral aspect. Strong expression associated with a single pontine
CC       structure, the inferior olivary nucleus.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the G-protein coupled receptor 1 family.
