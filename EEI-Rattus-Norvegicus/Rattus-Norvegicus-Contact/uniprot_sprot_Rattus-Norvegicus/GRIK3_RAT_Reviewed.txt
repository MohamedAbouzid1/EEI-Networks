CC   -!- FUNCTION: Ionotropic glutamate receptor that functions as a cation-
CC       permeable ligand-gated ion channel, gated by L-glutamate and the
CC       glutamatergic agonist kainic acid. Binding of the excitatory
CC       neurotransmitter L-glutamate induces a conformation change, leading to
CC       the opening of the cation channel, and thereby converts the chemical
CC       signal to an electrical impulse. The receptor then desensitizes rapidly
CC       and enters a transient inactive state, characterized by the presence of
CC       bound agonist (PubMed:1322826, PubMed:1371217, PubMed:21907808). In
CC       association with GRIK2, involved in presynaptic facilitation of
CC       glutamate release at hippocampal mossy fiber synapses (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:B1AS29, ECO:0000269|PubMed:1322826,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:1371217, ECO:0000269|PubMed:21907808}.
CC   -!- FUNCTION: [Isoform GluR7B]: Ionotropic glutamate receptor that
CC       functions as a ligand-gated cation channel, gated by L-glutamate and
CC       the glutamatergic agonist kainic acid. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9390526}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY:
CC       Reaction=Ca(2+)(in) = Ca(2+)(out); Xref=Rhea:RHEA:29671,
CC         ChEBI:CHEBI:29108; Evidence={ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:B1AS29};
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homotetramer, and heterotetramer with GRIK4 or GRIK5
CC       (PubMed:20951142, PubMed:21907808). Can form functional heteromeric
CC       receptors with GRIK2 (By similarity). Interacts with PRKCABP
CC       (PubMed:11122333). Interacts with NETO2 (PubMed:19217376).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:B1AS29, ECO:0000269|PubMed:11122333,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19217376, ECO:0000269|PubMed:20951142,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:21907808}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: [Isoform GluR7A]: Homomeric GluR7A forms functional kainate
CC       receptors which have very low sensitivity to glutamate. Can form
CC       functional heteromeric receptors with GRIK4 and GRIK5.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9390526}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: [Isoform GluR7B]: Homomeric GluR7B forms functional kainate
CC       receptors. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9390526}.
