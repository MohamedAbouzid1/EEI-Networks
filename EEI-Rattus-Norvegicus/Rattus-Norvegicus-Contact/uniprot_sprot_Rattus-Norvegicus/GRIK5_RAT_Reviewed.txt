CC   -!- FUNCTION: Ionotropic glutamate receptor that functions as a cation-
CC       permeable ligand-gated ion channel, gated by L-glutamate and the
CC       glutamatergic agonist kainic acid. Cannot form functional channels on
CC       its own and produces channel activity only in heteromeric assembly with
CC       GRIK1, GRIK2 and GRIK3 subunits. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:1373632,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:25139762, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9254673,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9390526}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homotetramer. Heterotetramer with GRIK2 (PubMed:21791290,
CC       PubMed:22509486). Can form functional heteromeric receptors with GRIK1,
CC       GRIK2 and GRIK3 (PubMed:25139762, PubMed:9390526).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:21791290, ECO:0000269|PubMed:22509486,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:25139762, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9390526}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000269|PubMed:1373632,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:22509486}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000255}. Postsynaptic cell membrane
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12954862}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000255}. Presynaptic cell membrane
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q61626}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000255}. Note=Association with GRIK2 is required for its cell
CC       membrane localization. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:22509486}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: High expression in the cerebral cortex, pyriform
CC       cortex, caudate-putamen, hippocampal complex, medial habenulata and
CC       granule cell layer of the cerebellum. Weak expression in globus
CC       pallidus, thalamus, colliculi and the reticular thalamic nucleus.
CC       Expressed at high levels in the trigeminal ganglion neurons.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12954862, ECO:0000269|PubMed:1373632,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9254673}.
CC   -!- DEVELOPMENTAL STAGE: From embryonic day 14 through postnatal day 1,
CC       high expression in spinal cord, brain and some areas of the PNS. At 17
CC       dpc and 19 dpc, high expression in spinal cord, mesencephalon and
CC       telencephalic structures as in olfactory neurons and in nasal
CC       epithelium. Strong expression also in the pituitary.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:1373632}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the glutamate-gated ion channel (TC 1.A.10.1)
CC       family. GRIK5 subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
