CC   -!- FUNCTION: Functions as a cation- and diacylglycerol (DAG)-regulated
CC       nucleotide exchange factor activating Ras through the exchange of bound
CC       GDP for GTP (PubMed:12493770). In neutrophils, participates in a
CC       phospholipase C-activating N-formyl peptide-activated GPCR (G protein-
CC       coupled receptor) signaling pathway by promoting Ras-mediated
CC       activation of PIK3CG/PI3Kgamma to promote neutrophil functional
CC       responses (By similarity). In CD117(+) dendritic cells and mast cells,
CC       participates in an lipopolysaccharide (LPS)-activated signaling pathway
CC       that stimulates the production of interferon-gamma and other pro-
CC       inflammatory cytokines by natural killer (NK) cells (By similarity).
CC       May function in mast cell differentiation (PubMed:12493770). Does not
CC       appear to be required for the development of B-cells, DC-cells, T-
CC       cells, or NK-cells (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8BTM9,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:12493770}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8TDF6}. Cell
CC       membrane {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8TDF6}. Note=Recruited to membranes
CC       upon activation by DAG. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8BTM9}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed by mast cells and their progenitors (at
CC       protein level). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12217396}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The phorbol-ester/DAG-type zinc finger mediates the binding and
CC       the functional activation by DAG. {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the RASGRP family. {ECO:0000305}.
