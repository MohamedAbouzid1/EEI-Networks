CC   -!- FUNCTION: Ribosome-binding protein that promotes ribosome hibernation,
CC       a process during which ribosomes are stabilized in an inactive state
CC       and preserved from proteasomal degradation (By similarity). Acts via
CC       its association with EEF2/eEF2 factor at the A-site of the ribosome,
CC       promoting ribosome stabilization in an inactive state compatible with
CC       storage (By similarity). Plays a key role in ribosome hibernation in
CC       the mature oocyte by promoting ribosome stabilization (By similarity).
CC       Ribosomes, which are produced in large quantities during oogenesis, are
CC       stored and translationally repressed in the oocyte and early embryo (By
CC       similarity). Also binds RNA, regulating transcription and pre-mRNA
CC       splicing. Binds (via C-terminus) to poly(U) RNA. Seems to play a role
CC       in PML-nuclear bodies formation (By similarity). Negatively regulates
CC       DNA-binding activity of the transcription factor MEF2C in myocardial
CC       cells in response to mechanical stress (PubMed:15862299).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q5JVS0, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q5XJA5,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15862299}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Associates with ribosomes; promoting ribosome stabilization
CC       (By similarity). Interacts with EEF2/eEF2; promoting ribosome
CC       stabilization (By similarity). Interacts with FMR1. Interacts with FXR1
CC       and FXR2. Interacts with CHD3 (via C-terminus). Interacts (via C-
CC       terminus) with RACK1. Interacts with p53/TP53. Interacts (via N-
CC       terminus) with SRSF9; this interaction is direct. Interacts with
CC       SYNCRIP; this interaction is direct (By similarity). Interacts with
CC       MEF2C (via N-terminus); this interaction decreases DNA-binding activity
CC       of MEF2C in myocardial cells in response to mechanical stress
CC       (PubMed:15862299). Interacts with PRMT1 (via N-terminus). Interacts
CC       with SPIN1 (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q5JVS0,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q5XJA5, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9JKS5,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15862299}.

CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15862299}. Cytoplasm, Stress granule
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q5JVS0}. Cytoplasm, sarcoplasm

CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q5JVS0}. Note=Transported into the nuclear
CC       compartment in activated leukocytes. Inhibition of methylation alters
CC       its distribution between the nuclear and cytoplasmic compartments.
CC       Methylation may be required for its localization in subnuclear
CC       structures, such as nucleoli, nuclear speckles, Cajal bodies and Gemini

CC       cytoplasmic stress granules (By similarity). In myocardial cells,

CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15862299}.
