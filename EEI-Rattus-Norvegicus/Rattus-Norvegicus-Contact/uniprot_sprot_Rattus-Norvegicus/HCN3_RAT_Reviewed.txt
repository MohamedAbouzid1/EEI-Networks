CC   -!- FUNCTION: Hyperpolarization-activated ion channel that are permeable to
CC       sodium and potassium ions, with an about 3:1 preference for potassium
CC       ions. Contributes to the native pacemaker currents in heart (If) and in
CC       neurons (Ih). In particular, plays a pivotal role in maintaining
CC       excitability and promoting rhythmic burst firing within hypothalamic
CC       nuclei. Exerts a significant influence on the configuration of the
CC       cardiac action potential waveform. Does not appear to play a prominent
CC       role in the processing of acute, neuropathic, or inflammatory pain.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O88705}.

CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: Inhibited by Cs(1+) and ivabradine. Unlike HCN2
CC       and HCN4, HCN3 is insensitive to cyclic nucleotides, such as cAMP or
CC       cGMP. This lack of sensitivity of HCN3, despite harboring a functional
CC       cyclic nucleotide-binding domain (CNBD), may be explained by its
CC       shorter C-terminal sequence, which may alter the normal autoinhibition
CC       of the channel. Phosphatidylinositol-4,5-bisphosphate (PIP(2)) shifts
CC       HCN3 activation to more depolarized potentials and accelerated
CC       activation kinetics. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O88705}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homotetramer. The potassium channel is composed of a homo- or
CC       heterotetrameric complex of pore-forming subunits (By similarity).
CC       Interacts with HCN1 (By similarity). Interacts with KCTD3; this
CC       interaction increases cell surface expression and current density of
CC       this channel. Interacts with PEX5L (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O88705, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9UL51}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O88705};
CC       Multi-pass membrane protein {ECO:0000255}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The segment S4 is the voltage-sensor and is characterized by a
CC       series of positively charged amino acids at every third position. The
CC       ion-conducting pore region is between segment S5 and S6.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O60741}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the potassium channel HCN family. {ECO:0000305}.
