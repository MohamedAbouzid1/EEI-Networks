CC   -!- FUNCTION: Hyperpolarization-activated ion channel that are permeable to
CC       Na(+) and K(+) ions with very slow activation and inactivation.
CC       Exhibits higher selectivity for K(+) over Na(+) ions. Contributes to
CC       the native pacemaker currents in heart (If) that regulate the rhythm of
CC       heart beat. Contributes to the native pacemaker currents in neurons
CC       (Ih) (By similarity). May mediate responses to sour stimuli
CC       (PubMed:11675786). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Y3Q4,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:11675786}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY:
CC       Reaction=K(+)(in) = K(+)(out); Xref=Rhea:RHEA:29463, ChEBI:CHEBI:29103;
CC         Evidence={ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Y3Q4};
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY:
CC       Reaction=Na(+)(in) = Na(+)(out); Xref=Rhea:RHEA:34963,
CC         ChEBI:CHEBI:29101; Evidence={ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Y3Q4};
CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: Activated by cAMP and at 100 times higher
CC       concentrationsand to a lesser extent by cGMP and cCMP. cAMP binding
CC       causes a conformation change that leads to the assembly of an active
CC       tetramer and channel opening. Binding of cAMP removes a tonic
CC       inhibition conferred by cyclic nucleotide-binding domain (CNBD) on
CC       channel opening. Cyclic dinucleotides can modulate HCN4 channel; cyclic
CC       dinucleotides acting as potent antagonists of cAMP. Inhibited by
CC       extracellular Cs(+) ions (By similarity). Auxiliary subunits can also
CC       regulate HCN4 channel. IRAG1 causes a gain-of-function by shifting HCN4
CC       activation to more depolarized membrane potentials in the absence of
CC       cAMP. In contrast, IRAG2 causes a loss-of-function by inhibiting cAMP-
CC       dependent potentiation of HCN4 activation (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O70507, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Y3Q4}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homotetramer (By similarity). The potassium channel is
CC       composed of a homo- or heterotetrameric complex of pore-forming
CC       subunits (By similarity). Interacts with PEX5L with a 4:4 HCN4:PEX5L
CC       stoichiometry; reduces the effects of cAMP on the voltage-dependence
CC       and rate of activation (By similarity). Interacts with IRAG1; regulates
CC       HCN4 channel activity (By similarity). Interacts with IRAG2; regulates
CC       HCN4 channel activity (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O70507,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9TV66, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Y3Q4}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11675786};
CC       Multi-pass membrane protein {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Y3Q4}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Highly expressed in pyramidal and granule layer of
CC       the hippocampus, in thalamus anterior nucleus, in the supraoptic
CC       nucleus in hypothalamus, in cerebellum, and in trapezoid nuclei and
CC       superior olivary complex in the auditory system. Detected in a subset
CC       of elongated cells in taste buds. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11000485,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:11675786}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: Contains six transmembrane segments (S1-S6) and an intervening
CC       P-loop. The segment S4 is the voltage-sensor and is characterized by a
CC       series of positively charged amino acids at every third position, while
CC       the S5-S6 segments together with the P-loop form a centrally located
CC       pore of the channel. Contains a cyclic nucleotide-binding domain (CNBD)
CC       in their C-terminal region. The CNBD is connected to the pore forming
CC       transmembrane segment via the C-linker. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Y3Q4}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: Contains a unique pocket located in the cytosolic C-terminal
CC       domain, identified as a likely binding site for di-cyclic nucleotides.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Y3Q4}.
CC   -!- PTM: S-palmitoylated. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Y3Q4}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the potassium channel HCN family. {ECO:0000305}.
