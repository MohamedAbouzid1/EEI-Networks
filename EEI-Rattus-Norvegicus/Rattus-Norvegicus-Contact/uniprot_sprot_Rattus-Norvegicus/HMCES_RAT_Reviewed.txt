CC   -!- FUNCTION: Sensor of abasic sites in single-stranded DNA (ssDNA)
CC       required to preserve genome integrity by promoting error-free repair of
CC       abasic sites. Acts as an enzyme that recognizes and binds abasic sites
CC       in ssDNA at replication forks and chemically modifies the lesion by
CC       forming a covalent cross-link with DNA: forms a stable thiazolidine
CC       linkage between a ring-opened abasic site and the alpha-amino and
CC       sulfhydryl substituents of its N-terminal catalytic cysteine residue.
CC       Promotes error-free repair by protecting abasic sites from translesion
CC       synthesis (TLS) polymerases and endonucleases that are error-prone and
CC       would generate mutations and double-strand breaks. The HMCES DNA-
CC       protein cross-link is then either reversed or degraded. HMCES is able
CC       to catalyze the reversal of its thiazolidine cross-link and cycle
CC       between a cross-link and a non-cross-linked state depending on DNA
CC       context: mediates self-reversal of the thiazolidine cross-link in
CC       double stranded DNA, allowing APEX1 to initiate downstream repair of
CC       abasic sites. The HMCES DNA-protein cross-link can also be degraded by
CC       the SPRTN metalloprotease following unfolding by the BRIP1/FANCJ
CC       helicase. Has preference for ssDNA, but can also accommodate double-
CC       stranded DNA with 3' or 5' overhang (dsDNA), and dsDNA-ssDNA 3'
CC       junction. Plays a protective role during somatic hypermutation of
CC       immunoglobulin genes in B-cells: acts via its ability to form covalent
CC       cross-links with abasic sites, thereby limiting the accumulation of
CC       deletions in somatic hypermutation target regions (By similarity). Also
CC       involved in class switch recombination (CSR) in B-cells independently
CC       of the formation of a DNA-protein cross-link: acts by binding and
CC       protecting ssDNA overhangs to promote DNA double-strand break repair
CC       through the microhomology-mediated alternative-end-joining (Alt-EJ)
CC       pathway. Acts as a protease: mediates autocatalytic processing of its
CC       N-terminal methionine in order to expose the catalytic cysteine (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8R1M0,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q96FZ2}.
CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: Formation and reversal of DNA-protein cross-link
CC       depends on DNA context. Catalyzes formation of the thiazolidine linkage
CC       in presence of abasic sites in single-stranded DNA. Mediates the
CC       reversal of the thiazolidine cross-link in presence of double stranded
CC       DNA. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q96FZ2}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts (via PIP-box motif) with PCNA.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q96FZ2}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Chromosome {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q96FZ2}.
CC       Note=Recruited to chromatin following DNA damage. Localizes to
CC       replication forks. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q96FZ2}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The N-terminal catalytic Cys-2 residue forms a thiazolidine
CC       linkage to a ring-opened DNA abasic site. Glu-127 catalyzes reversal of
CC       the thiazolidine linkage; self-reversal is favoured by duplex DNA
CC       formation (By similarity). Glu-127 is also involved in sensing abasic
CC       sites in single-stranded DNA (ssDNA). His-209 stabilizes the abasic
CC       sites by forming a hydrogen bond with the O4' hydroxyl group (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P76318,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q96FZ2}.
CC   -!- PTM: Ubiquitinated; the covalent HMCES DNA-protein cross-link is
CC       ubiquitinated, leading to its degradation by the proteasome.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q96FZ2}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the SOS response-associated peptidase family.
CC       {ECO:0000305}.
