CC   -!- FUNCTION: G-protein-coupled receptor for histamine, a biogenic amine
CC       that functions as an immune modulator and a neurotransmitter
CC       (PubMed:7678492). Through the H1 receptor, histamine mediates the
CC       contraction of smooth muscles and increases capillary permeability due
CC       to contraction of terminal venules. Also mediates neurotransmission in
CC       the central nervous system and thereby regulates circadian rhythms,
CC       emotional and locomotor activities as well as cognitive functions (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P70174,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:7678492}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000269|PubMed:7678492};
CC       Multi-pass membrane protein {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P35367}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: Histamine activates the receptor by forming hydrogen bonds with
CC       transmembrane domains 3 and 6, squashing the ligand-binding pocket on
CC       the extracellular side and opening the cavity for G-protein engagement
CC       on the intracellular side. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P35367}.
CC   -!- PTM: Phosphorylation at sites in the second and third cytoplasmic loops
CC       independently contribute to agonist-induced receptor down-regulation.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P35367}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the G-protein coupled receptor 1 family.
CC       {ECO:0000255|PROSITE-ProRule:PRU00521}.
