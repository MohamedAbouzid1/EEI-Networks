CC   -!- FUNCTION: Cell surface receptor that plays a role in the regulation of
CC       IL-13-mediated responses. Functions as a decoy receptor that inhibits
CC       IL-13- and IL-4-mediated signal transduction via the JAK-STAT pathway
CC       and thereby modulates immune responses and inflammation. Serves as a
CC       functional signaling receptor for IL-13 in an alternative pathway
CC       involving AP-1 ultimately leading to the production of TGFB1.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q14627}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts with IL4RA. Interacts with high affinity to
CC       interleukin-13 (IL13), but not to interleukin-4 (IL4).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q14627}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q14627};
CC       Single-pass type I membrane protein {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q14627}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The WSXWS motif appears to be necessary for proper protein
CC       folding and thereby efficient intracellular transport and cell-surface
CC       receptor binding. The cytoplasmic domain functions as an inhibitor of
CC       IL-4 or IL-13-dependent activation of the Jak-Stat pathway.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q14627}.
CC   -!- PTM: Cleaved by MMP8 leading to a soluble form that is also able to
CC       interact with IL13. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q14627}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the type I cytokine receptor family. Type 5
CC       subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
