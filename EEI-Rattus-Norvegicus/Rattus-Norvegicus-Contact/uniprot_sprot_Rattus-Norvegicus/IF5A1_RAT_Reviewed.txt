CC   -!- FUNCTION: Translation factor that promotes translation elongation and
CC       termination, particularly upon ribosome stalling at specific amino acid
CC       sequence contexts (By similarity). Binds between the exit (E) and
CC       peptidyl (P) site of the ribosome and promotes rescue of stalled
CC       ribosome: specifically required for efficient translation of
CC       polyproline-containing peptides as well as other motifs that stall the
CC       ribosome. Acts as a ribosome quality control (RQC) cofactor by joining
CC       the RQC complex to facilitate peptidyl transfer during CAT tailing step
CC       (By similarity). Also involved in actin dynamics and cell cycle
CC       progression, mRNA decay and probably in a pathway involved in stress
CC       response and maintenance of cell wall integrity. With syntenin SDCBP,
CC       functions as a regulator of p53/TP53 and p53/TP53-dependent apoptosis.
CC       Regulates also TNF-alpha-mediated apoptosis. Mediates effects of
CC       polyamines on neuronal process extension and survival (By similarity).
CC       May play an important role in brain development and function, and in
CC       skeletal muscle stem cell differentiation (PubMed:18606156,
CC       PubMed:19006180). Is required for autophagy by assisting the ribosome
CC       in translating the ATG3 protein at a specific amino acid sequence, the
CC       'ASP-ASP-Gly' motif, leading to the increase of the efficiency of ATG3
CC       translation and facilitation of LC3B lipidation and autophagosome
CC       formation (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P23301,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P63241, ECO:0000269|PubMed:18606156,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19006180}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Binds to 80S ribosomes. Actively translating ribosomes show
CC       mutually exclusive binding of eIF5a (EIF5A or EIF5A2) and EEF2/eEF2 (By
CC       similarity). Interacts with DAPL1; interaction takes place at the
CC       polypeptide exit tunnel of hibernating ribosomes and prevents
CC       translation (By similarity). Interacts with DHPS. Interacts with SDCBP.
CC       Interacts with DOHH (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P63241,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q6NX89}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P63241}. Nucleus
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P63241}. Endoplasmic reticulum membrane
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P63241}; Peripheral membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P63241}; Cytoplasmic side
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P63241}. Note=Hypusine modification promotes the
CC       nuclear export and cytoplasmic localization and there was a dynamic
CC       shift in the localization from predominantly cytoplasmic to primarily
CC       nuclear under apoptotic inducing conditions. Nuclear export of
CC       hypusinated protein is mediated by XPO4.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P63241}.
CC   -!- PTM: Acetylated by PCAF/KAT2B, regulating its subcellular localization
CC       (By similarity). Deacetylated by SIRT2 (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P63241}.
CC   -!- PTM: Lys-50 undergoes hypusination, a unique post-translational
CC       modification that consists in the addition of a butylamino group from
CC       spermidine to lysine side chain, leading to the formation of the
CC       unusual amino acid hypusine. eIF-5As are the only known proteins to
CC       undergo this modification, which is essential for their function.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P63241}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the eIF-5A family. {ECO:0000305}.
