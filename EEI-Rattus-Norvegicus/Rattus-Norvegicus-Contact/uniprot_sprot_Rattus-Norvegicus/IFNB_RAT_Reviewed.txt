CC   -!- FUNCTION: Type I interferon cytokine that plays a key role in the
CC       innate immune response to infection, developing tumors and other
CC       inflammatory stimuli. Signals via binding to high-affinity (IFNAR2) and
CC       low-affinity (IFNAR1) heterodimeric receptor, activating the canonical
CC       Jak-STAT signaling pathway resulting in transcriptional activation or
CC       repression of interferon-regulated genes that encode the effectors of
CC       the interferon response, such as antiviral proteins, regulators of cell
CC       proliferation and differentiation, and immunoregulatory proteins (By
CC       similarity). Signals mostly via binding to a IFNAR1-IFNAR2
CC       heterodimeric receptor, but can also function with IFNAR1 alone and
CC       independently of Jak-STAT pathways. Elicits a wide variety of
CC       responses, including antiviral and antibacterial activities, and can
CC       regulate the development of B-cells, myelopoiesis and
CC       lipopolysaccharide (LPS)-inducible production of tumor necrosis factor.
CC       Plays a role in neuronal homeostasis by regulating dopamine turnover
CC       and protecting dopaminergic neurons: acts by promoting neuronal
CC       autophagy and alpha-synuclein clearance, thereby preventing
CC       dopaminergic neuron loss. IFNB1 is more potent than interferon-alpha
CC       (IFN-alpha) in inducing the apoptotic and antiproliferative pathways
CC       required for control of tumor cell growth (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P01574, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P01575}.
