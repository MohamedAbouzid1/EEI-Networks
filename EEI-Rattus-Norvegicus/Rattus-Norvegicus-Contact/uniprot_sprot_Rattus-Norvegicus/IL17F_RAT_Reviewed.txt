CC   -!- FUNCTION: Effector cytokine of innate and adaptive immune system
CC       involved in antimicrobial host defense and maintenance of tissue
CC       integrity. IL17A-IL17F signals via IL17RA-IL17RC heterodimeric receptor
CC       complex, triggering homotypic interaction of IL17RA and IL17RC chains
CC       with TRAF3IP2 adapter through SEFIR domains. This leads to downstream
CC       TRAF6-mediated activation of NF-kappa-B and MAPkinase pathways
CC       ultimately resulting in transcriptional activation of cytokines,
CC       chemokines, antimicrobial peptides and matrix metalloproteinases, with
CC       potential strong immune inflammation. IL17A-IL17F is primarily involved
CC       in host defense against extracellular bacteria and fungi by inducing
CC       neutrophilic inflammation. As signature effector cytokine of T-helper
CC       17 cells (Th17), primarily induces neutrophil activation and
CC       recruitment at infection and inflammatory sites. Stimulates the
CC       production of antimicrobial beta-defensins DEFB1, DEFB103A, and
CC       DEFB104A by mucosal epithelial cells, limiting the entry of microbes
CC       through the epithelial barriers. IL17F homodimer can signal via IL17RC
CC       homodimeric receptor complex, triggering downstream activation of TRAF6
CC       and NF-kappa-B signaling pathway. Via IL17RC induces transcriptional
CC       activation of IL33, a potent cytokine that stimulates group 2 innate
CC       lymphoid cells and adaptive T-helper 2 cells involved in pulmonary
CC       allergic response to fungi. Likely via IL17RC, promotes sympathetic
CC       innervation of peripheral organs by coordinating the communication
CC       between gamma-delta T cells and parenchymal cells. Stimulates
CC       sympathetic innervation of thermogenic adipose tissue by driving TGFB1
CC       expression. Regulates the composition of intestinal microbiota and
CC       immune tolerance by inducing antimicrobial proteins that specifically
CC       control the growth of commensal Firmicutes and Bacteroidetes.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q7TNI7, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q96PD4}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer; disulfide-linked (By similarity). Heterodimer with
CC       IL17A (IL17A-IL17F) (By similarity). Forms complexes with IL17RA and
CC       IL17RC receptors with 2:1 binding stoichiometry: two receptor chains
CC       for one interleukin molecule. IL17F homodimer forms predominantly
CC       complexes with IL17RC homodimer, whereas IL17A-IL17F favors complexes
CC       with IL17RA-IL17RC. IL17RA and IL17RC chains cannot distinguish between
CC       IL17A and IL17F molecules, potentially enabling the formation of
CC       topologically distinct complexes (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q7TNI7, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q96PD4}.
