CC   -!- FUNCTION: Multifunctional cytokine mainly produced by T-cells that
CC       plays a regulatory role in immune response, tissue homeostasis, host
CC       defense, and oncogenesis. Possesses antiviral functions and induces the
CC       type I interferon response during influenza infection. Signals through
CC       two receptor complexes IL20RA/IL20RB or IL20RB/IL22RA1. In turn,
CC       stimulates the JAK1-STAT3 and MAPK pathways and promotes the secretion
CC       of pro-inflammatory mediators including IL8 and MMP1 (By similarity).
CC       Intracellularly, maintains endoplasmic reticulum homeostasis by
CC       restricting the eIF2alpha-CHOP pathway-mediated stress signal (By
CC       similarity). In addition, acts as a quality control mechanism for the
CC       ubiquitin proteasome system by alerting the cell to proteasome
CC       dysfunction through activation of PKR/EIF2AK2 (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q13007, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q925S4}.
