CC   -!- FUNCTION: Homodimeric cytokine expressed predominantly by T-lymphocytes
CC       and NK cells that plays an important role in the survival,
CC       differentiation, and chemotaxis of eosinophils (PubMed:10446387). Acts
CC       also on activated and resting B-cells to induce immunoglobulin
CC       production, growth, and differentiation (By similarity).
CC       Mechanistically, exerts its biological effects through a receptor
CC       composed of IL5RA subunit and the cytokine receptor common subunit
CC       beta/CSF2RB. Binding to the receptor leads to activation of various
CC       kinases including LYN, SYK and JAK2 and thereby propagates signals
CC       through the RAS-MAPK and JAK-STAT5 pathways respectively (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P04401,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P05113, ECO:0000269|PubMed:10446387}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer; disulfide-linked. Interacts with IL5RA. Interacts
CC       with CSF2RB. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P05113}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Secreted.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the IL-5 family. {ECO:0000305}.
