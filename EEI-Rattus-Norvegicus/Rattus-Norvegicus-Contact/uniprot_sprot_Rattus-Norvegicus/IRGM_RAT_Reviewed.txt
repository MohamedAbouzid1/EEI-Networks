CC   -!- FUNCTION: Immunity-related GTPase that plays important roles in innate
CC       immunity and inflammatory response (By similarity). Acts as a dynamin-
CC       like protein that binds to intracellular membranes and promotes
CC       remodeling and trafficking of those membranes (By similarity). Required
CC       for clearance of acute protozoan and bacterial infections by
CC       interacting with autophagy and lysosome regulatory proteins, thereby
CC       promoting the fusion of phagosomes with lysosomes for efficient
CC       degradation of cargo including microbes (By similarity). Regulates
CC       selective autophagy, including xenophagy and mitophagy, both directly
CC       and indirectly (By similarity). Directly regulates autophagy by acting
CC       as a molecular adapter that promotes the coassembly of the core
CC       autophagy machinery to mediate antimicrobial defense: Irgm (1)
CC       activates AMPK, which in turn phosphorylates ULK1 and BECN1 to induce
CC       autophagy, (2) promotes the coassembly of ULK1 and BECN1, enhancing
CC       BECN1-interacting partners and (3) influences the composition of the
CC       BECN1 complex, by competing with the negative regulators BCL2 and
CC       RUBCN, to trigger autophagy (By similarity). Also activates autophagy
CC       by promoting recruitment of STX17 to autophagosomes (By similarity). In
CC       collaboration with ATG8 proteins, regulate lysosomal biogenesis, a
CC       fundamental process for any autophagic pathway, by promoting TFEB
CC       dephosphorylation (By similarity). Also modulates autophagy by
CC       assisting with autophagosome formation and preventing lysosomal
CC       deacidification (By similarity). Regulates autophagy by affecting
CC       mitochondrial fusion and fission (By similarity). Also involved in M1
CC       macrophage activation for the production of proinflammatory cytokines
CC       (By similarity). While activating autophagy, acts as a key negative
CC       regulator of the inflammatory and interferon responses both by (1)
CC       promoting mitophagy and (2) mediating autophagy-dependent degradation
CC       of effectors of the inflammatory response (By similarity). Promotes
CC       degradation of damaged and IFNG/IFN-gamma-stressed mitochondria via
CC       mitophagy, preventing cytosolic release of ligands that activate
CC       inflammation (By similarity). Negatively regulates interferon-signaling
CC       in hematopoietic stem cells, preserving hematopoietic stem cell number
CC       and function (By similarity). Promotes expansion of activated CD4(+) T-
CC       cells by inhibiting IFNG/IFN-gamma signaling, thereby preventing Ifng-
CC       mediated cell death of CD4(+) T-cells (By similarity). Acts as a
CC       suppressor of inflammation by promoting recruitment of inflammation
CC       effectors, such as CGAS, RIGI/RIG-I and NLRP3, to autophagosome
CC       membranes, leading to their SQSTM1/p62-dependent autophagic degradation
CC       (By similarity). Also directly inhibits assembly of the NLRP3
CC       inflammasome by preventing the association between NLRP3 and PYCARD (By
CC       similarity). Acts as a negative regulator of antiviral innate immune
CC       response by suppressing the RIPK2-dependent pro-inflammatory response:
CC       mediates recruitment of RIPosomes, composed of RIPK2 and NOD1 or NOD2,
CC       to autophagosome membranes, promoting their SQSTM1/p62-dependent
CC       autophagic degradation (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:A1A4Y4,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q60766}.
