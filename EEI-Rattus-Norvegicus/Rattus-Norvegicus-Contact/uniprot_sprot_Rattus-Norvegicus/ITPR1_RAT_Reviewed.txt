CC   -!- FUNCTION: Inositol 1,4,5-trisphosphate-gated calcium channel that, upon
CC       inositol 1,4,5-trisphosphate binding, mediates calcium release from the
CC       endoplasmic reticulum (ER) (PubMed:2165071, PubMed:22547632,
CC       PubMed:36376291). Undergoes conformational changes upon ligand binding,
CC       suggesting structural flexibility that allows the channel to switch
CC       from a closed state, capable of interacting with its ligands such as
CC       1,4,5-trisphosphate and calcium, to an open state, capable of
CC       transferring calcium ions across the ER membrane (PubMed:36376291).
CC       Cytoplasmic calcium released from the ER triggers apoptosis by the
CC       activation of CAMK2 complex (By similarity). Part of a complex composed
CC       of HSPA9, ITPR1 and VDAC1 that regulates mitochondrial calcium-
CC       dependent apoptosis by facilitating calcium transport from the ER lumen
CC       to the mitochondria intermembrane space thus providing calcium for the
CC       downstream calcium channel MCU that directly releases it into
CC       mitochondria matrix (By similarity). Involved in the regulation of
CC       epithelial secretion of electrolytes and fluid through the interaction
CC       with AHCYL1 (By similarity). Regulates fertilization and egg activation
CC       by tuning the frequency and amplitude of calcium oscillations (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P11881, ECO:0000269|PubMed:2165071,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:22547632, ECO:0000269|PubMed:36376291}.

CC       Reaction=Ca(2+)(in) = Ca(2+)(out); Xref=Rhea:RHEA:29671,
CC         ChEBI:CHEBI:29108; Evidence={ECO:0000269|PubMed:22547632,
CC         ECO:0000269|PubMed:36376291};
CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: Inositol 1,4,5-trisphosphate-gated calcium channel
CC       activity is regulated by cytosolic calcium in a biphasic manner, with
CC       low concentrations causing activation and higher concentrations
CC       inhibiting channel opening, giving rise to calcium oscillations
CC       (PubMed:22547632). ATP increases the open probability of IP3R1 by
CC       synergizing with the activating effect of these two primarily ligands,
CC       inositol 1,4,5-trisphosphate and calcium (PubMed:22547632). Inositol
CC       1,4,5-trisphosphate-gated calcium channel activity is activated by zinc
CC       ions (By similarity). Inositol 1,4,5-trisphosphate-gated calcium
CC       channel activity is inhibited by CALM1 in a calcium-dependent manner
CC       (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P11881,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q14643, ECO:0000269|PubMed:22547632}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homotetramer (PubMed:10506212, PubMed:36376291). Homodimer
CC       (PubMed:21892169, PubMed:22286060). Interacts with ERP44 in a pH-,
CC       redox state- and calcium-dependent manner which results in the
CC       inhibition the calcium channel activity. The strength of this
CC       interaction inversely correlates with calcium concentration (By
CC       similarity). Part of cGMP kinase signaling complex at least composed of
CC       ACTA2/alpha-actin, CNN1/calponin H1, PLN/phospholamban, PRKG1 and ITPR1
CC       (By similarity). Interacts with IRAG1 (By similarity). Interacts with
CC       CABP1 (via N-terminus) (PubMed:12032348). Interacts with TESPA1.
CC       Interacts (when not phosphorylated) with AHCYL1 (when phosphorylated);
CC       the interaction suppresses inositol 1,4,5-trisphosphate binding to
CC       ITPR1 and is increased in the presence of BCL2L10. Interacts with
CC       AHCYL2 (with lower affinity than with AHCYL1). Interacts with BCL2L10;
CC       the interaction is increased in the presence of AHCLY1. Interacts with
CC       BOK (via BH4 domain); protects ITPR1 from proteolysis by CASP3 during
CC       apoptosis (By similarity). Interacts with TRPC4 (PubMed:11163362).
CC       Interacts with CHGA and CHGB (By similarity). Interacts with CALM1;
CC       this interaction inhibits inositol 1,4,5 trisphosphate binding in both
CC       the presence and absence of calcium and inositol 1,4,5 trisphosphate-
CC       induced calcium release in the presence of calcium. Interacts with the
CC       complex composed by ERLIN1, ERLIN2 and RNF170 through ERLIN2; this
CC       interaction triggers its ubiquitin-proteasomal degradation (By
CC       similarity). Interacts with HSPA9; this interaction couples ITPR1 to
CC       VDAC1 (PubMed:17178908). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P11881,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q14643, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9TU34,

CC   -!- DOMAIN: The ITPR1 structure has a large solenoid CY assembly built
CC       around the central helical bundle made of the C-terminal domains from
CC       four IP3R1 subunits (PubMed:36376291). The solenoid scaffold includes
CC       domains responsible for binding of ligands and regulatory proteins and
CC       is connected via an allosteric nexus at the cytosolic-membrane
CC       interface to the transmembrane channel assembly (PubMed:36376291). Six
CC       transmembrane helices from each subunit form the central ion-conduction
CC       pore (PubMed:36376291). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:36376291}.
CC   -!- PTM: Polyubiquitinated (PubMed:18955483). Polyubiquitination targets
CC       ITPR1 for proteasomal degradation (PubMed:18955483). Approximately 40%
CC       of the ITPR1-associated ubiquitin is monoubiquitin, and polyubiquitins
CC       are both 'Lys-48'- and 'Lys-63'-linked (PubMed:18955483).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18955483}.
CC   -!- PTM: Phosphorylation by cAMP kinase (PKA) enhances calcium release
