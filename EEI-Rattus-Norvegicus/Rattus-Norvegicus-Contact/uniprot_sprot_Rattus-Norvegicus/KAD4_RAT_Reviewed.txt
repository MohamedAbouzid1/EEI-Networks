CC   -!- FUNCTION: Broad-specificity mitochondrial nucleoside phosphate kinase
CC       involved in cellular nucleotide homeostasis by catalyzing nucleoside-
CC       phosphate interconversions. Similar to other adenylate kinases,
CC       preferentially catalyzes the phosphorylation of the nucleoside
CC       monophosphate AMP with ATP as phosphate donor to produce ADP.
CC       Phosphorylates only AMP when using GTP as phosphate donor. In vitro,
CC       can also catalyze the phosphorylation of CMP, dAMP and dCMP and use GTP
CC       as an alternate phosphate donor. Moreover, exhibits a diphosphate
CC       kinase activity, producing ATP, CTP, GTP, UTP, TTP, dATP, dCTP and dGTP
CC       from the corresponding diphosphate substrates with either ATP or GTP as
CC       phosphate donors. Plays a role in controlling cellular ATP levels by
CC       regulating phosphorylation and activation of the energy sensor protein
CC       kinase AMPK. Plays a protective role in the cellular response to
CC       oxidative stress. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P27144}.
