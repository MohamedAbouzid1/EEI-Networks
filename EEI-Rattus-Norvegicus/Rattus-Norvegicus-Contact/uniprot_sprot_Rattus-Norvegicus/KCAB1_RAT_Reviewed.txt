CC   -!- FUNCTION: Regulatory subunit of the voltage-gated potassium (Kv)
CC       channels composed of pore-forming and potassium-conducting alpha
CC       subunits and of regulatory beta subunits (By similarity). The beta-
CC       1/KCNAB1 cytoplasmic subunit mediates closure of delayed rectifier
CC       potassium channels by physically obstructing the pore via its N-
CC       terminal domain and increases the speed of channel closure for other
CC       family members (PubMed:10650996, PubMed:15618540, PubMed:16504945,
CC       PubMed:18222921, PubMed:8183366). Promotes the inactivation of KCNA1,
CC       KCNA2, KCNA4, KCNA5 and KCNA6 alpha subunit-containing channels
CC       (PubMed:10064591, PubMed:10650996, PubMed:15618540, PubMed:16504945,
CC       PubMed:8183366). Displays nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide phosphate
CC       (NADPH)-dependent aldoketoreductase activity by catalyzing the NADPH-
CC       dependent reduction of a variety of endogenous aldehydes and ketones
CC       (PubMed:18222921). The binding of NADPH is required for efficient down-
CC       regulation of potassium channel activity (PubMed:18222921). Oxidation
CC       of the bound NADPH restrains N-terminal domain from blocking the
CC       channel, thereby decreasing N-type inactivation of potassium channel
CC       activity (PubMed:18222921, PubMed:21436029).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q14722, ECO:0000269|PubMed:10064591,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:10650996, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15618540,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:16504945, ECO:0000269|PubMed:18222921,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:21436029, ECO:0000269|PubMed:8183366}.

CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homotetramer (PubMed:18222921). Interaction with tetrameric
CC       potassium channel alpha subunits gives rise to a heterooctamer
CC       (Probable). Identified in potassium channel complexes containing KCNA1,
CC       KCNA2, KCNA4, KCNA5, KCNA6, KCNAB1 and KCNAB2 (PubMed:10064591,
CC       PubMed:16504945, PubMed:9334400). Part of a complex containing KCNA1,
CC       KCNA4 and LGI1; interaction with LGI1 inhibits down-regulation of KCNA1
CC       channel activity (PubMed:16504945). Interacts with the dimer formed by
CC       GNB1 and GNG2; this enhances KCNA1 binding (PubMed:10064591). Interacts
CC       with SQSTM1 (PubMed:10477520). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10064591,
