CC   -!- FUNCTION: Regulatory subunit of the voltage-gated potassium (Kv)
CC       channels composed of pore-forming and potassium-conducting alpha
CC       subunits and of regulatory beta subunits (PubMed:8549760). The beta-
CC       3/KCNAB3 subunit may mediate closure of potassium channels (By
CC       similarity). Inactivates Kv1.4/KCNA4 alpha subunit-containing Kv
CC       channel current but not Kv1.1/KCNA1 or Kv1.5/KCNA5 channels
CC       (PubMed:8549760). May display nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide
CC       phosphate (NADPH)-dependent aldoketoreductase activity (By similarity).
CC       The binding of oxidized and reduced NADP(H) cofactors may be required
CC       for the regulation of potassium channel activity (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P63144, ECO:0000269|PubMed:8549760}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Forms heteromultimeric complex with alpha subunits
CC       (PubMed:8549760). Interacts with KCNA5 and KCNB2 (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P97382, ECO:0000269|PubMed:8549760}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q14722}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Predominantly expressed in brain. Strongest
CC       expression in olfactory bulb and thalamic nuclei. Not detected in
CC       heart, spleen, lung, liver, skeletal muscle, kidney and testis.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:8549760}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the shaker potassium channel beta subunit
CC       family. {ECO:0000305}.
