CC   -!- FUNCTION: Voltage-gated potassium channel that opens in response to the
CC       voltage difference across the membrane, forming a potassium-selective
CC       channel through which potassium ions pass in accordance with their
CC       electrochemical gradient (PubMed:12592408, PubMed:1378392,
CC       PubMed:1840526). The channel displays rapid activation and inactivation
CC       kinetics (PubMed:12592408, PubMed:1378392, PubMed:1840526).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12592408, ECO:0000269|PubMed:1378392,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:1840526}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY:
CC       Reaction=K(+)(in) = K(+)(out); Xref=Rhea:RHEA:29463, ChEBI:CHEBI:29103;
CC         Evidence={ECO:0000269|PubMed:12592408, ECO:0000269|PubMed:1378392,
CC         ECO:0000269|PubMed:1840526};
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homotetramer (Probable). Heterotetramer of potassium channel
CC       proteins. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12592408, ECO:0000305}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Membrane; Multi-pass membrane protein.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The cytoplasmic N-terminus mediates N-type inactivation.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:1378392}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: Composed of the tetramerization T1 domain, six membrane
CC       spanning regions including voltage-sensing (S1-S4) and pore domains
CC       (S5- S6) and a variable C-terminal domain. {ECO:0000305}.
CC   -!- PTM: Phosphorylation of serine residues in the inactivation gate
CC       inhibits rapid channel closure. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q03721}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the potassium channel family. C (Shaw) (TC
CC       1.A.1.2) subfamily. Kv3.4/KCNC4 sub-subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
