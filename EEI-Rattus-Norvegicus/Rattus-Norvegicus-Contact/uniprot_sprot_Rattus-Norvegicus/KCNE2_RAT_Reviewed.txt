CC   -!- FUNCTION: Ancillary protein that functions as a regulatory subunit of
CC       the voltage-gated potassium (Kv) channel complex composed of pore-
CC       forming and potassium-conducting alpha subunits and of regulatory beta
CC       subunits. KCNE2 beta subunit modulates the gating kinetics and enhances
CC       stability of the channel complex (PubMed:11420311, PubMed:19219384).
CC       Alters the gating of the delayed rectifier Kv channel containing KCNB1
CC       alpha subunit (PubMed:19219384). Associates with KCNH2/HERG alpha
CC       subunit Kv channel to form the rapidly activating component of the
CC       delayed rectifying potassium current (IKr) in heart (By similarity).
CC       May associate with KCNQ2 and/or KCNQ3 alpha subunits to modulate the
CC       native M-type current (By similarity). May associate with HCN1 and HCN2
CC       channel subunits to increase potassium current (PubMed:11420311). Forms
CC       a heterooligomer complex with KCNQ1/KVLQT1 alpha subunits which leads
CC       to currents with an apparently instantaneous activation, a rapid
CC       deactivation process and a linear current-voltage relationship and
CC       decreases the amplitude of the outward current (By similarity). KCNQ1-
CC       KCNE2 channel associates with Na(+)-coupled myo-inositol symporter in
CC       the apical membrane of choroid plexus epithelium and regulates the myo-
CC       inositol gradient between blood and cerebrospinal fluid with an impact
CC       on neuron excitability (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9D808,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Y6J6, ECO:0000269|PubMed:11420311,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19219384}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts with KCNB1 (PubMed:19219384). Associates with
CC       KCNH2/ERG1 (By similarity). May associate with KCNQ2 and KCNQ3 (By
CC       similarity). Associates with HCN1 and probably HCN2 (PubMed:11420311).
CC       Heteromultimer with KCNC2. Interacts with KCNC2 (PubMed:14679187).
CC       Interacts with KCNQ1; forms a heterooligomer complex that targets to
CC       the membrane raft and leading to currents with an apparently
CC       instantaneous activation, a rapid deactivation process and a linear
CC       current-voltage relationship and decreases the amplitude of the outward
CC       current (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Y6J6,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:11420311, ECO:0000269|PubMed:14679187,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19219384}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000269|PubMed:14679187,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19219384}; Single-pass type I membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000305}. Apical cell membrane {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9D808};
CC       Single-pass membrane protein {ECO:0000255}. Note=Colocalizes with KCNB1
CC       at the plasma membrane. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19219384}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed in the heart (PubMed:19219384).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19219384}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the potassium channel KCNE family.
CC       {ECO:0000305}.
