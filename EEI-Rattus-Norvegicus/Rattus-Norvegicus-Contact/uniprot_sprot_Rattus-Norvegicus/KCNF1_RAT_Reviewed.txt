CC   -!- FUNCTION: Regulatory alpha-subunit of the voltage-gated potassium (Kv)
CC       channel which, when coassembled with KCNB1 or KCNB2, can modulate their
CC       expression and their gating kinetics by acting on deactivation upon
CC       repolarization and inactivation during maintained depolarization
CC       (PubMed:9305895, PubMed:9696692). Accelerates inactivation but has
CC       relatively little effect on deactivation (PubMed:9696692). Coexpression
CC       with KCNB1 or KCNB2 markedly slows inactivation (PubMed:9305895). Each
CC       modulatory subunit has its own specific properties of regulation, and
CC       can lead to extensive inhibitions, to large changes in kinetics, and/or
CC       to large shifts in the voltage dependencies of the inactivation process
CC       (PubMed:9305895). The gating kinetics depends on the nature and
CC       stoichiometry of the associated regulatory sunbunit (PubMed:9305895,
CC       PubMed:9696692). Fails to produce a potassium current when expressed
CC       alone (PubMed:9305895). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9305895,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9696692}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Heterotetramer with KCNB1 or KCNB2.
CC       {ECO:0000305|PubMed:9305895, ECO:0000305|PubMed:9696692}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q14721};
CC       Multi-pass membrane protein {ECO:0000255}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed in brain namely in the piriform cortex,
CC       olfactory tubercle, and medial habenular nucleus (PubMed:1740690). Also
CC       expressed in the medial amygdaloid nuclei and the lateral amygdaloid
CC       area (PubMed:1740690). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:1740690}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the potassium channel family. F (TC 1.A.1.2)
CC       subfamily. Kv5.1/KCNF1 sub-subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
