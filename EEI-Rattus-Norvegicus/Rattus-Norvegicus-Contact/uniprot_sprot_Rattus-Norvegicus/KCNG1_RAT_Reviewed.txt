CC   -!- FUNCTION: Regulatory alpha-subunit of the voltage-gated potassium (Kv)
CC       channel which, when coassembled with KCNB1 or KCNB2, can modulate their
CC       expression and their gating kinetics by acting on deactivation upon
CC       repolarization and inactivation during maintained depolarization
CC       (PubMed:8980147, PubMed:9305895, PubMed:9696692). Potassium channel
CC       subunit that does not form functional channels by itself
CC       (PubMed:8980147, PubMed:9305895). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:8980147,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9305895, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9696692}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Heterotetramer with KCNB1 or KCNB2.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9696692, ECO:0000305|PubMed:8980147,
CC       ECO:0000305|PubMed:9305895}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q14721};
CC       Multi-pass membrane protein {ECO:0000305}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The transmembrane segment S4 functions as a voltage-sensor and
CC       is characterized by a series of positively charged amino acids at every
CC       third position. Channel opening and closing is effected by a
CC       conformation change that affects the position and orientation of the
CC       voltage-sensor paddle formed by S3 and S4 within the membrane. A
CC       transmembrane electric field that is positive inside would push the
CC       positively charged S4 segment outwards, thereby opening the pore, while
CC       a field that is negative inside would pull the S4 segment inwards and
CC       close the pore. Changes in the position and orientation of S4 are then
CC       transmitted to the activation gate formed by the inner helix bundle via
CC       the S4-S5 linker region. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P63142}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the potassium channel family. G (TC 1.A.1.2)
CC       subfamily. Kv6.1/KCNG1 sub-subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
