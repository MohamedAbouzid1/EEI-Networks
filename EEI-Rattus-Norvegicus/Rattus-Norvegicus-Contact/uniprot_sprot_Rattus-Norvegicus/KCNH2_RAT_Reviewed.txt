CC   -!- FUNCTION: Pore-forming (alpha) subunit of voltage-gated inwardly
CC       rectifying potassium channel (PubMed:11212207, PubMed:9664620).
CC       Characterized by unusual gating kinetics by producing relatively small
CC       outward currents during membrane depolarization and large inward
CC       currents during subsequent repolarization which reflect a rapid
CC       inactivation during depolarization and quick recovery from inactivation
CC       but slow deactivation (closing) during repolarization (PubMed:11212207,
CC       PubMed:9664620). Channel properties are modulated by cAMP and subunit
CC       assembly. Forms a stable complex with KCNE1 or KCNE2, and that this
CC       heteromultimerization regulates inward rectifier potassium channel
CC       activity (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q12809,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:11212207, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9664620}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY:
CC       Reaction=K(+)(in) = K(+)(out); Xref=Rhea:RHEA:29463, ChEBI:CHEBI:29103;
CC         Evidence={ECO:0000269|PubMed:11212207, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9664620};
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: The potassium channel is probably composed of a homo- or
CC       heterotetrameric complex of pore-forming alpha subunits that can
CC       associate with modulating beta subunits. Interacts with DNAJB12 and
CC       DNAJB14; chaperones DNAJB12 and DNAJB14 promote tetramerization (By
CC       similarity). Heteromultimer with KCNH6/ERG2 and KCNH7/ERG3
CC       (PubMed:11212207). Interacts with ALG10B (PubMed:14525949). Forms a
CC       stable complex with KCNE1 or KCNE2, and that this heteromultimerization
CC       regulates Inward rectifier potassium channel activity. Interacts with
CC       CANX. The core-glycosylated, but not the fully glycosylated form
CC       interacts with RNF207. Interacts with NDFIP1 and NDFIP2; this
CC       interaction decreases the cell membrane expression by targeting KCNH2,
CC       through interaction with NEDD4L, for the degradation through the
CC       multivesicular bodies (MVBs)-lysosomal pathway (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q12809, ECO:0000269|PubMed:11212207,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:14525949}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q12809};
CC       Multi-pass membrane protein {ECO:0000255}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Highly expressed in brain and testis, slightly less
CC       so in heart, adrenal, retina and thymus (PubMed:10718922,
CC       PubMed:9012748). Detected at lower levels in lung, soleus, tibialis,
CC       and at very low levels in cornea and lens (PubMed:10718922,
CC       PubMed:9012748). A shorter transcript is detected in skeletal muscle
CC       (PubMed:10718922). Found in pituitary (PubMed:10718922).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10718922, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9012748}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The S4-S5 linker acts as a signal integrator where it both
CC       couples voltage-sensor domain (VSD) movement to pore opening and
CC       closure, as well as providing a binding site for other domains that
CC       regulate activation and/or deactivation of the channel.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q12809}.
CC   -!- PTM: Phosphorylated on serine and threonine residues. Phosphorylation
CC       by PKA inhibits ion conduction. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q12809}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the potassium channel family. H (Eag) (TC
CC       1.A.1.20) subfamily. Kv11.1/KCNH2 sub-subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
