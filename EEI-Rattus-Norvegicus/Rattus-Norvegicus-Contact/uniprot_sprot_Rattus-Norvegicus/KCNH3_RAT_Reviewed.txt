CC   -!- FUNCTION: Pore-forming (alpha) subunit of a voltage-gated inwardly
CC       rectifying potassium channel (PubMed:9824707). Charactherized by a fast
CC       rate of activation during depolarization followed by a rapid
CC       inactivation at much more depolarized value causing inward
CC       rectification due to a C-type inactivation mechanism (PubMed:9824707).
CC       Exhibits a rapid recovery from inactivation (PubMed:9824707).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9824707}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY:
CC       Reaction=K(+)(in) = K(+)(out); Xref=Rhea:RHEA:29463, ChEBI:CHEBI:29103;
CC         Evidence={ECO:0000269|PubMed:9824707};
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: The potassium channel is probably composed of a homo- or
CC       heterotetrameric complex of pore-forming alpha subunits that can
CC       associate with modulating beta subunits. Interacts with KCNE1 and
CC       KCNE3; these interactions regulate KCNH3 trafficking to the plasma
CC       membrane and its subsequent voltage-gated potassium channel activity.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9WVJ0}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9WVJ0};
CC       Multi-pass membrane protein {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9WVJ0}.
CC       Note=Expression on the cell membrane requires at least one of the three
CC       glycosylation sites to carry a sugar chain irrespective of their
CC       positions. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9WVJ0}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Highly expressed in adult and embryonic brain, in
CC       particular in cerebellum, brain stem, hippocampus, cortex and striatum
CC       (PubMed:10718922, PubMed:9824707). Also found in pituitary
CC       (PubMed:10455180). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10455180,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:10718922, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9824707}.
CC   -!- PTM: N-glycosylated. N-glycosylation mediates traffick to the cell
CC       membrane but is not necessary for voltage-gated potassium channel
CC       activity. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9WVJ0}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the potassium channel family. H (Eag) (TC
CC       1.A.1.20) subfamily. Kv12.2/KCNH3 sub-subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
