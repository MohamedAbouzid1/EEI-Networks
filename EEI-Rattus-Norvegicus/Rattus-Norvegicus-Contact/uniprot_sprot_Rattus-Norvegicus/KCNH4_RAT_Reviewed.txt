CC   -!- FUNCTION: Pore-forming (alpha) subunit of a voltage-gated delayed
CC       rectifier (PubMed:9824707). Activates at more negative voltages,
CC       exhibits fast prepulse-independent activation kinetics and deactivates
CC       much more slowly, but shows no inactivation (PubMed:9824707).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9824707}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY:
CC       Reaction=K(+)(in) = K(+)(out); Xref=Rhea:RHEA:29463, ChEBI:CHEBI:29103;
CC         Evidence={ECO:0000269|PubMed:9824707};
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: The potassium channel is probably composed of a homo- or
CC       heterotetrameric complex of pore-forming alpha subunits that can
CC       associate with modulating beta subunits.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9UQ05}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Membrane {ECO:0000255}; Multi-pass membrane
CC       protein {ECO:0000255}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Highly expressed in adult testis, and in adult and
CC       embryonic brain (PubMed:9824707). In adult brain found in piriform
CC       cortex, olfactory tubercle, cerebral cortex, hippocampus pyramidial
CC       cells and dentate gyrus and basal ganglia of caudate/putamen and
CC       accumbens nucleus (PubMed:11425889). Detected at intermediate levels in
CC       lung, spinal cord, and pituitary (PubMed:9824707).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11425889, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9824707}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the potassium channel family. H (Eag) (TC
CC       1.A.1.20) subfamily. Kv12.3/KCNH4 sub-subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
