CC   -!- FUNCTION: Pore-forming (alpha) subunit of a voltage-gated delayed
CC       rectifier potassium channel that mediates outward-rectifying potassium
CC       currents which, on depolarization, reaches a steady-state level and do
CC       not inactivate (PubMed:10594062, PubMed:10882483). The kinetic is
CC       characterized by a slow activation time course and a small voltage
CC       dependence of the activation time constants, therefore, starts to open
CC       at more negative voltages (PubMed:10594062, PubMed:10882483). The
CC       activation kinetics depend on the prepulse potential and external
CC       divalent cation concentration (PubMed:10594062, PubMed:10882483). The
CC       time course of activation is biphasic with a fast and a slowly
CC       activating current component (PubMed:10594062, PubMed:10882483). With
CC       negative prepulses, the current activation is delayed and slowed down
CC       several fold, whereas more positive prepulses speed up activation,
CC       therefore the activation rate depends on holding potential
CC       (PubMed:10594062, PubMed:10882483). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10594062,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:10882483}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY:
CC       Reaction=K(+)(in) = K(+)(out); Xref=Rhea:RHEA:29463, ChEBI:CHEBI:29103;
CC         Evidence={ECO:0000269|PubMed:10594062, ECO:0000269|PubMed:10882483};
CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: Inhibited by low nanomolar concentrations of
CC       cytosolic calcium. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10882483}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homotetramer. The potassium channel is probably composed of a
CC       homo- or heterotetrameric complex of pore-forming alpha subunits that
CC       can associate with modulating beta subunits. Heteromultimer with
CC       KCNH1/EAG. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8NCM2}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Membrane; Multi-pass membrane protein.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Detected in adult testis and in embryonic and adult
CC       brain, but not in other tissues (PubMed:10594062). Highly expressed in
CC       specific brain areas, such as neocortex, olfactory bulb, primary
CC       olfactory cortex and brain stem (PubMed:10594062, PubMed:10882483). In
CC       cortex, expression is concentrated in a narrow band toward the middle
CC       lamella (layer IV) (PubMed:10594062, PubMed:10882483). Moderately
CC       expressed in spinal cord, dorsal thalamic nuclei, medial hypothalamus,
CC       colliculus, lateral lemniscus, pontine nuclei and Islands of Calleja
CC       (PubMed:10594062, PubMed:10882483). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10594062,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:10882483}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: Contains a voltage sensor domain (VSD) formed from the S1-S4
CC       transmembrane helices, a pore domain formed from the S5-pore loop-S6
CC       domain and the C-terminal cyclic nucleotide binding homology domain
CC       (CNBHD). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8NCM2}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The segment S4 is probably the voltage-sensor and is
CC       characterized by a series of positively charged amino acids at every
CC       third position. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8NCM2}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the potassium channel family. H (Eag) (TC
CC       1.A.1.20) subfamily. Kv10.2/KCNH5 sub-subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
