CC   -!- FUNCTION: Pore-forming (alpha) subunit of a voltage-gated delayed
CC       rectifier potassium channel that mediates outward-rectifying potassium
CC       currents (PubMed:9714851). Elicits a slowly activating, non-
CC       inactivating and slowly deactivation outwards potassium current at
CC       depolarizating voltages from -30 mV to +50mV (PubMed:9714851). Shows no
CC       obvious change in the activation rate from different holding potentials
CC       (PubMed:9714851). Activation is strongly dependent on the pH of the
CC       external solution (PubMed:9714851). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9714851}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY:
CC       Reaction=K(+)(in) = K(+)(out); Xref=Rhea:RHEA:29463, ChEBI:CHEBI:29103;
CC         Evidence={ECO:0000269|PubMed:9714851};
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: The potassium channel is probably composed of a homo- or
CC       heterotetrameric complex of pore-forming alpha subunits that can
CC       associate with modulating beta subunits. {ECO:0000305}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Membrane {ECO:0000255}; Multi-pass membrane
CC       protein {ECO:0000255}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Detected in superior cervical, mesenteric and
CC       coeliac ganglia (PubMed:11425889). Expressed in brain (piriform cortex,
CC       olfactory tubercle, cerebral cortex, hippocampus pyramidial cells and
CC       dentate gyrus and basal ganglia of caudate/putamen and accumbens
CC       nucleus) (PubMed:11425889). Expressed in pituitary (PubMed:10718922).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10718922, ECO:0000269|PubMed:11425889}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the potassium channel family. H (Eag) (TC
CC       1.A.1.20) subfamily. Kv12.1/KCNH8 sub-subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
