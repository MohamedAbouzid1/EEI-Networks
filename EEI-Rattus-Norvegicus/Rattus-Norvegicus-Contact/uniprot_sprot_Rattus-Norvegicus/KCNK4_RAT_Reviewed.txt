CC   -!- FUNCTION: K(+) channel that conducts voltage-dependent outward
CC       rectifying currents upon membrane depolarization. Voltage sensing is
CC       coupled to K(+) electrochemical gradient in an 'ion flux gating' mode
CC       where outward but not inward ion flow opens the gate. Converts to
CC       voltage-independent 'leak' conductance mode upon stimulation by various
CC       stimuli including mechanical membrane stretch, basic pH, heat and
CC       lipids (PubMed:11374070, PubMed:31630908). Homo- and heterodimerizes to
CC       form functional channels with distinct regulatory and gating properties
CC       (By similarity). At trigeminal A-beta afferent nerves, the heterodimer
CC       of KCNK2/TREK-1 and KCNK4/TRAAK is mostly coexpressed at nodes of
CC       Ranvier where it conducts voltage-independent mechanosensitive and
CC       thermosensitive currents, allowing rapid action potential
CC       repolarization, high speed and high frequence saltatory conduction on
CC       myelinated nerves to ensure prompt sensory responses (PubMed:31630908).
CC       Permeable to other monovalent cations such as Rb(+) and Cs(+) (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9NYG8,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:11374070, ECO:0000269|PubMed:31630908}.

CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: Activated by various stimuli including
CC       intracellular basic pH, mechanical stretch and heat and polyunsaturated
CC       fatty acids such as arachidonic acid. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11374070,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15677687}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer; disulfide-linked. Forms heterodimers with other 2-
CC       pore domain K(+) channel subunits, such as KCNK2 and KCNK10.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O88454}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11374070};
CC       Multi-pass membrane protein {ECO:0000305}. Cell projection, axon
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:31630908}. Note=Localizes at the Ranvier nodes of
CC       myelinated afferent nerves. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:31630908}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Detected in brain, and at much lower levels in
CC       liver, skeletal muscle and testis. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11374070}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: Each subunit contributes two pore-forming domains 1 and 2 which
CC       assemble to form a single pore with M2 and M4 transmembrane helices
CC       lining the central cavity and M1 and M3 facing the lipid bilayer. The
CC       transmembrane helices are bridged by the selectivity filters 1 and 2
CC       carrying a signature sequence TxTTxGYGD that coordinate the permeant
CC       ions. Up to four ions can simultaneously occupy the selectivity filter
CC       and at least two elementary charges must translocate across the filter
CC       to convert it into the open conformation.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P57789}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: Channel opening is brought about by a conformation change that
CC       involves buckling of the second transmembrane helix and affects the
CC       position and orientation of the fourth transmembrane helix.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9NYG8}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the two pore domain potassium channel (TC
CC       1.A.1.8) family. {ECO:0000255|RuleBase:RU003857}.
