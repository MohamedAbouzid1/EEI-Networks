CC   -!- FUNCTION: K(+) channel that conducts voltage-dependent outward
CC       rectifying currents upon membrane depolarization. Voltage sensing is
CC       coupled to K(+) electrochemical gradient in an 'ion flux gating' mode
CC       where outward but not inward ion flow opens the gate. Converts to
CC       voltage-independent 'leak' conductance mode upon stimulation by various
CC       stimuli including mechanical membrane stretch, acidic pH, heat and
CC       lipids. Homo- and heterodimerizes to form functional channels with
CC       distinct regulatory and gating properties (By similarity). In
CC       trigeminal ganglia sensory neurons, the heterodimer of KCNK10/TREK-2
CC       and KCNK18/TRESK inhibits neuronal firing and neurogenic inflammation
CC       by stabilizing the resting membrane potential at K(+) equilibrium
CC       potential as well as by regulating the threshold of action potentials
CC       and the spike frequency (By similarity). Permeable to other monovalent
CC       ions such as Rb(+) and Cs(+) (By similarity) (PubMed:10747911).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P57789, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8BUW1,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:10747911}.

CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: Activated by stimuli such as mechanical stretch,
CC       acidic pH and polyunsaturated free fatty acids.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10747911}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer; disulfide-linked (By similarity). Forms
CC       heterodimers with other 2-pore domain K(+) channel subunits, such as
CC       KCNK2, KCNK4 and KCNK18 (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P57789,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8BUW1}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8BUW1};
CC       Multi-pass membrane protein {ECO:0000255}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed mainly in the cerebellum, spleen, and
CC       testis. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10747911}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: Each subunit contributes two pore-forming domains 1 and 2 which
CC       assemble to form a single pore with M2 and M4 transmembrane helices
CC       lining the central cavity and M1 and M3 facing the lipid bilayer. The
CC       transmembrane helices are bridged by the selectivity filters 1 and 2
CC       carrying a signature sequence TxTTxGYGD that coordinate the permeant
CC       ions. Up to four ions can simultaneously occupy the selectivity filter
CC       and at least two elementary charges must translocate across the filter
CC       to convert it into the open conformation.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P57789}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the two pore domain potassium channel (TC
CC       1.A.1.8) family. {ECO:0000305}.
