CC   -!- FUNCTION: K(+) channel that conducts outward and inward currents at
CC       depolarized and hyperpolarized membrane potentials, respectively. The
CC       outward rectifying currents are voltage-dependent, coupled to K(+)
CC       electrochemical gradient across the membrane, whereas the inward
CC       currents can be induced in response to activation of Ca(2+)-mobilizing
CC       receptors (PubMed:16192517). Homo- and heterodimerizes to form
CC       functional channels with distinct regulatory and gating properties (By
CC       similarity). In trigeminal ganglia sensory neurons, the heterodimers of
CC       KCNK18/TRESK and KCNK2/TREK-1 or KCNK10/TREK-2 inhibit neuronal firing
CC       and neurogenic inflammation by stabilizing the resting membrane
CC       potential at K(+) equilibrium potential as well as by regulating the
CC       threshold of action potentials and the spike frequency (By similarity).
CC       In thymocytes, conducts K(+) currents upon T cell receptor (TCR)
CC       signaling leading to sustained Ca(2+) influx and NF-kappa-B activation,
CC       FOXP3 transcription and positive selection of regulatory T cell (Treg)
CC       progenitor subsets (By similarity). Appears to mediate the analgesics
CC       effects of hydroxy-alpha-sanshool, a metabolite naturally present in
CC       Schezuan pepper and other Xanthoxylum plants (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q6VV64, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16192517}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY:
CC       Reaction=K(+)(in) = K(+)(out); Xref=Rhea:RHEA:29463, ChEBI:CHEBI:29103;
CC         Evidence={ECO:0000269|PubMed:16192517};
CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: Activated by volatile anesthetics, such as
CC       isoflurane and inhibited by local anesthetics such as bupivacaine and
CC       lidocaine. Inhibited by extracellular acidic pH (PubMed:16192517).
CC       Inhibited by Zn(2+) ions (PubMed:16192517).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16192517}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer. Heterodimer with KCNK2. Heterodimer with KCNK10.
CC       Interacts with calcineurin. Interacts with YWHAH, in a phosphorylation-
CC       dependent manner. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q6VV64}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000250}; Multi-pass membrane
CC       protein {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: Each subunit contributes two pore-forming domains 1 and 2 which
CC       assemble to form a single pore with M2 and M4 transmembrane helices
CC       lining the central cavity and M1 and M3 facing the lipid bilayer. The
CC       transmembrane helices are bridged by the selectivity filters 1 and 2
CC       carrying a signature sequence TxTTxGYGD that coordinate the permeant
CC       ions. Up to four ions can simultaneously occupy the selectivity filter
CC       and at least two elementary charges must translocate across the filter
CC       to convert it into the open conformation.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q7Z418}.
CC   -!- PTM: Phosphorylation of Ser-275 is required for the binding of 14-3-
CC       3eta/YWHAH. Calcineurin-mediated dephosphorylation of Ser-287 enhances
CC       channel activity. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q6VV64}.
CC   -!- PTM: N-glycosylated. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q6VV64}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the two pore domain potassium channel (TC
CC       1.A.1.8) family. {ECO:0000305}.
