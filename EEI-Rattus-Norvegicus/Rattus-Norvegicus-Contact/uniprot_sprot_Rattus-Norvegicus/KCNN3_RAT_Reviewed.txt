CC   -!- FUNCTION: Small conductance calcium-activated potassium channel that
CC       mediates the voltage-independent transmembrane transfer of potassium
CC       across the cell membrane through a constitutive interaction with
CC       calmodulin which binds the intracellular calcium allowing its opening
CC       (PubMed:11245600, PubMed:11533126). The current is characterized by a
CC       voltage-independent activation, an intracellular calcium concentration
CC       increase-dependent activation and a single-channel conductance of 10
CC       picosiemens (PubMed:11245600, PubMed:11533126). Also presents an
CC       inwardly rectifying current, thus reducing its already small outward
CC       conductance of potassium ions, which is particularly the case when the
CC       membrane potential displays positive values, above + 20 mV (By
CC       similarity). Activation is followed by membrane hyperpolarization
CC       (PubMed:11533126). Thought to regulate neuronal excitability by
CC       contributing to the slow component of synaptic afterhyperpolarization
CC       (PubMed:11533126). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9UGI6,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:11245600, ECO:0000269|PubMed:11533126}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY:
CC       Reaction=K(+)(in) = K(+)(out); Xref=Rhea:RHEA:29463, ChEBI:CHEBI:29103;
CC         Evidence={ECO:0000269|PubMed:11245600, ECO:0000269|PubMed:11533126};
CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: Inhibited by bee venom neurotoxin apamin.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11245600, ECO:0000269|PubMed:11533126}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer (By similarity). Heteromultimer with KCNN2 or KCNN1;
CC       this modulates plasma membrane expression and consequently the small
CC       conductance calcium-activated potassium channel activity
CC       (PubMed:14559917). The complex is composed of 4 channel subunits each
CC       of which binds to a calmodulin subunit which regulates the channel
CC       activity through calcium-binding (By similarity). Interacts with CALM1
CC       (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P70604,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9UGI6, ECO:0000269|PubMed:14559917}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11245600,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:14559917}; Multi-pass membrane protein. Cytoplasm,
CC       myofibril, sarcomere, Z line {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P58391}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The coiled-coil domaim mediates heteromeic assembly.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9UGI6}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the potassium channel KCNN family. KCa2.3/KCNN3
CC       subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
