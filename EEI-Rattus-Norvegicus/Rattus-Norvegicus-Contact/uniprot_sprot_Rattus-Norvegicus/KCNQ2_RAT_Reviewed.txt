CC   -!- FUNCTION: Pore-forming subunit of the voltage-gated potassium (Kv) M-
CC       channel which is responsible for the M-current, a key controller of
CC       neuronal excitability (PubMed:11038262, PubMed:11230508,
CC       PubMed:24349250). M-channel is composed of pore-forming subunits KCNQ2
CC       and KCNQ3 assembled as heterotetramers (By similarity). The native M-
CC       current has a slowly activating and deactivating potassium conductance
CC       which plays a critical role in determining the subthreshold electrical
CC       excitability of neurons as well as the responsiveness to synaptic
CC       inputs (PubMed:11230508). M-channel is selectively permeable in vitro
CC       to other cations besides potassium, in decreasing order of affinity
CC       K(+) > Rb(+) > Cs(+) > Na(+) (By similarity). M-channel association
CC       with SLC5A3/SMIT1 alters channel ion selectivity, increasing Na(+) and
CC       Cs(+) permeation relative to K(+) (PubMed:28793216). Suppressed by
CC       activation of the muscarinic acetylcholine receptor CHRM1
CC       (PubMed:11230508). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O43526,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:11038262, ECO:0000269|PubMed:11230508,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:24349250, ECO:0000269|PubMed:28793216}.

CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: Phosphatidylinositol-4,5-bisphosphate (PIP2)
CC       potentiates the activation of KCNQ channels by enhancing the electro-
CC       mechanical coupling of the voltage-sensing domain (VSD) and the pore-
CC       forming domain (PD). In the closed state of the channel, PIP2 is
CC       anchored at the S2-S3 loop; upon channel activation, PIP2 interacts
CC       with the S4-S5 linker and is involved in channel gating (By
CC       similarity). Calcium suppresses KCNQ2 and KCNQ2-KCNQ3 channel currents,
CC       with calcium-bound calmodulin inducing a change in channel
CC       configuration which leads to the reduction of channel affinity for PIP2
CC       and subsequent current suppression (PubMed:24349250).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O43526, ECO:0000269|PubMed:24349250}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Heterotetramer with KCNQ3; forms heterotetrameric M-channel
CC       responsible for the M-current (PubMed:11230508). Homotetrameric; forms
CC       a functional homotetrameric channel resulting in the expression of a
CC       small M-current (By similarity). Interacts with calmodulin; the
CC       interaction is calcium-independent, constitutive and participates in
CC       the proper assembly of a functional M-channel (PubMed:24349250). May
CC       associate with KCNE2 (By similarity). Interacts with IQCJ-SCHIP1 (By
CC       similarity). Interacts (via the pore module) with SLC5A3/SMIT1; forms a
CC       coregulatory complex that alters ion selectivity, voltage dependence
CC       and gating kinetics of the channel (PubMed:28793216). Interacts with
CC       AKAP5; the interaction may help KCNQ2 channel complex to retain
CC       calcium-bound calmodulin (PubMed:24349250).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O43526, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Z351,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:11230508, ECO:0000269|PubMed:24349250,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:28793216}.

CC   -!- DOMAIN: Each subunit contains six transmembrane segments (S1-S6) with
CC       S1-S4 forming one voltage sensing domain (VSD) and S5-S6 contributing
CC       to form one quarter of an interlocking pore-forming domain (PD).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O43526}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The S4-S5 linker preferentially interacts with PIP2 in the
CC       open-state KCNQ2 channel, whereas the S2-S3 loop interacts with PIP2 in
CC       the closed state. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O43526}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The intracellular C-terminal domain is bound constitutively by
CC       calmodulin (CaM). This domain plays key functions in channel
CC       tetramerization, trafficking, and gating.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O43526}.
CC   -!- PTM: KCNQ2/KCNQ3 heteromeric current can be increased by intracellular
CC       cyclic AMP, an effect that depends on phosphorylation of Ser-52 in the
CC       N-terminal region. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O43526}.
CC   -!- PTM: KCNQ2/KCNQ3 are ubiquitinated by NEDD4L. Ubiquitination leads to
CC       protein degradation. Degradation induced by NEDD4L is inhibited by
CC       USP36. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O43526}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: When coexpressed with KCNQ3 subunit in CHO cells or
CC       Xenopus oocytes, isoform B was found to have significantly different
CC       deactivation-activation kinetics. The kinetics was 2.5 times more
CC       slowly than the kinetics of other isoforms. The presence of exon 15a in
CC       isoform B accounts for the slow deactivation-activation kinetics.
CC       Alternative splicing of the KCNQ2 gene may contribute to the variation
CC       in M-current kinetics seen in vivo.
