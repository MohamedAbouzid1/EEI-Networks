CC   -!- FUNCTION: Pore-forming subunit of the voltage-gated potassium (Kv) M-
CC       channel which is responsible for the M-current, a key controller of
CC       neuronal excitability (PubMed:24349250, PubMed:9836639). M-channel is
CC       composed of pore-forming subunits KCNQ2 and KCNQ3 assembled as
CC       heterotetramers, each subunit containing a voltage sensing domain (VSD)
CC       and a pore-forming domain (PD) (By similarity). The native M-current
CC       has a slowly activating and deactivating potassium conductance which
CC       plays a critical role in determining the subthreshold electrical
CC       excitability of neurons as well as the responsiveness to synaptic
CC       inputs (PubMed:9836639). M-channel is selectively permeable in vitro to
CC       other cations besides potassium, in decreasing order of affinity K(+) >
CC       Rb(+) > Cs(+) > Na(+). M-channel association with SLC5A3/SMIT1 alters
CC       channel ion selectivity, increasing Na(+) and Cs(+) permeation relative
CC       to K(+). Suppressed by activation of M1 muscarinic acetylcholine
CC       receptors. KCNQ3 also associates with KCNQ5 to form a functional
CC       channel in vitro and may also contribute to the M-current in brain (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O43525,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O43526, ECO:0000269|PubMed:24349250,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9836639}.

CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: Phosphatidylinositol-4,5-bisphosphate (PIP2)
CC       potentiates the activation of KCNQ channels by enhancing the electro-
CC       mechanical coupling of the voltage-sensing domain (VSD) and the pore-
CC       forming domain (PD). In the closed state of the channel, PIP2 is
CC       anchored at the S2-S3 loop; upon channel activation, PIP2 interacts
CC       with the S4-S5 linker and is involved in channel gating (By
CC       similarity). Calcium suppresses KCNQ2-KCNQ3 channel currents, with
CC       calcium-bound calmodulin inducing a change in channel configuration
CC       which leads to the reduction of channel affinity for PIP2 and
CC       subsequent current suppression (PubMed:24349250). M-channel is blocked
CC       by XE991 (PubMed:9836639). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O43526,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:24349250, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9836639}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Heterotetramer with KCNQ2; forms heterotetrameric M-channel
CC       responsible for the native M-current (PubMed:9836639). Interacts with
CC       calmodulin; the interaction is calcium-independent, constitutive and
CC       participates in the proper assembly of a functional M-channel
CC       (PubMed:24349250). Heteromultimer with KCNQ5 (By similarity). May
CC       associate with KCNE2 (By similarity). Interacts with IQCJ-SCHIP1
CC       (PubMed:27979964). Interacts (via the pore module) with SLC5A3/SMIT1;
CC       forms a coregulatory complex that alters ion selectivity, voltage
CC       dependence and gating kinetics of the channel (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O43525, ECO:0000269|PubMed:24349250,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:27979964, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9836639}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9836639};
CC       Multi-pass membrane protein {ECO:0000255}.

CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed in brain and sympathetic ganglia. In
CC       brain, expressed in cortex, hippocampus and at much lower levels in
CC       cerebellum. In sympathetic ganglia, expressed at approximately equal
CC       levels in both superior cervical ganglia and prevertebral ganglia.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: Each subunit contains six transmembrane segments (S1-S6) with
CC       S1-S4 forming one voltage sensing domain (VSD) and S5-S6 contributing
CC       to form one quarter of an interlocking pore-forming domain (PD).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O43526}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The S4-S5 linker preferentially interacts with PIP2 in the
CC       open-state KCNQ2 channel, whereas the S2-S3 loop interacts with PIP2 in
CC       the closed state. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O43526}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The intracellular C-terminal domain is bound constitutively by
CC       calmodulin (CaM). This domain plays key functions in channel
CC       tetramerization, trafficking, and gating.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O43525}.
CC   -!- PTM: KCNQ2/KCNQ3 are ubiquitinated by NEDD4L. Ubiquitination leads to
CC       protein degradation. Degradation induced by NEDD4L is inhibited by
CC       USP36. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O43525}.

CC       subfamily. Kv7.3/KCNQ3 sub-subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
