CC   -!- FUNCTION: Probably important in the regulation of neuronal
CC       excitability. May underlie a potassium current involved in regulating
CC       the excitability of sensory cells of the cochlea.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homotetramer. May form heteromultimers with KCNQ3 (By
CC       similarity). Interacts with HSP90AB1; promotes cell surface expression
CC       of KCNQ4 (By similarity). {ECO:0000250, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P56696}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Basal cell membrane; Multi-pass membrane protein.
CC       Note=Situated at the basal membrane of cochlear outer hair cells.
CC       {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed in both the inner (IHCs) and the outer
CC       hair cells (OHCs) of the cochlea. Reciprocal longitudinal gradients of
CC       expression is present in IHCs and OHCs. The strongest expression in
CC       IHCs is in the base of the cochlea and in the apex for OHCs. A basal to
CC       apical gradient of expression is also present in both type I and type
CC       II spiral ganglion cells.
CC   -!- DEVELOPMENTAL STAGE: At P0 expression is primarily in the basal hook to
CC       lower middle turn of the cochlea and progressively expands toward the
CC       apex over time. By P8, the expression is evident in both IHCs and OHCs
CC       along the entire length of the cochlea. Then the adult pattern begins
CC       to emerge, as expression in basal OHCs and in apical IHCs decreases.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The segment S4 is probably the voltage-sensor and is
CC       characterized by a series of positively charged amino acids at every
CC       third position. {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the potassium channel family. KQT (TC 1.A.1.15)
CC       subfamily. Kv7.4/KCNQ4 sub-subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
