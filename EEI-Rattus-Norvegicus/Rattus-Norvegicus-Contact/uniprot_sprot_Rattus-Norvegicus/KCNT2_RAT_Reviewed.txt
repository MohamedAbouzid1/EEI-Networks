CC   -!- FUNCTION: Sodium-activated and chloride-activated potassium channel.
CC       Produces rapidly activating outward rectifier K(+) currents
CC       (PubMed:14684870, PubMed:17699666, PubMed:19403831, PubMed:29069600).
CC       Contributes to regulate neuronal excitability (PubMed:29069600).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:14684870, ECO:0000269|PubMed:17699666,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19403831, ECO:0000269|PubMed:29069600}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY:
CC       Reaction=K(+)(in) = K(+)(out); Xref=Rhea:RHEA:29463, ChEBI:CHEBI:29103;
CC         Evidence={ECO:0000269|PubMed:14684870, ECO:0000269|PubMed:29069600};
CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: Are normally in a closed state unless activated by
CC       an increase in intracellular Na(+) and Cl(-) (PubMed:14684870,
CC       PubMed:17699666, PubMed:29069600). Inhibited upon stimulation of G-
CC       protein coupled receptors, such as CHRM1 and GRM1 (By similarity).
CC       There is conflicting data about the effect of ATP on KNCT2 channels
CC       activity. Intracellular ATP was initially report to inhibit the channel
CC       activity (By similarity). However, others studies conclude that KNCT2
CC       channels are not inhibited by intracellular ATP (PubMed:17699666).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q6UVM3, ECO:0000269|PubMed:14684870,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:17699666, ECO:0000269|PubMed:29069600}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homotetramer (By similarity). Forms heteromer with KCNT1;
CC       heteromeric channels differ from those of homomeric channels in their
CC       unitary conductance, kinetic behavior, subcellular localization, and
CC       response to activation of protein kinase C (PubMed:19403831).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q5JUK3, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19403831}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15717307,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:16687497, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19403831}; Multi-pass
CC       membrane protein {ECO:0000255}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Detected in brain, and at low levels in heart.
CC       Detected in brainstem, including auditory neurons such as the medial
CC       nucleus of the trapezoid body. Detected in the olfactory bulb, red
CC       nucleus, facial nucleus, pontine nucleus, oculomotor nucleus,
CC       substantia nigra, deep cerebellar nuclei, vestibular nucleus, and the
CC       thalamus. Detected in hippocampal CA1, CA2, and CA3 regions, the
CC       dentate gyrus, supraoptic nucleus, hypothalamus, dorsal root ganglion,
CC       and cortical layers II, III, and V. Detected in striatum cholinergic
CC       interneurons. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:14684870,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15717307, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16687497,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:17699666}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The consensus ATP binding site (1032-GPKHSGKT-1039) seems to be
CC       non-functional. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q6UVM3}.
CC   -!- PTM: Phosphorylated by protein kinase C. Phosphorylation of the C-
CC       terminal domain inhibits channel activity.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q6UVM3}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the potassium channel family. Calcium-activated
CC       (TC 1.A.1.3) subfamily. KCa4.2/KCNT2 sub-subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
