CC   -!- FUNCTION: Testis-specific potassium channel activated by both
CC       intracellular pH and membrane voltage that mediates export of K(+)
CC       (PubMed:32335912). Represents the primary spermatozoan K(+) current.
CC       The channel underlies a pH-triggered membrane hyperpolarization during
CC       the process of sperm capacitation, as sperm encounter the alkaline
CC       environment near the ovum in the female reproductive tract, thereby
CC       playing an essential for male fertility (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:A8MYU2, ECO:0000269|PubMed:32335912}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY:
CC       Reaction=K(+)(in) = K(+)(out); Xref=Rhea:RHEA:29463, ChEBI:CHEBI:29103;
CC         Evidence={ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O54982};
CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: Regulated by changes in cytosolic pH; activated by
CC       alkalization. Not activated by intracellular Ca(2+) (PubMed:32335912).
CC       VU0546110 acts as a selective inhibitor (By similarity). The auxiliary
CC       subunit LRRC52 shifts the activation of KCNU1 to more negative
CC       potentials at a given pH (PubMed:32335912).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:A8MYU2, ECO:0000269|PubMed:32335912}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homotetramer; which constitutes the activated potassium
CC       channel (By similarity). Interacts with LRRC52; this interaction
CC       changes channel gating properties, such as shifting gating to more
CC       negative potentials at a given pH (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:A8MYU2, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q12791}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O54982};
CC       Multi-pass membrane protein {ECO:0000255}. Cell projection, cilium,
CC       flagellum membrane {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:A8MYU2}; Multi-pass membrane
CC       protein {ECO:0000255}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The S4 segment, which is characterized by a series of
CC       positively charged amino acids at every third position, is part of the
CC       voltage-sensor. {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The pore-forming domain (also referred as P region) is imbedded
CC       into the membrane, and forms the selectivity filter of the pore. It
CC       contains the signature sequence of potassium channels that displays
CC       selectivity to potassium (By similarity). {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The RCK N-terminal domain mediates the homotetramerization,
CC       thereby promoting the assembly of monomers into functional potassium
CC       channel. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q12791}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The C-terminal cytosolic region confers the pH-dependence.
CC       {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the potassium channel family. Calcium-activated
CC       (TC 1.A.1.3) subfamily. KCa1.1/KCNMA1 sub-subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
