CC   -!- FUNCTION: Metastasis suppressor protein. May regulate events downstream
CC       of cell-matrix adhesion, perhaps involving cytoskeletal reorganization.
CC       Generates a C-terminally amidated peptide, metastin which functions as
CC       the endogenous ligand of the G-protein coupled receptor GPR54. The
CC       receptor is also essential for normal gonadotropin-released hormone
CC       physiology and for puberty. The hypothalamic KiSS1/GPR54 system is a
CC       pivotal factor in central regulation of the gonadotropic axis at
CC       puberty and in adulthood. Intracerebroventricular administration
CC       induces an increase in serum LH and FSH levels in prepubertal male and
CC       female as well as in adult animals. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15242985,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15593369}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Secreted.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Highest levels in the cecum and colon. Moderate
CC       levels present in the liver, spleen, kidney, ovary, uterus and small
CC       intestine. Low levels in the stomach, pancreas and placenta. Expressed
CC       only moderately in the placenta. Persistent expression is detected in
CC       hypothalamus throughout postnatal development, with maximum expression
CC       levels at puberty in both male and female. Hypothalamic expression is
CC       sensitive to neonatal imprinting by estrogen. Expression is higher in
CC       the hypothalamus than in the brainstem and spinal cord. In the brain,
CC       metastin-like immunoreactivity is found mainly in three groups of
CC       cells: dorsomedial hypothalamic nucleus, nucleus of the solitary tract,
CC       and caudal ventrolateral medulla. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15157736,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15242985, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15593369}.
CC   -!- DEVELOPMENTAL STAGE: Expression observed in trophoblast giant cells
CC       (TGCs), the placenta-derived cell lineage aligned at the boundary
CC       between the uterus and placenta, at 12.5 dpc. Expressions gradually
CC       decreased during placental maturation, and the signal is no more
CC       detectable in the cells at 18.5 dpc. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15157736}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the KISS1 family. {ECO:0000305}.
