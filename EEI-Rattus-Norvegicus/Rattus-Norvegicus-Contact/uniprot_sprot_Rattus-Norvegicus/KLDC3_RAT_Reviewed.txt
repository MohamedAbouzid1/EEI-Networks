CC   -!- FUNCTION: Substrate-recognition component of a Cul2-RING (CRL2) E3
CC       ubiquitin-protein ligase complex of the DesCEND (destruction via C-end
CC       degrons) pathway, which recognizes a C-degron located at the extreme C
CC       terminus of target proteins, leading to their ubiquitination and
CC       degradation. The C-degron recognized by the DesCEND pathway is usually
CC       a motif of less than ten residues and can be present in full-length
CC       proteins, truncated proteins or proteolytically cleaved forms. The
CC       CRL2(KLHDC3) complex specifically recognizes proteins with a glycine
CC       (Gly) at the C-terminus, leading to their ubiquitination and
CC       degradation: recognizes the C-terminal -Arg-(Xaa)n-Arg-Gly, -Arg-
CC       (Xaa)n-Lys-Gly, and -Arg-(Xaa)n-Gln-Gly degrons. The CRL2(KLHDC3)
CC       complex mediates ubiquitination and degradation of truncated SELENOV
CC       and SEPHS2 selenoproteins produced by failed UGA/Sec decoding, which
CC       end with a glycine. May be involved in meiotic recombination process.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9BQ90}.
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Protein modification; protein ubiquitination.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9BQ90}.

CC       KLHDC3. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9BQ90}.
