CC   -!- FUNCTION: Substrate-specific adapter of a BCR (BTB-CUL3-RBX1) E3
CC       ubiquitin ligase complex that acts as a regulator of ion transport in
CC       the distal nephron. The BCR(KLHL3) complex acts by mediating
CC       ubiquitination and degradation of WNK1 and WNK4, two activators of Na-
CC       Cl cotransporter SLC12A3/NCC in distal convoluted tubule cells of
CC       kidney, thereby regulating NaCl reabsorption. The BCR(KLHL3) complex
CC       also mediates ubiquitination and degradation of WNK3 (By similarity).
CC       The BCR(KLHL3) complex also mediates ubiquitination of CLDN8, a tight-
CC       junction protein required for paracellular chloride transport in the
CC       kidney, leading to its degradation (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:E0CZ16, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9UH77}.
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Protein modification; protein ubiquitination.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9UH77}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer. Component of the BCR(KLHL3) E3 ubiquitin ligase
CC       complex, at least composed of CUL3 and KLHL3 and RBX1 (By similarity).
CC       Interacts with CLDN8 (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:E0CZ16,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9UH77}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm, cytoskeleton
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9UH77}. Cytoplasm, cytosol
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9UH77}.
CC   -!- PTM: Phosphorylation at Ser-433 by PKA or PKC decreases the interaction
CC       with WNK1 and WNK4, leading to inhibit their degradation by the
CC       BCR(KLHL3) complex. Phosphorylated at Ser-433 by PKC in response to
CC       angiotensin II signaling, decreasing ability to promote degradation of
CC       WNK1 and WNK4, leading to activation of Na-Cl cotransporter
CC       SLC12A3/NCC. Phosphorylation at Ser-433 is increased by insulin.
CC       Dephosphorylated at Ser-433 by calcineurin PPP3CA, promoting
CC       degradation of WNK1 and WNK4. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9UH77}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the KLHL3 family. {ECO:0000305}.
