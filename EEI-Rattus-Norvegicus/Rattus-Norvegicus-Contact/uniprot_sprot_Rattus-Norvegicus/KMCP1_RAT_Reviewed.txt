CC   -!- FUNCTION: Antiporter that transports inorganic anions (sulfate,
CC       sulfite, thiosulfate and phosphate) and, to a lesser extent, a variety
CC       of dicarboxylates (e.g. malonate, malate and citramalate) and, even
CC       more so, aspartate. The sulfate/sulfate exchange is much higher than
CC       the phosphate/phosphate and malate/malate exchanges. The transport
CC       affinities is higher for sulfate and thiosulfate than for any other
CC       substrate. May catalyze the export of sulfite and thiosulfate (the
CC       hydrogen sulfide degradation products) from the mitochondria, thereby
CC       modulating the level of the hydrogen sulfide. Also may mediate a very
CC       low unidirectional transport of sulfate, phosphate and (S)-malate.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q5SVS4}.
