CC   -!- FUNCTION: Lysosomal membrane glycoprotein which plays an important role
CC       in lysosome biogenesis, lysosomal pH regulation and autophagy
CC       (PubMed:11082038, PubMed:8662539). Acts as an important regulator of
CC       lysosomal lumen pH regulation by acting as a direct inhibitor of the
CC       proton channel TMEM175, facilitating lysosomal acidification for
CC       optimal hydrolase activity (By similarity). Plays an important role in
CC       chaperone-mediated autophagy, a process that mediates lysosomal
CC       degradation of proteins in response to various stresses and as part of
CC       the normal turnover of proteins with a long biological half-live
CC       (PubMed:11082038, PubMed:8662539). Functions by binding target
CC       proteins, such as GAPDH, NLRP3 and MLLT11, and targeting them for
CC       lysosomal degradation (PubMed:11082038, PubMed:18644871,
CC       PubMed:8662539). In the chaperone-mediated autophagy, acts downstream
CC       of chaperones, such as HSPA8/HSC70, which recognize and bind substrate
CC       proteins and mediate their recruitment to lysosomes, where target
CC       proteins bind LAMP2 (PubMed:18644871). Plays a role in lysosomal
CC       protein degradation in response to starvation (PubMed:11082038).
CC       Required for the fusion of autophagosomes with lysosomes during
CC       autophagy (By similarity). Cells that lack LAMP2 express normal levels
CC       of VAMP8, but fail to accumulate STX17 on autophagosomes, which is the
CC       most likely explanation for the lack of fusion between autophagosomes
CC       and lysosomes (By similarity). Required for normal degradation of the
CC       contents of autophagosomes (By similarity). Required for efficient MHC
CC       class II-mediated presentation of exogenous antigens via its function
CC       in lysosomal protein degradation; antigenic peptides generated by
CC       proteases in the endosomal/lysosomal compartment are captured by
CC       nascent MHC II subunits (By similarity). Is not required for efficient
CC       MHC class II-mediated presentation of endogenous antigens (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P13473,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:11082038, ECO:0000269|PubMed:18644871,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:8662539}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Monomer (PubMed:18644871). Forms large homooligomers
CC       (PubMed:11082038, PubMed:18644871). Interacts (via its cytoplasmic
CC       region) with HSPA8; HSPA8 mediates recruitment of proteins with a KFERQ
CC       motif to the surface of the lysosome for chaperone-mediated autophagy
CC       (PubMed:18644871). Interacts with HSP90 in the lysosome lumen; this
CC       enhances LAMP2 stability (PubMed:18644871). Interacts with MLLT11 (By
CC       similarity). Interacts with ABCB9 (By similarity). Interacts with FURIN
CC       (By similarity). Interacts with CT55; this interaction may be important
CC       for LAMP2 protein stability (By similarity). Interacts with TMEM175;
CC       inhibiting the proton channel activity of TMEM175 (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P13473, ECO:0000269|PubMed:11082038,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:18644871}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Lysosome membrane {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11082038,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:1794981, ECO:0000269|PubMed:18644871,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:2590192, ECO:0000269|PubMed:8662539}; Single-pass
CC       type I membrane protein {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11082038}. Endosome
CC       membrane {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P13473}; Single-pass type I membrane
CC       protein {ECO:0000255|PROSITE-ProRule:PRU00740}. Cell membrane
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P13473}; Single-pass type I membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000255|PROSITE-ProRule:PRU00740}. Cytoplasmic vesicle,
CC       autophagosome membrane {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P17047}. Note=This
CC       protein shuttles between lysosomes, endosomes, and the plasma membrane.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P13473}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Detected in liver, kidney, spleen and macrophages
CC       (at protein level). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:1794981}.
