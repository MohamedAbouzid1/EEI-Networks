CC   -!- FUNCTION: [Lamin-A/C]: Lamins are intermediate filament proteins that
CC       assemble into a filamentous meshwork, and which constitute the major
CC       components of the nuclear lamina, a fibrous layer on the nucleoplasmic
CC       side of the inner nuclear membrane. Lamins provide a framework for the
CC       nuclear envelope, bridging the nuclear envelope and chromatin, thereby
CC       playing an important role in nuclear assembly, chromatin organization,
CC       nuclear membrane and telomere dynamics. Lamin A and C also regulate
CC       matrix stiffness by conferring nuclear mechanical properties. The
CC       structural integrity of the lamina is strictly controlled by the cell
CC       cycle, as seen by the disintegration and formation of the nuclear
CC       envelope in prophase and telophase, respectively. Lamin A and C are
CC       present in equal amounts in the lamina of mammals. Also invoved in DNA
CC       repair: recruited by DNA repair proteins XRCC4 and IFFO1 to the DNA
CC       double-strand breaks (DSBs) to prevent chromosome translocation by
CC       immobilizing broken DNA ends. Required for normal development of
CC       peripheral nervous system and skeletal muscle and for muscle satellite
CC       cell proliferation. Required for osteoblastogenesis and bone formation.
CC       Also prevents fat infiltration of muscle and bone marrow, helping to
CC       maintain the volume and strength of skeletal muscle and bone. Required
CC       for cardiac homeostasis. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P02545}.
CC   -!- FUNCTION: [Prelamin-A/C]: Prelamin-A/C can accelerate smooth muscle
CC       cell senescence. It acts to disrupt mitosis and induce DNA damage in
CC       vascular smooth muscle cells (VSMCs), leading to mitotic failure,
CC       genomic instability, and premature senescence.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P02545}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer of lamin A and lamin C. Lamin dimers then assemble
CC       into dimeric head-to-tail polymers. Ultimately, two head-to-tail
CC       polymers assemble laterally into a protofilament with a uniformly
CC       shaped rod of 3.5 nm in diameter. Interacts with lamin-associated
CC       polypeptides IA, IB and TMPO-alpha, RB1 and with emerin (By
CC       similarity). Interacts with SREBF1, SREBF2, SUN2 and TMEM43 (By
CC       similarity). Interacts with TMEM201 (By similarity). Proteolytically
CC       processed isoform A interacts with NARF. Interacts with SUN1. Interacts
CC       with MLIP. Interacts with DMPK; may regulate nuclear envelope
CC       stability. Interacts with SUV39H1; the interaction increases stability
CC       of SUV39H1. Interacts with SYNE2. Interacts with ITSN1 isoform 2.
CC       Interacts with IFFO1; enables the formation of an interior
CC       nucleoskeleton that is recruited to DNA double-strand breaks (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P02545,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P48678}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: [Prelamin-A/C]: Interacts with EMD.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P02545}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: [Isoform Lamin C]: Interacts (via C-terminus) with LEMD2 (via
CC       N-terminus) (in vitro). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P02545}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Nucleus lamina {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P02545}.
CC       Nucleus envelope {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P02545}. Nucleus, nucleoplasm
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P02545}. Nucleus matrix
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P02545}. Note=Farnesylation of prelamin-A/C
CC       facilitates nuclear envelope targeting and subsequent cleavage by
CC       ZMPSTE24/FACE1 to remove the farnesyl group produces mature lamin-A/C,
CC       which can then be inserted into the nuclear lamina. EMD is required for
CC       proper localization of non-farnesylated prelamin-A/C. Also localizes to
CC       the micronuclear envelope in response to response to genome
CC       instability. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P02545}.

CC   -!- PTM: Proteolytic cleavage of the C-terminal of 18 residues of prelamin-
CC       A/C results in the production of lamin-A/C. The prelamin-A/C maturation
CC       pathway includes farnesylation of CAAX motif by protein
CC       farnesyltransferase (FNTA and FNTB), removal of the last three amino
CC       acids (-AAX) by RCE1/FACE2 and/or ZMPSTE24, methylation of the C-
CC       terminal cysteine by ICMT and endoproteolytic removal of the last 15 C-
CC       terminal amino acids by ZMPSTE24. Proteolytic cleavage requires prior
CC       farnesylation and methylation, and absence of these blocks cleavage.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P02545}.
CC   -!- PTM: Farnesylation of prelamin-A/C facilitates nuclear envelope
CC       targeting. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P02545}.
CC   -!- PTM: Phosphorylation plays a key role in lamin organization,
CC       subcellular localization and nuclear envelope disintegration.
CC       Phosphorylation by CDK1 at Ser-22 and Ser-392 at the onset of mitosis
CC       drives lamin disassembly and nuclear envelope breakdown.
CC       Phosphorylation at Ser-22 and Ser-392 during interphase promotes
CC       localization to the nucleoplasm and regulates lamina assembly.
CC       Phosphorylation at Ser-22, Ser-392 and Ser-629 during interphase causes
CC       redistribution between the nucleus and the cytoplasm. Phosphorylation
CC       at Ser-22 by CDK1 regulates matrix stiffness. Phosphorylation status of
CC       Ser-22 determines its localization between double-strand break (DSB)
CC       sites and the nuclear matrix. Phosphorylated by ATR at Ser-282 in
CC       response to DNA damage, leading to lamin disassembly and nuclear
CC       envelope rupture. Phosphorylation also regulates stability in
CC       micronuclei arising from genome instability: phosphorylation at Ser-395
CC       by ATR in response to genome instability and double-stranded DNA breaks
CC       primes LMNA for subsequent phosphorylation at Ser-392 by CDK1 and
CC       micronuclei envelope rupture. The rupture of micronuclear envelope
CC       triggers the cGAS-STING pathway thereby activating the type I
CC       interferon response and innate immunity.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P02545}.
CC   -!- PTM: Acetylation by KAT8 is required for nuclear architecture.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P48678}.
CC   -!- PTM: Sumoylation is necessary for the localization to the nuclear
CC       envelope. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P02545}.
