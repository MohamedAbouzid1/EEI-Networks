CC   -!- FUNCTION: Key component of the Ragulator complex, a multiprotein
CC       complex involved in amino acid sensing and activation of mTORC1, a
CC       signaling complex promoting cell growth in response to growth factors,
CC       energy levels, and amino acids. Activated by amino acids through a
CC       mechanism involving the lysosomal V-ATPase, the Ragulator plays a dual
CC       role for the small GTPases Rag (RagA/RRAGA, RagB/RRAGB, RagC/RRAGC
CC       and/or RagD/RRAGD): it (1) acts as a guanine nucleotide exchange factor
CC       (GEF), activating the small GTPases Rag and (2) mediates recruitment of
CC       Rag GTPases to the lysosome membrane. Activated Ragulator and Rag
CC       GTPases function as a scaffold recruiting mTORC1 to lysosomes where it
CC       is in turn activated. LAMTOR1 is directly responsible for anchoring the
CC       Ragulator complex to the lysosomal membrane. LAMTOR1 wraps around the
CC       other subunits of the Ragulator complex to hold them in place and
CC       interacts with the Rag GTPases, thereby playing a key role in the
CC       recruitment of the mTORC1 complex to lysosomes (By similarity). Also
CC       involved in the control of embryonic stem cells differentiation via
CC       non-canonical RagC/RRAGC and RagD/RRAGD activation: together with FLCN,
CC       it is necessary to recruit and activate RagC/RRAGC and RagD/RRAGD at
CC       the lysosomes, and to induce exit of embryonic stem cells from
CC       pluripotency via non-canonical, mTOR-independent TFE3 inactivation (By
CC       similarity). Also required for late endosomes/lysosomes biogenesis it
CC       may regulate both the recycling of receptors through endosomes and the
CC       MAPK signaling pathway through recruitment of some of its components to
CC       late endosomes. May be involved in cholesterol homeostasis regulating
CC       LDL uptake and cholesterol release from late endosomes/lysosomes. May
CC       also play a role in RHOA activation (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q6IAA8, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9CQ22}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Part of the Ragulator complex composed of LAMTOR1, LAMTOR2,
CC       LAMTOR3, LAMTOR4 and LAMTOR5. LAMTOR4 and LAMTOR5 form a heterodimer
CC       that interacts, through LAMTOR1, with a LAMTOR2, LAMTOR3 heterodimer.
CC       Interacts with LAMTOR2 and LAMTOR3; the interaction is direct. The
CC       Ragulator complex interacts with both the mTORC1 complex and
CC       heterodimers constituted of the Rag GTPases RagA/RRAGA, RagB/RRAGB,
CC       RagC/RRAGC and RagD/RRAGD; regulated by amino acid availability. The
CC       Ragulator complex interacts with SLC38A9; the probable amino acid
CC       sensor. Component of the lysosomal folliculin complex (LFC), composed
CC       of FLCN, FNIP1 (or FNIP2), RagA/RRAGA or RagB/RRAGB GDP-bound,
CC       RagC/RRAGC or RagD/RRAGD GTP-bound, and Ragulator. Associates with the
CC       lysosomal V-ATPase complex; interaction promotes the guanine nucleotide
CC       exchange factor (GEF) of the Ragulator complex. Interacts with MMP14.
CC       Interacts with CDKN1B; prevents the interaction of CDKN1B with RHOA
CC       leaving RHOA in a form accessible to activation by ARHGEF2. Interacts
CC       with PIP4P1. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q6IAA8}.
