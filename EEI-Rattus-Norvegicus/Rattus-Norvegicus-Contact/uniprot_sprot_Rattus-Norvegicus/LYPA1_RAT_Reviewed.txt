CC   -!- FUNCTION: Acts as an acyl-protein thioesterase (PubMed:9624183).
CC       Hydrolyzes fatty acids from S-acylated cysteine residues in proteins
CC       such as trimeric G alpha proteins or HRAS (PubMed:9624183). Acts as a
CC       palmitoyl thioesterase that catalyzes depalmitoylation of proteins,
CC       such as ADRB2, KCNMA1 and SQSTM1 (By similarity). Acts as a negative
CC       regulator of autophagy by mediating palmitoylation of SQSTM1,
CC       decreasing affinity between SQSTM1 and ATG8 proteins and recruitment of
CC       ubiquitinated cargo proteins to autophagosomes (By similarity). Acts as
CC       a lysophospholipase and hydrolyzes lysophosphatidylcholine (lyso-PC)
CC       (By similarity). Also hydrolyzes lysophosphatidylethanolamine (lyso-
CC       PE), lysophosphatidylinositol (lyso-PI) and lysophosphatidylserine
CC       (lyso-PS) (PubMed:8631810, PubMed:9644627). Has much higher
CC       thioesterase activity than lysophospholipase activity (By similarity).
CC       Contributes to the production of lysophosphatidic acid (LPA) during
CC       blood coagulation by recognizing and cleaving plasma phospholipids to
CC       generate lysophospholipids which in turn act as substrates for ENPP2 to
CC       produce LPA (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O75608,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:8631810, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9624183,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9644627}.
