CC   -!- FUNCTION: Acts as a modulator of nicotinic acetylcholine receptors
CC       (nAChRs) function in the brain (PubMed:27344019). Inhibits nicotine-
CC       induced Ca(2+) influx through nAChRs (By similarity). In vitro,
CC       specifically inhibits alpha-3:beta-4 and alpha-7 nAChR currents in an
CC       allosteric manner (By similarity). Acts as a positive regulator of
CC       Wnt/beta-catenin signaling (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q66IA6, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q86Y78,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:27344019}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts with nicotinic acetylcholine receptors (nAChRs)
CC       including CHRNA3, CHRNA4, CHRNA5, CHRNA6, CHRNA7, CHRNB2 and CHRNB4 (By
CC       similarity). Interacts (via NxI motif) with LRP6 (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q86Y78}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Secreted {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q86Y78}.
CC       Cytoplasm {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q86Y78}. Cell membrane
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:27344019}; Lipid-anchor, GPI-anchor {ECO:0000255}.
CC       Synapse, synaptosome {ECO:0000269|PubMed:27344019}. Membrane raft
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q66IA6}. Cell projection, dendrite
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:34631692}. Perikaryon
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:34631692}. Note=Colocalizes with alpha-3:beta-
CC       4- and alpha-7- nicotinic acetylcholine receptors (nAChRs) in the
CC       primary cortex and hippocampus. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:34631692}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed at high levels in the cortex and
CC       cerebellum of the brain, at moderate levels in the lung, kidney, and
CC       liver, and at low levels in the heart and prostate (at protein level)
CC       (PubMed:27344019). Expressed in neurons (at protein level)
CC       (PubMed:34631692). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:27344019,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:34631692}.
CC   -!- DEVELOPMENTAL STAGE: Highly expressed during early development, showing
CC       high levels in the first postnatal days, followed by a decrease toward
CC       adulthood. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:27344019}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The UPAR/Ly6 domain is sufficient for inhibiting alpha-3:beta-4
CC       and alpha-7-dependent nAChR currents. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q86Y78}.
