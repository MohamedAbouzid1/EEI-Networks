CC   -!- FUNCTION: Plus-end tracking protein (+TIP) that binds to the plus-end
CC       of microtubules and regulates the dynamics of the microtubule
CC       cytoskeleton. Recruits other +TIP proteins to microtubules by binding
CC       to a conserved Ser-X-Leu-Pro (SXLP) motif in their polypeptide chains.
CC       Promotes cytoplasmic microtubule nucleation and elongation. Involved in
CC       mitotic spindle positioning by stabilizing microtubules and promoting
CC       dynamic connection between astral microtubules and the cortex during
CC       mitotic chromosome segregation. Assists chromosome alignment in
CC       metaphase by recruiting the SKA complex to the spindle and stabilizing
CC       its interactions with microtubule bundles (K-fibers). Also acts as a
CC       regulator of minus-end microtubule organization: interacts with the
CC       complex formed by AKAP9 and PDE4DIP, leading to recruit CAMSAP2 to the
CC       Golgi apparatus, thereby tethering non-centrosomal minus-end
CC       microtubules to the Golgi, an important step for polarized cell
CC       movement. Promotes elongation of CAMSAP2-decorated microtubule
CC       stretches on the minus-end of microtubules. Acts as a regulator of
CC       autophagosome transport via interaction with CAMSAP2 (By similarity).
CC       Functions downstream of Rho GTPases and DIAPH1 in stable microtubule
CC       formation (By similarity). May play a role in cell migration (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q15691,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q61166}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer. Heterodimer with MAPRE3. Interacts with DCTN1,
CC       DCTN2, TERF1 and dynein intermediate chain (By similarity). Interaction
CC       with DIAPH1 and DIAPH2 (By similarity). Interacts (via C-terminal
CC       residues 206-211) with APC (via C-terminal residues 2674-2845); the
CC       interaction inhibits association with and bundling of F-actin (By
CC       similarity). Interacts with CLASP2, DST, KIF2C and STIM1; probably
CC       required for their targeting to the growing microtubule plus ends.
CC       Interacts with MTUS2; interaction is direct and probably targets MTUS2
CC       to microtubules. Interacts (via C-terminus) with SKA1 (via SXIP motif);
CC       the interaction is direct and stabilizes the kinetochore-microtubule
CC       attachment of the SKA1 complex. Interacts with APC2. Interacts with
CC       CLASP1. Interacts with CDK5RAP2 (By similarity). According to another
CC       report, MAPRE1 does not interact with CDK5RAP2 (PubMed:19553473).
CC       Interacts with MACF1 (By similarity). Interacts with RABL2/RABL2A;
CC       binds preferentially to GTP-bound RABL2 (By similarity). Interacts with
CC       KCNAB2 (PubMed:21357749). Interacts (via C-terminus) with CLIP1.
CC       Interacts with SLAIN2 and SLAIN1. Interacts with KIF18B; this
CC       interaction is required for efficient accumulation of KIF18B at
CC       microtubule plus ends. Interacts with MISP. Interacts with KNSTRN.
CC       Interacts with NCKAP5L. Interacts with AKAP9. Interacts with PDE4DIP;
CC       this interaction, which is PDE4DIP isoform-specific, is required for
CC       its recruitment to the Golgi apparatus. Interacts with CAMSAP2 (By
CC       similarity). May form a pericentrosomal complex with AKAP9, CDK5RAP2
CC       and PDE4DIP isoform 2/MMG8/SMYLE; within this complex, MAPRE1 binding
CC       to CDK5RAP2 may be mediated by PDE4DIP (By similarity). Contrary to
CC       other mammalian species, does not interact with CDK5RAP2, possibly due
CC       to the lack of conservation of the MAPRE1-binding motif in rat CDK5RAP2
CC       (Probable). Interacts with AKNA (By similarity). Interacts with GAS2L1,
CC       GAS2L2, and GAS2L3 (By similarity). Interacts with RARRES1 and AGBL2
CC       (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q15691,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q61166, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19553473,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:21357749, ECO:0000305|PubMed:19553473}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm, cytoskeleton
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q15691}. Cytoplasm, cytoskeleton, microtubule
CC       organizing center, centrosome {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q15691}. Golgi
CC       apparatus {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q15691}. Cytoplasm, cytoskeleton,
CC       spindle {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q15691}. Cytoplasm, cytoskeleton,
CC       spindle pole {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q15691}. Note=Associated with the
CC       microtubule growing distal tips. In addition to localizing to
CC       microtubule plus-ends, also exhibits some localization along the length
CC       of the microtubules. Recruitment to the Golgi apparatus requires the
CC       presence of PDE4DIP isoform 13/MMG8/SMYLE.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q15691}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: Composed of two functionally independent domains. The N-
CC       terminal domain forms a hydrophobic cleft involved in microtubule
CC       binding and the C-terminal is involved in the formation of mutually
CC       exclusive complexes with APC and DCTN1. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q15691}.
CC   -!- PTM: Acetylation at Lys-220 by KAT2B/PCAF promotes dynamic kinetochore-
CC       microtubule interactions in early mitosis.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q15691}.
CC   -!- PTM: Crotonylated by KAT5 during mitosis, promoting astral microtubule
CC       plasticity and dynamic connection between astral microtubules and the
CC       cortex during mitotic chromosome segregation, thereby ensuring accurate
CC       spindle positioning in mitosis. Decrotonylated by HDAC3.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q15691}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the MAPRE family. {ECO:0000305}.
