CC   -!- FUNCTION: MCH inhibits ACTH secretion at the end of the light on period
CC       which corresponds to the peak of the circadian rhythm in ACTH. Inhibits
CC       also stress induced ACTH release during the light off period of the
CC       cycle. Involved as a neurotransmitter or neuromodulator in a broad
CC       array of neuronal functions. Stimulates sexual behavior when injected
CC       into the ventromedial nucleus, this effect is antagonized by NEI. In
CC       the medial preoptic area, stimulates anxiety and sexual behavior.
CC       Antagonizes inhibitory effect of melanotropin alpha on exploration
CC       behavior.
CC   -!- FUNCTION: NEI can influence differentiation of neuronal processes in
CC       brain neurons. Affects the content of neurofilament protein in
CC       neuritogenesis (in vitro). May also be a neuromodulatory factor. In
CC       behavioral tests, it stimulates exploration and anxiety when injected
CC       into the ventromedial nucleus. Also stimulates grooming, locomotion and
CC       rearing. May antagonize the inhibitory effect of mch on ACTH release.
CC       Reduces dopamine and dopac release in the ventromedial nucleus.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Secreted.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: MCH is present in all regions of the brain and in
CC       neurointermediate lobe of the pituarity gland, with highest
CC       concentrations in the hypothalamus. Also expressed to a much lesser
CC       extent in stomach, lamina propria of both duodenum and colon, ovary,
CC       thymus, pancreas, adrenal gland and testis (spermatogonia, early
CC       spermatocytes and Sertoli cells). Weak expression in heart and lung.
CC       The other peptides are expressed at least in Sertoli cells, nei being
CC       also expressed in brain, stomach and proximal duodenum. In brain
CC       exclusively mature mch and nei peptides are present. In peripheral
CC       tissues a large product, encompassing the NEI and MCH domains of the
CC       precursor, is found predominantly. At low levels fully processed MCH
CC       and NEI peptides are present in gut. No expression in peripheral blood.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:7617126, ECO:0000269|PubMed:7783849,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:8593803}.
CC   -!- DEVELOPMENTAL STAGE: Expression is strongly increased in hypothalamus
CC       between postnatal days 12 and 20, to reach high constant values in
CC       adult.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Inhibited by neurogenic stress or osmotic stress.
CC   -!- PTM: Pro-MCH is processed differentially in the brain and in peripheral
CC       organs producing two neuropeptides; NEI and MCH. A third peptide, NGE,
CC       may also be produced. Preferential processing in neurons by prohormone
CC       convertase 2 (PC2) generates NEI. MCH is generated in neurons of the
CC       lateral hypothalmic area by several prohormone convertases including
CC       PC1/3, PC2 and PC5/6. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10037747,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:1327720}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the melanin-concentrating hormone family.
CC       {ECO:0000305}.
