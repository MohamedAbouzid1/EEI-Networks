CC   -!- FUNCTION: May be part of a complex containing NF2/merlin that
CC       participates in cellular signaling to the actin cytoskeleton downstream
CC       of tyrosine kinase signaling pathways (By similarity). Component of the
CC       Mediator complex, a coactivator involved in the regulated transcription
CC       of nearly all RNA polymerase II-dependent genes. Mediator functions as
CC       a bridge to convey information from gene-specific regulatory proteins
CC       to the basal RNA polymerase II transcription machinery. Mediator is
CC       recruited to promoters by direct interactions with regulatory proteins
CC       and serves as a scaffold for the assembly of a functional preinitiation
CC       complex with RNA polymerase II and the general transcription factors.
CC       {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Forms a ternary complex with NF2/merlin and GRB2. Binds to
CC       actin (By similarity). Component of the Mediator complex, which is
CC       probably composed of MED1, MED4, MED6, MED7, MED8, MED9, MED10, MED11,
CC       MED12, MED13, MED13L, MED14, MED15, MED16, MED17, MED18, MED19, MED20,
CC       MED21, MED22, MED23, MED24, MED25, MED26, MED27, MED29, MED30, MED31,
CC       CCNC, CDK8 and CDC2L6/CDK11. The MED12, MED13, CCNC and CDK8 subunits
CC       form a distinct module termed the CDK8 module. Mediator containing the
CC       CDK8 module is less active than Mediator lacking this module in
CC       supporting transcriptional activation. Individual preparations of the
CC       Mediator complex lacking one or more distinct subunits have been
CC       variously termed ARC, CRSP, DRIP, PC2, SMCC and TRAP. {ECO:0000250,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:12149480}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Nucleus {ECO:0000250}. Cytoplasm {ECO:0000250}.
CC       Membrane {ECO:0000250}. Note=May be also cytoplasmic and membrane-
CC       associated. {ECO:0000250}.
