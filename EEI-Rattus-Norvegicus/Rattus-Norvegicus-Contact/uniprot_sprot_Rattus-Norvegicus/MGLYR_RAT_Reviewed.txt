CC   -!- FUNCTION: Metabotropic receptor for glycine that controls synapse
CC       formation and function in the brain. Acts as an atypical G-protein
CC       coupled receptor that recruits and regulates the RGS7-GNB5 complex
CC       instead of activating G proteins. In absence of glycine ligand,
CC       promotes the GTPase activator activity of RGS7, increasing the GTPase
CC       activity of G protein alpha subunits, thereby driving them into their
CC       inactive GDP-bound form. Glycine-binding changes the conformation of
CC       the intracellular surface, inhibiting the GTPase activator activity of
CC       the RGS7-GNB5 complex, promoting G protein alpha subunits into their
CC       active GTP-bound form and regulating cAMP levels. Also able to bind
CC       taurine, a compound closely related to glycine, but with a two-fold
CC       lower affinity. Glycine receptor-dependent regulation of cAMP controls
CC       key ion channels, kinases and neurotrophic factors involved in neuronal
CC       excitability and synaptic transmission (By similarity). Plays a pivotal
CC       role in regulating mood and cognition via its ability to regulate
CC       neuronal excitability in L2/L3 pyramidal neurons of the prefrontal
CC       cortex. Also involved in spatial learning by regulating hippocampal CA1
CC       neuronal excitability. Acts as a synaptic organizer in the hippocampus,
CC       required for proper mossy fiber-CA3 neurocircuitry establishment,
CC       structure and function: induces presynaptic differentiation in
CC       contacting axons via its interaction with GPC4. In addition to glycine,
CC       may also act as a receptor for osteocalcin (BGLAP) hormone:
CC       osteocalcin-binding initiates a signaling response that prevents
CC       neuronal apoptosis in the hippocampus and regulates the synthesis of
CC       neurotransmitters (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q5T848,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8C419}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer. Associates with the RGS7-GNB5 complex, promoting
CC       its localization to the cell membrane and regulating its GTPase
CC       activator activity. Interacts (via VCPWE motifs) with GNAO1 (By
CC       similarity). Interacts with GPC4 (PubMed:30290982). Interacts with
CC       EGFLAM (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q5T848,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8C419, ECO:0000269|PubMed:30290982}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q5T848};
CC       Multi-pass membrane protein {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q5T848}.
CC       Postsynaptic cell membrane {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8C419}; Multi-pass
CC       membrane protein {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q5T848}. Presynaptic cell
CC       membrane {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8C419}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q5T848}. Nucleus {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q5T848}.
CC       Note=Mainly localizes to the postsynaptic membrane, with a small
CC       portion to the presynaptic membrane (By similarity). Trafficks between
CC       the nucleus and the cell membrane; it is unclear how a multi-pass
CC       membrane protein can traffick between the nucleus and the cell membrane
CC       (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q5T848,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8C419}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The Cache-like region shares similarity with the Cache domain,
CC       a well-known receptor for amino acids. It acts as a ligand-binding
CC       module that recognizes and binds glycine and taurine.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q5T848}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the G-protein coupled receptor 3 family.
CC       {ECO:0000305}.
