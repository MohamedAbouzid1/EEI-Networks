CC   -!- FUNCTION: Calcium sensor of the mitochondrial calcium uniporter (MCU)
CC       channel, which senses calcium level via its EF-hand domains. MICU1 and
CC       MICU2 (or MICU3) form a disulfide-linked heterodimer that stimulates
CC       and inhibits MCU activity, depending on the concentration of calcium.
CC       At low calcium levels, MICU1 occludes the pore of the MCU channel,
CC       preventing mitochondrial calcium uptake. At higher calcium levels,
CC       calcium-binding to MICU1 and MICU2 (or MICU3) induces a conformational
CC       change that weakens MCU-MICU1 interactions and moves the MICU1-MICU2
CC       heterodimer away from the pore, allowing calcium permeation through the
CC       MCU channel. Also required to protect against manganese toxicity by
CC       preventing manganese uptake by MCU: mechanistically, manganese-binding
CC       to its EF-hand domains does not induce any conformational change,
CC       maintaining MCU pore occlusion. Acts as a regulator of mitochondrial
CC       cristae structure independently of its ability to regulate the
CC       mitochondrial calcium uniporter channel. Regulates glucose-dependent
CC       insulin secretion in pancreatic beta-cells by regulating mitochondrial
CC       calcium uptake. Induces T-helper 1-mediated autoreactivity, which is
CC       accompanied by the release of IFNG. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9BPX6}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Heterodimer; disulfide-linked; heterodimerizes with MICU2 or
CC       MICU3. Homodimer; disulfide-linked. Component of the uniplex complex,
CC       composed of MCU, EMRE/SMDT1, MICU1 and MICU2 (or MICU3) in a 4:4:1:1
CC       stoichiometry. The composition of calcium sensors within the uniplex
CC       complex can differ depending on tissues: a MICU1 homodimer can be
CC       present instead of the MICU1-MICU2 heterodimer in skeletal-muscle and
CC       kidney. MICU1 is recruited to the uniplex complex by EMRE/SMDT1, and it
CC       associates with MCU at low calcium levels, occluding the pore of the
CC       MCU channel. Associates with the MICOS complex. Interacts with
CC       SLC25A23. Interacts with CHCHD4/MIA40; which introduces the interchain
CC       disulfide bond with MICU2. Interacts (when methylated) with UCP2;
CC       leading to decrease the calcium sensitivity of MICU1.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9BPX6}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Mitochondrion intermembrane space
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9BPX6}. Mitochondrion inner membrane
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9BPX6}. Note=Recruited to the mitochondrial
CC       inner membrane by EMRE/SMDT1. Also localizes to mitochondrial cristae
CC       junctions. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9BPX6}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The EF-hand domains have high affinity for calcium and act as
CC       sensors of calcium levels. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9BPX6}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The polybasic region mediates interaction with EMRE/SMDT1 and
CC       association with the uniplex complex. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9BPX6}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: Lysine and arginine residues in the K/R-ring mediate
CC       electrostatic interactions with MCU and play a key role in MCU
CC       inhibition in absence of calcium. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9BPX6}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The C-helix plays a key role in mitochondrial calcium uptake,
CC       probably by mediating interaction with MICU2.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9BPX6}.
CC   -!- PTM: Phosphorylation at Ser-124 by AKT1 impairs its maturation and
CC       stability. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8VCX5}.
CC   -!- PTM: Asymmetric dimethylation at Arg-457 by PRMT1 decreases the calcium
CC       sensitivity of MICU1 by promoting interaction with UCP2.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9BPX6}.
CC   -!- PTM: Degraded by YME1L1 when not complexed as homodimer or heterodimer.
CC       Not degraded when complexed as homodimer or heterodimer; the presence
CC       of the interchain disulfide bond protecting MICU1 from degradation by
CC       YME1L1. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9BPX6}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the MICU1 family. MICU1 subfamily.
CC       {ECO:0000305}.
