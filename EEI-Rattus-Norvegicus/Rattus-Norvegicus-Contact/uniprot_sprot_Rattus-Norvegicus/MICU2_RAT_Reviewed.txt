CC   -!- FUNCTION: Calcium sensor of the mitochondrial calcium uniporter (MCU)
CC       channel, which senses calcium level via its EF-hand domains. MICU1 and
CC       MICU2 form a disulfide-linked heterodimer that stimulates and inhibits
CC       MCU activity, depending on the concentration of calcium. At low calcium
CC       levels, MICU1 occludes the pore of the MCU channel, preventing
CC       mitochondrial calcium uptake. At higher calcium levels, calcium-binding
CC       to MICU1 and MICU2 induces a conformational change that weakens MCU-
CC       MICU1 interactions and moves the MICU1-MICU2 heterodimer away from the
CC       pore, allowing calcium permeation through the MCU channel.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8IYU8}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Heterodimer; disulfide-linked; heterodimerizes with MICU1.
CC       Component of the uniplex complex, composed of MCU, EMRE/SMDT1, MICU1
CC       and MICU2 in a 4:4:1:1 stoichiometry. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8IYU8}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Mitochondrion intermembrane space
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8IYU8}. Mitochondrion inner membrane
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8IYU8}. Note=Recruited to the mitochondrial
CC       inner membrane via its association with the uniplex complex.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8IYU8}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: EF-hand domains 1 and 4 have high affinity for calcium and act
CC       as sensors of mitochondrial matrix calcium levels. EF-hand domains 2
CC       and 3 are degenerate. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8IYU8}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the MICU1 family. MICU2 subfamily.
CC       {ECO:0000305}.
