CC   -!- FUNCTION: Tissue-specific calcium sensor of the mitochondrial calcium
CC       uniporter (MCU) channel, which specifically regulates MCU channel
CC       activity in the central nervous system and skeletal muscle
CC       (PubMed:31862210). Senses calcium level via its EF-hand domains:
CC       compared to MICU1 and MICU2, MICU3 has a higher affinity for calcium
CC       (PubMed:31862210). MICU1 and MICU3 form a disulfide-linked heterodimer
CC       that stimulates and inhibits MCU activity, depending on the
CC       concentration of calcium (PubMed:31862210). At low calcium levels,
CC       MICU1 occludes the pore of the MCU channel, preventing mitochondrial
CC       calcium uptake (PubMed:31862210). At higher calcium levels, calcium-
CC       binding to MICU1 and MICU3 induces a conformational change that weakens
CC       MCU-MICU1 interactions and moves the MICU1-MICU3 heterodimer away from
CC       the pore, allowing calcium permeation through the MCU channel
CC       (PubMed:31862210). The high calcium affinity of MICU3 lowers the
CC       calcium threshold necessary for calcium permeation through the MCU
CC       channel (PubMed:31862210). The MICU1-MICU3 heterodimer promotes
CC       flexibility of neurotransmission in neuronal cells by enhancing
CC       mitochondrial calcium uptake in presynapses (PubMed:31862210). It is
CC       also required to increase mitochondrial calcium uptake in skeletal
CC       muscle cells, thereby increasing ATP production (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9CTY5, ECO:0000269|PubMed:31862210}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Heterodimer; disulfide-linked; heterodimerizes with MICU1 (By
CC       similarity). Component of the uniplex complex, composed of MCU,
CC       EMRE/SMDT1, MICU1 and MICU3 in a 4:4:1:1 stoichiometry (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q86XE3, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8IYU8}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Mitochondrion intermembrane space
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8IYU8}. Mitochondrion inner membrane
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8IYU8}. Note=Recruited to the mitochondrial
CC       inner membrane via its association with the uniplex complex.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8IYU8}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: EF-hand domains 1 and 3 have high affinity for calcium and act
CC       as sensors of mitochondrial matrix calcium levels (By similarity). EF-
CC       hand domain 2 is degenerate (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q86XE3}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the MICU1 family. MICU3 subfamily.
CC       {ECO:0000305}.
