CC   -!- FUNCTION: Core component of the MRN complex, which plays a central role
CC       in double-strand break (DSB) repair, DNA recombination, maintenance of
CC       telomere integrity and meiosis. The MRN complex is involved in the
CC       repair of DNA double-strand breaks (DSBs) via homologous recombination
CC       (HR), an error-free mechanism which primarily occurs during S and G2
CC       phases. The complex (1) mediates the end resection of damaged DNA,
CC       which generates proper single-stranded DNA, a key initial steps in HR,
CC       and is (2) required for the recruitment of other repair factors and
CC       efficient activation of ATM and ATR upon DNA damage. Within the MRN
CC       complex, MRE11 possesses both single-strand endonuclease activity and
CC       double-strand-specific 3'-5' exonuclease activity. After DSBs, MRE11 is
CC       loaded onto DSBs sites and cleaves DNA by cooperating with RBBP8/CtIP
CC       to initiate end resection. MRE11 first endonucleolytically cleaves the
CC       5' strand at DNA DSB ends to prevent non-homologous end joining (NHEJ)
CC       and licence HR. It then generates a single-stranded DNA gap via 3' to
CC       5' exonucleolytic degradation to create entry sites for EXO1- and DNA2-
CC       mediated 5' to 3' long-range resection, which is required for single-
CC       strand invasion and recombination. RBBP8/CtIP specifically promotes the
CC       endonuclease activity of MRE11 to clear protein-DNA adducts and
CC       generate clean double-strand break ends. The MRN complex is also
CC       required for DNA damage signaling via activation of the ATM and ATR
CC       kinases: the nuclease activity of MRE11 is not required to activate ATM
CC       and ATR. The MRN complex is also required for the processing of R-loops
CC       (By similarity). The MRN complex is involved in the activation of the
CC       cGAS-STING pathway induced by DNA damage during tumorigenesis: the MRN
CC       complex acts by displacing CGAS from nucleosome sequestration, thereby
CC       activating it (By similarity). In telomeres the MRN complex may
CC       modulate t-loop formation (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P49959, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q61216}.

CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: Interaction with SAMHD1 stimulates the double-
CC       strand-specific 3'-5' exonuclease activity. RBBP8/CtIP specifically
CC       promotes the endonuclease activity to clear protein-DNA adducts and
CC       generate clean double-strand break ends. DYNLL1-binding inhibits the
CC       activity of MRE11. MRE11 activity is inhibited by C1QBP: in absence of
CC       DNA damage, C1QBP interacts with unphosphorylated MRE11, preventing
CC       formation and activity of the MRN complex.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P49959}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Component of the MRN complex composed of two heterodimers
CC       RAD50 and MRE11 associated with a single NBN. The MRN complexes
CC       dimerize on DNA to form joined MRN-MRN oligomers required for DNA
CC       double-strand break repair. As part of the MRN complex, interacts with
CC       MCM9; the interaction recruits the complex to DNA repair sites.
CC       Component of the BASC complex, at least composed of BRCA1, MSH2, MSH6,
CC       MLH1, ATM, BLM, RAD50, MRE11 and NBN. Found in a complex with TERF2.
CC       Interacts with DCLRE1C/Artemis and DCLRE1B/Apollo. Interacts with ATF2.
CC       Interacts with EXD2. Interacts with MRNIP. Interacts with SAMHD1;
CC       leading to stimulate 3'-5' exonuclease activity. Interacts (when
CC       ubiquitinated) with UBQLN4 (via its UBA domain) (By similarity).
CC       Interacts with CYREN (via XLF motif) (By similarity). Interacts with
CC       GFI1; promoting methylation by PRMT1. Interacts with DYNLL1; inhibiting
CC       the activity of MRE11. Interacts with C1QBP and RAD50; interaction
CC       takes place in absence of DNA damage to form the MRC (MRE11-RAD50-
CC       C1QBP) complex that inhibits the activity of MRE11 (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P49959, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q61216}.

CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P49959}.
CC   -!- PTM: SUMOylated by PIAS1, stabilizing MRE11 on chromatin during end

CC   -!- PTM: Ufmylation at Lys-282 promotes MRE11 activity and is required for
CC       activation of the ATM and ATR kinases by the MRN complex.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P49959}.
