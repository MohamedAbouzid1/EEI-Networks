CC   -!- FUNCTION: Lipid phosphatase that specifically dephosphorylates the D-3
CC       position of phosphatidylinositol 3-phosphate and phosphatidylinositol
CC       3,5-bisphosphate, generating phosphatidylinositol and
CC       phosphatidylinositol 5-phosphate. Binds with high affinity to
CC       phosphatidylinositol 3,5-bisphosphate (PtdIns(3,5)P2) but also to
CC       phosphatidylinositol 3-phosphate (PtdIns(3)P), phosphatidylinositol 4-
CC       phosphate (PtdIns(4)P), and phosphatidylinositol 5-phosphate
CC       (PtdIns(5)P), phosphatidic acid and phosphatidylserine (By similarity).
CC       Negatively regulates ER-Golgi protein transport (PubMed:23188820).
CC       Probably in association with MTMR9, plays a role in the late stages of
CC       macropinocytosis by dephosphorylating phosphatidylinositol 3-phosphate
CC       in membrane ruffles. Acts as a negative regulator of KCNN4/KCa3.1
CC       channel activity in CD4(+) T-cells possibly by decreasing intracellular
CC       levels of phosphatidylinositol 3-phosphate. Negatively regulates
CC       proliferation of reactivated CD4(+) T-cells. In complex with MTMR9,
CC       negatively regulates DNA damage-induced apoptosis. The formation of the
CC       MTMR6-MTMR9 complex stabilizes both MTMR6 and MTMR9 protein levels (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Y217,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:23188820}.
