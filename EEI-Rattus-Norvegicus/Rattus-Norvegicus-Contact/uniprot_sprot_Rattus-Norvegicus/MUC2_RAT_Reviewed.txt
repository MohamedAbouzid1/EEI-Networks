CC   -!- FUNCTION: Coats the epithelia of the intestines and other mucus
CC       membrane-containing organs to provide a protective, lubricating barrier
CC       against particles and infectious agents at mucosal surfaces. Major
CC       constituent of the colon mucus, which is mainly formed by large
CC       polymeric networks of MUC2 secreted by goblet cells that cover the
CC       exposed surfaces of intestine. MUC2 networks form hydrogels that guard
CC       the underlying epithelium from pathogens and other hazardous matter
CC       entering from the outside world, while permitting nutrient absorption
CC       and gas exchange. Acts as a divalent copper chaperone that protects
CC       intestinal cells from copper toxicity and facilitates nutritional
CC       copper unptake into cells. Binds both Cu(2+) and its reduced form,
CC       Cu(1+), at two juxtaposed binding sites: Cu(2+), once reduced to Cu(1+)
CC       by vitamin C (ascorbate) or other dietary antioxidants, transits to the
CC       other binding site. MUC2-bound Cu(1+) is protected from oxidation in
CC       aerobic environments, and can be released for nutritional delivery to
CC       cells. Mucin gels store antimicrobial molecules that participate in
CC       innate immunity. Mucin glycoproteins also house and feed the
CC       microbiome, lubricate tissue surfaces, and may facilitate the removal
CC       of contaminants and waste products from the body (By similarity).
CC       Goblet cells synthesize two forms of MUC2 mucin that differ in branched
CC       chain O-glycosylation and the site of production in the colon: a (1)
CC       'thick' mucus that wraps the microbiota to form fecal pellets is
CC       produced in the proximal, ascending colon. 'Thick' mucus transits along
CC       the descending colon and is lubricated by a (2) 'thin' MUC2 mucus
CC       produced in the distal colon which adheres to the 'thick' mucus (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q02817,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q80Z19}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homomultimer; disulfide-linked. The N- and C-terminus mediate
CC       their assembly into higher order structures to form filaments (By
CC       similarity). The CTCK domains of two polypeptides associate in the
CC       endoplasmic reticulum to generate intermolecularly disulfide-bonded
CC       dimers (By similarity). These dimers progress to the Golgi apparatus,
CC       which is a more acidic environment than the endoplasmic reticulum.
CC       Under acidic conditions, the N-termini form non-covalent intermolecular
CC       interactions that juxtapose assemblies of the third VWD domain (VWD3)
CC       from different CTCK-linked dimers. The VWD3 assemblies then become
CC       disulfide bonded to one another to produce long, disulfide-linked
CC       polymers that remain highly compact until secretion. Interacts with
CC       FCGBP. Interacts with AGR2; disulfide-linked (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q02817, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9HC84}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Secreted {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q02817}. Note=In
CC       the intestine, secreted into the inner and outer mucus layers (By
CC       similarity). Before secretion, mucin polymers are stored in dedicated
CC       secretory vesicles (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q02817,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q80Z19}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed in intestine and airway.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:8027037}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The CTCK domain mediates interchain disulfide bonds with
CC       another molecule of MUC2. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9HC84}.
CC   -!- PTM: O-glycosylated. O-glycosylation is required for mucin assembly (By
CC       similarity). Goblet cells synthesize two forms of mucin that differ in
CC       branched chain O-glycosylation and the site of production in the colon
CC       (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q02817,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q80Z19}.
CC   -!- PTM: May undergo proteolytic cleavage in the outer mucus layer of the
CC       colon, contributing to the expanded volume and loose nature of this
CC       layer which allows for bacterial colonization in contrast to the inner
CC       mucus layer which is dense and devoid of bacteria.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q80Z19}.
CC   -!- PTM: At low pH of 6 and under, undergoes autocatalytic cleavage in
CC       vitro in the N-terminal region of the fourth VWD domain. It is likely
CC       that this also occurs in vivo and is triggered by the low pH of the
CC       late secretory pathway. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q02817}.
