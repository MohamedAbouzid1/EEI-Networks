CC   -!- FUNCTION: Myosins are actin-based motor molecules with ATPase activity.
CC       Unconventional myosins serve in intracellular movements. Their highly
CC       divergent tails are presumed to bind to membranous compartments, which
CC       would be moved relative to actin filaments. Involved in glucose
CC       transporter recycling in response to insulin by regulating movement of
CC       intracellular GLUT4-containing vesicles to the plasma membrane.
CC       Component of the hair cell's (the sensory cells of the inner ear)
CC       adaptation-motor complex. Acts as a mediator of adaptation of
CC       mechanoelectrical transduction in stereocilia of vestibular hair cells.
CC       Binds phosphoinositides and links the actin cytoskeleton to cellular
CC       membranes (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9WTI7,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:22918957}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts (via its IQ motifs) with CABP1 and CIB1; the
CC       interaction with CABP1 and CIB1 is calcium-dependent (By similarity).
CC       Interacts (via tail domain) with PLEKHB1 (via PH domain); the
CC       interaction is not affected by the presence or absence of calcium and
CC       CALM (By similarity). Interacts with POLR1A (By similarity). Interacts
CC       with POLR2A (By similarity). Component of the B-WICH complex, at least
CC       composed of SMARCA5/SNF2H, BAZ1B/WSTF, SF3B1, DEK, MYO1C, ERCC6,
CC       MYBBP1A and DDX21 (By similarity). Interacts (via its IQ motifs) with
CC       CALM; this precludes interaction with YWHAB (By similarity). Interacts
CC       with YWHAB; this precludes interaction with CALM (By similarity).
CC       Interacts with RPS6 (By similarity). Interacts with actin (By
CC       similarity). Interacts with LLPH (By similarity). Interacts with GLUT4
CC       (PubMed:22918957). Interacts (via its IQ motifs) with SH3BGRL3; the
CC       interaction is dependent on calcium and takes place at membrane ruffles
CC       (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O00159,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9WTI7, ECO:0000269|PubMed:22918957}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O00159}. Nucleus
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O00159}. Cytoplasm, cell cortex
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9WTI7}. Cell projection, stereocilium membrane
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q92002}. Cytoplasmic vesicle
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:22918957}. Cell projection, ruffle membrane
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:22918957}. Note=Colocalizes with CABP1 and CIB1 at
CC       cell margin, membrane ruffles and punctate regions on the cell membrane
CC       (By similarity). Colocalizes in adipocytes with GLUT4 at actin-based
CC       membranes (By similarity). Colocalizes with GLUT4 at insulin-induced
CC       ruffles at the cell membrane (PubMed:22918957). Localizes transiently
CC       at cell membrane to region known to be enriched in PIP2 (By
CC       similarity). Activation of phospholipase C results in its
CC       redistribution to the cytoplasm (By similarity). Colocalizes with RNA
CC       polymerase II (By similarity). Translocates to nuclear speckles upon
CC       exposure to inhibitors of RNA polymerase II transcription (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O00159,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9WTI7, ECO:0000269|PubMed:22918957}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: Binds directly to large unilamellar vesicles (LUVs) containing
CC       phosphatidylinositol 4,5-bisphosphate (PIP2) or inositol 1,4,5-
CC       trisphosphate (InsP3). The PIP2-binding site corresponds to a putative
CC       PH domain present in its tail domain. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9WTI7}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the TRAFAC class myosin-kinesin ATPase
CC       superfamily. Myosin family. {ECO:0000305}.

CC       be confused with the conventional myosin-1 (MYH1). {ECO:0000305}.
