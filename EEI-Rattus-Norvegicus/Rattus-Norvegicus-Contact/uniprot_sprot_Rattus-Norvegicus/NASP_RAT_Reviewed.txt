CC   -!- FUNCTION: Component of the histone chaperone network (By similarity).
CC       Binds and stabilizes histone H3-H4 not bound to chromatin to maintain a
CC       soluble reservoir and modulate degradation by chaperone-mediated
CC       autophagy (By similarity). Required for DNA replication, normal cell
CC       cycle progression and cell proliferation. Forms a cytoplasmic complex
CC       with HSP90 and H1 linker histones and stimulates HSP90 ATPase activity.
CC       NASP and H1 histone are subsequently released from the complex and
CC       translocate to the nucleus where the histone is released for binding to
CC       DNA. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P49321, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q99MD9}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Binds to linker H1 histones (By similarity). Interacts with
CC       histones H2A, H2B, H3 and H4 (By similarity). Interacts with histone
CC       H3.3 (By similarity). Interacts with histones H3 and H4; NASP is a
CC       histone chaperone that stabilizes and maintains a soluble pool of
CC       istone H3-H4 dimers (By similarity). Interacts with ASF1A and ASF1B;
CC       the interaction is probably indirect and mediated by H3-H4 (By
CC       similarity). Also binds to HSP90 in the cytoplasm. This interaction
CC       stimulates binding of NASP to H1-6/H1T (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P49321, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q99MD9}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q99MD9}. Nucleus
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q99MD9}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the NASP family. {ECO:0000305}.
