CC   -!- FUNCTION: Component of the MRN complex, which plays a central role in
CC       double-strand break (DSB) repair, DNA recombination, maintenance of
CC       telomere integrity and meiosis. The MRN complex is involved in the
CC       repair of DNA double-strand breaks (DSBs) via homologous recombination
CC       (HR), an error-free mechanism which primarily occurs during S and G2
CC       phases. The complex (1) mediates the end resection of damaged DNA,
CC       which generates proper single-stranded DNA, a key initial steps in HR,
CC       and is (2) required for the recruitment of other repair factors and
CC       efficient activation of ATM and ATR upon DNA damage. The MRN complex
CC       possesses single-strand endonuclease activity and double-strand-
CC       specific 3'-5' exonuclease activity, which are provided by MRE11, to
CC       initiate end resection, which is required for single-strand invasion
CC       and recombination. Within the MRN complex, NBN acts as a protein-
CC       protein adapter, which specifically recognizes and binds phosphorylated
CC       proteins, promoting their recruitment to DNA damage sites. Recruits
CC       MRE11 and RAD50 components of the MRN complex to DSBs in response to
CC       DNA damage. Promotes the recruitment of PI3/PI4-kinase family members
CC       ATM, ATR, and probably DNA-PKcs to the DNA damage sites, activating
CC       their functions. Mediates the recruitment of phosphorylated RBBP8/CtIP
CC       to DSBs, leading to cooperation between the MRN complex and RBBP8/CtIP
CC       to initiate end resection. RBBP8/CtIP specifically promotes the
CC       endonuclease activity of the MRN complex to clear DNA ends containing
CC       protein adducts. The MRN complex is also required for the processing of
CC       R-loops. NBN also functions in telomere length maintenance via its
CC       interaction with TERF2: interaction with TERF2 during G1 phase
CC       preventing recruitment of DCLRE1B/Apollo to telomeres. NBN also
CC       promotes DNA repair choice at dysfunctional telomeres: NBN
CC       phosphorylation by CK2 promotes non-homologous end joining repair at
CC       telomeres, while unphosphorylated NBN promotes microhomology-mediated
CC       end-joining (MMEJ) repair. Enhances AKT1 phosphorylation possibly by
CC       association with the mTORC2 complex. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O60934}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Component of the MRN complex composed of two heterodimers
CC       RAD50 and MRE11 associated with a single NBN. The MRN complexes
CC       dimerize on DNA to form joined MRN-MRN oligomers required for DNA
CC       double-strand break repair. The MRN complexes dimerize on DNA to form
CC       joined MRN-MRN oligomers required for DNA double-strand break repair.
CC       As part of the MRN complex, interacts with MCM9; the interaction
CC       recruits the complex to DNA repair sites. Component of the BASC
CC       complex, at least composed of BRCA1, MSH2, MSH6, MLH1, ATM, BLM, RAD50,
CC       MRE11 and NBN. Interacts with histone H2AX; this requires
CC       phosphorylation of H2AX on 'Ser-139' and promotes NBN recruitment to
CC       DNA damage sites. Interacts with (phosphorylated) MDC1; promoting NBN
CC       recruitment to DNA damage sites. Interacts with (phosphorylated) RAD17;
CC       promoting NBN recruitment to DNA damage sites. Interacts (via FxF/Y
CC       motif) with ATM. Interacts with HJURP. Interacts with INTS3. Interacts
CC       with KPNA2. Interacts with TERF2; interaction is disrupted upon NBN
CC       phosphorylation by CDK2. Interacts with (phosphorylated) RBBP8/CtIP;
CC       the interaction links the role of the MRN complex in DNA double-strand
CC       break sensing to resection. Interacts with SP100; recruits NBN to PML
CC       bodies. Interacts with ATF2. Interacts with MTOR, MAPKAP1 isoform 2 and
CC       RICTOR; indicative for an association with the mTORC2 complex.
CC       Interacts with MRNIP. Interacts with UFL1; promoting UFL1 recruitment
CC       to double-strand breaks following DNA damage (By similarity). Interacts
CC       with CYREN (via XLF motif) (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O60934, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9R207}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Nucleus {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O60934}.
CC       Chromosome {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O60934}. Nucleus, PML body
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O60934}. Chromosome, telomere
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O60934}. Note=Localizes to discrete nuclear foci
CC       after treatment with genotoxic agents. Localizes to DNA double-strand
CC       breaks (DSBs); recruited to DNA damage sites via association with
CC       phosphorylated proteins, such as phosphorylated H2AX, phosphorylated
CC       MDC1 and phosphorylated RAD17. Acetylation of 'Lys-5' of histone H2AX
CC       (H2AXK5ac) promotes NBN/NBS1 assembly at the sites of DNA damage.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O60934}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Present at approximately equal levels in the heart
CC       at fetal day 17, at relatively constant levels at postnatal days 10, 17
CC       and 21 and at slightly lower levels in the adult heart. Barely
CC       detectable in the brain. Not detected in kidney, very low levels in
CC       liver and skeletal muscle and moderate levels in heart, lung and brain
CC       (at protein level). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10908350}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The FHA and BRCT domains specifically recognize and bind
CC       phosphorylated proteins. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O60934}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The C-terminal domain contains a MRE11-binding site, and this
CC       interaction is required for the nuclear localization of the MRN
CC       complex. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O60934}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The FxF/Y motif (also named EEXXXDDL motif) is required for the
CC       interaction with ATM and its recruitment to sites of DNA damage and
CC       promote the phosphorylation of ATM substrates, leading to the events of
CC       DNA damage response. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O60934}.
CC   -!- PTM: Phosphorylated by ATM in response of ionizing radiation, and such
CC       phosphorylation is responsible intra-S phase checkpoint control and
CC       telomere maintenance. Phosphorylated at Ser-433 by CDK2 in S/G2 phases
CC       abolishes interaction with TERF2, enabling DCLRE1B/Apollo recruitment
CC       to telomeres. Phosphorylation at Ser-433 in response to dysfunctional
CC       telomeres promotes non-homologous end joining repair at telomeres,
CC       while dephosphorylation by PPP1CA promotes microhomology-mediated end-
CC       joining (MMEJ) repair. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O60934}.
CC   -!- PTM: Ubiquitinated at Lys-436 via 'Lys-6'-linked ubiquitin chains by
CC       RNF8, promoting NBN recruitment to DNA double-strand breaks (DSBs).
CC       Ubiquitinated at Lys-687 via 'Lys-63'-linked ubiquitin chains by PELI1:
CC       ubiquitination takes place following PELI1 phosphorylation and promotes
CC       ATM activation and DNA repair. Ubiquitinated at Lys-732 via 'Lys-63'-
CC       linked ubiquitin chains by the SCF(SKP2) complex: ubiquitination takes
CC       place following SKP2 phosphorylation and promotes ATM activation and
CC       DNA repair. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O60934}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the Nibrin family. {ECO:0000305}.
