CC   -!- FUNCTION: Mitochondrial sodium/calcium antiporter that mediates sodium-
CC       dependent calcium efflux from mitochondrion, by mediating the exchange
CC       of 3 sodium ions per 1 calcium ion. Plays a central role in
CC       mitochondrial calcium homeostasis by mediating mitochondrial calcium
CC       extrusion: calcium efflux is essential for mitochondrial function and
CC       cell survival, notably in cardiomyocytes (By similarity). Regulates
CC       rates of glucose-dependent insulin secretion in pancreatic beta-cells
CC       during the first phase of insulin secretion: acts by mediating efflux
CC       of calcium from mitochondrion, thereby affecting cytoplasmic calcium
CC       responses. Required for store-operated Ca(2+) entry (SOCE) and Ca(2+)
CC       release-activated Ca(2+) (CRAC) channel regulation: sodium transport by
CC       SLC8B1 leads to promote calcium-shuttling that modulates mitochondrial
CC       redox status, thereby regulating SOCE activity (By similarity).
CC       Involved in B-lymphocyte chemotaxis (By similarity). Able to transport
CC       Ca(2+) in exchange of either Li(+) or Na(+), explaining how Li(+)
CC       catalyzes Ca(2+) exchange. In contrast to other members of the family
CC       its function is independent of K(+) (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q6J4K2, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q925Q3}.
