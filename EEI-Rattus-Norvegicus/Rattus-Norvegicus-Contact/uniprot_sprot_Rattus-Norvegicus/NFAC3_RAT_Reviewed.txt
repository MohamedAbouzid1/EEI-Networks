CC   -!- FUNCTION: Acts as a regulator of transcriptional activation. Binds to
CC       the TNFSF11/RANKL promoter region and promotes TNFSF11 transcription
CC       (By similarity). Binding to the TNFSF11 promoter region is increased by
CC       high levels of Ca(2+) which induce NFATC3 expression and may lead to
CC       regulation of TNFSF11 expression in osteoblasts (By similarity). Plays
CC       a role in promoting mesenteric arterial wall remodeling in response to
CC       the intermittent hypoxia-induced increase in EDN1 and ROCK signaling
CC       (By similarity). As a result NFATC3 colocalizes with F-actin filaments,
CC       translocates to the nucleus and promotes transcription of the smooth
CC       muscle hypertrophy and differentiation marker ACTA2 (By similarity).
CC       Promotes lipopolysaccharide-induced apoptosis and hypertrophy in
CC       cardiomyocytes (PubMed:30980393). Following JAK/STAT signaling
CC       activation and as part of a complex with NFATC4 and STAT3, binds to the
CC       alpha-beta E4 promoter region of CRYAB and activates transcription in
CC       cardiomyocytes (PubMed:19538478). In conjunction with NFATC4, involved
CC       in embryonic heart development via maintenance of cardiomyocyte
CC       survival, proliferation and differentiation (By similarity). Plays a
CC       role in the inducible expression of cytokine genes in T-cells,
CC       especially in the induction of the IL-2 (By similarity). Required for
CC       thymocyte maturation during DN3 to DN4 transition and during positive
CC       selection (By similarity). Positively regulates macrophage-derived
CC       polymicrobial clearance, via binding to the promoter region and
CC       promoting transcription of NOS2 resulting in subsequent generation of
CC       nitric oxide (By similarity). Involved in Ca(2+)-mediated
CC       transcriptional responses upon Ca(2+) influx via ORAI1 CRAC channels.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P97305, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q12968,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19538478, ECO:0000269|PubMed:30980393}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: NFATC proteins bind to DNA as monomers (By similarity). Member
CC       of the multicomponent NFATC transcription complex that consists of at
CC       least two components, a pre-existing cytoplasmic component NFATC2 and
CC       an inducible nuclear component NFATC1 (By similarity). Other members
CC       such as NFATC4, or members of the activating protein-1 family, MAF,
CC       GATA4 and Cbp/p300 can also bind the complex (By similarity). Component
CC       of a promoter-binding complex composed of STAT3, NFATC3 and NFATC4;
CC       complex formation is enhanced by calcineurin (By similarity). Interacts
CC       with TRIM17; this interaction prevents NFATC3 nuclear localization (By
CC       similarity). Interacts with and ubiquitinated by STUB1/CHIP;
CC       HSPA1A/HSP70 is required as a co-chaperone (PubMed:30980393).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P97305, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q12968,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:30980393}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19538478,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:30980393}. Nucleus {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19538478,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:30980393}. Note=The subcellular localization of
CC       NFATC plays a key role in the regulation of gene transcription (By
CC       similarity). Rapid nuclear exit of NFATC is thought to be one mechanism
CC       by which cells distinguish between sustained and transient calcium
CC       signals (By similarity). Cytoplasmic when phosphorylated and nuclear
CC       after activation, that is controlled by calcineurin-mediated
CC       dephosphorylation (By similarity). Translocation to the nucleus is
CC       increased in the presence of calcium in pre-osteoblasts (By
CC       similarity). Translocates to the nucleus in the presence of EDN1
CC       following colocalization with F-actin filaments, translocation is ROCK-
CC       dependent (PubMed:19538478). Translocates to the nucleus in response to
CC       lipopolysaccharide treatment of macrophages (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P97305, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19538478}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed in cardiomyocytes (at protein level).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19538478}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Induced by lipopolysaccharides.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:30980393}.
