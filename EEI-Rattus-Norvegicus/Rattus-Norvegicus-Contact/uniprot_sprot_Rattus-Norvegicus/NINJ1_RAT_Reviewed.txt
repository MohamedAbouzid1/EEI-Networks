CC   -!- FUNCTION: [Ninjurin-1]: Effector of various programmed cell death, such
CC       as pyroptosis and necroptosis, which mediates plasma membrane rupture
CC       (cytolysis) (By similarity). Oligomerizes in response to death stimuli
CC       and forms ring-like structures on the plasma membrane: acts by cutting
CC       and shedding membrane disks, like a cookie cutter, leading to membrane
CC       damage and loss that cannot be repaired by the cell (By similarity).
CC       Plasma membrane rupture leads to release intracellular molecules named
CC       damage-associated molecular patterns (DAMPs) that propagate the
CC       inflammatory response (By similarity). Mechanistically, mediates plasma
CC       membrane rupture by introducing hydrophilic faces of 2 alpha helices
CC       into the hydrophobic membrane (By similarity). Induces plasma membrane
CC       rupture downstream of Gasdermin (GSDMA, GSDMB, GSDMC, GSDMD, or GSDME)
CC       or MLKL during pyroptosis or necroptosis, respectively (By similarity).
CC       Acts as an effector of PANoptosis downstream of CASP1, CASP4, CASP8 and
CC       RIPK3 (By similarity). Also induces plasma membrane rupture in response
CC       to cell swelling caused by osmotic stress and ferroptosis downstream of
CC       lipid peroxidation (By similarity). Acts as a regulator of Toll-like
CC       receptor 4 (TLR4) signaling triggered by lipopolysaccharide (LPS)
CC       during systemic inflammation; directly binds LPS (By similarity).
CC       Involved in leukocyte migration during inflammation by promoting
CC       transendothelial migration of macrophages via homotypic binding (By
CC       similarity). Promotes the migration of monocytes across the brain
CC       endothelium to central nervous system inflammatory lesions (By
CC       similarity). Also acts as a homophilic transmembrane adhesion molecule
CC       involved in various processes such as axonal growth, cell chemotaxis
CC       and angiogenesis (By similarity). Promotes cell adhesion by mediating
CC       homophilic interactions via its extracellular N-terminal adhesion motif
CC       (N-NAM) (PubMed:19595672). Involved in the progression of the
CC       inflammatory stress by promoting cell-to-cell interactions between
CC       immune cells and endothelial cells (By similarity). Plays a role in
CC       nerve regeneration by promoting maturation of Schwann cells (By
CC       similarity). Acts as a regulator of angiogenesis (PubMed:33028854).
CC       Promotes the formation of new vessels by mediating the interaction
CC       between capillary pericyte cells and endothelial cells (By similarity).
CC       Promotes osteoclasts development by enhancing the survival of prefusion
CC       osteoclasts (By similarity). Also involved in striated muscle growth
CC       and differentiation (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O70131,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q92982, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19595672,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:33028854}.
CC   -!- FUNCTION: [Secreted ninjurin-1]: Secreted form generated by cleavage,
CC       which has chemotactic activity. Acts as an anti-inflammatory mediator
CC       by promoting monocyte recruitment, thereby ameliorating
CC       atherosclerosis. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O70131}.
CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: [Ninjurin-1]: In normal conditions, NINJ1 is
CC       inactivated. In response to death stimuli, homooligomerizes and
CC       disrupts membrane integrity by introducing the hydrophilic faces of
CC       alpha1 and alpha2 helices into the hydrophobic membrane (By
CC       similarity). Homooligomerization and ability to mediate plasma membrane
CC       rupture is inhibited by glycine; it is unclear whether glycine directly
CC       or indirectly inhibits homooligomerization (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q92982}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: [Ninjurin-1]: Homooligomer; in response to death stimuli,
CC       homooligomerizes into long, highly branched filaments and large, ring-
CC       shaped structures in the membrane. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q92982}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: [Ninjurin-1]: Cell membrane
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q92982}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q92982}. Synaptic cell membrane
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O70131}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000255}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: [Secreted ninjurin-1]: Secreted
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q92982}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: By nerve injury. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:8780658}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: [Ninjurin-1]: Composed of 4 alpha helices: 2 hydrophobic
CC       transmembrane regions (alpha3 and alpha4) and 2 alpha helices (alpha1
CC       and alpha2). Alpha1 and alpha2 feature one hydrophobic side and a
CC       hydrophilic side. In normal conditions, NINJ1 is inactivated and alpha1
CC       and alpha2 helices are not inserted into the membrane. Following NINJ1
CC       activation, alpha1 and alpha2 helices insert into the membrane and
CC       drive NINJ1 oligomerization via interactions between alpha3 and alpha4
CC       and the hydrophobic face of alpha1 from an adjacent subunit. Such
CC       structures disrupt membrane integrity and form a lesion through the
CC       introduction of the hydrophilic faces of alpha1 and alpha2 into the
CC       hydrophobic membrane. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q92982}.
CC   -!- PTM: [Ninjurin-1]: Cleaved by MMP9 protease to generate the Secreted
CC       ninjurin-1 form. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O70131}.
CC   -!- PTM: [Ninjurin-1]: N-linked glycosylation is required for
CC       homooligomerization. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O70131}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the ninjurin family. {ECO:0000305}.
