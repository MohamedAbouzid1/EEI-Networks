CC   -!- FUNCTION: Plays a critical role in the sodium-dependent reabsorption of
CC       bile acids from the lumen of the small intestine (PubMed:7860756).
CC       Transports various bile acids, unconjugated or conjugated, such as
CC       cholate and taurocholate. Also responsible for bile acid transport in
CC       the renal proximal tubules, a salvage mechanism that helps conserve
CC       bile acids. Works collaboratively with the Na(+)-taurocholate
CC       cotransporting polypeptide (NTCP), the organic solute transporter
CC       (OST), and the bile salt export pump (BSEP), to ensure efficacious
CC       biological recycling of bile acids during enterohepatic circulation (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q12908,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:7860756}.
