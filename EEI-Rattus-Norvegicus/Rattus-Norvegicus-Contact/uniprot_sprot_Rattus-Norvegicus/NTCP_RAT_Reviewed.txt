CC   -!- FUNCTION: As a major transporter of conjugated bile salts from plasma
CC       into the hepatocyte, it plays a key role in the enterohepatic
CC       circulation of bile salts necessary for the solubilization and
CC       absorption of dietary fat and fat-soluble vitamins (PubMed:1961729). It
CC       is strictly dependent on the extracellular presence of sodium
CC       (PubMed:1961729). It exhibits broad substrate specificity and
CC       transports various bile acids, such as taurocholate, cholate, as well
CC       as non-bile acid organic compounds, such as estrone sulfate
CC       (PubMed:9486191). Works collaboratively with the ileal transporter
CC       (NTCP2), the organic solute transporter (OST), and the bile salt export
CC       pump (BSEP), to ensure efficacious biological recycling of bile acids
CC       during enterohepatic circulation (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q14973, ECO:0000269|PubMed:1961729,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9486191}.
