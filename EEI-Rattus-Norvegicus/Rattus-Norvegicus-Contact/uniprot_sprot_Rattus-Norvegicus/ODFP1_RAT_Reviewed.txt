CC   -!- FUNCTION: Component of the outer dense fibers (ODF) of spermatozoa. ODF
CC       are filamentous structures located on the outside of the axoneme in the
CC       midpiece and principal piece of the mammalian sperm tail and may help
CC       to maintain the passive elastic structures and elastic recoil of the
CC       sperm tail.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts (via leucine zipper motif) with TCP11 (By
CC       similarity). Interacts with SPAG4 (PubMed:10373309). Interacts with
CC       KLC3 (PubMed:12594206). Interacts with CCDC42 (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q14990, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q61999,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:10373309, ECO:0000269|PubMed:12594206}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell projection, cilium, flagellum
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q61999}. Cytoplasm, cytoskeleton
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q61999}. Cytoplasm, cytoskeleton, microtubule
CC       organizing center, centrosome {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q61999}.
CC       Note=Localizes to the manchette in elongating spermatids and to the
CC       sperm flagellum. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q61999}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Testis. Specifically located to the round spermatid
CC       layer and to the luminally-oriented cytoplasm of elongated spermatids.
CC   -!- DEVELOPMENTAL STAGE: First detected in 30-day old rats after which,
CC       levels increase during spermatid elongation. Levels decrease at the
CC       time of spermatid assembly and disappear just before spermiation.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The C-terminal contains many C-X-P repeats.
