CC   -!- FUNCTION: Metalloprotease that is part of the quality control system in
CC       the inner membrane of mitochondria (PubMed:30926535). Activated in
CC       response to various mitochondrial stress, leading to the proteolytic
CC       cleavage of target proteins, such as OPA1, UQCC3 and DELE1
CC       (PubMed:30926535). Involved in the fusion of the mitochondrial inner
CC       membranes by mediating cleavage of OPA1 at S1 position, generating the
CC       soluble OPA1 (S-OPA1), which cooperates with the membrane form (L-OPA1)
CC       to coordinate the fusion of mitochondrial inner membranes (By
CC       similarity). Following stress conditions that induce loss of
CC       mitochondrial membrane potential, mediates cleavage of OPA1, leading to
CC       excess production of soluble OPA1 (S-OPA1) and negative regulation of
CC       mitochondrial fusion (By similarity). Involved in mitochondrial
CC       safeguard in response to transient mitochondrial membrane
CC       depolarization (flickering) by catalyzing cleavage of OPA1, leading to
CC       excess production of S-OPA1, preventing mitochondrial hyperfusion (By
CC       similarity). Also acts as a regulator of apoptosis: upon BAK and BAX
CC       aggregation, mediates cleavage of OPA1, leading to the remodeling of
CC       mitochondrial cristae and allowing the release of cytochrome c from
CC       mitochondrial cristae (By similarity). In depolarized mitochondria, may
CC       also act as a backup protease for PINK1 by mediating PINK1 cleavage and
CC       promoting its subsequent degradation by the proteasome (By similarity).
CC       May also cleave UQCC3 in response to mitochondrial depolarization (By
CC       similarity). Also acts as an activator of the integrated stress
CC       response (ISR): in response to mitochondrial stress, mediates cleavage
CC       of DELE1 to generate the processed form of DELE1 (S-DELE1), which
CC       translocates to the cytosol and activates EIF2AK1/HRI to trigger the
CC       ISR (By similarity). Its role in mitochondrial quality control is
CC       essential for regulating lipid metabolism as well as to maintain body
CC       temperature and energy expenditure under cold-stress conditions (By
CC       similarity). Binds cardiolipin, possibly regulating its protein
CC       turnover (By similarity). Required for the stability of the respiratory
CC       supercomplexes (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q96E52,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9D8H7, ECO:0000269|PubMed:30926535}.

CC       maturation before OMA1 autocatalytic cleavage (By similarity). Degraded
CC       by YMEL1 in response to membrane depolarization (By similarity).

CC       promotes degradation of OMA1 in a cardiolipin-binding manner (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q96E52,
