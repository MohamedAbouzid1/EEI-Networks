CC   -!- FUNCTION: Pore-forming subunit of two major inward rectifying Ca(2+)
CC       channels at the plasma membrane: Ca(2+) release-activated Ca(2+) (CRAC)
CC       channels and arachidonate-regulated Ca(2+)-selective (ARC) channels (By
CC       similarity). Assembles with ORAI2 and ORAI3 to form hexameric CRAC
CC       channels that mediate Ca(2+) influx upon depletion of endoplasmic
CC       reticulum Ca(2+) store and channel activation by Ca(2+) sensor STIM1, a
CC       process known as store-operated Ca(2+) entry (SOCE). Various pore
CC       subunit combinations may account for distinct CRAC channel
CC       spatiotemporal and cell-type specific dynamics. ORAI1 mainly
CC       contributes to the generation of Ca(2+) plateaus involved in sustained
CC       Ca(2+) entry and is dispensable for cytosolic Ca(2+) oscillations,
CC       whereas ORAI2 and ORAI3 generate oscillatory patterns. CRAC channels
CC       assemble in Ca(2+) signaling microdomains where Ca(2+) influx is
CC       coupled to calmodulin and calcineurin signaling and activation of NFAT
CC       transcription factors recruited to ORAI1 via AKAP5. Activates
CC       NFATC2/NFAT1 and NFATC3/NFAT4-mediated transcriptional responses. CRAC
CC       channels are the main pathway for Ca(2+) influx in T cells and promote
CC       the immune response to pathogens by activating NFAT-dependent cytokine
CC       and chemokine transcription (By similarity). Assembles with ORAI3 to
CC       form channels that mediate store-independent Ca(2+) influx in response
CC       to inflammatory metabolites arachidonate or its derivative leukotriene
CC       C4, termed ARC and LRC channels respectively (By similarity). Plays a
CC       prominent role in Ca(2+) influx at the basolateral membrane of mammary
CC       epithelial cells independently of the Ca(2+) content of endoplasmic
CC       reticulum or Golgi stores. May mediate transepithelial transport of
CC       large quantities of Ca(2+) for milk secretion (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8BWG9, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q96D31}.

CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Oligomerizes in homomeric and heteromeric ORAI complexes.
CC       Native CRAC channels most likely consist of hexameric ORAI heteromers,
CC       implying that diverse ORAI1, ORAI2 and ORAI3 subunit combinations with
CC       distinct biophysical properties can operate in a cell-type specific
CC       way. ARC channels are heteropentamers consisting of three ORAI1 and two
CC       ORAI3 subunits. Interacts with STIM1 and STIM2; this regulates channel
CC       activity. Interacts with CALM; this may displace STIM1 and STIM2 and
CC       might thereby modulate channel activity. Interacts (via N-terminus)
CC       with AKAP5 upon store depletion. Interacts with CRACR2A/EFCAB4B; the
CC       interaction is direct and takes place in absence of Ca(2+). Forms a
CC       complex with CRACR2A/EFCAB4B and STIM1 at low concentration of Ca(2+),
CC       the complex dissociates at elevated Ca(2+) concentrations. Interacts
CC       with ASPH (isoform 8). Interacts with SLC35G1. Interacts with UBQLN1.
CC       Interacts with ADCY8; interaction is calcium store depletion
CC       independent; interaction occurs in membrane raft; interaction increases
CC       markedly after store depletion; positively regulates SOCE-induced
CC       adenylate cyclase activity; contributes to the targeting of ADCY8 to
CC       discrete regions of the plasma membrane that are shielded from other
CC       calcium events. Interacts with EFHB; the interaction takes place upon
CC       Ca(2+)-store depletion. Interacts (via N- and C-termini) with ATP2C2
CC       (via N-terminus); this interaction regulates Ca(2+) influx at the
CC       plasma membrane. Interacts with TSPAN18; this interaction regulates
CC       ORAI1 exit from the endoplasmic (ER), and/or Golgi, and trafficking to
CC       the cell surface. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q96D31}.
