CC   -!- FUNCTION: Pore-forming subunit of two major inward rectifying Ca(2+)
CC       channels at the plasma membrane: Ca(2+) release-activated Ca(2+) (CRAC)
CC       channels and arachidonate-regulated Ca(2+)-selective (ARC) channels.
CC       Assembles with ORAI1 and ORAI2 to form hexameric CRAC channels that
CC       mediate Ca(2+) influx upon depletion of endoplasmic reticulum Ca(2+)
CC       store and channel activation by Ca(2+) sensor STIM1, a process known as
CC       store-operated Ca(2+) entry (SOCE). Various pore subunit combinations
CC       may account for distinct CRAC channel spatiotemporal and cell-type
CC       specific dynamics. ORAI1 mainly contributes to the generation of Ca(2+)
CC       plateaus involved in sustained Ca(2+) entry and is dispensable for
CC       cytosolic Ca(2+) oscillations, whereas ORAI2 and ORAI3 generate
CC       oscillatory patterns. CRAC channels assemble in Ca(2+) signaling
CC       microdomains where Ca(2+) influx is coupled to calmodulin and
CC       calcineurin signaling and activation of NFAT transcription factors
CC       recruited to ORAI1 via AKAP5. CRAC channels are the main pathway for
CC       Ca(2+) influx in T cells and promote the immune response to pathogens
CC       by activating NFAT-dependent cytokine and chemokine transcription.
CC       Assembles with ORAI1 to form channels that mediate store-independent
CC       Ca(2+) influx in response to inflammatory metabolites arachidonate or
CC       its derivative leukotriene C4, termed ARC and LRC channels
CC       respectively. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9BRQ5}.

CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: CRAC channels are regulated by fast Ca(2+)-
CC       dependent inactivation (FCDI), a mechanism that limits Ca(2+) influx
CC       and cell toxicity. ORAI3 subunit displays prominent FCDI. Inhibited by
CC       lanthanides such as Gd(3+) ions. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9BRQ5}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Oligomerizes in homomeric and heteromeric ORAI complexes.
CC       Native CRAC channels most likely consist of hexameric ORAI heteromers,
CC       implying that diverse ORAI1, ORAI2 and ORAI3 subunit combinations with
CC       distinct biophysical properties can operate in a cell-type specific
CC       way. ARC channels are heteropentamers consisting of three ORAI1 and two
CC       ORAI3 subunits. Interacts with STIM1; this regulates channel activity.
CC       Interacts with CRACR2A/EFCAB4B. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9BRQ5}.
