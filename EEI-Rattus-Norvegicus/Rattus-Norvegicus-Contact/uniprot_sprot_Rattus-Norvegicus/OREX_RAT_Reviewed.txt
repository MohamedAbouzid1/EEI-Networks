CC   -!- FUNCTION: Neuropeptides that play a significant role in the regulation
CC       of food intake and sleep-wakefulness, possibly by coordinating the
CC       complex behavioral and physiologic responses of these complementary
CC       homeostatic functions (PubMed:11283317, PubMed:11340621,
CC       PubMed:9491897). A broader role in the homeostatic regulation of energy
CC       metabolism, autonomic function, hormonal balance and the regulation of
CC       body fluids, is also suggested (PubMed:9491897). A modulation effect on
CC       luteinizing hormone-releasing hormone (LHRH) secretion also suggests a
CC       more minor contribution to the regulation of reproductive function
CC       (PubMed:11340621, PubMed:9879756). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9491897,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9879756, ECO:0000303|PubMed:11283317,
CC       ECO:0000303|PubMed:11340621}.
CC   -!- FUNCTION: [Orexin-A]: Binds to orexin receptors HCRTR1/OX1R and
CC       HCRTR2/OX2R with a high affinity (PubMed:9491897). Stimulates food
CC       intake (PubMed:9491897). Modulates pituitary luteinizing hormone
CC       secretion in an ovarian steroid-dependent manner (PubMed:9879756).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9491897, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9879756}.
CC   -!- FUNCTION: [Orexin-B]: Binds to orexin receptor HCRTR2/OX2R only
CC       (PubMed:9491897). Stimulates food intake (PubMed:9491897). Modulates
CC       pituitary luteinizing hormone secretion in an ovarian steroid-dependent
CC       manner (PubMed:9879756). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9491897,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9879756}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Rough endoplasmic reticulum
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9419374}. Cytoplasmic vesicle
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9419374}. Synapse {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9419374}.
CC       Note=Associated with perikaryal rough endoplasmic reticulum as well as
CC       cytoplasmic large granular vesicles at synapses.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9419374}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Mainly expressed in the brain, with expression in
CC       neurons in the dorsal lateral hypothalamic area (at protein level)
CC       (PubMed:9419374). Produced by a small group of neurons restricted to
CC       the lateral and posterior hypothalamus and perifornical areas
CC       (PubMed:9491897). Positive neurons project widely throughout the entire
CC       neuroaxis (PubMed:9491897). Particularly abundant projections in the
CC       cerebral cortex, olfactory bulb, hippocampus, amygdala, septum,
CC       diagonal band of Broca, bed nucleus of the stria terminalis, thalamus,
CC       anterior and posterior hypothalamus, midbrain, brainstem, and spinal
CC       cord (PubMed:9491897). Also expressed in the enteric nervous system and
CC       pancreas (PubMed:9491897). In small amount, also detected in the testis
CC       (PubMed:9491897). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9419374,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9491897}.
CC   -!- DEVELOPMENTAL STAGE: Detected in the brain as early as embryonic day
CC       18, but expression increases dramatically after the third postnatal
CC       week. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9419374}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: By nutritional state, up-regulated by fasting, fluid
CC       deprivation and insulin-induced hypoglycemia. Orexin-A immunoreactivity
CC       varies diurnally and peaks during the dark phase, in the pons and the
CC       location of locus coeruleus. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9491897}.
CC   -!- PTM: Specific enzymatic cleavages at paired basic residues yield the
CC       different active peptides. {ECO:0000303|PubMed:9491897}.
