CC   -!- FUNCTION: Proton-selective ion channel. Biphasically modulated by acid
CC       and alkali, mediating proton influx and efflux in response to
CC       extracellular acid and base stimulation, respectively. Sour taste
CC       receptor, which carries inward currents in response to extracellular
CC       acidification. Sensor for ammonium chloride (NH(4)Cl) in taste receptor
CC       cells. NH(4)Cl acts by increasing the intracellular pH, thereby
CC       generating a driving force for proton entry through OTOP1 channel.
CC       Might also participate in alkaline sensation. Plays a role in the
CC       regulation of Ca(2+) flux in response to purigenic (ATP, ADP and UDP)
CC       stimuli, leading to increase in cytosolic Ca(2+) due to influx of
CC       extracellular calcium. May play this role by inhibiting P2Y
CC       purinoceptor-mediated Ca(2+) release in a Ca(2+)-dependent manner and
CC       promote an influx of Ca(2+) in response to ATP. Through this mechanism
CC       and possibly others, plays a role in the formation and function of
CC       calcium carbonate-based structures in the vestibular system of the
CC       inner ear, called otoconia, that sense gravity and linear acceleration.
CC       In obesity, may attenuate adipose tissue inflammation, through the
CC       negative regulation of IFNG signaling, hence may play an adaptive role
CC       in the maintainance of metabolic homeostasis. Following alkali
CC       activation, may also be permeable Na(+), K(+), Cs(+) and Li(+).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q80VM9}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY:

CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: Activated by both acid and alkali, with proton
CC       influx in response to extracellular acid and proton efflux during
CC       alkali stimulation. Inhibited by Zn(2+); this inhibition is thought to
CC       be pH-sensitive. Currents evoked in response to mild acid (pH 6.0)
CC       stimulus may also be mildly potentiated by exposure to Zn(2+).
CC       Activated by NH(4)Cl. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q80VM9}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer (By similarity). Interacts with STAT1, independently
CC       of STAT1 phosphorylation status (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q7ZWK8, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q80VM9}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q80VM9};
CC       Multi-pass membrane protein {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q7ZWK8}. Cell
CC       projection, microvillus {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q80VM9}. Note=Found in
CC       the gelatinous membrane overlying the inner ear macular epithelium.
CC       Also detected in the apical microvilli in inner ear supporting cells.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q80VM9}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: Residues involved in the gating by extracellular Zn (2+) and pH
CC       are located in the extracellular loops between transmembrane domain 5-6
CC       and transmembrane domain 11-12. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q80VM9}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the otopetrin family. {ECO:0000305}.
