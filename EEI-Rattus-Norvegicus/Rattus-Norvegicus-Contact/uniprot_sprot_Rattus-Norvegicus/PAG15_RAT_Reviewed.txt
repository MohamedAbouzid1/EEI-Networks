CC   -!- FUNCTION: Has dual calcium-independent phospholipase and O-
CC       acyltransferase activities with a potential role in glycerophospholipid
CC       homeostasis and remodeling of acyl groups of lipophilic alcohols
CC       present in acidic cellular compartments (PubMed:15294901). Catalyzes
CC       hydrolysis of the ester bond of the fatty acyl group attached at sn-1
CC       or sn-2 position of phospholipids (phospholipase A1 or A2 activity) and
CC       transfer it to the hydroxyl group at the first carbon of lipophilic
CC       alcohols (O-acyltransferase activity) (PubMed:15294901). Among
CC       preferred fatty acyl donors are phosphatidylcholines,
CC       phosphatidylethanolamines, phosphatidylglycerols and
CC       phosphatidylserines. Favors sn-2 over sn-1 deacylation of unsaturated
CC       fatty acyl groups of phosphatidylcholines, phosphatidylethanolamines,
CC       and phosphatidylglycerols (By similarity). Among preferred fatty acyl
CC       acceptors are natural lipophilic alcohols including short-chain
CC       ceramide N-acetyl-sphingosine (C2 ceramide), alkylacylglycerols,
CC       monoacylglycerols, and acylethanolamides such as anandamide and
CC       oleoylethanolamide. Selectively hydrolyzes the sn-1 fatty acyl group of
CC       truncated oxidized phospholipids and may play a role in detoxification
CC       of reactive oxidized phospholipids during oxidative stress (By
CC       similarity). Required for normal phospholipid degradation in alveolar
CC       macrophages with potential implications in the clearance of pulmonary
CC       surfactant, which is mainly composed of dipalmitoylphosphatidylcholine
CC       (1,2-dihexadecanoyl-sn-glycero-3-phosphocholine) (PubMed:15294901).
CC       Involved in the first step of bis(monoacylglycero)phosphate (BMP) de
CC       novo synthesis from phosphatidylglycerol (1,2-diacyl-sn-glycero-3-
CC       phospho-(1'-sn-glycerol), PG) (By similarity). BMP is an important
CC       player in cargo sorting and degradation, regulation of cellular
CC       cholesterol levels and intercellular communication. At neutral pH,
CC       hydrolyzes the sn-1 fatty acyl group of the lysophosphatidylcholines
CC       (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8NCC3,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8VEB4, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15294901}.
