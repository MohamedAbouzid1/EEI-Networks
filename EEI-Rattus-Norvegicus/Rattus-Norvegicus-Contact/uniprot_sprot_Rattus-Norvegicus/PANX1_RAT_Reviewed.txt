CC   -!- FUNCTION: Ion channel involved in a variety of physiological functions
CC       such as blood pressure regulation, apoptotic cell clearance and
CC       oogenesis (By similarity). Forms anion-selective channels with
CC       relatively low conductance and an order of permeabilities:
CC       nitrate>iodide>chlroride>>aspartate=glutamate=gluconate (By
CC       similarity). Can release ATP upon activation through phosphorylation or
CC       cleavage at C-terminus. May play a role as a Ca(2+)-leak channel to
CC       regulate ER Ca(2+) homeostasis (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q96RD7, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9JIP4}.
CC   -!- FUNCTION: [Caspase-activated pannexin-1]: During apoptosis, the C
CC       terminal tail is cleaved by caspases, which opens the main pore acting
CC       as a large-pore ATP efflux channel with a broad distribution, which
CC       allows the regulated release of molecules and ions smaller than 1 kDa,
CC       such as nucleotides ATP and UTP, and selective plasma membrane
CC       permeability to attract phagocytes that engulf the dying cells.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q96RD7}.
