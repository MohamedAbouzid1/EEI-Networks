CC   -!- FUNCTION: Promotes cell proliferation. Modulates apoptotic pathways.
CC       Increases mitogen-activated protein kinase activity and STK26 activity.
CC       Important for cell migration, and for normal structure and assembly of
CC       the Golgi complex. Part of the striatin-interacting phosphatase and
CC       kinase (STRIPAK) complexes. STRIPAK complexes have critical roles in
CC       protein (de)phosphorylation and are regulators of multiple signaling
CC       pathways including Hippo, MAPK, nuclear receptor and cytoskeleton
CC       remodeling. Different types of STRIPAK complexes are involved in a
CC       variety of biological processes such as cell growth, differentiation,
CC       apoptosis, metabolism and immune regulation (By similarity). Important
CC       for KDR/VEGFR2 signaling. Increases the stability of KDR/VEGFR2 and
CC       prevents its breakdown. Required for normal cardiovascular development.
CC       Required for normal angiogenesis, vasculogenesis and hematopoiesis
CC       during embryonic development (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8VE70, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9BUL8}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer. Interacts (via C-terminus) with CCM2. Interacts
CC       (via C-terminus) with PXN. Interacts (via N-terminus) with STK25.
CC       Interacts (via N-terminus) with STK26. Interacts (via N-terminus) with
CC       STK24. Interacts with GOLGA2. Identified in a complex with KRIT1 and
CC       CCM2. Interacts with KDR/VEGFR2. Interaction with KDR/VEGFR2 is
CC       enhanced by stimulation with VEGFA. Interacts with RIPOR1 (via C-
CC       terminus); this interaction is required for the association of RIPOR1
CC       with either STK24 and STK26 kinases and occurs in a Rho-independent
CC       manner. Part of the core of STRIPAK complexes composed of PP2A
CC       catalytic and scaffolding subunits, the striatins (PP2A regulatory
CC       subunits), the striatin-associated proteins MOB4, STRIP1 and STRIP2,
CC       PDCD10 and members of the STE20 kinases, such as STK24 and STK26.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9BUL8}.
