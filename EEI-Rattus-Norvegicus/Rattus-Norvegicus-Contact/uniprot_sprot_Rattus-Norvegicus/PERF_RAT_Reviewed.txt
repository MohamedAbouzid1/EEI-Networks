CC   -!- FUNCTION: Pore-forming protein that plays a key role in granzyme-
CC       mediated programmed cell death, and in defense against virus-infected
CC       or neoplastic cells (PubMed:2809217). Can insert into the membrane of
CC       target cells in its calcium-bound form, oligomerize and form large
CC       pores (By similarity). Promotes cytolysis and apoptosis of target cells
CC       by mediating the passage and uptake of cytotoxic granzymes (By
CC       similarity). Facilitates the delivery of cationic cargo protein, while
CC       anionic or neural proteins are not delivered efficiently (By
CC       similarity). Perforin pores allow the release of mature caspase-7
CC       (CASP7) into the extracellular milieu (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P10820, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P14222,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:2809217}.

CC         Evidence={ECO:0000255|PROSITE-ProRule:PRU00041};
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Monomer, as soluble protein. Homooligomer; homooligomerizes to
CC       form a pore-forming ring. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P10820}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytolytic granule {ECO:0000269|PubMed:2809217}.
CC       Secreted {ECO:0000269|PubMed:2809217}. Cell membrane
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:2809217}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:2809217}. Endosome lumen
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P14222}. Note=Stored in cytolytic granules of
CC       cytolytic T-lymphocytes and secreted into the cleft between T-
CC       lymphocyte and target cell. Inserts into the cell membrane of target
CC       cells and forms pores. Membrane insertion and pore formation requires a
CC       major conformation change. May be taken up via endocytosis involving
CC       clathrin-coated vesicles and accumulate in a first time in large early
CC       endosomes (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P14222}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Detected in large granular lymphocytes and
CC       lymphokine-activated killer cells. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:2809217}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: Perforin consists of three domains: (1) the MACPF domain, which
CC       includes the central machinery of pore formation, (2) the EGF-like
CC       domain, which forms a 'shelf-like' assembly connecting the MACPF and C2
CC       domains, and (3) the C2 domain, which mediates calcium-dependent
CC       binding to lipid membranes. The C2 domain is critical for initial
CC       calcium-dependent interaction with lipid membranes of the target cell:
CC       calcium-binding causes a significant structural rearrangement, leading
CC       to oligomerization and deployment of the two transmembrane beta-strands
CC       (named CH1/TMH1 and CH2/TMH2) that enter the membrane as amphipathic
CC       beta-hairpins. The third calcium-binding site (Ca(2+) 3), which
CC       constitutes the weakest affinity site, triggers structural
CC       rearrangements in the C2 domain that facilitate its interaction with
CC       lipid membranes. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P10820}.
CC   -!- PTM: N-glycosylated. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P10820}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the complement C6/C7/C8/C9 family.
CC       {ECO:0000305}.
