CC   -!- FUNCTION: Component of a retrotranslocation channel required for
CC       peroxisome organization by mediating export of the PEX5 receptor from
CC       peroxisomes to the cytosol, thereby promoting PEX5 recycling (By
CC       similarity). The retrotranslocation channel is composed of PEX2, PEX10
CC       and PEX12; each subunit contributing transmembrane segments that
CC       coassemble into an open channel that specifically allows the passage of
CC       PEX5 through the peroxisomal membrane (By similarity). PEX12 also
CC       regulates PEX5 recycling by activating the E3 ubiquitin-protein ligase
CC       activity of PEX10 (By similarity). When PEX5 recycling is compromised,
CC       PEX12 stimulates PEX10-mediated polyubiquitination of PEX5, leading to
CC       its subsequent degradation (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O00623, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q04370}.
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Protein modification; protein ubiquitination.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O00623}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Component of the PEX2-PEX10-PEX12 retrotranslocation channel,
CC       composed of PEX2, PEX10 and PEX12. Interacts with PEX19 via its
CC       cytoplasmic domain. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O00623}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Peroxisome membrane {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9354782};
CC       Multi-pass membrane protein {ECO:0000255}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The three subunits of the retrotranslocation channel (PEX2,
CC       PEX10 and PEX12) coassemble in the membrane into a channel with an open
CC       10 Angstrom pore. The RING-type zinc-fingers that catalyze PEX5
CC       receptor ubiquitination are positioned above the pore on the cytosolic
CC       side of the complex. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:G2Q5N0}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The RING-type zinc-finger is degenerated and only coordinates
CC       one zinc ions, preventing E3 ubiquitin-protein ligase activity.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q04370}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the pex2/pex10/pex12 family. {ECO:0000305}.
