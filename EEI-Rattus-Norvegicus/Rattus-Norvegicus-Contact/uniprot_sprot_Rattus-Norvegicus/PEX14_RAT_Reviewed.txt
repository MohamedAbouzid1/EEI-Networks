CC   -!- FUNCTION: Component of the PEX13-PEX14 docking complex, a translocon
CC       channel that specifically mediates the import of peroxisomal cargo
CC       proteins bound to PEX5 receptor (PubMed:19122147). The PEX13-PEX14
CC       docking complex forms a large import pore which can be opened to a
CC       diameter of about 9 nm (By similarity). Mechanistically, PEX5 receptor
CC       along with cargo proteins associates with the PEX14 subunit of the
CC       PEX13-PEX14 docking complex in the cytosol, leading to the insertion of
CC       the receptor into the organelle membrane with the concomitant
CC       translocation of the cargo into the peroxisome matrix. Plays a key role
CC       for peroxisome movement through a direct interaction with tubulin (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O75381,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P53112, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19122147}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts with PEX13; forming the PEX13-PEX14 docking complex
CC       (By similarity). Interacts with PEX5 (via WxxxF/Y motifs)
CC       (PubMed:19122147). Interacts with PEX19 (By similarity). Interacts with
CC       tubulin (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O75381,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19122147}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Peroxisome membrane {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12488033,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:30414318}; Single-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:30414318}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the peroxin-14 family. {ECO:0000305}.
