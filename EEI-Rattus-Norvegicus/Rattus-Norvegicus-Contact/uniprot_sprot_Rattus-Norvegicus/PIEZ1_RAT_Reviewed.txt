CC   -!- FUNCTION: Pore-forming subunit of a mechanosensitive non-specific
CC       cation Piezo channel. Conductance to monovalent alkali ions is highest
CC       for K(+), intermediate for Na(+) and lowest for Li(+). Divalent ions
CC       except for Mn(2+) permeate the channel but more slowly than the
CC       monovalent ions and they also reduce K(+) currents. Generates currents
CC       characterized by a linear current-voltage relationship that are
CC       sensitive to ruthenium red and gadolinium (By similarity). Piezo
CC       channels are homotrimeric three-blade propeller-shaped structure that
CC       utilize a cap-motion and plug-and-latch mechanism to gate their ion-
CC       conducting pathways (By similarity). In inner ear hair cells, PIEZO1/2
CC       subunits may constitute part of the mechanotransducer (MET) non-
CC       selective cation channel complex where they may act as pore-forming
CC       ion-conducting component in the complex (By similarity). Plays a key
CC       role in epithelial cell adhesion by maintaining integrin activation
CC       through R-Ras recruitment to the ER, most probably in its activated
CC       state, and subsequent stimulation of calpain signaling. In the kidney,
CC       may contribute to the detection of intraluminal pressure changes and to
CC       urine flow sensing. Acts as a shear-stress sensor that promotes
CC       endothelial cell organization and alignment in the direction of blood
CC       flow through calpain activation (By similarity). Plays a key role in
CC       blood vessel formation and vascular structure in both development and
CC       adult physiology (By similarity). Acts as a sensor of
CC       phosphatidylserine (PS) flipping at the plasma membrane and governs
CC       morphogenesis of muscle cells. In myoblasts, flippase-mediated PS
CC       enrichment at the inner leaflet of plasma membrane triggers channel
CC       activation and Ca2+ influx followed by Rho GTPases signal transduction,
CC       leading to assembly of cortical actomyosin fibers and myotube formation
CC       (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:E2JF22,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q91X60, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q92508}.

CC       the cell surface. Divalent ions decrease the single-channel
CC       permeability of K(+). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q92508}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Forms the Piezo channel composed of a homotrimer.
CC       Heterotrimeric interaction may occur between PIEZO1 and Piezo2.
CC       Interacts with PKD2. Interacts with STOML3. Interacts with TMC1, TMC2,
CC       PCDH15 and CIB2; the interaction may be part of the MET complex.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:E2JF22}.
