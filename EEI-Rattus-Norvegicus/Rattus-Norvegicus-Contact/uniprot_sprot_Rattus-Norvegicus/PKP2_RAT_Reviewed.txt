CC   -!- FUNCTION: Regulates focal adhesion turnover resulting in changes in
CC       focal adhesion size, cell adhesion and cell spreading, potentially via
CC       transcriptional modulation of beta-integrins (By similarity). Required
CC       to maintain gingival epithelial barrier function (By similarity).
CC       Important component of the desmosome that is also required for
CC       localization of desmosome component proteins such as DSC2, DSG2 and JUP
CC       to the desmosome cell-cell junction (By similarity). Required for the
CC       formation of desmosome cell junctions in cardiomyocytes, thereby
CC       required for the correct formation of the heart, specifically
CC       trabeculation and formation of the atria walls (PubMed:21617128). Loss
CC       of desmosome cell junctions leads to mis-localization of DSP and DSG2
CC       resulting in disruption of cell-cell adhesion and disordered
CC       intermediate filaments (By similarity). Modulates profibrotic gene
CC       expression in cardiomyocytes via regulation of DSP expression and
CC       subsequent activation of downstream TGFB1 and MAPK14/p38 MAPK signaling
CC       (PubMed:26858265). Required for cardiac sodium current propagation and
CC       electrical synchrony in cardiac myocytes, via ANK3 stabilization and
CC       modulation of SCN5A/Nav1.5 localization to cell-cell junctions
CC       (PubMed:19661460, PubMed:21617128). Required for mitochondrial
CC       function, nuclear envelope integrity and positive regulation of SIRT3
CC       transcription via maintaining DES localization at its nuclear envelope
CC       and cell tip anchoring points, and thereby preserving regulation of the
CC       transcriptional program (By similarity). Maintenance of nuclear
CC       envelope integrity protects against DNA damage and transcriptional
CC       dysregulation of genes, especially those involved in the electron
CC       transport chain, thereby preserving mitochondrial function and
CC       protecting against superoxide radical anion generation (By similarity).
CC       May play a role in junctional plaques (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q99959, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9CQ73,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19661460, ECO:0000269|PubMed:21617128,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:26858265}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts with DSC2 (By similarity). Interacts with JUP (By
CC       similarity). Interacts with KRT5/CK5, KRT8/CK8, KRT14/CK14, KRT18/CK18
CC       and VIM (By similarity). Interacts (via N-terminus) with MARK3/C-TAK1
CC       (By similarity). Interacts with DSP (By similarity). Interacts with
CC       DSG1, DSG2 and DSG3 (By similarity). Interacts (via N-terminus) with
CC       CTNNB1 (By similarity). Interacts with CDH1 (By similarity). Interacts
CC       with the RNA polymerase III (Pol III) complex proteins POLR3A/RPC155,
CC       POLR3F/RPC39 and POLR3C/RPC82 (By similarity). Interacts with CTNNA3
CC       (By similarity). Interacts (via N-terminus) with SCN5A/Nav1.5
