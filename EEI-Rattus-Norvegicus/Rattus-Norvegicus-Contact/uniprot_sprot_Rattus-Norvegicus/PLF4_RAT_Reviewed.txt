CC   -!- FUNCTION: Chemokine released during platelet aggregation that plays a
CC       role in different biological processes including hematopoiesis, cell
CC       proliferation, differentiation, and activation. Acts via different
CC       functional receptors including CCR1, CXCR3A or CXCR3B. Upon interaction
CC       with CXCR3A receptor, induces activated T-lymphocytes migration
CC       mediated via downstream Ras/extracellular signal-regulated kinase (ERK)
CC       signaling. Neutralizes the anticoagulant effect of heparin by binding
CC       more strongly to heparin than to the chondroitin-4-sulfate chains of
CC       the carrier molecule. Plays a role in the inhibition of hematopoiesis
CC       and in the maintenance of hematopoietic stem cell (HSC) quiescence.
CC       Chemotactic for neutrophils and monocytes via CCR1. Inhibits
CC       endothelial cell proliferation. In cooperation with toll-like receptor
CC       8/TLR8, induces chromatin remodeling and activates inflammatory gene
CC       expression via the TBK1-IRF5 axis. In addition, induces myofibroblast
CC       differentiation and collagen synthesis in different precursor cells,
CC       including endothelial cells, by stimulating endothelial-to-mesenchymal
CC       transition. Interacts with thrombomodulin/THBD to enhance the
CC       activation of protein C and thus potentiates its anticoagulant
CC       activity. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P02776}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homotetramer. Interacts with TNFAIP6 (via Link domain).
CC       Interacts with CCR1. Interacts with CXCR3. Interacts with THBD; this
CC       interaction enhances generation of activated protein C.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P02776}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Secreted.
CC   -!- PTM: O-linked glycan consists of Gal-GalNAc disaccharide which is
CC       modified with sialic acid residues (microheterogeneity).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:8033893}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the intercrine alpha (chemokine CxC) family.
CC       {ECO:0000305}.
