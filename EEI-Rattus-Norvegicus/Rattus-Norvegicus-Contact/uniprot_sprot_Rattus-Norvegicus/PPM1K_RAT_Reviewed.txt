CC   -!- FUNCTION: Serine/threonine-protein phosphatase component of
CC       macronutrients metabolism. Together with BCKDK serves as a metabolic
CC       regulatory node that coordinates branched-chain amino acids (BCAAs) and
CC       protein synthesis with glucose and lipid metabolism via two distinct
CC       phosphoprotein targets: BCKDHA/E1a subunit of the branched-chain alpha-
CC       ketoacid dehydrogenase (BCKDH) complex and ACLY, a lipogenic enzyme of
CC       Krebs cycle (By similarity) (PubMed:29779826). At high levels of
CC       branched-chain ketoacids (BCKAs), dephosphorylates and activates
CC       mitochondrial BCKDH complex, a multisubunit complex consisting of three
CC       components, heterotetrameric E1 composed of BCKDHA and BCKDHB chains,
CC       24-meric E2 core composed of DBT and homodimeric E3 composed of DLD,
CC       each involved in different steps of BCAA catabolism. Tightly associates
CC       with the E2 subunit of BCKDH complex and dephosphorylates Ser-333 of
CC       BCKDHA chain of the E1 subunit likely through on-off binding to
CC       individual E2 subunits of the 24-meric E2 core to increase the
CC       efficiency of the dephosphorylation reaction (By similarity). Appears
CC       to dephosphorylate and inactivate cytosolic ACLY in response to changes
CC       of cellular carbohydrate abundance. Overnutrition and in particular
CC       high-fructose diet, activates MLXIPL/ChREBP leading to increased BCKDK
CC       to PPM1K ratio, phosphorylation of ACLY on Ser-454 and activation of
CC       its enzymatic activity that ultimately results in the generation of
CC       acetyl-CoA and malonyl-CoA immediate substrates of de novo lipogenesis
CC       (By similarity) (PubMed:29779826). Recognizes phosphosites having SxS
CC       or RxxS motifs and strictly depends on Mn(2+) ions for the phosphatase
CC       activity (By similarity). Regulates Ca(2+)-induced opening of
CC       mitochondrial transition pore and apoptotic cell death (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8N3J5, ECO:0000269|PubMed:29779826}.

CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts with E1 and E2 components of the branched-chain
CC       alpha-ketoacid dehydrogenase (BCKDH) complex. Interacts with both
CC       BCKDHA and BCKDHB chains of the E1 subunit. Interacts with the 24-meric
CC       DBT/E2 core of the BCKD complex with a 1:1 stoichiometry; the N-
CC       terminal region (residues 49-61) of PPM1K and C-terminal linker of the
CC       lipoyl domain of DBT/E2 (residues 145-160) are critical for this
CC       interaction whereas the lipoyl prosthetic group is dispensable (By
CC       similarity). Competes with BCKDK for binding to DBT/E2; this
CC       interaction is modulated by branched-chain alpha-keto acids (BCKAs). At
CC       steady state, BCKDH holoenzyme preferentially binds BCKDK and BCKDHA/E1
CC       is phosphorylated. In response to high levels of BCKAs, BCKDK is
CC       replaced by PPM1K leading to BCKDHA/E1 dephosphorylation (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8N3J5}.
