CC   -!- FUNCTION: Involved in male fertility. Required for manchette
CC       development and acrosome biogenesis during spermiogenesis (By
CC       similarity). Binds in vitro to phospholipids, including
CC       phosphatidylinositol 3-phosphate (PtdIns(3)P), phosphatidylinositol
CC       4,5-bisphosphate (PtdIns(4,5)P2), phosphatidylinositol 4-phosphate
CC       (PtdIns(4)P) and phosphatidic acid (PA). Contrary to other profilin
CC       family members, does not bind to actin in vitro (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8NHR9, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9D6I3}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19419568}. Note=In
CC       round spermatids, mainly observed in the acroplaxome. During the
CC       progression of spermiogenesis, relocalizes to the developing manchette
CC       of spermatids step 8 (S8). Coinciding with the initiation of manchette
CC       disassembly in spermatids S14, seen in the cytoplasm subjacent to the
CC       disassembling manchette. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15591451,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19419568}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed in testis, in seminiferous tubules (at
CC       protein level) (PubMed:15591451, PubMed:19419568). Expressed in
CC       spermatocytes and spermatids, but not in spermatogonium
CC       (PubMed:15591451). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15591451,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19419568}.
CC   -!- DEVELOPMENTAL STAGE: Expression in seminiferous tubules overlaps with
CC       the onset of meiosis, starting from spermatocytes of the late pachytene
CC       stages and proceeding throughout meiosis to the round spermatids.
CC       Detected in nearly all stages of spermiogenesis (at protein level).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15591451, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19419568}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the profilin family. {ECO:0000305}.
