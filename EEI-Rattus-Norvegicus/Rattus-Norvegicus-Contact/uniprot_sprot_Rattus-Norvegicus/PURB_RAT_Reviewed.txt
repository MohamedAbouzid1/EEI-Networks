CC   -!- FUNCTION: Transcriptional regulator which can act as an activator or a
CC       repressor. Represses the transcription of ACTA2 in fibroblasts and
CC       smooth muscle cells via its ability to interact with the purine-rich
CC       strand of a MCAT- containing element in the 5' flanking region of the
CC       gene. Represses the transcription of MYOCD, capable of repressing all
CC       isoforms of MYOCD but the magnitude of the repressive effects is most
CC       notable for the SMC- specific isoforms. Promotes hepatic glucose
CC       production by activating the transcription of ADCY6, leading to cAMP
CC       accumulation, increased PKA activity, CREB activation, and increased
CC       transcription of PCK1 and G6PC genes. Has capacity to bind repeated
CC       elements in single-stranded DNA such as the purine-rich single strand
CC       of the PUR element located upstream of the MYC gene (By similarity).
CC       Participates in transcriptional and translational regulation of alpha-
CC       MHC expression in cardiac myocytes by binding to the purine-rich
CC       negative regulatory (PNR) element (PubMed:12933792). Modulates
CC       constitutive liver galectin-3 gene transcription by binding to its
CC       promoter (PubMed:16309856). May play a role in the dendritic transport
CC       of a subset of mRNAs (PubMed:11032866). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O35295,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q96QR8, ECO:0000269|PubMed:11032866,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:12933792, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16309856}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer, heterodimer with PURA and heterotrimer with PURA
CC       and YBX1/Y-box protein 1. Interacts with MYOCD and SRF.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O35295}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Nucleus {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O35295}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed in muscle cells and in the liver.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12933792, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16309856}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Induced in the liver 48 hours after tetrachloromethane
CC       (CCL4) administration. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16309856}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the PUR DNA-binding protein family.
CC       {ECO:0000305}.
