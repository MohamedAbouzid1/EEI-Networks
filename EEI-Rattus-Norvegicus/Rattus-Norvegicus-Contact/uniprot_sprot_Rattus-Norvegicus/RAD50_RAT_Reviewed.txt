CC   -!- FUNCTION: Component of the MRN complex, which plays a central role in
CC       double-strand break (DSB) repair, DNA recombination, maintenance of
CC       telomere integrity and meiosis. The MRN complex is involved in the
CC       repair of DNA double-strand breaks (DSBs) via homologous recombination
CC       (HR), an error-free mechanism which primarily occurs during S and G2
CC       phases. The complex (1) mediates the end resection of damaged DNA,
CC       which generates proper single-stranded DNA, a key initial steps in HR,
CC       and is (2) required for the recruitment of other repair factors and
CC       efficient activation of ATM and ATR upon DNA damage. The MRN complex
CC       possesses single-strand endonuclease activity and double-strand-
CC       specific 3'-5' exonuclease activity, which are provided by MRE11, to
CC       initiate end resection, which is required for single-strand invasion
CC       and recombination. Within the complex, RAD50 is both required to bind
CC       DNA ends and hold them in close proximity and regulate the activity of
CC       MRE11 (By similarity). RAD50 provides an ATP-dependent control of MRE11
CC       by positioning DNA ends into the MRE11 active site: ATP-binding induces
CC       a large structural change from an open form with accessible MRE11
CC       nuclease sites into a closed form (By similarity). The MRN complex is
CC       also required for DNA damage signaling via activation of the ATM and
CC       ATR kinases: the nuclease activity of MRE11 is not required to activate
CC       ATM and ATR. The MRN complex is also required for the processing of R-
CC       loops. In telomeres the MRN complex may modulate t-loop formation (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q92878,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9X1X1}.

CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Component of the MRN complex composed of two heterodimers
CC       RAD50 and MRE11 associated with a single NBN. The MRN complexes
CC       dimerize on DNA to form joined MRN-MRN oligomers required for DNA
CC       double-strand break repair. As part of the MRN complex, interacts with
CC       MCM8 and MCM9; the interaction recruits the complex to DNA repair
CC       sites. Component of the BASC complex, at least composed of BRCA1, MSH2,
CC       MSH6, MLH1, ATM, BLM, RAD50, MRE11 and NBN. Found in a complex with
CC       TERF2. Interacts with RINT1. Interacts with BRCA1 via its N-terminal
CC       domain. Interacts with DCLRE1C/Artemis. Interacts with MRNIP (By
CC       similarity). Interacts with CYREN (via XLF motif) (By similarity).
CC       Interacts with C1QBP and MRE11; interaction takes place in absence of
CC       DNA damage to form the MRC (MRE11-RAD50-C1QBP) complex that inhibits
CC       the activity of MRE11 (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P70388,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q92878}.

CC       after treatment with genotoxic agents. Localizes to DNA double-strand
CC       breaks (DSBs). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q92878}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Present at low levels in the heart at fetal-day 17,
CC       at relatively constant levels at postnatal days 10, 17 and 21 and at
CC       slightly lower levels in the adult heart. Detected in liver, kidney and
CC       lung. Barely detectable in skeletal muscle with slightly higher levels
CC       observed in brain and the ventricles of the heart (at protein level).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10908350}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The zinc-hook, which separates the large intramolecular coiled
CC       coil regions, contains 2 Cys residues that coordinate one molecule of
CC       zinc with the help of the 2 Cys residues of the zinc-hook of another
CC       RAD50 molecule, thereby forming a V-shaped homodimer. The two heads of
CC       the homodimer, which constitute the ATP-binding domain, interact with
CC       the MRE11 homodimer. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P58301}.
CC   -!- PTM: Phosphorylation at Ser-635 by ATM in response to DNA damage is
CC       required for double-strand break (DSB) repair.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q92878}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the SMC family. RAD50 subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
