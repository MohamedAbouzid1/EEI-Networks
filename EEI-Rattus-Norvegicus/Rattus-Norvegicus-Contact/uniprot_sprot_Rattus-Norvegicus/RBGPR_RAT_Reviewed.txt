CC   -!- FUNCTION: Regulatory subunit of the Rab3 GTPase-activating (Rab3GAP)
CC       complex composed of Rab3gap1 and Rab3gap2, which has GTPase-activating
CC       protein (GAP) activity towards various Rab3 subfamily members (RAB3A,
CC       RAB3B, RAB3C and RAB3D), RAB5A and RAB43, and guanine nucleotide
CC       exchange factor (GEF) activity towards RAB18 (By similarity). As part
CC       of the Rab3GAP complex, acts as a GAP for Rab3 proteins by converting
CC       active RAB3-GTP to the inactive form RAB3-GDP (By similarity). Rab3
CC       proteins are involved in regulated exocytosis of neurotransmitters and
CC       hormones (By similarity). The Rab3GAP complex, acts as a GEF for RAB18
CC       by promoting the conversion of inactive RAB18-GDP to the active form
CC       RAB18-GTP (By similarity). Required for recruiting and activating RAB18
CC       at the endoplasmic reticulum (ER) membrane where it maintains proper ER
CC       structure (By similarity). Required for normal eye and brain
CC       development (By similarity). May participate in neurodevelopmental
CC       processes such as proliferation, migration and differentiation before
CC       synapse formation, and non-synaptic vesicular release of
CC       neurotransmitters (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q15042,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9H2M9}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: The Rab3 GTPase-activating complex is a heterodimer composed
CC       of Rab3gap1 and Rab3gap2 (PubMed:9733780). The Rab3 GTPase-activating
CC       complex interacts with DMXL2 (PubMed:11809763). Interacts with LMAN1
CC       (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9H2M9,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:11809763, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9733780}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9733780}.
CC       Endoplasmic reticulum {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9H2M9}. Note=In neurons,
CC       it is enriched in the synaptic soluble fraction.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9733780}.
