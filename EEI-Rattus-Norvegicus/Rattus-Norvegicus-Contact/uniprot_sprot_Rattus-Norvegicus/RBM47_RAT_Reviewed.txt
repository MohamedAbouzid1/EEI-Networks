CC   -!- FUNCTION: Single-stranded RNA-binding protein that functions in a
CC       variety of RNA processes, including alternative splicing, RNA
CC       stabilization, and RNA editing. Functions as an enzyme-substrate
CC       adapter for the cytidine deaminase APOBEC1. With APOBEC1 forms an mRNA
CC       editing complex involved into cytidine to uridine editing of a variety
CC       of mRNA molecules. Through the binding of their 3'UTR, also stabilizes
CC       a variety of mRNAs and regulates the expression of genes such as the
CC       interferon alpha/beta receptor and interleukin-10. Also involved in the
CC       alternative splicing of several genes including TJP1. Binds the pre-
CC       mRNA (U)GCAUG consensus sequences in downstream intronic regions of
CC       alternative exons regulating their exclusion and inclusion into mRNAs
CC       (By similarity). Independently of its RNA-binding activity, could
CC       negatively regulate MAVS by promoting its lysosomal degradation (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:A0A8M1NHK4,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:A0AV96}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer. Interacts with A1CF. Interacts with APOBEC1; form
CC       an mRNA editing complex. Interacts with RBPMS.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:A0AV96}.
