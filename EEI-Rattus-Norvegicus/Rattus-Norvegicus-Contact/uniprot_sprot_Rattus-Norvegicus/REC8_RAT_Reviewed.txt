CC   -!- FUNCTION: Required during meiosis for separation of sister chromatids
CC       and homologous chromosomes. Proteolytic cleavage of REC8 on chromosome
CC       arms by separin during anaphase I allows for homologous chromosome
CC       separation in meiosis I and cleavage of REC8 on centromeres during
CC       anaphase II allows for sister chromatid separation in meiosis II (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8C5S7}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts (phosphorylated and unphosphorylated form) with
CC       SMC3. Interacts with SYCP3. Interacts (phosphorylated and
CC       unphosphorylated form) with SMC1B. Does not interact with SMC1A (By
CC       similarity). Interacts with RAD51. Forms a complex with EWSR1, PRDM9,
CC       SYCP3 and SYCP1; complex formation is dependent of phosphorylated form
CC       of REC8 and requires PRDM9 bound to hotspot DNA; EWSR1 joins PRDM9 with
CC       the chromosomal axis through REC8 (By similarity). {ECO:0000250,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8C5S7, ECO:0000269|PubMed:12615909}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Nucleus {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12615909}. Chromosome
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12615909}. Chromosome, centromere
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12615909}. Note=In meiotic chromosomes, localized
CC       along axial elements in prophase from the leptotene to diplotene
CC       stages. At later prophase stages, diakinesis and metaphase I, localized
CC       along interstitial axes of chromosomes including both centromere and
CC       arm regions. No longer detected in arm regions in anaphase I but
CC       persists on centromere regions until metaphase II.
