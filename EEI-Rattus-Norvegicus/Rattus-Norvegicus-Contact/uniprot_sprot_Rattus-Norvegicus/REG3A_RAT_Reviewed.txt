CC   -!- FUNCTION: Bactericidal C-type lectin. The lack of the EPN motif may
CC       explain its inability to bind peptidoglycan.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O09037}.
CC   -!- FUNCTION: Acts as a hormone in response to different stimuli like anti-
CC       inflammatory signals, such as IL17A, or gut microbiome. Secreted by
CC       different cell types to activate its receptor EXTL3 and induce cell
CC       specific signaling pathways. Induced by IL17A in keratinocytes,
CC       regulates keratinocyte proliferation and differentiation after skin
CC       injury via activation of EXTL3-PI3K-AKT signaling pathway. In parallel,
CC       inhibits skin inflammation through the inhibition of inflammatory
CC       cytokines such as IL6 and TNF. In pancreas, is able to permealize beta-
CC       cells membrane and stimulate their proliferation.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q06141}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: [Regenerating islet-derived protein 3-alpha 15 kDa form]:
CC       Forms a hexameric membrane-permeabilizing oligomeric pore on membrane
CC       phospholipids. The hexamer is formed by three dimers related by helical
CC       symmetry. Forms filaments, filamentation traps pore complexes and
CC       limits damage to host cells. Interacts with EXTL3.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q06141}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Secreted {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q06141}.
CC       Note=Found in the apical region of pancreatic acinar cells.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q06141}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Low expression found in healthy pancreas.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Appears in pancreatic juice after induction of pancreatic
CC       inflammation.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: Lacks the EPN motif and the presence of Gln instead of Glu at
CC       amino-acid position 114 may explain its inability to bind
CC       peptidoglycan. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O09037}.
CC   -!- PTM: Proteolytic processing by trypsin removes an inhibitory N-terminal
CC       propeptide and is essential for peptidoglycan binding and antibacterial
CC       activity. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q06141}.
