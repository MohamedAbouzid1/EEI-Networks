CC   -!- FUNCTION: Bactericidal C-type lectin which acts against several
CC       intestinal Gram-positive bacteria and Gram-negative bacteria. Lacks
CC       antibacterial activity against S.typhimurium. May play a role in
CC       protection against infection with S.enteritidis by inhibiting its
CC       translocation from the gut lumen into intestinal tissues and further
CC       extraintestinal tissues. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P35230}.
CC   -!- FUNCTION: Acts as a hormone in response to different stimuli. Secreted
CC       by different cell types to activate its receptor EXTL3 and induce cell
CC       specific signaling pathways. In pancreas, is able stimulate cell
CC       proliferation. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P35230}.
CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: [Regenerating islet-derived protein 3-beta 15 kDa
CC       form]: Lipopolysaccharide inhibits pore-forming activity, explaining
CC       why is bactericidal for Gram-positive but not Gram-negative bacteria.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q06141}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: [Regenerating islet-derived protein 3-beta 15 kDa form]: Forms
CC       a hexameric membrane-permeabilizing oligomeric pore on membrane
CC       phospholipids. The hexamer is formed by three dimers related by helical
CC       symmetry. Forms filaments, filamentation traps pore complexes and
CC       limits damage to host cells. Interacts with EXTL3.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q06141}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Secreted {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P35230}.
CC       Note=Found in the apical region of pancreatic acinar cells.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P35230}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Constitutively expressed in intestine.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:8238345}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Appears in pancreatic juice after induction of pancreatic
CC       inflammation. Secreted also by pituitary cells; the secretion there is
CC       stimulated by GH-releasing hormone and inhibited by somatostatin.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:1722211, ECO:0000269|PubMed:7895644,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:8238345}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The EPN motif is essential for recognition of the peptidoglycan
CC       carbohydrate backbone and for efficient bacterial killing with Glu-114
CC       playing a key role in peptidoglycan binding and bactericidal activity.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q06141}.
CC   -!- PTM: Proteolytic processing by trypsin removes an inhibitory N-terminal
CC       propeptide and is essential for peptidoglycan binding and antibacterial
CC       activity. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P35230}.
