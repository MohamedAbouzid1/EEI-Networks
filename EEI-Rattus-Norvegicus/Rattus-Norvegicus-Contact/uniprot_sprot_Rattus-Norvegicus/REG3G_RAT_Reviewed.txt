CC   -!- FUNCTION: Bactericidal C-type lectin which acts exclusively against
CC       Gram-positive bacteria and mediates bacterial killing by binding to
CC       surface-exposed carbohydrate moieties of peptidoglycan. Restricts
CC       bacterial colonization of the intestinal epithelial surface and
CC       consequently limits activation of adaptive immune responses by the
CC       microbiota. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O09049}.
CC   -!- FUNCTION: Acts as a hormone in response to different stimuli like anti-
CC       inflammatory signals, such as IL17A, or gut microbiome. Is secreted by
CC       different cell types to activate its receptor EXTL3 and induce cell
CC       specific signaling pathways. Induced by IL17A in keratinocytes,
CC       regulates keratinocyte proliferation and differentiation after skin
CC       injury. In parallel, inhibits skin inflammation through the inhibition
CC       of inflammatory cytokines such as IL6 and TNF. Induced by IL22 in lung
CC       epithelial cells, inhibits cytokine production and regulates allergic
CC       airway inflammation. Induced in small intestine by inulin-enriched diet
CC       and Lactobacillus gasseri enriched microbiome, plays a role in the
CC       improvement of gut barrier function, the regulation of energy balance
CC       and glucose levels. Modulates microbiota composition in duodenal
CC       contents. Produced by nociceptor in response to endotoxins, prevents
CC       endotoxic death by targeting kynurenine pathway in microglia.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O09049}.
CC   -!- FUNCTION: [Regenerating islet-derived protein 3-gamma 16.5 kDa form]:
CC       Has bacteriostatic activity. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O09049}.
CC   -!- FUNCTION: [Regenerating islet-derived protein 3-gamma 15 kDa form]: Has
CC       bactericidal activity against L.monocytogenes and methicillin-resistant
CC       S.aureus. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O09049}.
CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: [Regenerating islet-derived protein 3-gamma 15 kDa
CC       form]: Lipopolysaccharide inhibits pore-forming activity, explaining
CC       why is bactericidal for Gram-positive but not Gram-negative bacteria.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q06141}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: [Regenerating islet-derived protein 3-gamma 15 kDa form]:
CC       Forms a hexameric membrane-permeabilizing oligomeric pore on membrane
CC       phospholipids. The hexamer is formed by three dimers related by helical
CC       symmetry. Forms filaments, filamentation traps pore complexes and
CC       limits damage to host cells. Interacts with EXTL3.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q06141}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Secreted {ECO:0000269|PubMed:23588857}. Cytoplasm
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:23588857}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed in injured skeletal muscles and sciatic
CC       nerve (at protein level). Expressed in the pancreas. Expression
CC       increases during the acute phase of pancreatitis.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:23588857, ECO:0000269|PubMed:7717998}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Up-regulated in injured skeletal muscles and peripheral
CC       nerve. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:23588857}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The EPN motif is essential for recognition of the peptidoglycan
CC       carbohydrate backbone and for efficient bacterial killing with Glu-114
CC       playing a key role in peptidoglycan binding and bactericidal activity.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q06141}.
CC   -!- PTM: Proteolytic processing by trypsin removes an inhibitory N-terminal
CC       propeptide and is essential for peptidoglycan binding and antibacterial
CC       activity. {ECO:0000250}.
