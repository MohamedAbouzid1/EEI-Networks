CC   -!- FUNCTION: Small GTPase that acts as an allosteric activator of the
CC       canonical mTORC1 complex, an evolutionarily conserved central nutrient
CC       sensor that stimulates anabolic reactions and macromolecule
CC       biosynthesis to promote cellular biomass generation and growth. In
CC       response to nutrients, growth factors or amino acids, specifically
CC       activates the protein kinase activity of MTOR, the catalytic component
CC       of the mTORC1 complex: acts by causing a conformational change that
CC       allows the alignment of residues in the active site of MTOR, thereby
CC       enhancing the phosphorylation of ribosomal protein S6 kinase (RPS6KB1
CC       and RPS6KB2) and EIF4EBP1 (4E-BP1). RHEB is also required for
CC       localization of the TSC-TBC complex to lysosomal membranes. In response
CC       to starvation, RHEB is inactivated by the TSC-TBC complex, preventing
CC       activation of mTORC1. Has low intrinsic GTPase activity.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q15382}.

CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: Alternates between an inactive form bound to GDP
CC       and an active form bound to GTP. Inactivated by the TSC-TBC complex via
CC       the GTPase activating protein (GAP) domain of TSC2.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q15382}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Associates with the mTORC1 complex (MTOR, MLST8 and RPTOR) in
CC       a guanyl nucleotide-independent manner. Interacts with TSC2. Interacts
CC       (when prenylated) with PDE6D; this promotes release from membranes (By
CC       similarity). Autoinhibited by Tyr-35, which constrains the active site
CC       conformation, restricting the access of the catalytic Asp-65 to the
CC       nucleotide-binding pocket (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q15382}.

CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed at high levels in normal adult cortex as

CC   -!- PTM: Farnesylation is important for efficiently activating mTORC1-

CC   -!- PTM: Polyubiquitinated in response to amino acid, promoting its

CC       with the TSC-TBC complex. Deubiquitinated at Lys-8 by USP4, promoting
CC       mTORC1 activation. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q15382}.
