CC   -!- FUNCTION: Oxygenase that can act as both a histone lysine demethylase
CC       and a ribosomal histidine hydroxylase (By similarity). Specifically
CC       demethylates 'Lys-4' (H3K4me) and 'Lys-36' (H3K36me) of histone H3,
CC       thereby playing a central role in histone code. Preferentially
CC       demethylates trimethylated H3 'Lys-4' (H3K4me3) and monomethylated H3
CC       'Lys-4' (H3K4me1) residues, while it has weaker activity for
CC       dimethylated H3 'Lys-36' (H3K36me2). Acts as a regulator of osteoblast
CC       differentiation via its interaction with SP7/OSX by demethylating
CC       H3K4me and H3K36me, thereby inhibiting SP7/OSX-mediated promoter
CC       activation. Also catalyzes demethylation of non-histone proteins, such
CC       as CGAS: demethylation of monomethylated CGAS promotes interaction
CC       between CGAS and PARP1, followed by PARP1 inactivation (By similarity).
CC       Also catalyzes the hydroxylation of 60S ribosomal protein L8 on 'His-
CC       216', thereby playing a role in ribosome biogenesis. Participates in
CC       MYC-induced transcriptional activation (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9H6W3, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9JJF3}.
