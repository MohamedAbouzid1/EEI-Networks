CC   -!- FUNCTION: Functions as an RNA ligase, in vitro. The ligation reaction
CC       entails three nucleotidyl transfer steps. In the first step, the RNA
CC       ligase reacts with ATP in the absence of nucleic acid to form a
CC       covalent ligase-AMP intermediate and release pyrophosphate. In step 2,
CC       the ligase-AMP binds to the nucleic acid and transfers the adenylate to
CC       the 5'-PO4 terminus to form an adenylylated intermediate. In step 3,
CC       the RNA ligase directs the attack of the 3'-OH on the 5'-
CC       phosphoanhydride linkage, resulting in a repaired 3'-5' phosphodiester
CC       and release of AMP. Exhibits selectivity for single-stranded RNA
CC       substrates and may not have nick-sealing activity on double-stranded
CC       DNA-RNA hybrids. May play a role in maintaining RNA integrity under
CC       stress conditions, for example in response to reactive oxygen species
CC       (ROS). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8N999}.
