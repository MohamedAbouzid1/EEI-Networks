CC   -!- FUNCTION: E2-dependent E3 ubiquitin-protein ligase that functions as a
CC       RIGI coreceptor in the sensing of viral RNAs in cell cytoplasm and the
CC       activation of the antiviral innate immune response. Together with the
CC       UBE2D3, UBE2N and UB2V1 E2 ligases, catalyzes the 'Lys-63'-linked
CC       polyubiquitination of RIGI oligomerized on viral RNAs, an essential
CC       step in the activation of the RIG-I signaling pathway. Through a
CC       ubiquitin-independent parallel mechanism, which consists in bridging
CC       RIGI filaments forming on longer viral RNAs, further activates the RIG-
CC       I signaling pathway. This second mechanism that synergizes with the
CC       ubiquitin-dependent one would thereby allow an RNA length-dependent
CC       regulation of the RIG-I signaling pathway. Associated with the E2
CC       ligase UBE2N, also constitutively synthesizes unanchored 'Lys-63'-
CC       linked polyubiquitin chains that may also activate the RIG-I signaling
CC       pathway. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8IUD6}.
