CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalytic core component of RNA polymerase I (Pol I), a DNA-
CC       dependent RNA polymerase which synthesizes ribosomal RNA precursors
CC       using the four ribonucleoside triphosphates as substrates. Transcribes
CC       47S pre-rRNAs from multicopy rRNA gene clusters, giving rise to 5.8S,
CC       18S and 28S ribosomal RNAs (By similarity) (PubMed:9422795). Pol I-
CC       mediated transcription cycle proceeds through transcription initiation,
CC       transcription elongation and transcription termination stages. During
CC       transcription initiation, Pol I pre-initiation complex (PIC) is
CC       recruited by the selectivity factor 1 (SL1/TIF-IB) complex bound to the
CC       core promoter that precedes an rDNA repeat unit. The PIC assembly bends
CC       the promoter favoring the formation of the transcription bubble and
CC       promoter escape. Once the polymerase has escaped from the promoter it
CC       enters the elongation phase during which RNA is actively polymerized,
CC       based on complementarity with the template DNA strand. Highly
CC       processive, assembles in structures referred to as 'Miller trees' where
CC       many elongating Pol I complexes queue and transcribe the same rDNA
CC       coding regions. At terminator sequences downstream of the rDNA gene,
CC       PTRF interacts with Pol I and halts Pol I transcription leading to the
CC       release of the RNA transcript and polymerase from the DNA (By
CC       similarity) (PubMed:9422795). Forms Pol I active center together with
CC       the largest subunit POLR1A/RPA1. Appends one nucleotide at a time to
CC       the 3' end of the nascent RNA, with POLR1A/RPA1 contributing a Mg(2+)-
CC       coordinating DxDGD motif, and POLR1B/RPA2 participating in the
CC       coordination of a second Mg(2+) ion and providing lysine residues
CC       believed to facilitate Watson-Crick base pairing between the incoming
CC       nucleotide and the template base. Typically, Mg(2+) ions direct a 5'
CC       nucleoside triphosphate to form a phosphodiester bond with the 3'
CC       hydroxyl of the preceding nucleotide of the nascent RNA, with the
CC       elimination of pyrophosphate. Has proofreading activity: Pauses and
CC       backtracks to allow the cleavage of a missincorporated nucleotide via
CC       POLR1H/RPA12. High Pol I processivity is associated with decreased
CC       transcription fidelity (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P10964,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9H9Y6, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9422795}.

CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Component of the RNA polymerase I (Pol I) complex consisting
CC       of 13 subunits: a ten-subunit catalytic core composed of POLR1A/RPA1,
CC       POLR1B/RPA2, POLR1C/RPAC1, POLR1D/RPAC2, POLR1H/RPA12, POLR2E/RPABC1,
CC       POLR2F/RPABC2, POLR2H/RPABC3, POLR2K/RPABC4 and POLR2L/RPABC5; a mobile
CC       stalk subunit POLR1F/RPA43 protruding from the core and additional
CC       subunits homologous to general transcription factors POLR1E/RPA49 and
CC       POLR1G/RPA34. Part of Pol I pre-initiation complex (PIC), in which Pol
CC       I core assembles with RRN3 and promoter-bound UTBF and SL1/TIF-IB
CC       complex. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9H9Y6, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9422795}.
