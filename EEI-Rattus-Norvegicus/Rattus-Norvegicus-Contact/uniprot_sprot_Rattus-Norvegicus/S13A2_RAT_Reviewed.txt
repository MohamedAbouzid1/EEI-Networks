CC   -!- FUNCTION: Low-affinity sodium-dicarboxylate cotransporter, that
CC       mediates the entry of citric acid cycle intermediates, such as
CC       succinate, citrate, fumarate and alpha-ketoglutarate (2-oxoglutarate)
CC       into the small intestine and renal proximal tubule (PubMed:9691021,
CC       PubMed:9694847). Transports the dicarboxylate into the cell with a
CC       probable stoichiometry of 3 Na(+) for 1 divalent dicarboxylate,
CC       rendering the process electrogenic (PubMed:9691021, PubMed:9694847).
CC       Citrate is transported in protonated form as a divalent anion, rather
CC       than the trivalent form which is normally found in blood
CC       (PubMed:9694847). Has a critical role in renal dicarboxylate transport
CC       (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9ES88,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9691021, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9694847}.
