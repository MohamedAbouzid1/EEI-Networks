CC   -!- FUNCTION: High-affinity sodium/citrate cotransporter that mediates
CC       citrate entry into cells, which is a critical participant of
CC       biochemical pathways (PubMed:12177002, PubMed:12826022,
CC       PubMed:14656221). May function in various metabolic processes in which
CC       citrate has a critical role such as energy production (Krebs cycle),
CC       fatty acid synthesis, cholesterol synthesis, glycolysis, and
CC       gluconeogenesis (By similarity). Transports citrate into the cell in a
CC       Na(+)-dependent manner, recognizing the trivalent form of citrate
CC       (physiological pH) rather than the divalent form (PubMed:12177002,
CC       PubMed:12826022, PubMed:14656221). Can recognize succinate as a
CC       substrate, but its affinity for succinate is several fold lower than
CC       for citrate (PubMed:12177002, PubMed:14656221). The stoichiometry is
CC       probably 4 Na(+) for each carboxylate, irrespective of whether the
CC       translocated substrate is divalent or trivalent, rendering the process
CC       electrogenic (PubMed:12177002, PubMed:14656221). Involved in the
CC       regulation of citrate levels in the brain (By similarity).
