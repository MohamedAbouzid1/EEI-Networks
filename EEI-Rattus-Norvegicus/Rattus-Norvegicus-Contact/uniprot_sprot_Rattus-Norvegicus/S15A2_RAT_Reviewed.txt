CC   -!- FUNCTION: Proton-coupled amino-acid transporter that transports
CC       oligopeptides of 2 to 4 amino acids with a preference for dipeptides
CC       (PubMed:8639691). Transports neutral and anionic dipeptides with a
CC       proton to peptide stoichiometry of 2:1 or 3:1 (PubMed:8639691). In
CC       kidney, involved in the absorption of circulating di- and tripeptides
CC       from the glomerular filtrate. Can also transport beta-lactam
CC       antibiotics, such as the aminocephalosporin cefadroxil, and other
CC       antiviral and anticancer drugs (By similarity). Transports the
CC       dipeptide-like aminopeptidase inhibitor bestatin (By similarity). Also
CC       able to transport carnosine (PubMed:15056281). Involved in innate
CC       immunity by promoting the detection of microbial pathogens by NOD-like
CC       receptors (NLRs) (By similarity). Mediates transport of bacterial
CC       peptidoglycans across the plasma membrane or, in macrophages, the
CC       phagosome membrane: catalyzes the transport of certain bacterial
CC       peptidoglycans, such as muramyl dipeptide (MDP), the NOD2 ligand (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q16348,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15056281, ECO:0000269|PubMed:8639691}.
