CC   -!- FUNCTION: Multifunctional anion transporter that operates via two
CC       distinct transport mechanisms, namely proton-coupled anion cotransport
CC       and membrane potential-dependent anion transport (By similarity).
CC       Electroneutral proton-coupled acidic monosaccharide symporter, with a
CC       sugar to proton stoichiometry of 1:1. Exports glucuronic acid and free
CC       sialic acid derived from sialoglycoconjugate degradation out of
CC       lysosomes, driven by outwardly directed lysosomal pH gradient. May
CC       regulate lysosome function and metabolism of sialylated conjugates that
CC       impact oligodendrocyte lineage differentiation and myelinogenesis in
CC       the central nervous system (By similarity) (PubMed:15516337,
CC       PubMed:20424173). Electrogenic proton-coupled nitrate symporter that
CC       transports nitrate ions across the basolateral membrane of salivary
CC       gland acinar cells, with nitrate to proton stoichiometry of 2:1. May
CC       contribute to nitrate clearance from serum by salivary glands, where it
CC       is further concentrated and secreted in the saliva (By similarity).
CC       Uses membrane potential to drive the uptake of acidic amino acids and
CC       peptides into synaptic vesicles. Responsible for synaptic vesicular
CC       storage of L-aspartate and L-glutamate in pinealocytes as well as
CC       vesicular uptake of N-acetyl-L-aspartyl-L-glutamate neuropeptide,
CC       relevant to aspartegic-associated glutamatergic neurotransmission and
CC       activation of metabotropic receptors that inhibit subsequent
CC       transmitter release (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8BN82,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9NRA2, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15516337,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:20424173}.
CC   -!- FUNCTION: Receptor for CM101, a polysaccharide produced by group B
CC       Streptococcus with antipathoangiogenic properties.
