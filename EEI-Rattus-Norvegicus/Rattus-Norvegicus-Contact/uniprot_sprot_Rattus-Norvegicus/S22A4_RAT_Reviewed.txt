CC   -!- FUNCTION: Transporter that mediates the transport of endogenous and
CC       microbial zwitterions and organic cations (PubMed:18670092). Functions
CC       as a Na(+)-dependent and pH-dependent high affinity microbial symporter
CC       of potent food-derived antioxidant ergothioeine (PubMed:18670092).
CC       Transports one sodium ion with one ergothioeine molecule
CC       (PubMed:18670092). Involved in the absorption of ergothioneine from the
CC       luminal/apical side of the small intestine and renal tubular cells, and
CC       into non-parenchymal liver cells, thereby contributing to maintain
CC       steady-state ergothioneine level in the body (By similarity). Also
CC       mediates the bidirectional transport of acetycholine, although the
CC       exact transport mechanism has not been fully identified yet (By
CC       similarity). Most likely exports anti-inflammatory acetylcholine in
CC       non-neuronal tissues, thereby contributing to the non-neuronal
CC       cholinergic system (By similarity). Displays a general physiological
CC       role linked to better survival by controlling inflammation and
CC       oxidative stress, which may be related to ergothioneine and
CC       acetycholine transports (By similarity). May contribute to regulate the
CC       transport of cationic compounds in testis across the blood-testis-
CC       barrier (PubMed:17616214). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9H015,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:17616214, ECO:0000269|PubMed:18670092}.

CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed in intestine, liver and kidney. Weakly
CC       expressed in brain, thymus, lung, spleen, heart and skin. In brain, it
CC       is expressed in cerebellum, especially in the granular layer, in
CC       hippocampus and cortex. In kidney, it is expressed in cortex and
CC       medulla with relatively more abundance in the cortical-medullary
CC       junction. In heart, it is expressed in myocardium and valves. Expressed
CC       labyrinthine zone of the placenta (PubMed:10825452). Expressed in
CC       Sertoli cells in testis (PubMed:17616214).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10825452, ECO:0000269|PubMed:17616214}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: Mediates the Na(+)-independent and pH-dependent
CC       bidirectional transport of xenobiotic tetraethylammonium (TEA).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10825452}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the major facilitator (TC 2.A.1) superfamily.
CC       Organic cation transporter (TC 2.A.1.19) family. {ECO:0000305}.
CC   -!- CAUTION: Unlike human or mouse orthologs, does not mediate carnitine
CC       uptake. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10825452}.
