CC   -!- FUNCTION: Electroneutral antiporter that translocates urate across the
CC       apical membrane of proximal tubular cells in exchange for monovalent
CC       organic or inorganic anions (PubMed:21074513). Involved in renal
CC       reabsorption of urate and helps maintaining blood levels of uric acid
CC       (PubMed:21074513). Mediates urate uptake by an exchange with organic
CC       anions such as (S)-lactate and nicotinate, and inorganic anion Cl(-)
CC       (PubMed:21074513). Other inorganic anions such as Br(-), I(-) and
CC       NO3(-) may also act as counteranions that exchange for urate (By
CC       similarity). Also mediates orotate tubular uptake coupled with
CC       nicotinate efflux and to a lesser extent with lactate efflux, therefore
CC       displaying a potential role in orotate renal reabsorption. Orotate
CC       transport is Cl(-)-dependent (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q96S37, ECO:0000269|PubMed:21074513}.
