CC   -!- FUNCTION: Mitochondrial transporter required for glutathione import
CC       into mitochondria. Glutathione, which plays key roles in oxidative
CC       metabolism, is produced exclusively in the cytosol and is imported in
CC       many organelles. Mitochondrial glutathione is required for the activity
CC       and stability of proteins containing iron-sulfur clusters, as well as
CC       erythropoiesis. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9BZJ4}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY:
CC       Reaction=glutathione(in) = glutathione(out); Xref=Rhea:RHEA:74819,
CC         ChEBI:CHEBI:57925; Evidence={ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9BZJ4};
CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: The activity of SLC25A39 is regulated by levels of
CC       mitochondrial glutathione via its ability to bind [2Fe-2S] iron-sulfur
CC       cluster. Upon physiological levels of mitochondrial glutathione,
CC       glutathione prevents iron-sulfur-binding to SLC25A39 promoting cleavage
CC       and degradation by AFG3L2. Upon depletion of mitochondrial glutathione,
CC       SLC25A39 binds iron-sulfur, preventing cleavage and degradation by
CC       AFG3L2. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9BZJ4}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Mitochondrion inner membrane
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9BZJ4}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000255}.
CC   -!- PTM: Cleaved and degraded by AFG3L2; degradation by AFG3L2 is regulated
CC       by the ability of SLC25A39 to bind iron-sulfur. In absence of
CC       mitochondrial glutathione, SLC25A39 binds iron-sulfur, preventing
CC       cleavage and degradation by AFG3L2. The presence of mitochondrial
CC       glutathione prevents iron-sulfur-binding to SLC25A39, promoting
CC       cleavage and degradation by AFG3L2. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9BZJ4}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the mitochondrial carrier (TC 2.A.29) family.
CC       {ECO:0000305}.
