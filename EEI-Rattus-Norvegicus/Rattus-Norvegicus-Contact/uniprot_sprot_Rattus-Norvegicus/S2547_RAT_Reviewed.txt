CC   -!- FUNCTION: Mitochondrial NAD(+) transporter that acts as a 'metabolic
CC       gate' in hepatic lipogenesis. Provides NAD(+) substrate to
CC       mitochondrial SIRT3 deacetylase and enables its NAD(+)-dependent
CC       activities in mitochondrial energy metabolism. This triggers downstream
CC       activation of PRKAA1/AMPK-alpha signaling cascade that negatively
CC       regulates sterol regulatory element-binding protein (SREBP)
CC       transcriptional activities and ATP-consuming lipogenesis to restore
CC       cellular energy balance. May transport other mitochondrial metabolites
CC       having an aromatic nucleotide and phosphate groups, such as acetyl-CoA.
CC       Does not transport amino acids. The transport mechanism remains to be
CC       elucidated. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q6IS41}.
