CC   -!- FUNCTION: Sulfate transporter which mediates sulfate uptake into
CC       chondrocytes in order to maintain adequate sulfation of proteoglycans
CC       which is needed for cartilage development (PubMed:9575183). Mediates
CC       electroneutral anion exchange of sulfate ions for oxalate ions, sulfate
CC       and oxalate ions for chloride and/or hydroxyl ions and chloride ions
CC       for bromide, iodide and nitrate ions (By similarity). The coupling of
CC       sulfate transport to both hydroxyl and chloride ions likely serves to
CC       ensure transport at both acidic pH when most sulfate uptake is mediated
CC       by sulfate-hydroxide exchange and alkaline pH when most sulfate uptake
CC       is mediated by sulfate-chloride exchange (By similarity). Essential for
CC       chondrocyte proliferation, differentiation and cell size expansion (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q62273,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9575183}.
