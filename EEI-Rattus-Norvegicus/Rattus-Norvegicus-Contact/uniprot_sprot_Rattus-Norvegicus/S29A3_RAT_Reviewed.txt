CC   -!- FUNCTION: Uniporter that mediates the facilitative transport of
CC       nucleoside across lysosomal and mitochondrial membranes. Functions as a
CC       non-electrogenic Na(+)-independent transporter. Substrate transport is
CC       pH-dependent and enhanced under acidic condition, probably reflecting
CC       the location of the transporter in acidic intracellular compartments.
CC       Proton is not a cotransporting ion but most likely change the
CC       ionization state of the transporter which dictates transport-
CC       permissible/impermissible conformation for nucleoside translocation.
CC       May direct the nucleoside transport from lysosomes to cytosol or
CC       cytosol to mitochondria to facilitate the fundamental function of
CC       salvage synthesis of nucleic acids. Involved in the transport of
CC       nucleosides (adenosine, guanosine, uridine, thymidine, cytidine and
CC       inosine) and deoxynucleosides (deoxyadenosine, deoxycytidine). Also
CC       mediates transport of purine nucleobases (adenine, guanine) and
CC       pyrimidine nucleobases (uracil). Also able to transport monoamine
CC       neurotransmitters dopamine, serotonin, noradrenaline and tyramine.
CC       Capable of transporting ATP (By similarity). Mediates nucleoside export
CC       from lysosomes in macrophages, which regulates macrophage functions and
CC       numbers (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q99P65,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9BZD2}.
