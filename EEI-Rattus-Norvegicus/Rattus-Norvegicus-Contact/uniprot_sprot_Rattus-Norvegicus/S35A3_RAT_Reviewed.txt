CC   -!- FUNCTION: Transports diphosphate-N-acetylglucosamine (UDP-GlcNAc) from
CC       the cytosol into the lumen of the Golgi apparatus, functioning as an
CC       antiporter that exchanges UDP-N-acetyl-alpha-D-glucosamine for UMP. May
CC       supply UDP-GlcNAc as substrate for Golgi-resident glycosyltransferases
CC       that generate highly branched, multiantennary complex N-glycans and
CC       keratan sulfate. However, the exact role of SLC35A3 still needs to be
CC       elucidated, it could be a member of a catalytically more efficient
CC       multiprotein complex rather than function independently as a single
CC       transporter. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Y2D2}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY:

CC         ChEBI:CHEBI:57705, ChEBI:CHEBI:57865;
CC         Evidence={ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Y2D2};
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts with SLC35A2; the interaction is reduced in the
CC       presence of SLC35A4. Found in a complex with SLC35A2 and SLC35A4.
CC       Interacts with MGAT4B. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Y2D2}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Golgi apparatus membrane

CC   -!- PTM: O-Glcnacylation regulates the stability of SLC35A3 and the

CC       subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
