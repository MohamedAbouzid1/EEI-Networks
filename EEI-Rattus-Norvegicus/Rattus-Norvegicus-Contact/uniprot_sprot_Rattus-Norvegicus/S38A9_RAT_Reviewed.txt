CC   -!- FUNCTION: Lysosomal amino acid transporter involved in the activation
CC       of mTORC1 in response to amino acid levels. Probably acts as an amino
CC       acid sensor of the Rag GTPases and Ragulator complexes, 2 complexes
CC       involved in amino acid sensing and activation of mTORC1, a signaling
CC       complex promoting cell growth in response to growth factors, energy
CC       levels, and amino acids. Following activation by amino acids, the
CC       Ragulator and Rag GTPases function as a scaffold recruiting mTORC1 to
CC       lysosomes where it is in turn activated. SLC38A9 mediates transport of
CC       amino acids with low capacity and specificity with a slight preference
CC       for polar amino acids. Acts as an arginine sensor. Following activation
CC       by arginine binding, mediates transport of L-glutamine, leucine and
CC       tyrosine with high efficiency, and is required for the efficient
CC       utilization of these amino acids after lysosomal protein degradation.
CC       However, the transport mechanism is not well defined and the role of
CC       sodium is not clear. Can disassemble the lysosomal folliculin complex
CC       (LFC), and thereby triggers GAP activity of FLCN:FNIP2 toward RRAGC.
CC       Acts as an cholesterol sensor that conveys increases in lysosomal
CC       cholesterol, leading to lysosomal recruitment and activation of mTORC1
CC       via the Rag GTPases. Guanine exchange factor (GEF) that, upon arginine
CC       binding, stimulates GDP release from RRAGA and therefore activates the
CC       Rag GTPase heterodimer and the mTORC1 pathway in response to nutrient
CC       sufficiency. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8NBW4}.

CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Associated component of the Ragulator complex (composed of
CC       LAMTOR1, LAMTOR2, LAMTOR3, LAMTOR4 and LAMTOR5). Associated component
CC       of the Rag GTPases heterodimers (composed of RRAGA, RRAGB, RRAGC and
CC       RRAGD); this interaction is independent of the Ragulator complex but
CC       depends on the nucleotide loading state of the Rag GTPase heterodimer.
CC       Interacts with TM4SF5. Interacts with NPC1; this interaction inhibits
CC       cholesterol-mediated mTORC1 activation via its sterol transport
CC       activity. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8NBW4}.

CC       Multi-pass membrane protein {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q08BA4}. Late
CC       endosome membrane {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8NBW4}; Multi-pass membrane
CC       protein {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q08BA4}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The cytosolic N-terminus part of the protein mediates
CC       interaction with the Ragulator complex. The cytosolic N-terminus part
CC       of the protein destabilizes the LFC and thereby triggers GAP activity
CC       of FLCN:FNIP2 toward RRAGC. The cytosolic N-terminus part of the
CC       protein mediates interaction with the Rag GTPase heterodimer in a RRAGA
CC       GDP-loaded state dependent and upon arginine binding, leading to the
CC       GDP release and SLC38A9 dissociation from the activated Rag GTPase
CC       heterodimer (By similarity). The cytosolic N-terminus part of the
CC       protein exists at least in two distinct conformations; The first is
CC       when the N-terminus is bound snugly in the arginine binding site (in
CC       the absence of arginine, low luminal arginine state) and the second is
CC       where the N-terminus is released and the substrate-binding site is
CC       occupied by arginine (in the presence of arginine, high luminal
CC       arginine state) (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q08BA4,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8NBW4}.
