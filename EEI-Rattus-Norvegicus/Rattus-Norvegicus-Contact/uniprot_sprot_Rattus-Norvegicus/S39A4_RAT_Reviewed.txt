CC   -!- FUNCTION: Selective transporter that mediates the uptake of Zn(2+).
CC       Plays an essential role for dietary zinc uptake from small intestine
CC       (By similarity). The Zn(2+) uniporter activity is regulated by zinc
CC       availability. Exhibits also polyspecific binding and transport of
CC       Cu(2+), Cd(2+) and possibly Ni(2+) but at higher concentrations (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q6P5W5,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q78IQ7}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY:
CC       Reaction=Zn(2+)(in) = Zn(2+)(out); Xref=Rhea:RHEA:29351,
CC         ChEBI:CHEBI:29105; Evidence={ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q6P5W5};
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer; homodimerization is mediated by the transmembrane
CC       domain. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q6P5W5}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q78IQ7};
CC       Multi-pass membrane protein {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:A0A0H3LM39}.
CC       Recycling endosome membrane {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q78IQ7}; Multi-pass
CC       membrane protein {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:A0A0H3LM39}. Apical cell
CC       membrane {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q78IQ7}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:A0A0H3LM39}. Note=Colocalized with TFRC in the
CC       recycling endosomes. Cycles between endosomal compartments and the
CC       plasma membrane in response to zinc availability. Translocates to the
CC       apical membrane during zinc deficiency. Expressed on the apical surface
CC       of the intestinal wall. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q78IQ7}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed in duodenum, jejunum, and ileum.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:22466565}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The two metal binding sites M1 and M2 that are halfway through
CC       the membrane form a binuclear metal center. M1 is essential to Zn(2+)
CC       transport, while the other, M2 appears to have an auxiliary role
CC       presumably by acting as an additional transport site that can modulate
CC       the properties of the primary transport site. The binuclear metal
CC       center plays a key role in zinc sensing.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q6P5W5}.
CC   -!- PTM: The extracellular N-terminal ectodomain is cleaved when cells are
CC       Zn(2+) deficient, N-terminally cleaved SLC39A4 is internalized at a
CC       faster rate. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q78IQ7}.
CC   -!- PTM: Under excess Zn(2+) conditions, SLC39A4 on the cell surface is
CC       rapidly endocytosed, ubiquitinated and degraded.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q6P5W5}.
CC   -!- PTM: Glycosylated. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q6P5W5}.
