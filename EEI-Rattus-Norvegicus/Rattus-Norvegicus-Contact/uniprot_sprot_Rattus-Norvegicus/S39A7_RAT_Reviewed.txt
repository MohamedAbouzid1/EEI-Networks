CC   -!- FUNCTION: Transports Zn(2+) from the endoplasmic reticulum (ER)/Golgi
CC       apparatus to the cytosol, playing an essential role in the regulation
CC       of cytosolic zinc levels. Acts as a gatekeeper of zinc release from
CC       intracellular stores, requiring post-translational activation by
CC       phosphorylation on residues, resulting in activation of multiple
CC       downstream pathways leading to cell growth and proliferation. Has an
CC       essential role in B cell development and is required for proper B cell
CC       receptor signaling (By similarity). Plays an important role in
CC       maintaining intestinal epithelial homeostasis and skin dermis
CC       development by regulating ER function. Controls cell signaling pathways
CC       involved in glucose metabolism in skeletal muscle (By similarity). Has
CC       a protective role against ER stress in different biological contexts
CC       (PubMed:28232492). Mediates Zn(2+)-induced ferroptosis (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q31125, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q92504,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:28232492}.
