CC   -!- FUNCTION: Electroneutral divalent metal cation:bicarbonate symporter of
CC       the plasma membrane mediating the cellular uptake of zinc and
CC       manganese, two divalent metal cations important for development, tissue
CC       homeostasis and immunity (PubMed:22898811). Transports an
CC       electroneutral complex composed of a divalent metal cation and two
CC       bicarbonate anions or alternatively a bicarbonate and a selenite anion
CC       (PubMed:22898811). Thereby, it also contributes to the cellular uptake
CC       of selenium, an essential trace metal and micronutrient
CC       (PubMed:22898811). Also imports cadmium a non-essential metal which is
CC       cytotoxic and carcinogenic (PubMed:22898811). May also transport iron
CC       and cobalt through membranes (PubMed:22898811). Through zinc import,
CC       indirectly regulates the metal-dependent transcription factor MTF1 and
CC       the expression of some metalloproteases involved in cartilage
CC       catabolism and also probably heart development. Also indirectly
CC       regulates the expression of proteins involved in cell morphology and
CC       cytoskeleton organization. Indirectly controls innate immune function
CC       and inflammatory response by regulating zinc cellular uptake which in
CC       turn modulates the expression of genes specific of these processes.
CC       Protects, for instance, cells from injury and death at the onset of
CC       inflammation (By similarity). By regulating zinc influx into monocytes
CC       also directly modulates their adhesion to endothelial cells and
CC       arteries (By similarity). Reclaims manganese from the bile at the
CC       apical membrane of hepatocytes, thereby regulating the activity of the
CC       manganese-dependent enzymes through the systemic levels of the
CC       nutrient. Also participates in manganese reabsorption in the proximal
CC       tubule of the kidney. By mediating the extracellular uptake of
CC       manganese by cells of the blood-brain barrier, may also play a role in
CC       the transport of the micronutrient to the brain. With manganese
CC       cellular uptake also participates in mitochondrial proper function (By
CC       similarity). Finally, also probably functions intracellularly,
CC       translocating zinc from lysosome to cytosol to indirectly enhance the
CC       expression of specific genes during TCR-mediated T cell activation (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q91W10,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9C0K1, ECO:0000269|PubMed:22898811}.
