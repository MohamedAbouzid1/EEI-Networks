CC   -!- FUNCTION: Transports zinc ions across cell and organelle membranes into
CC       the cytoplasm and regulates intracellular zinc homeostasis.
CC       Participates in the zinc ions efflux out of the secretory compartments.
CC       Regulates intracellular zinc level, resulting in the enhancement of
CC       AKT1 and MAPK3/MAPK1 (Erk1/2) phosphorylation in response to the BCR
CC       activation (By similarity). Also functions as a membrane androgen
CC       receptor that mediates, through a G protein, the non-classical androgen
CC       signaling pathway, characterized by the activation of MAPK3/MAPK1
CC       (Erk1/2) and transcription factors CREB1 or ATF1 (PubMed:27164415,
CC       PubMed:35625396). This pathway contributes to CLDN1 and CLDN5
CC       expression and tight junction formation between adjacent Sertoli cells
CC       (PubMed:27164415, PubMed:35625396). Mediates androgen-induced vascular
CC       endothelial cell proliferation through activation of an inhibitory G
CC       protein leading to the AKT1 and MAPK3/MAPK1 (Erk1/2) activation which
CC       in turn modulate inhibition (phosphorylation) of GSK3B and CCND1
CC       transcription. Moreover, has dual functions as a membrane-bound
CC       androgen receptor and as an androgen-dependent zinc transporter both of
CC       which are mediated through an inhibitory G protein (Gi) that mediates
CC       both MAP kinase and zinc signaling leading to the androgen-dependent
CC       apoptotic process (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9NUM3,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:27164415, ECO:0000269|PubMed:35625396}.
