CC   -!- FUNCTION: Transports Fe(2+) from the inside of a cell to the outside of
CC       the cell, playing a key role for maintaining systemic iron homeostasis
CC       (By similarity). Transports iron from intestinal, splenic, hepatic
CC       cells, macrophages and erythrocytes into the blood to provide iron to
CC       other tissues. Controls therefore dietary iron uptake, iron recycling
CC       by macrophages and erythrocytes, and release of iron stores in
CC       hepatocytes (By similarity). When iron is in excess in serum,
CC       circulating HAMP/hepcidin levels increase resulting in a degradation of
CC       SLC40A1, thus limiting the iron efflux to plasma (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9JHI9, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9NP59}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY:
CC       Reaction=Fe(2+)(in) = Fe(2+)(out); Xref=Rhea:RHEA:28486,
CC         ChEBI:CHEBI:29033; Evidence={ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9NP59};
CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: During elevated serum iron levels, liver-derived
CC       hepcidin/HAMP negatively regulates cell surface SLC40A1 by inducing its
CC       ubiquitination, internalization, and degradation. Indeed, hepcidin/HAMP
CC       affinity towards ferroportin/SLC40A1 increases by 80-fold in the
CC       presence of iron. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9NP59}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Identified in a complex with STOM. Interacts with HAMP;
CC       affinity of the peptide hormone HAMP for SLC40A1 increases by 80-fold
CC       in the presence of iron and the interaction promotes SLC40A1
CC       ubiquitination and degradation. Part of a complex composed of
CC       SLC40A1/ferroportin, TF/transferrin and HEPH/hephaestin that transfers
CC       iron from cells to transferrin. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9NP59}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9NP59};
CC       Multi-pass membrane protein {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9NP59}. Basolateral
CC       cell membrane {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9NP59}; Multi-pass membrane
CC       protein {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9NP59}. Note=Localized to the
CC       basolateral membrane of polarized epithelial cells.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9NP59}.
CC   -!- PTM: Polyubiquitinated by RNF217; leading to proteasomal degradation
CC       (By similarity). Under conditions of high systemic iron levels, both
CC       the hormone peptide hepcidin/HAMP and holo(iron bound)-transferrin/TF
CC       induce the ubiquitination, internalization and proteasomal degradation
CC       of SLC40A1 to control iron release from cells (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9JHI9, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9NP59}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the ferroportin (FP) (TC 2.A.100) family. SLC40A
CC       subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
CC   -!- CAUTION: Manganese (Mn) transport by SLC40A1 remains controversial.
CC       Some in vitro studies have suggested that SLC40A1 transports minimal
CC       amounts of Mn(2+) (By similarity). Other groups have suggested that it
CC       does not. The affinity of SLC40A1 for manganese is extremely low
CC       compared with iron, implying that any SLC40A1-mediated Mn transport in
CC       vivo would likely be trivial (By similarity). A recent study examined
CC       the role of SLC40A1 in Mn homeostasis by using Tmprss6-O mice, which
CC       express high levels of hepcidin/HAMP and therefore have very low
CC       SLC40A1 levels in their tissues. These mice show frank iron deficiency
CC       and reduced iron levels in most tissues, but manganese levels are
CC       largely unaffected (By similarity). These studies suggest that
CC       manganese is propably not the physiological substrate of SLC40A1.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9JHI9, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9NP59,
CC       ECO:0000305}.
