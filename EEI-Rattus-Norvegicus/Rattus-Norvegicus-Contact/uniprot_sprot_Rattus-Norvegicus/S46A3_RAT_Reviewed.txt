CC   -!- FUNCTION: Lysosomal proton-coupled steroid conjugate and bile acid
CC       transporter. Preferentially recognizes lipophilic steroid conjugates or
CC       bile acis as endogenous substrates and seems to mediate escape from
CC       lysosomes to the cytoplasm (By similarity). Modulates hepatic cytosolic
CC       copper homeostasis, maybe acting as a lysosomal copper transporter and
CC       sequestering copper ions in the lysosome (By similarity). Delivers
CC       pathogen-associated molecular patterns to cytosolic pattern recognition
CC       receptors as part of the innate immune response to microbes.
CC       Selectively transports bacterial muramyl dipeptide (MDP) into the
CC       cytosol for recognition by NOD2, triggering inflammatory responses (By
CC       similarity). Likely acts as a redundant importer of cyclic GMP-AMP
CC       dinucleotides (cGAMPs) in monocyte and macrophage cell lineages. The
CC       transport mechanism, its electrogenicity and stoichiometry remain to be
CC       elucidated (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q7Z3Q1,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9DC26}.
