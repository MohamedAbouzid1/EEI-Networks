CC   -!- FUNCTION: Small GTPase that cycles between an active GTP-bound and an
CC       inactive GDP-bound state and mainly functions in vesicle-mediated
CC       endoplasmic reticulum (ER) to Golgi transport. The active GTP-bound
CC       form inserts into the endoplasmic reticulum membrane where it recruits
CC       the remainder of the coat protein complex II/COPII. The coat protein
CC       complex II assembling and polymerizing on endoplasmic reticulum
CC       membrane is responsible for both the sorting of cargos and the
CC       deformation and budding of membranes into vesicles destined to the
CC       Golgi. In contrast to SAR1A, SAR1B specifically interacts with the
CC       cargo receptor SURF4 to mediate the transport of lipid-carrying
CC       lipoproteins including APOB and APOA1 from the endoplasmic reticulum to
CC       the Golgi and thereby, indirectly regulates lipid homeostasis. In
CC       addition to its role in vesicle trafficking, can also function as a
CC       leucine sensor regulating TORC1 signaling and more indirectly cellular
CC       metabolism, growth and survival. In absence of leucine, interacts with
CC       the GATOR2 complex via MIOS and inhibits TORC1 signaling. The binding
CC       of leucine abrogates the interaction with GATOR2 and the inhibition of
CC       the TORC1 signaling. This function is completely independent of the
CC       GTPase activity of SAR1B. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Y6B6}.

CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: Small GTPases activation is mediated by guanine

CC       bound GTP is stimulated by GTPase activating proteins (GAP) (By
CC       similarity). Activated by the guanine nucleotide exchange factor
CC       PREB/SEC12 that facilitates the loading of SAR1B with GTP (By
CC       similarity). GTP hydrolysis is stimulated by SEC23/24 (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9QVY3, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Y6B6}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer; upon association with membrane. Part of the coat
CC       protein complex II/COPII, composed of SEC23/24 and SEC13/31
CC       heterodimers, that it helps recruit and assemble on endoplasmic
CC       reticulum (ER) membranes at ER exit site. Interacts with PREB; PREB
CC       acts as a guanine nucleotide exchange factor facilitating the
CC       activation of SAR1B by loading it with GTP. Interacts with SURF4;
CC       recruits the cargo receptor SURF4 and its lipoprotein cargos to COPII-
CC       coated ER to Golgi transport vesicles. Interacts with MIOS; the
CC       interaction is direct, disrupted by the binding of leucine and mediates
CC       the interaction of SAR1B with the GATOR2 complex to negatively regulate
CC       the TORC1 signaling upon leucine deprivation.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Y6B6}.

CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Y6B6}. Note=Active at endoplasmic reticulum
CC       exit sites (ERES) where it inserts into the membrane and recruits the
CC       remainder of the coat protein complex II/COPII. Upon leucine
CC       deprivation, associates with lysosomal membranes to repress TORC1
