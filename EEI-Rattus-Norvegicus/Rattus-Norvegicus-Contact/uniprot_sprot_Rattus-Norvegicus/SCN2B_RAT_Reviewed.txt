CC   -!- FUNCTION: Regulatory subunit of multiple voltage-gated sodium (Nav)
CC       channels directly mediating the depolarization of excitable membranes.
CC       Navs, also called VGSCs (voltage-gated sodium channels) or VDSCs
CC       (voltage-dependent sodium channels), operate by switching between
CC       closed and open conformations depending on the voltage difference
CC       across the membrane. In the open conformation they allow Na(+) ions to
CC       selectively pass through the pore, along their electrochemical
CC       gradient. The influx of Na+ ions provokes membrane depolarization,
CC       initiating the propagation of electrical signals throughout cells and
CC       tissues (PubMed:15178439, PubMed:22992729, PubMed:26894959). The
CC       accessory beta subunits participate in localization and functional
CC       modulation of the Nav channels (PubMed:15178439, PubMed:22992729,
CC       PubMed:26894959). Modulates the activity of SCN1A/Nav1.1, SCN2A/Nav1.2,
CC       SCN2A/Nav1.3, SCN5A/Nav1.5, SCN8A/Nav1.6, SCN9A/Nav1.7 and
CC       SCN10A/Nav1.8 (By similarity) (PubMed:15178439, PubMed:22992729,
CC       PubMed:26894959). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O60939,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15178439, ECO:0000269|PubMed:22992729,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:26894959}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Voltage-gated sodium (Nav) channel consists of an ion-
CC       conducting pore-forming alpha subunit functional on its own that is
CC       regulated by one or more beta subunits (PubMed:22992729,
CC       PubMed:26894959). The beta subunit SCN2B is disulfide-linked to the
CC       pore-forming alpha subunit (PubMed:22992729, PubMed:26894959).
CC       Interacts with SCN1A; regulatory subunit of SCN1A/Nav1.1
CC       (PubMed:22992729). Interacts with SCN2A; regulatory subunit of
CC       SCN2A/Nav1.2 (PubMed:26894959). Interacts with SCN3A; regulatory
CC       subunit of SCN3A/Nav1.3. Interacts with SCN5A; regulatory subunit of
CC       SCN5A/Nav1.5. Interacts with SCN8A; regulatory subunit of SCN8A/Nav1.6.
CC       Interacts with SCN9A; regulatory subunit of SCN9A/Nav1.7 (By
CC       similarity). Interacts with SCN10A; regulatory subunit of SCN10A/Nav1.8
CC       (PubMed:15178439). Interacts with TNR; may play a crucial role in
CC       clustering and regulation of activity of SCN2B-containing Nav channels
CC       at nodes of Ranvier (PubMed:9861042). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O60939,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15178439, ECO:0000269|PubMed:22992729,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:26894959, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9861042}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000269|PubMed:22992729};
CC       Single-pass type I membrane protein {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O60939}.
CC       Cell projection, axon {ECO:0000269|PubMed:22992729}. Note=Clusters at
CC       the axon initial segment and node of Ranvier, the specialized neuronal
CC       subcellular domains involved in action potentials generation and
CC       propagation. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:22992729}.
CC   -!- DEVELOPMENTAL STAGE: Detected in the earliest phase of neurogenesis in
CC       brain, and expression is greatly increased concomitant with axon
CC       extension and synaptogenesis.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the sodium channel auxiliary subunit SCN2B (TC
CC       8.A.17) family. {ECO:0000305}.
