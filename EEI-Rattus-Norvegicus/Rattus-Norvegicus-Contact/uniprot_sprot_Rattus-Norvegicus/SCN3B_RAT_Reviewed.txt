CC   -!- FUNCTION: Regulatory subunit of multiple voltage-gated sodium (Nav)
CC       channels directly mediating the depolarization of excitable membranes.
CC       Navs, also called VGSCs (voltage-gated sodium channels) or VDSCs
CC       (voltage-dependent sodium channels), operate by switching between
CC       closed and open conformations depending on the voltage difference
CC       across the membrane. In the open conformation they allow Na(+) ions to
CC       selectively pass through the pore, along their electrochemical
CC       gradient. The influx of Na+ ions provokes membrane depolarization,
CC       initiating the propagation of electrical signals throughout cells and
CC       tissues (PubMed:10688874, PubMed:11922146). The accessory beta subunits
CC       participate in localization and functional modulation of the Nav
CC       channels (PubMed:10688874, PubMed:11470829, PubMed:11922146). Voltage-
CC       gated sodium channels regulatory subunit that modulates channel gating
CC       kinetics (PubMed:10688874, PubMed:11922146). Modulates the activity of
CC       SCN2A/Nav1.2, causing a hyperpolarizing shift in the voltage-dependence
CC       of inactivation and increasing the fraction of channels operating in
CC       the fast gating mode (PubMed:10688874, PubMed:11922146). Also able to
CC       induce unique persistent SCN2A/Nav1.2-mediated sodium currents
CC       (PubMed:11922146). Could modulate the activity of SCN10A/Nav1.8
CC       (PubMed:14975698, PubMed:15178439). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10688874,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:11470829, ECO:0000269|PubMed:11922146,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:14975698, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15178439}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Voltage-gated sodium (Nav) channel consists of an ion-
CC       conducting pore-forming alpha subunit functional on its own that is
CC       regulated by one or more beta subunits (PubMed:11922146,
CC       PubMed:15178439). Forms homodimers and homotrimers. SCN3B is non-
CC       covalently associated with alpha subunits and induces the formation of
CC       alpha subunit oligomers, including trimers (PubMed:11922146). Interacts
CC       with SCN5A/Nav1.5; regulatory subunit of SCN5A/Nav1.5 (By similarity).
CC       Interacts with SCN7A/Nav2.1; probable regulatory subunit of
CC       SCN7A/Nav2.1 (By similarity). Interacts with SCN10A; regulatory subunit
CC       of SCN10A/Nav1.8. Interacts with NFASC; probably involved in targeting
CC       the sodium channels to the nodes of Ranvier (PubMed:11470829).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9NY72, ECO:0000269|PubMed:11470829,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:11922146, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15178439}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9NY72};
CC       Single-pass type I membrane protein {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9NY72}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed broadly in neurons in the central and
CC       peripheral nervous systems, but not in glia and most non-neuronal
CC       cells. Weak detection in lung and adrenal gland.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11922146}.
CC   -!- PTM: Intramolecular disulfide bonds favor the voltage-gated sodium
CC       channel oligomeric complex assembly. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9NY72}.
CC   -!- PTM: N-glycosylated. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9NY72}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the sodium channel auxiliary subunit SCN3B (TC
CC       8.A.17) family. {ECO:0000305}.
