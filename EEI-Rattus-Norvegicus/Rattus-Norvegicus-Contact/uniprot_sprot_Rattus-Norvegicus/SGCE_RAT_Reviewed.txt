CC   -!- FUNCTION: Component of the sarcoglycan complex, a subcomplex of the
CC       dystrophin-glycoprotein complex which forms a link between the F-actin
CC       cytoskeleton and the extracellular matrix.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O43556}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane, sarcolemma {ECO:0000250}; Single-
CC       pass membrane protein {ECO:0000250}. Cytoplasm, cytoskeleton
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P82350}. Cell projection, dendrite
CC       {ECO:0000250}. Golgi apparatus {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: In both neural tissues including cerebellar cortex,
CC       striatum, cerebral cortex, thalamus and hippocampus, and non-neural
CC       tissues including quadriceps muscle, liver, kidney, spleen, lung,
CC       testis and heart. Widely distributed in the brain, with a robust signal
CC       obtained from regions with dense neuronal packing such as the pyramidal
CC       cell layer of the hippocampus, cerebellar molecular layer, and cerebral
CC       cortex. Levels are highest in kidney, moderate in brain and lung, and
CC       low in skeletal muscle, liver, spleen and testis.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:14625080}.
CC   -!- DEVELOPMENTAL STAGE: Expressed at embryonic day 20, postnatal days 1,
CC       7, 14, 36, six months and one and a half years. Highest level at 20
CC       dpc. In muscle, expression is over 10 times higher at 20 dpc and during
CC       the early postnatal period than in adults. In adults, expression levels
CC       are several-fold higher in brain, particularly in the cerebellar
CC       cortex, than in muscle. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:14625080}.
CC   -!- PTM: N-glycosylated. {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- PTM: Ubiquitinated, leading to its degradation by the proteasome.
CC       {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the sarcoglycan alpha/epsilon family.
CC       {ECO:0000255}.
