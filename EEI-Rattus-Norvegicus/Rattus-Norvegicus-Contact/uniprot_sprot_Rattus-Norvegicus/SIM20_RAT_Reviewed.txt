CC   -!- FUNCTION: [Small integral membrane protein 20]: Component of the MITRAC
CC       (mitochondrial translation regulation assembly intermediate of
CC       cytochrome c oxidase complex) complex, that regulates cytochrome c
CC       oxidase assembly (By similarity). Promotes the progression of complex
CC       assembly after the association of Mt-Co1/Cox11 with Cox4I1 and Cox6c
CC       (By similarity). Chaperone-like assembly factor required to stabilize
CC       newly synthesized Mt-Co1/Cox1 and to prevent its premature turnover (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8N5G0}.
CC   -!- FUNCTION: [Phoenixin-14]: Peptide involved in a broad spectrum of
CC       regulatory functions (PubMed:27440717, PubMed:28844870,
CC       PubMed:28965207, PubMed:29364701, PubMed:30609107). Is a ligand for
CC       GPR173 (PubMed:27440717). As part of the reproductive cycle, it
CC       regulates gonadotropin-releasing hormone (GnRH) signaling in the
CC       hypothalamus and pituitary gland which augments the release of
CC       luteinizing hormone (PubMed:22963497, PubMed:27440717). More
CC       specifically, it regulates the expression of transcription factors
CC       CEBPB and POU2F1/OCT1 through the cAMP-PKA signaling pathway, which
CC       subsequently regulate the expression of GNRHR and KISS1 (By
CC       similarity). Plays a protective role in memory retention through
CC       activation of GNRHR (By similarity). Regulates the secretion of AVP by
CC       hypothalamic neurons (PubMed:29364701). Plays a role in the
CC       transduction of the itch sensation (By similarity). Induces anxiolytic
CC       effects, reducing behavior associated with anxiety (By similarity).
CC       Regulates food intake as well as satiation and satiety by increasing
CC       NUCB2 expression in neurons (PubMed:28844870, PubMed:30930149). In the
CC       ovary, it regulates follicular growth by stimulating granulosa cell
CC       proliferation by increasing the expression of GPR173, CREB1, CYP19A1,
CC       KITLG, FSHR, and LHCGR (By similarity). It also increases the
CC       production of estradiol (E2) (By similarity). In the heart, it
CC       regulates contractility and relaxation by activating the AKT1-NOS3 and
CC       MAPK1-MAPK3 signaling pathways (PubMed:28965207). It also plays a
CC       cardioprotective role during ischemia, where it activates the SAFE and
CC       RISK pathways (PubMed:28965207). Stimulates the proliferation and
CC       differentiation of preadipocytes (PubMed:30251651). In pancreatic islet
CC       cells, it induces proliferation of islet cells as well as the
CC       production of INS1 and INS2 through activation of the MAPK1-MAPK3
CC       signaling pathways (PubMed:31422055). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:D3Z7Q2,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8N5G0, ECO:0000269|PubMed:22963497,
