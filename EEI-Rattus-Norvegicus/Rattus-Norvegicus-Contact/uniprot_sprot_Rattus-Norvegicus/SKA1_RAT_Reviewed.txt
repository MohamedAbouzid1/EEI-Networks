CC   -!- FUNCTION: Component of the SKA complex, a microtubule plus end-binding
CC       complex of the outer kinetochore that stabilizes spindle microtubule-
CC       kinetochore attachments, promotes alignment of chromosomes at the
CC       mitotic spindle equator (chromosome congression) and assists
CC       suppression of the spindle assembly checkpoint. Kinetochores,
CC       consisting of a centromere-associated inner segment and a microtubule-
CC       contacting outer segment, play a crucial role in chromosome segregation
CC       by mediating the physical connection between centromeric DNA and
CC       spindle microtubules. The outer kinetochore is made up of the ten-
CC       subunit KMN network complex, comprising the MIS12, NDC80 and KNL1
CC       complexes, and auxiliary microtubule-associated components such as the
CC       SKA complex; together they connect the outer kinetochore with the inner
CC       kinetochore, bind microtubules, and mediate interactions with mitotic
CC       checkpoint proteins that delay anaphase until chromosomes are
CC       bioriented on the spindle. The SKA complex is loaded onto bioriented
CC       kinetochores and it facilitates chromosome congression by stabilizing
CC       microtubules together with MAPRE1, and end-on attachment of the NDC80
CC       complex to depolymerizing spindle microtubules, thereby assisting the
CC       poleward-moving kinetochore in withstanding microtubule pulling forces.
CC       The complex associates with dynamic microtubule plus-ends and can track
CC       both depolymerizing and elongating microtubules. The complex recruits
CC       protein phosphatase 1 (PP1) to the kinetochore in prometaphase and
CC       metaphase, to oppose spindle assembly checkpoint signaling and promote
CC       the onset of anaphase. In the complex, it mediates interactions with
CC       microtubules. It also stimulates AURKB/Aurora B catalytic activity (By
CC       similarity). During meiosis the SKA complex stabilizes the meiotic
CC       spindle and is required for its migration to the cortex (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q96BD8,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9CPV1}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Component of the SKA complex, composed of SKA1, SKA2 and SKA3.
CC       The SKA complex is a homodimer organized around a central W-shaped
CC       coiled-coil structure, formed by the interacting domains of SKA1, SKA2,
CC       and SKA3, each end of the 'W' is extended further by the C-terminal
CC       microtubule-binding domains of SKA1 and SKA3; the complex forms
CC       extended structures on microtubules. Interacts (via SXLP motif) with
CC       MAPRE1 (via C-terminus); the interaction is direct and stabilizes the
CC       kinetochore-microtubule attachment of the SKA1 complex. Interacts (via
CC       C-terminus) with protein phosphatase PP1 subunit PPP1CA; the
CC       interaction is direct and required for recruitment of PPP1CA to the
CC       kinetochore. Interacts with the NDC80 complex; the interaction is
CC       required to establish kinetochore-microtubule end-on attachments.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q96BD8}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm, cytoskeleton, spindle
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q96BD8}. Chromosome, centromere, kinetochore
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q96BD8}. Cytoplasm, cytoskeleton, microtubule
CC       organizing center, centrosome {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q96BD8}.
CC       Note=Localizes to bioriented kinetochores and spindle microtubules
CC       during metaphase in a NDC80 complex-dependent manner (By similarity).
CC       The SKA complex begins to concentrate at kinetochores before
CC       microtubule attachment but reaches maximum levels on bioriented
CC       metaphase chromosomes (By similarity). Localizes both to microtubule
CC       plus-ends and along the length of microtubules (By similarity). The
CC       localization of the SKA complex to kinetochores is positively regulated
CC       by kinase CDK1 (By similarity). The localization of the SKA complex to
CC       kinetochores is negatively regulated by protein serine/threonine kinase
CC       AURKB, and this action is opposed directly or indirectly by the PP1 and
CC       PP2A protein phosphatase complexes (By similarity). Localizes at the
CC       centrosome during interphase and prophase (By similarity). Localizes to
CC       the meiotic spindle, but not to kinetochores, from the stage of
CC       germinal vesicle breakdown (GVBD) to meiosis II (MII) (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q96BD8, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9CPV1}.
