CC   -!- FUNCTION: Plasma membrane Na(+)/H(+) antiporter. Mediates the
CC       electroneutral exchange of intracellular H(+) ions for extracellular
CC       Na(+) in 1:1 stoichiometry. Responsible for regulating intracellular pH
CC       homeostasis, in particular in neural tissues (PubMed:9933642). Acts as
CC       a negative regulator of dendritic spine growth. Plays a role in
CC       postsynaptic remodeling and signaling. Can also contribute to
CC       organellar pH regulation, with consequences for receptor tyrosine
CC       kinase trafficking (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q14940,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9933642}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY:
CC       Reaction=H(+)(out) + Na(+)(in) = H(+)(in) + Na(+)(out);
CC         Xref=Rhea:RHEA:29419, ChEBI:CHEBI:15378, ChEBI:CHEBI:29101;
CC         Evidence={ECO:0000269|PubMed:9933642};
CC       PhysiologicalDirection=right-to-left; Xref=Rhea:RHEA:29421;
CC         Evidence={ECO:0000305|PubMed:9933642};
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts with CHP1 and CHP2 (PubMed:12576672). Interacts with
CC       ARRB2; facilitates the endocytosis of SLC9A5 from the plasma membrane
CC       (By similarity). Interacts with RACK1; this interaction positively
CC       regulates SLC9A5 activity and promotes SLC9A5 localization to focal
CC       adhesions (By similarity). Interacts with SCAMP2; this interaction
CC       regulates SLC9A5 cell-surface targeting and SLC9A5 activity (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q14940}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q14940};
CC       Multi-pass membrane protein {ECO:0000255}. Recycling endosome membrane
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q14940}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000255}. Cell projection, dendritic spine membrane
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q14940}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000255}. Synaptic cell membrane {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q14940};
CC       Multi-pass membrane protein. Cell junction, focal adhesion
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q14940}. Note=Cycles between recycling endosome
CC       and plasma membrane in response to diverse stimuli. Its internalization
CC       is clathrin- and beta-arrestin dependent and its plasma membrane
CC       insertion from the recycling endosomes requires phosphoinositide 3-
CC       kinase (PIK3CA) and SCAMP2. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q14940}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Highly expressed in brain. Strongly expressed in
CC       the dentate gyrus. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9933642}.
CC   -!- PTM: Phosphorylated by PRKAA2; promotes its accumulation at the cell
CC       surface. Phosphorylated by CSNK2A1 in a manner favoring its beta-
CC       arrestin binding and endocytosis. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q14940}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the monovalent cation:proton antiporter 1 (CPA1)
CC       transporter (TC 2.A.36) family. {ECO:0000305}.
