CC   -!- FUNCTION: Na(+)/H(+) antiporter. Mediates the electoneutral exchange of
CC       intracellular H(+) ions for extracellular Na(+) in 1:1 stoichiometry.
CC       Acts as an Na(+)/H(+) exchanger in the trans-Golgi. Contributes to the
CC       regulation of pH regulation of Golgi apparatus, and consequently, in
CC       protein trafficking and endosomal morphology (By similarity). In germ
CC       cells, plays a crucial role in acrosome biogenesis and sperm
CC       development, probably by playing a role in the fusion of the Golgi-
CC       derived vesicles that form the acrosomal cap (By similarity). Can also
CC       be active at the cell surface of specialized cells (PubMed:22088432).
CC       In the small intestine, at the cell membrane, plays a major
CC       physiological role in transepithelial absorption of Na(+) and regulates
CC       intracellular pH homeostasis of intestinal epithelial cells
CC       (PubMed:18209477, PubMed:34288721). Acts as an important regulator of
CC       mucosal integrity in the intestine and in the stomach, could mediate
CC       the pH fluctuation necessary for mucin exocytosis or assist membrane
CC       trafficking of other proteins. Plays a role in photoreceptor survival
CC       and in the maintenance of intracellular pH homeostasis in retinal
CC       pigment epithelium (RPE cells) (By similarity).
