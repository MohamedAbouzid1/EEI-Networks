CC   -!- FUNCTION: Mediates the Na(+)-independent transport of organic anions
CC       such as taurocholate, cholate, 17-beta-glucuronosyl estradiol,
CC       prostaglandin E2, estrone 3-sulfate, L-thyroxine (T4), the cardiac
CC       glycosides ouabain and digoxin and thyroid hormones (PubMed:19129463).
CC       May play an especially important role in the brain accumulation and
CC       toxicity of digoxin and in the hepatobiliary and renal excretion of
CC       cardiac glycosides. Shows a pH-sensitive substrate specificity which
CC       may be ascribed to the protonation state of the binding site and leads
CC       to a stimulation of substrate transport in an acidic microenvironment
CC       (PubMed:19129463). Hydrogencarbonate/HCO3(-) acts as the probable
CC       counteranion that exchanges for organic anions (PubMed:19129463).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19129463}.
