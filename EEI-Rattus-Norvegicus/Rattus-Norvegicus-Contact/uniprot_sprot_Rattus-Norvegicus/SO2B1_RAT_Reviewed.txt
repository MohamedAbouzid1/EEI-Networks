CC   -!- FUNCTION: Mediates the Na(+)-independent transport of organic anions
CC       such as taurocholate, the prostaglandins D2 (PGD2), E1 (PGE1) and E2
CC       (PGE2), leukotriene C4, thromboxane B2 and L-thyroxine
CC       (PubMed:10973807). Also plays a role in the reuptake of neuropeptides
CC       such as substance P/TAC1 and vasoactive intestinal peptide/VIP released
CC       from retinal neurons. May act as a heme transporter that promotes
CC       cellular iron availability. Also transports heme by-product
CC       coproporphyrin III (CPIII), and may be involved in their hepatic
CC       disposition (By similarity). May contribute to regulate the transport
CC       of organic compounds in testis across the blood-testis-barrier (By
CC       similarity). Shows a pH-sensitive substrate specificity which may be
CC       ascribed to the protonation state of the binding site and leads to a
CC       stimulation of substrate transport in an acidic microenvironment. The
CC       exact transport mechanism has not been yet deciphered but most likely
CC       involves an anion exchange, coupling the cellular uptake of organic
CC       substrate with the efflux of an anionic compound.
CC       Hydrogencarbonate/HCO3(-) acts as a probable counteranion that
CC       exchanges for organic anions. Cytoplasmic glutamate may also act as
CC       counteranion in the placenta (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O94956, ECO:0000269|PubMed:10973807}.
