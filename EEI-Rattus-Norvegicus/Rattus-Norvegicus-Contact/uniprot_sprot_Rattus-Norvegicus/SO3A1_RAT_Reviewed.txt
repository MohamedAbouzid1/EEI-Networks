CC   -!- FUNCTION: Putative organic anion antiporter with apparent broad
CC       substrate specificity. Recognizes various substrates including thyroid
CC       hormone L-thyroxine, prostanoids such as prostaglandin E1 and E2, bile
CC       acids such as taurocholate, glycolate and glycochenodeoxycholate and
CC       peptide hormones such as L-arginine vasopressin, likely operating in a
CC       tissue-specific manner (By similarity) (PubMed:14631946). The transport
CC       mechanism, its electrogenicity and potential tissue-specific
CC       counterions remain to be elucidated (Probable).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9UIG8, ECO:0000269|PubMed:14631946,
CC       ECO:0000305}.

CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed in many brain regions, including frontal
CC       cortex, brain stem and cerebellum. Associated with neuronal bodies in a
CC       punctated matter. Detected at the arcuate nucleus and the choroid
CC       plexus (at protein level). Little expression, if any, in
CC       oligodendrocytes. In the cardiovascular system, detected in cardiac
CC       muscle cells and endothelial cells of aorta, coronary artery and left
CC       ventricular endocardium (at protein level). In the respiratory system,
CC       detected in alveolar epithelial cells and in mucosal epithelium of the
CC       trachea (at protein level). In the reproductive system, detected in
CC       spermatozoa, oocytes, smooth muscle cells of the ovary, epithelium of
CC       the glandula uterine, smooth muscle cells of the myometrium and
CC       epithelium of the endometrium (at protein level). In the kidney,
CC       detected in afferent and efferent arterioles, and the epithelium of
CC       distal tubules and collecting tubules (at protein level).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:14631946, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16971491}.
