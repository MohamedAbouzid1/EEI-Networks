CC   -!- FUNCTION: Mediates the transport of organic anions such as steroids
CC       (estrone 3-sulfate, chenodeoxycholate, glycocholate) and thyroid
CC       hormones (3,3',5-triiodo-L-thyronine (T3), L-thyroxine (T4)), in the
CC       kidney. Capable of transporting cAMP and pharmacological substances
CC       such as digoxin, ouabain and methotrexate (PubMed:14993604). Transport
CC       is independent of sodium, chloride ion, and ATP (PubMed:14993604,
CC       PubMed:22768102). Transport activity is stimulated by an acidic
CC       extracellular environment due to increased substrate affinity to the
CC       transporter (PubMed:14993604, PubMed:22768102). The driving force for
CC       this transport activity is currently not known (PubMed:22768102). The
CC       role of hydrogencarbonate (HCO3(-), bicarbonate) as the probable
CC       counteranion that exchanges for organic anions is still not well
CC       defined (PubMed:14993604, PubMed:22768102). Functions as an uptake
CC       transporter at the apical membrane, suggesting a role in renal
CC       reabsorption (PubMed:22768102). Involved in the renal secretion of the
CC       uremic toxin ADMA (N(omega),N(omega)-dimethyl-L-arginine or
CC       asymmetrical dimethylarginine), which is associated to cardiovascular
CC       events and mortality, and the structurally related amino acids L-
CC       arginine and L-homoarginine (a cardioprotective biomarker). Can act
CC       bidirectionally, suggesting a dual protective role of this transport
CC       protein; exporting L-homoarginine after being synthesized in proximal
CC       tubule cells, and mediating uptake of ADMA from the blood into proximal
CC       tubule cells where it is degraded by the enzyme dimethylarginine
CC       dimethylaminohydrolase 1 (DDAH1) (By similarity). May be involved in
CC       sperm maturation by enabling directed movement of organic anions and
CC       compounds within or between cells. This ion-transporting process is
CC       important to maintain the strict epididymal homeostasis necessary for
CC       sperm maturation. May have a role in secretory functions since seminal
CC       vesicle epithelial cells are assumed to secrete proteins involved in
CC       decapacitation by modifying surface proteins to facilitate the
CC       acquisition of the ability to fertilize the egg (By similarity).
