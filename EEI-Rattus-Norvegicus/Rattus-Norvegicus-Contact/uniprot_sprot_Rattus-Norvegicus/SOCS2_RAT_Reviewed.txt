CC   -!- FUNCTION: Substrate-recognition component of a cullin-5-RING E3
CC       ubiquitin-protein ligase complex (ECS complex, also named CRL5
CC       complex), which mediates the ubiquitination and subsequent proteasomal
CC       degradation of target proteins, such as EPOR and GHR. Specifically
CC       recognizes and binds phosphorylated proteins via its SH2 domain,
CC       promoting their ubiquitination. The ECS(SOCS2) complex acts as a key
CC       regulator of growth hormone receptor (GHR) levels by mediating
CC       ubiquitination and degradation of GHR, following GHR phosphorylation by
CC       JAK2. The ECS(SOCS2) also catalyzes ubiquitination and degradation of
CC       JAK2-phosphorylated EPOR. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O14508}.
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Protein modification; protein ubiquitination.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O14508}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Substrate-recognition component of the ECS(SOCS2) complex,
CC       composed of SOCS2, CUL5, ELOB, ELOC and RNF7/RBX2. Interacts with
CC       IGF1R. Interacts with DCUN1D1. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O14508}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O14508}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The SOCS box domain mediates the interaction with the Elongin
CC       BC complex, an adapter module in different E3 ubiquitin ligase
CC       complexes. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O14508}.
CC   -!- PTM: Ubiquitinated; mediated by AREL1 and leading to its subsequent
CC       proteasomal degradation. Ubiquitination is dependent on its
CC       phosphorylation at Ser-52, by PKC (By similarity). Ubiquitination is
CC       stimulated by LPS (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O14508,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O35717}.
CC   -!- PTM: Phosphorylation at Ser-52 by PKC facilitates its ubiquitination
CC       and proteasomal degradation. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O14508}.
