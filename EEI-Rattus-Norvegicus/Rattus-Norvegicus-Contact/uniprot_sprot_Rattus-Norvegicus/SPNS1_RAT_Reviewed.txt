CC   -!- FUNCTION: Plays a critical role in the phospholipid salvage pathway
CC       from lysosomes to the cytosol. Mediates the rate-limiting, proton-
CC       dependent, lysosomal efflux of lysophospholipids, which can then be
CC       reacylated by acyltransferases in the endoplasmic reticulum to form
CC       phospholipids. Selective for zwitterionic headgroups such as
CC       lysophosphatidylcholine (LPC) and lysophosphatidylethanolamine (LPE),
CC       can also transport lysophosphatidylglycerol (LPG), but not other
CC       anionic lysophospholipids, sphingosine, nor sphingomyelin. Transports
CC       lysophospholipids with saturated, monounsaturated, and polyunsaturated
CC       fatty acids, such as 1-hexadecanoyl-sn-glycero-3-phosphocholine, 1-(9Z-
CC       octadecenoyl)-sn-glycero-3-phosphocholine and 1-(4Z,7Z,10Z,13Z,16Z,19Z-
CC       docosahexaenoyl)-sn-glycero-3-phosphocholine, respectively. Can also
CC       transport lysoplasmalogen (LPC with a fatty alcohol) such as 1-(1Z-
CC       hexadecenyl)-sn-glycero-3-phosphocholine. Essential player in lysosomal
CC       homeostasis. Crucial for cell survival under conditions of nutrient
CC       limitation. May be involved in necrotic or autophagic cell death.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9H2V7}.
