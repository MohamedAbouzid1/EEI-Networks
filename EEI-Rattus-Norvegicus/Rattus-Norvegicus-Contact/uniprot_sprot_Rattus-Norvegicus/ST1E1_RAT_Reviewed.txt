CC   -!- FUNCTION: Sulfotransferase that utilizes 3'-phospho-5'-adenylyl sulfate
CC       (PAPS) as sulfonate donor to catalyze the sulfate conjugation of
CC       estradiol and estrone (PubMed:7857871). Is a key enzyme in estrogen
CC       homeostasis, the sulfation of estrogens leads to their inactivation.
CC       Also sulfates dehydroepiandrosterone (DHEA), pregnenolone, (24S)-
CC       hydroxycholesterol and xenobiotic compounds like ethinylestradiol,
CC       equalenin, diethyl stilbesterol and 1-naphthol at significantly lower
CC       efficiency. Does not sulfonate cortisol, testosterone and dopamine. May
CC       play a role in gut microbiota-host metabolic interaction. O-sulfonates
CC       4-ethylphenol (4-EP), a dietary tyrosine-derived metabolite produced by
CC       gut bacteria. The product 4-EPS crosses the blood-brain barrier and may
CC       negatively regulate oligodendrocyte maturation and myelination,
CC       affecting the functional connectivity of different brain regions
CC       associated with the limbic system. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P49888,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:7857871}.
