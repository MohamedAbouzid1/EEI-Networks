CC   -!- FUNCTION: Calmodulin-binding scaffolding protein which is the center of
CC       the striatin-interacting phosphatase and kinase (STRIPAK) complexes.
CC       STRIPAK complexes have critical roles in protein (de)phosphorylation
CC       and are regulators of multiple signaling pathways including Hippo,
CC       MAPK, nuclear receptor and cytoskeleton remodeling. Different types of
CC       STRIPAK complexes are involved in a variety of biological processes
CC       such as cell growth, differentiation, apoptosis, metabolism and immune
CC       regulation. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q13033}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Tetramerizes. Part of the core of STRIPAK complexes composed
CC       of PP2A catalytic and scaffolding subunits, the striatins (PP2A
CC       regulatory subunits), the striatin-associated proteins MOB4, STRIP1 and
CC       STRIP2, PDCD10 and members of the STE20 kinases, such as STK24 and
CC       STK26. The STRIPAK complex can be extended by adapter proteins such as
CC       SLMAP:SIKE1 or CTTNBP2NL. Interacts with CDC42BPB.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q13033}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm {ECO:0000250}. Membrane {ECO:0000250};
CC       Peripheral membrane protein {ECO:0000250}.
