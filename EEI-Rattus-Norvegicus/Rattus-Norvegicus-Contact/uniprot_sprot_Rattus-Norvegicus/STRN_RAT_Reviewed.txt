CC   -!- FUNCTION: Calmodulin-binding scaffolding protein which is the center of
CC       the striatin-interacting phosphatase and kinase (STRIPAK) complexes.
CC       STRIPAK complexes have critical roles in protein (de)phosphorylation
CC       and are regulators of multiple signaling pathways including Hippo,
CC       MAPK, nuclear receptor and cytoskeleton remodeling. Different types of
CC       STRIPAK complexes are involved in a variety of biological processes
CC       such as cell growth, differentiation, apoptosis, metabolism and immune
CC       regulation. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O43815}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Part of the core of STRIPAK complexes composed of PP2A
CC       catalytic and scaffolding subunits, the striatins (PP2A regulatory
CC       subunits), the striatin-associated proteins MOB4, STRIP1 and STRIP2,
CC       PDCD10 and members of the STE20 kinases, such as STK24 and STK26 (By
CC       similarity). Interacts with CTTNBP2; this interaction may regulate
CC       dendritic spine distribution of STRN. Activation of glutamate receptors
CC       weakens the interaction with CTTNBP2 (PubMed:23015759).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O43815, ECO:0000269|PubMed:23015759}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm {ECO:0000269|PubMed:23015759}. Membrane
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:23015759}; Peripheral membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:23015759}. Cell projection, dendritic spine
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:23015759}. Note=CTTNBP2-binding may regulate
CC       dendritic spine distribution. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:23015759}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Mainly expressed in the central nervous system.
CC       Mostly confined in dendrites, not in axons, and is most abundant in
CC       dendritic spines. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:8769426}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the WD repeat striatin family. {ECO:0000305}.
