CC   -!- FUNCTION: G protein-coupled receptor for succinate able to mediate
CC       signaling through Gq/GNAQ or Gi/GNAI second messengers depending on the
CC       cell type and the processes regulated (PubMed:15141213). Succinate-
CC       SUCNR1 signaling serves as a link between metabolic stress,
CC       inflammation and energy homeostasisn (By similarity). In macrophages,
CC       plays a range of immune-regulatory roles. During inflammation,
CC       succinate-SUCNR1 signaling may act as an anti-inflammatory mediator or
CC       boost inflammation depending on the inflammatory status of cells (By
CC       similarity). Hyperpolarizes M2 macrophages versus M1 phenotype through
CC       Gq signaling by regulating the transcription of genes involved in
CC       immune function (By similarity). In activated M1 macrophages, plays a
CC       pro-inflammatory role in response to LPS (By similarity). Expressed in
CC       dendritic cells, where it is involved in the sensing of immunological
CC       danger and enhances immunity. Mediates succinate triggered
CC       intracelleular calcium mobilization, induces migratory responses and
CC       acts in synergy with Toll-like receptor ligands for the production of
CC       proinflammatory cytokines as well as an enhancement of antigen-specific
CC       activation of helper T cells (By similarity). In the small intestine,
CC       mediates the activation of tuft cells by dietary succinate and triggers
CC       type 2 immunity. In adipocytes, plays an important role in the control
CC       of energy metabolism. In response to succinate, controls leptin
CC       expression in an AMPK-JNK-CEBPA-dependent as well as circadian clock-
CC       regulated manner. In muscle tissue, is expressed in non-muscle cells
CC       and coordinates muscle remodeling in response to the succinate produced
CC       during exercise training in a paracrine manner (By similarity). In
CC       retina, acts as a mediator of vessel growth during retinal development.
CC       In response to succinate, regulates the production of angiogenic
CC       factors, including VEGF, by retinal ganglion neurons (PubMed:18836459).
