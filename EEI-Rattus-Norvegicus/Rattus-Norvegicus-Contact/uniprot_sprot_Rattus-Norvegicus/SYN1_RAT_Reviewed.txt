CC   -!- FUNCTION: Neuronal phosphoprotein that coats synaptic vesicles, and
CC       binds to the cytoskeleton. Acts as a regulator of synaptic vesicles
CC       trafficking, involved in the control of neurotransmitter release at the
CC       pre-synaptic terminal (PubMed:11685225). Also involved in the
CC       regulation of axon outgrowth and synaptogenesis (By similarity). The
CC       complex formed with NOS1 and CAPON proteins is necessary for specific
CC       nitric-oxide functions at a presynaptic level (PubMed:11867766).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O88935, ECO:0000269|PubMed:11685225,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:11867766}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer (By similarity). Can form oligomers with SYN2 (By
CC       similarity). Interacts with CAPON (PubMed:11867766). Forms a ternary
CC       complex with NOS1 (PubMed:11867766). Isoform Ib interacts with PRNP (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O88935,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P17600, ECO:0000269|PubMed:11867766}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Synapse {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O88935}. Golgi
CC       apparatus {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O88935}. Presynapse
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11685225}. Cytoplasmic vesicle, secretory vesicle,
CC       synaptic vesicle {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11685225}. Note=Dissociates from
CC       synaptic vesicles and redistributes into the axon during action
CC       potential firing, in a step that precedes fusion of vesicles with the
CC       plasma membrane. Reclusters to presynapses after the cessation of
CC       synaptic activity. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11685225}.
