CC   -!- FUNCTION: Synaptic vesicle phosphoprotein that enhances spontaneous
CC       neurotransmitter release but does not effect induced neurotransmitter
CC       release (PubMed:17190793). Unlike other synaptotagmins, it does not
CC       bind Ca(2+) or phospholipids (PubMed:17190793). Essential for mossy-
CC       fiber long-term potentiation in the hippocampus (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q920N7, ECO:0000269|PubMed:17190793}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer (By similarity). Can also form heterodimers (By
CC       similarity). Interacts with SYT1 (PubMed:17190793).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9R0N7, ECO:0000269|PubMed:17190793}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasmic vesicle, secretory vesicle, synaptic
CC       vesicle membrane {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17190793}; Single-pass membrane
CC       protein {ECO:0000255}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed in the brain, specifically in neurons of
CC       the cerebellum, cortex, hippocampus, olfactory bulb, brainstem and
CC       spinal cord (at protein level). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17190793}.
CC   -!- PTM: Phosphorylation of Ser-97 is required for mossy-fiber long-term
CC       potentiation. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q920N7}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the synaptotagmin family. {ECO:0000305}.
CC   -!- CAUTION: Unlike classical synaptotagmins, lacks Ca(2+)-binding
CC       sequences and does not bind phospholipids.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17190793}.
