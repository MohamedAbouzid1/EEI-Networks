CC   -!- FUNCTION: Tyrosine--tRNA ligase that catalyzes the attachment of
CC       tyrosine to tRNA(Tyr) in a two-step reaction: tyrosine is first
CC       activated by ATP to form Tyr-AMP and then transferred to the acceptor
CC       end of tRNA(Tyr). Also acts as a positive regulator of poly-ADP-
CC       ribosylation in the nucleus, independently of its tyrosine--tRNA ligase
CC       activity. Activity is switched upon resveratrol-binding: resveratrol
CC       strongly inhibits the tyrosine--tRNA ligase activity and promotes
CC       relocalization to the nucleus, where YARS1 specifically stimulates the
CC       poly-ADP-ribosyltransferase activity of PARP1.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P54577}.

CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: Resveratrol strongly inhibits the tyrosine--tRNA

CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer. Interacts (when binding to resveratrol) with PARP1;

CC       PARP1. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P54577}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P54577}. Nucleus
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P54577}. Note=Cytoplasmic in normal conditions.
CC       Resveratrol-binding in response to serum starvation promotes
CC       relocalization to the nucleus. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P54577}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The nuclear localization signal, which mediates localization to
