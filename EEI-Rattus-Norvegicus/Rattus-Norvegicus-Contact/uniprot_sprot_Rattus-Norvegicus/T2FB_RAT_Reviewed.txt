CC   -!- FUNCTION: TFIIF is a general transcription initiation factor that binds
CC       to RNA polymerase II and helps to recruit it to the initiation complex
CC       in collaboration with TFIIB. It promotes transcription elongation.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P13984}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Heterodimer of an alpha and a beta subunit. Interacts with
CC       HTATSF1 and GPBP1 (By similarity). Interacts with URI1. Interacts with
CC       GTF2B (via N-terminus); this interaction is inhibited in presence of
CC       GTF2F1. Part of TBP-based Pol II pre-initiation complex (PIC), in which
CC       Pol II core assembles with general transcription factors and other
CC       specific initiation factors including GTF2E1, GTF2E2, GTF2F1, GTF2F2,
CC       TCEA1, ERCC2, ERCC3, GTF2H2, GTF2H3, GTF2H4, GTF2H5, GTF2A1, GTF2A2,
CC       GTF2B and TBP; this large multi-subunit PIC complex mediates DNA
CC       unwinding and targets Pol II core to the transcription start site where
CC       the first phosphodiester bond forms (By similarity). {ECO:0000250,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P13984}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Nucleus.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the TFIIF beta subunit family. {ECO:0000305}.
