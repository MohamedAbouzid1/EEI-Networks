CC   -!- FUNCTION: Intracellular G-protein coupled receptor for trace amines,
CC       which recognizes endogenous amine-containing metabolites such as beta-
CC       phenylethylamine (beta-PEA), 3-iodothyronamine (T1AM), isoamylamine
CC       (IAA), cadaverine (CAD), cyclohexylamine (CHA), p-tyramine (p-TYR),
CC       trimethylamine (TMA), octopamine and tryptamine (PubMed:15718104). Also
CC       functions as a receptor for various drugs and psychoactive substances,
CC       such as amphetamine and methamphetamine (By similarity). Unresponsive
CC       to classical biogenic amines, such as epinephrine and histamine and
CC       only partially activated by dopamine and serotonin (By similarity).
CC       Expressed in both the central and peripheral nervous system: TAAR1
CC       activation regulates the activity of several neurotransmitter signaling
CC       pathways by (1) decreasing the basal firing rates of the neurons
CC       involved and by (2) lowering the sensitivity of receptors to
CC       neurotransmitters (By similarity). Ligand binding causes a conformation
CC       change that triggers signaling via guanine nucleotide-binding proteins
CC       (G proteins) and modulates the activity of downstream effectors (By
CC       similarity). TAAR1 is coupled with different G(i)/G(o)-, G(s)- or
CC       G(q)/G(11) classes of G alpha proteins depending on the ligand (By
CC       similarity). CAD-binding is coupled to G(i)/G(o) G alpha proteins and
CC       mediates inhibition of adenylate cyclase activity (By similarity).
CC       T1AM- or beta-PEA-binding is coupled to G(s) G alpha proteins and
CC       mediates activation of adenylate cyclase activity (By similarity).
CC       CHA- or IAA-binding is coupled to G(q)/G(11) G alpha proteins and
CC       activates phospholipase C-beta, releasing diacylglycerol (DAG) and
CC       inositol 1,4,5-trisphosphate (IP3) second messengers (By similarity).
CC       TMA-binding is coupled with all three G(i)/G(o)-, G(s)- or G(q)/G(11) G
CC       alpha protein subtypes (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q96RJ0,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15718104}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Endomembrane system
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q96RJ0}. Endoplasmic reticulum membrane
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q96RJ0}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q96RJ0}. Cell membrane
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q96RJ0}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q96RJ0}. Note=Localizes mainly intracellularly.
CC       Partially colocalizes with the endoplasmic reticulum; also found at
CC       lower lever at the plasma membrane. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q96RJ0}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Widely distributed, but in low abundance,
CC       throughout the brain (PubMed:11459929). Highest levels detected in the
CC       olfactory bulb, nucleus accumbens/olfactory tubercle, prefrontal cortex
CC       and other cortical regions, midbrain regions consisting of substantia
CC       nigra and ventral tegmentum, cerebellum, and pons/medulla
CC       (PubMed:11459929). Among peripheral tissues, highest level observed in
CC       the liver, less expression in kidney, gastrointestinal tract, spleen,
CC       pancreas, and heart (PubMed:11459929). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11459929}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the G-protein coupled receptor 1 family.
CC       {ECO:0000255|PROSITE-ProRule:PRU00521}.
