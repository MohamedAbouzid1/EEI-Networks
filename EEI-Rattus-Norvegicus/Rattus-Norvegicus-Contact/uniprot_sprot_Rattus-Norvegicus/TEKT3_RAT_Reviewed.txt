CC   -!- FUNCTION: Microtubule inner protein (MIP) part of the dynein-decorated
CC       doublet microtubules (DMTs) in cilia and flagellar axoneme. Forms
CC       filamentous polymers in the walls of ciliary and flagellar microtubules
CC       (By similarity). Required for normal sperm mobility (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:A6H782, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q6X6Z7}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Microtubule inner protein component of sperm flagellar doublet
CC       microtubules (By similarity). Interacts with TEKT1, TEKT2, TEKT4 and
CC       TEKT5 (By similarity). Interacts with CCDC38 (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q6X6Z7, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9BXF9}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm, cytoskeleton, cilium axoneme
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:A6H782}. Cytoplasm, cytoskeleton, flagellum
CC       axoneme {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19423663, ECO:0000269|PubMed:21744413}.
CC       Cytoplasmic vesicle, secretory vesicle, acrosome outer membrane
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:21744413}; Peripheral membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000305}. Note=In spermatozoa, preferentially localizes to the
CC       flagella, but also found in the head (By similarity). In the sperm
CC       flagellum, localizes to the periaxonemal region where it associates
CC       with the mitochondrial sheath and outer dense fibers (PubMed:21744413).
CC       Not detected in the central axonemal region of the flagellum
CC       (PubMed:21744413). Associates with the acrosome membrane in the
CC       equatorial segment of the sperm head (PubMed:21744413). Also detected
CC       just below the plasma membrane in the post-acrosomal region where it
CC       might localize to the postacrosomal dense lamina (By similarity).
CC       However, other studies report little or no expression in the
CC       postacrosomal region (PubMed:21744413). Translocates from the
CC       postacrosomal region to the equatorial segment after sperm activation
CC       (By similarity). Retained in the postacromal region, but not the
CC       equatorial segment, following the acrosome reaction (By similarity).
CC       Some studies report strong expression in the anterior cap region (By
CC       similarity). However, other studies report little or no expression in
CC       the acrosomal cap (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:A6H782,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9BXF9, ECO:0000269|PubMed:21744413}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed in epididymal sperm (at protein level).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19423663, ECO:0000269|PubMed:21744413}.
CC   -!- PTM: N- and O-glycosylated. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:A6H782}.
CC   -!- PTM: May be proteolytically processed during the epididymal transit of
CC       spermatozoa. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:A6H782}.
CC   -!- PTM: Ubiquitinated, leading to its degradation. Deubiquitinated by
CC       USP16, promoting its stability. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q6X6Z7}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the tektin family. {ECO:0000305}.
