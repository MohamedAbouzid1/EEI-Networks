CC   -!- FUNCTION: Promotes EGF-induced cell migration by displacing tensin TNS3
CC       from the cytoplasmic tail of integrin ITGB1 which results in
CC       dissociation of TNS3 from focal adhesions, disassembly of actin stress
CC       fibers and initiation of cell migration. Suppresses ligand-induced
CC       degradation of EGFR by reducing EGFR ubiquitination in the presence of
CC       EGF. Increases MET protein stability by inhibiting MET endocytosis and
CC       subsequent lysosomal degradation which leads to increased cell
CC       survival, proliferation and migration. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8IZW8}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts (via SH2 domain) with Rho GTPase-activating protein
CC       DLC1 (via C-terminus); the interaction is independent of DLC1 tyrosine
CC       phosphorylation. Interacts with integrin ITGB1; the interaction
CC       displaces tensin TNS3 from the ITGB1 cytoplasmic tail and promotes
CC       ITGB1 stability. Interacts (via SH2 domain) with E3 ubiquitin-protein
CC       ligase CBL (phosphorylated on 'Tyr-781'); the interaction is enhanced
CC       in the presence of EGF and reduces interaction of CBL with EGFR.
CC       Interacts (via SH2 domain) with receptor tyrosine kinase MET (when
CC       phosphorylated); the interaction increases MET protein stability.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8IZW8}.

CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8IZW8}. Cytoplasm, cytoskeleton
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8IZW8}.
