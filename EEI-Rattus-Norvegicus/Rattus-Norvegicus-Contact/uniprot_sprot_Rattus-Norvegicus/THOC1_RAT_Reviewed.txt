CC   -!- FUNCTION: Component of the THO subcomplex of the TREX complex which is
CC       thought to couple mRNA transcription, processing and nuclear export,
CC       and which specifically associates with spliced mRNA and not with
CC       unspliced pre-mRNA (By similarity). Required for efficient export of
CC       polyadenylated RNA (By similarity). The THOC1-THOC2-THOC3 core complex
CC       alone is sufficient to bind export factor NXF1-NXT1 and promote ATPase
CC       activity of DDX39B (By similarity). TREX is recruited to spliced mRNAs
CC       by a transcription-independent mechanism, binds to mRNA upstream of the
CC       exon-junction complex (EJC) and is recruited in a splicing- and cap-
CC       dependent manner to a region near the 5' end of the mRNA where it
CC       functions in mRNA export to the cytoplasm via the TAP/NXF1 pathway (By
CC       similarity). Regulates transcriptional elongation of a subset of genes
CC       (By similarity). Involved in genome stability by preventing co-
CC       transcriptional R-loop formation (By similarity). May play a role in
CC       hair cell formation, hence may be involved in hearing (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q7SYB2, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q96FV9}.
CC   -!- FUNCTION: Participates in an apoptotic pathway which is characterized
CC       by activation of caspase-6, increases in the expression of BAK1 and
CC       BCL2L1 and activation of NF-kappa-B. This pathway does not require
CC       p53/TP53, nor does the presence of p53/TP53 affect the efficiency of
CC       cell killing. Activates a G2/M cell cycle checkpoint prior to the onset
CC       of apoptosis. Apoptosis is inhibited by association with RB1. Essential
CC       for early embryonic development. Required for normal gene expression
CC       during postnatal testis development (By similarity). May play a role in
CC       hair cell formation, hence may be involved in hearing (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q7SYB2}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Component of the THO subcomplex, which is composed of THOC1,
CC       THOC2, THOC3, THOC5, THOC6 and THOC7 (By similarity). The THO
CC       subcomplex interacts with DDX39B to form the THO-DDX39B complex which
CC       multimerizes into a 28-subunit tetrameric assembly (By similarity).
CC       Component of the transcription/export (TREX) complex at least composed
CC       of ALYREF/THOC4, DDX39B, SARNP/CIP29, CHTOP and the THO subcomplex; in
CC       the complex interacts with THOC2, THOC5 and THOC7 (By similarity). TREX
CC       seems to have a dynamic structure involving ATP-dependent remodeling
CC       (By similarity). Binds to the hypophosphorylated form of RB1. Interacts
CC       with RNA polymerase II. Interacts with LUZP4 (By similarity). Interacts
CC       with THOC5. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q7SYB2,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8R3N6, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q96FV9}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Nucleus {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8R3N6}. Nucleus,
CC       nucleoplasm {ECO:0000250}. Nucleus matrix {ECO:0000250}. Cytoplasm,
CC       cytosol {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8R3N6}. Note=Predominantly localized in
CC       the nucleus (By similarity). Shuttles between the nucleus and
CC       cytoplasm. Nuclear localization is required for induction of apoptotic
CC       cell death. Translocates to the cytoplasm during the early phase of
CC       apoptosis execution (By similarity). {ECO:0000250,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8R3N6}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: An intact death domain is needed for apoptosis.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q96FV9}.
CC   -!- PTM: Expression is altered specifically during apoptosis and is
CC       accompanied by the appearance of novel forms with smaller apparent
CC       molecular mass. {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- PTM: Polyubiquitinated, leading to proteasomal degradation; probably
CC       involves NEDD4. {ECO:0000250}.
