CC   -!- FUNCTION: Plays an important role in the control of DNA replication,
CC       maintenance of replication fork stability, maintenance of genome
CC       stability throughout normal DNA replication, DNA repair and in the
CC       regulation of the circadian clock (By similarity). Required to
CC       stabilize replication forks during DNA replication by forming a complex
CC       with TIPIN: this complex regulates DNA replication processes under both
CC       normal and stress conditions, stabilizes replication forks and
CC       influences both CHEK1 phosphorylation and the intra-S phase checkpoint
CC       in response to genotoxic stress. During DNA replication, inhibits the
CC       CMG complex ATPase activity and activates DNA polymerases catalytic
CC       activities, coupling DNA unwinding and DNA synthesis (By similarity).
CC       TIMELESS promotes TIPIN nuclear localization (By similarity). Plays a
CC       role in maintaining processive DNA replication past genomic guanine-
CC       rich DNA sequences that form G-quadruplex (G4) structures, possibly
CC       together with DDX1 (By similarity). Involved in cell survival after DNA
CC       damage or replication stress by promoting DNA repair (By similarity).
CC       In response to double-strand breaks (DSBs), accumulates at DNA damage
CC       sites and promotes homologous recombination repair via its interaction
CC       with PARP1 (By similarity). May be specifically required for the ATR-
CC       CHEK1 pathway in the replication checkpoint induced by hydroxyurea or
CC       ultraviolet light (By similarity). Involved in the determination of
CC       period length and in the DNA damage-dependent phase advancing of the
CC       circadian clock (By similarity). Negatively regulates CLOCK|NPAS2-
CC       ARTNL/BMAL1|ARTNL2/BMAL2-induced transactivation of PER1 possibly via
CC       translocation of PER1 into the nucleus (By similarity). May also play
CC       an important role in epithelial cell morphogenesis and formation of
CC       branching tubules (PubMed:10963667). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9UNS1,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:10963667}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Monomer (By similarity). Homodimer or homomultimer (By
CC       similarity). Component of the circadian core oscillator, which includes
CC       the CRY proteins, CLOCK or NPAS2, ARTNL/BMAL1 or ARTNL2/BMAL2, CSKN1D
CC       and/or CSNK1E, TIMELESS, and the PER proteins (By similarity).
CC       Interacts directly with PER2; the interaction with PER2 is via its
CC       second PAS domain (PubMed:11112428). Interacts directly with PER1 and
CC       PER3 (By similarity). Interacts with CRY2, CHEK1, ATR and ATRIP (By
CC       similarity). Interacts with CRY1 (By similarity). Interacts with CLSPN;
CC       the interaction is required for leading-strand replication. Interacts
CC       (via N-terminus) with TIPIN (By similarity). The TIMELESS-TIPIN
CC       heterodimer binds preferably to guanine-rich quadruplex-forming (G4)
CC       DNA structures (By similarity). Associates with the MCM2-7 complex.
CC       Interacts with DNA polymerases alpha, delta and epsilon. Interacts with
CC       DDX11; this interaction increases recruitment of both proteins onto
CC       chromatin in response to replication stress induction by hydroxyurea.
CC       Interacts with PARP1; interaction is direct and independent of poly-
CC       ADP-ribose (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9UNS1,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:11112428}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Nucleus {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11112428}. Chromosome
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9UNS1}. Note=In response to double-strand
CC       breaks (DSBs), accumulates at DNA damage sites via its interaction with
CC       PARP1. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9UNS1}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed in all tissues examined including brain,
CC       eye, lung, heart, liver, kidney, pancreas, placenta and testis. Highest
CC       levels of expression in eye, lung, liver, placenta and kidney.
CC       Expressed throughout the suprachiasmatic nucleus (SCN).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11112428, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15094047}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: In both brain and pancreas, no circadian oscillation was
CC       detected. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11112428, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15094047}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: Residues 1178-1195 comprise a putative nuclear localization
CC       signal; nuclear localization is required for the regulation of period
CC       length of the circadian clock. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9UNS1}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The DNA-binding domain (residues 813-951) binds to both single-
CC       stranded DNA (ssDNA) and double-stranded DNA (dsDNA), and has high
CC       affinity for DNA sequences rich in guanine that form G-quadruplex (G4)
CC       structures. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9UNS1}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The C-terminal domain, comprising the DNA-binding domain and
CC       the PARP1-binding region, is required for the replication past genomic
CC       guanine-rich DNA sequences that form G-quadruplex (G4) structures.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9UNS1}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the timeless family. {ECO:0000305}.
