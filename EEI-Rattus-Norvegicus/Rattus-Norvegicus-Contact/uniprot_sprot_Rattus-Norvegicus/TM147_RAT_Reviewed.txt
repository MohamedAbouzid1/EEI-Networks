CC   -!- FUNCTION: Component of the multi-pass translocon (MPT) complex that
CC       mediates insertion of multi-pass membrane proteins into the lipid
CC       bilayer of membranes. The MPT complex takes over after the SEC61
CC       complex: following membrane insertion of the first few transmembrane
CC       segments of proteins by the SEC61 complex, the MPT complex occludes the
CC       lateral gate of the SEC61 complex to promote insertion of subsequent
CC       transmembrane regions. Also acts as a negative regulator of CHRM3
CC       function, most likely by interfering with its trafficking to the cell
CC       membrane. Negatively regulates CHRM3-mediated calcium mobilization and
CC       activation of RPS6KA1/p90RSK activity. Regulates LBR localization to
CC       the nucleus inner membrane. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9BVK8}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Component of the back of Sec61 (BOS) complex, composed of
CC       NCLN/Nicalin, NOMO (NOMO1, NOMO2 or NOMO3) and TMEM147. The BOS complex
CC       is part of the multi-pass translocon (MPT) complex, composed of three
CC       subcomplexes, the GEL complex (composed of RAB5IF/OPTI and TMCO1), the
CC       BOS complex (composed of NCLN/Nicalin, NOMO and TMEM147) and the PAT
CC       complex (composed of WDR83OS/Asterix and CCDC47). The MPT complex
CC       associates with the SEC61 complex. Interacts with CHRM3, CHRM1 and
CC       AVPR2. Interacts with LBR; promoting LBR localization to the nucleus
CC       inner membrane. Interacts with DHCR7. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9BVK8}.
