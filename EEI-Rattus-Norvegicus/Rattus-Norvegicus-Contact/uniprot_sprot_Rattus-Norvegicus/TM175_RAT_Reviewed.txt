CC   -!- FUNCTION: Proton-activated proton channel that catalyzes proton efflux
CC       from endosomes and lysosomes to maintain a steady-state pH (By
CC       similarity). Activated at low pH (under pH 4.6) by luminal side
CC       protons: selectively mediates lysosomal proton release from lysosomes,
CC       eliciting a proton leak that balances V-ATPase activity to maintain pH
CC       homeostasis (By similarity). Regulation of lumenal pH stability is
CC       required for autophagosome-lysosome fusion (By similarity). Also acts
CC       as a potassium channel at higher pH, regulating potassium conductance
CC       in endosomes and lysosomes (By similarity). Constitutes the pore-
CC       forming subunit of the lysoK(GF) complex, a complex activated by
CC       extracellular growth factors (By similarity). The lysoK(GF) complex is
CC       composed of TMEM175 and AKT (AKT1, AKT2 or AKT3), a major target of
CC       growth factor receptors: in the complex, TMEM175 channel is opened by
CC       conformational changes by AKT, leading to its activation (By
CC       similarity). The lysoK(GF) complex is required to protect neurons
CC       against stress-induced damage (Probable) (PubMed:32799888).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9BSA9, ECO:0000305|PubMed:32799888}.

CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: Active at low pH (under pH 4.6): proton channel
CC       activity is activated by luminal side protons. Polyunsaturated fatty
CC       acids, such as arachidonic acid, also activate the channel activity.
CC       Proton channel activity is directly inhibited by LAMP1 or LAMP2,
CC       facilitating lysosomal acidification. Channel activity is activated
CC       following interaction with AKT (AKT1, AKT2 or AKT3): interaction
CC       promotes activation from closed to an open state. Activation by AKT is
CC       independent of AKT serine/threonine-protein kinase activity.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9BSA9}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer. Interacts with AKT (AKT1, AKT2 or AKT3); leading to
CC       formation of the lysoK(GF) complex, which activates the channel.
CC       Interacts with LAMP1; inhibiting the proton channel activity of
CC       TMEM175. Interacts with LAMP2; inhibiting the proton channel activity
CC       of TMEM175. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9BSA9}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Endosome membrane {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9BSA9};
CC       Multi-pass membrane protein {ECO:0000255}. Lysosome membrane
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:23436907}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000255}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: Composed of two modules of six transmembranes, forming a
CC       homodimer with a tetrameric architecture. The six transmembrane regions
CC       of each module are tightly packed within each subunit without
CC       undergoing domain swapping. Forms a central ion-conduction pore lined
CC       by the side chains of the pore-lining helices. Conserved isoleucine
CC       residues (Ile-43 in the first module and Ile-268 in the second module)
CC       in the center of the pore serve as the gate in the closed conformation.
CC       In the widened channel in the open conformation, the same residues
CC       establish a constriction essential for potassium selectivity.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9BSA9}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the TMEM175 family. {ECO:0000305}.
