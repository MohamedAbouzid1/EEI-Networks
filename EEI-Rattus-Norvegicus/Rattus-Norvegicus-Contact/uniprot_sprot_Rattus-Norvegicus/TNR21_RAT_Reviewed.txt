CC   -!- FUNCTION: Promotes apoptosis, possibly via a pathway that involves the
CC       activation of NF-kappa-B (By similarity). Can also promote apoptosis
CC       mediated by BAX and by the release of cytochrome c from the
CC       mitochondria into the cytoplasm (By similarity). Plays a role in
CC       neuronal apoptosis, including apoptosis in response to amyloid peptides
CC       derived from APP, and is required for both normal cell body death and
CC       axonal pruning (By similarity). Trophic-factor deprivation triggers the
CC       cleavage of surface APP by beta-secretase to release sAPP-beta which is
CC       further cleaved to release an N-terminal fragment of APP (N-APP) (By
CC       similarity). N-APP binds TNFRSF21; this triggers caspase activation and
CC       degeneration of both neuronal cell bodies (via caspase-3) and axons
CC       (via caspase-6) (By similarity). Negatively regulates oligodendrocyte
CC       survival, maturation and myelination (PubMed:21725297). Plays a role in
CC       signaling cascades triggered by stimulation of T-cell receptors, in the
CC       adaptive immune response and in the regulation of T-cell
CC       differentiation and proliferation (By similarity). Negatively regulates
CC       T-cell responses and the release of cytokines such as IL4, IL5, IL10,
CC       IL13 and IFNG by Th2 cells (By similarity). Negatively regulates the
CC       production of IgG, IgM and IgM in response to antigens (By similarity).
CC       May inhibit the activation of JNK in response to T-cell stimulation (By
CC       similarity). Also acts as a regulator of pyroptosis: recruits CASP8 in
CC       response to reactive oxygen species (ROS) and subsequent oxidation,
CC       leading to activation of GSDMC (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O75509, ECO:0000269|PubMed:21725297}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Associates with TRADD. Interacts with NGFR. Interacts with
CC       CASP8 (By similarity). Interacts with N-APP (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O75509, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9EPU5}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O75509};
CC       Single-pass type I membrane protein {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O75509}.
CC       Note=Endocytosed following oxidation in response to reactive oxygen
CC       species (ROS). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O75509}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Detected in brain (at protein level). Detected in
CC       corpus callosum oligodendrocytes. Detected in embryonic and adult
CC       brain. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:21725297}.
CC   -!- DEVELOPMENTAL STAGE: Detected at low levels in embryonic brain.
CC       Expression is increased in newborns and during the first two weeks
CC       after birth. Expression decreases thereafter and is low after three
CC       weeks and in adult life. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:21725297}.
CC   -!- PTM: Oxidized in response to reactive oxygen species (ROS), leading to
CC       endocytosis. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O75509}.
