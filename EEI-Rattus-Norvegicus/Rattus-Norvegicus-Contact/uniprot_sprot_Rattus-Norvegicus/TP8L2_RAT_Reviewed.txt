CC   -!- FUNCTION: Acts as a negative regulator of innate and adaptive immunity
CC       by maintaining immune homeostasis. Plays a regulatory role in the Toll-
CC       like signaling pathway by determining the strength of LPS-induced
CC       signaling and gene expression (By similarity). Inhibits TCR-mediated T-
CC       cell activation and negatively regulate T-cell function to prevent
CC       hyperresponsiveness (By similarity). Inhibits also autolysosome
CC       formation via negatively modulating MTOR activation by interacting with
CC       RAC1 and promoting the disassociation of the RAC1-MTOR complex (By
CC       similarity). Plays an essential role in NK-cell biology by acting as a
CC       checkpoint and displaying an expression pattern correlating with NK-
CC       cell maturation process and by negatively regulating NK-cell maturation
CC       and antitumor immunity (By similarity). Mechanistically, suppresses IL-
CC       15-triggered mTOR activity in NK-cells (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q6P589, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9D8Y7}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: May interact with CASP8; however, such result is unclear since
CC       could not reproduce the interaction with CASP8. Interacts with RAC1.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q6P589}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q6P589}. Nucleus
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q6P589}. Lysosome
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q6P589}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The central region was initially thought to constitute a DED
CC       (death effector) domain. However, 3D-structure data reveal a previously
CC       uncharacterized fold that is different from the predicted fold of a DED
CC       (death effector) domain. It consists of a large, hydrophobic central
CC       cavity that is poised for cofactor binding (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- PTM: Phosphorylated by TAK1/MAP3K7; this phosphorylation triggers
CC       association with BTRC and subsequent ubiquitination and degradation.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q6P589}.
CC   -!- PTM: Ubiquitinated in a BTRC-depdent manner; leading to degradation
CC       mediated through the proteasome pathway.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q6P589}.
