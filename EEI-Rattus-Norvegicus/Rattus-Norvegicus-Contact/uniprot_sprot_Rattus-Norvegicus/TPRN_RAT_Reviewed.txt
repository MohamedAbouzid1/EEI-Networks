CC   -!- FUNCTION: Essential for hearing (By similarity). Required for
CC       maintenance of stereocilia on both inner and outer hair cells (By
CC       similarity). Necessary for the integrity of the stereociliary rootlet
CC       (By similarity). May act as an actin cytoskeleton regulator involved in
CC       the regulation of actin dynamics at the pointed end in hair cells (By
CC       similarity). Forms rings at the base of stereocilia and binds actin
CC       filaments in the stereocilia which may stabilize the stereocilia (By
CC       similarity). Acts as a strong inhibitor of PPP1CA phosphatase activity
CC       (By similarity). Recruited to sites of DNA damage and may play a role
CC       in DNA damage repair (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:A2AI08,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q4KMQ1}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts with GRXCR2; the interaction restricts TPRN to the
CC       stereocilum basal region (By similarity). Interacts with actin ACTB;
CC       the interaction may stabilize stereocilia (By similarity). Interacts
CC       with CLIC5 (By similarity). Interacts with PTPRQ (By similarity). TPRN,
CC       CLIC5 and PTPQR form concentric rings at the base of stereocilia and
CC       may form a complex (By similarity). Interacts with phosphatase PPP1CA;
CC       the interaction results in inhibition of PPC1A phosphatase activity (By
CC       similarity). Interacts with DNA damage response proteins XRCC6/KU70,
CC       XRCC5/KU80, PARP1, TOP1 and TOP2A; these interactions recruit TPRN to
CC       sites of DNA damage where it may play a role in DNA repair (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:A2AI08,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q4KMQ1}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell projection, stereocilium
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:A2AI08}. Cell projection, microvillus
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:A2AI08}. Nucleus, nucleoplasm
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q4KMQ1}. Cytoplasm
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q4KMQ1}. Note=Localized prominently at the basal
CC       taper region of hair cell stereocilia (By similarity). Forms organized
CC       ring-like structures with diameters ranging from 150 to 200 nm in the
CC       stereocilium taper region (By similarity). Detected in microvilli of
CC       inner hair cell supporting cells (By similarity). Predominantly nuclear
CC       in non-stereocilium cells but can shuttle between the nucleus and the
CC       cytoplasm (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:A2AI08,
