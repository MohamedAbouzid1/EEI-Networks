CC   -!- FUNCTION: E3 ubiquitin ligase that plays essential roles in neuronal
CC       functions such as regulation of neuronal plasticity, learning, and
CC       memory (PubMed:20352094). In addition to its neuronal functions,
CC       participates in other biological processes such as innate immunity or
CC       cell cycle regulation. Component of the cytoskeleton-associated
CC       recycling or transport complex in neurons, polyubiquitinates gamma-
CC       actin, thus regulating neuronal plasticity, learning, and memory (By
CC       similarity). Ubiquitinates postsynaptic scaffold GKAP, a neuronal
CC       substrate involved in synaptic remodeling and thereby modulates
CC       dendritic spine morphology (PubMed:20352094). Positively regulates
CC       motility of microtubule-dependent motor protein KIF21B (By similarity).
CC       Induces growth arrest via its RING-dependent E3 ligase activity and
CC       ubiquinates CDKN1A. Positively regulates TLR3-mediated signaling by
CC       mediating 'Lys-63'-linked polyubiquitination of TLR3. In turn, promotes
CC       the recognition and sorting of polyubiquitinated TLR3 by the ESCRT
CC       complexes (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O75382,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9R1R2, ECO:0000269|PubMed:20352094}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY:

CC         cysteine + N(6)-ubiquitinyl-[acceptor protein]-L-lysine.;
CC         EC=2.3.2.27; Evidence={ECO:0000269|PubMed:20352094};
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Forms homooligomers. Interacts with TRIM2; this interaction
CC       reduces TRIM2 activity (By similarity). Associates with myosin-Vb
CC       (MYO5B) and alpha-actinin-4 (ACTN4) (PubMed:10391919, PubMed:10673389).
CC       Component of the CART complex, at least composed of ACTN4, HGS/HRS,
CC       MYO5B and TRIM3. Interacts with ZFYVE28/LST2 (By similarity). Interacts
CC       with KIF21B (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O75382,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9R1R2, ECO:0000269|PubMed:10391919,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:10673389}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O75382}. Early
CC       endosome {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O75382}. Golgi apparatus, trans-Golgi
CC       network {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9R1R2}. Cell projection, dendrite
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9R1R2}. Note=Mainly located in the Golgi
CC       apparatus and transported to the early endosomes upon stimulation with
CC       dsRNA. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O75382}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Highly expressed in the brain, moderate levels in
CC       the lung, very low levels in the liver, kidney and heart. In the brain,
CC       expression was highest in the cerebellum. Expression in the brain is
CC       found at low levels at embryonic day 15 and then increases during the
CC       first two postnatal weeks before decreasing through adulthood
CC       (PubMed:20352094). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:20352094}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The interaction with MYO5B is dependent upon its NHL repeats,
CC       which form a beta-propeller (NHL) domain containing six blades.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the TRIM/RBCC family. {ECO:0000305}.
