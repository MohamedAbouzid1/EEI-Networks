CC   -!- FUNCTION: Constitutively open nonselective divalent cation-conducting
CC       channels which mediate the influx of Ca(2+), Mg(2+), Mn(2+), Ba(2+),
CC       and Ni(2+) into the cytoplasm, leading to membrane depolarization.
CC       Impermeable to zinc ions. In addition, forms heteromultimeric ion
CC       channels with TRPM3 which are permeable for calcium and zinc ions.
CC       Plays an essential role for the depolarizing photoresponse of retinal
CC       ON bipolar cells (By similarity). In the dark, tonic release of
CC       glutamate activates the G-protein coupled receptor for glutamate GRM6,
CC       its activation induces the release of G(o) protein and the beta-gamma G
CC       protein dimer. Both subunits can interact and inactivate the TRPM1
CC       channel. A light onset, induces decrease in glutamate release and
CC       deactivation of GRM6 leading to channel opening and membrane
CC       depolarization (By similarity). May play a role in metastasis
CC       suppression (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q2TV84,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q7Z4N2}.

CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: Inhibited by extracellular zinc ions. Inhibited by
CC       intracellular Mg(2+). Activated by the neuroactive steroid pregnenolone
CC       sulfate (By similarity). Negatively regulated by activation of GRM6
CC       receptors in the ON-bipolar cells (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q2TV84, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q7Z4N2}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts with TRPM3; the interaction results in the formation
CC       of a heteromultimeric cation channel complex that are functionally
CC       different from the homomeric channels (By similarity). Interacts with
CC       GPR179 (By similarity). Associates with both guanine nucleotide-binding
CC       proteins G(o) and beta-gamma G protein dimer; implicated in directly
CC       regulating TRPM1 channel open-state (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q2TV84, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q7Z4N2}.
