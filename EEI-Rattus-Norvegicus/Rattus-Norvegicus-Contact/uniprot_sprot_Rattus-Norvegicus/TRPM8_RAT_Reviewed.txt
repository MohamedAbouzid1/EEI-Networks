CC   -!- FUNCTION: Non-selective ion channel permeable to monovalent and
CC       divalent cations, including Na(+), K(+), and Ca(2+), with higher
CC       permeability for Ca(2+) (PubMed:11882888). Activated by multiple
CC       factors, such as temperature, voltage, pressure, and changes in
CC       osmolality. Activated by cool temperatures (<23-28 degrees Celsius) and
CC       by chemical ligands evoking a sensation of coolness, such as menthol
CC       and icilin, therefore plays a central role in the detection of
CC       environmental cold temperatures (By similarity). TRPM8 is a voltage-
CC       dependent channel; its activation by cold or chemical ligands shifts
CC       its voltage thresholds towards physiological membrane potentials,
CC       leading to the opening of the channel (By similarity). In addition to
CC       its critical role in temperature sensing, regulates basal tear
CC       secretion by sensing evaporation-induced cooling and changes in
CC       osmolality (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q7Z2W7,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8R4D5, ECO:0000269|PubMed:11882888}.

CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: Activated by cold temperatures and by both natural
CC       and synthetic cooling compounds such as menthol and icilin (By
CC       similarity). Activation of the channel requires the presence of
CC       PI(4,5)P2; PI(4,5)P2 is necessary to gate the channel. Activated by
CC       intracellular Ca(2+) (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q7Z2W7,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8R4D5}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homotetramer (PubMed:20214891). Interacts (via N-terminus and
CC       C-terminus domains) with TCAF1; the interaction stimulates TRPM8
CC       channel activity. Interacts (via N-terminus and C-terminus domains)
CC       with TCAF2; the interaction inhibits TRPM8 channel activity (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q7Z2W7,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8R4D5, ECO:0000269|PubMed:20214891}.
