CC   -!- FUNCTION: Non-catalytic component of the TSC-TBC complex, a
CC       multiprotein complex that acts as a negative regulator of the canonical
CC       mTORC1 complex, an evolutionarily conserved central nutrient sensor
CC       that stimulates anabolic reactions and macromolecule biosynthesis to
CC       promote cellular biomass generation and growth (By similarity). The
CC       TSC-TBC complex acts as a GTPase-activating protein (GAP) for the small
CC       GTPase RHEB, a direct activator of the protein kinase activity of
CC       mTORC1 (By similarity). In absence of nutrients, the TSC-TBC complex
CC       inhibits mTORC1, thereby preventing phosphorylation of ribosomal
CC       protein S6 kinase (RPS6KB1 and RPS6KB2) and EIF4EBP1 (4E-BP1) by the
CC       mTORC1 signaling (By similarity). The TSC-TBC complex is inactivated in
CC       response to nutrients, relieving inhibition of mTORC1 (By similarity).
CC       Within the TSC-TBC complex, TSC1 stabilizes TSC2 and prevents TSC2
CC       self-aggregation (PubMed:16707451). Involved in microtubule-mediated
CC       protein transport via its ability to regulate mTORC1 signaling
CC       (PubMed:16707451). Also acts as a co-chaperone for HSP90AA1
CC       facilitating HSP90AA1 chaperoning of protein clients such as kinases,
CC       TSC2 and glucocorticoid receptor NR3C1 (By similarity). Increases ATP
CC       binding to HSP90AA1 and inhibits HSP90AA1 ATPase activity (By
CC       similarity). Competes with the activating co-chaperone AHSA1 for
CC       binding to HSP90AA1, thereby providing a reciprocal regulatory
CC       mechanism for chaperoning of client proteins (By similarity). Recruits
CC       TSC2 to HSP90AA1 and stabilizes TSC2 by preventing the interaction
CC       between TSC2 and ubiquitin ligase HERC1 (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q92574, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16707451}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Component of the TSC-TBC complex (also named Rhebulator
CC       complex), composed of 2 molecules of TSC1, 2 molecules of TSC2 and 1
CC       molecule of TBC1D7 (Probable) (PubMed:9809973). Probably forms a
CC       complex composed of chaperones HSP90 and HSP70, co-chaperones
CC       STIP1/HOP, CDC37, PPP5C, PTGES3/p23, TSC1 and client protein TSC2 (By
CC       similarity). Forms a complex composed of chaperones HSP90 and HSP70,
CC       co-chaperones CDC37, PPP5C, TSC1 and client protein TSC2, CDK4, AKT,
CC       RAF1 and NR3C1; this complex does not contain co-chaperones STIP1/HOP
CC       and PTGES3/p23 (By similarity). Forms a complex containing HSP90AA1,
CC       TSC1 and TSC2; TSC1 is required to recruit TCS2 to the complex (By
CC       similarity). Interacts (via C-terminus) with the closed form of
CC       HSP90AA1 (via the middle domain and TPR repeat-binding motif) (By
CC       similarity). Interacts with DOCK7 (By similarity). Interacts with FBXW5
CC       (By similarity). Interacts with WDR45B. Interacts with RPAP3 and URI1
CC       (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q92574,
CC       ECO:0000305|PubMed:9809973}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Lysosome membrane {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q92574};
CC       Peripheral membrane protein {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q92574}. Cytoplasm,
CC       cytosol {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q92574}. Note=Recruited to lysosomal
CC       membranes in a RHEB-dependent process in absence of nutrients. In
CC       response to nutrients, the complex dissociates from lysosomal membranes
CC       and relocalizes to the cytosol. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q92574}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Highly expressed in brain, spleen and kidney,
CC       followed by liver and heart. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10029074}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The C-terminal putative coiled-coil domain is necessary for
CC       interaction with TSC2. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q92574}.
CC   -!- PTM: Phosphorylation at Ser-505 does not affect interaction with TSC2.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q92574}.

CC       ubiquitination promotes TSC1/TSC2 complex stability.
