CC   -!- FUNCTION: Required during spermatogenesis and placenta development,
CC       participating in the repression of retrotransposable elements and
CC       prevent their mobilization. Collaborates with the Piwi-interacting RNA
CC       (piRNA) pathway, which mediates the repression of transposable elements
CC       during meiosis by forming complexes composed of piRNAs and Piwi
CC       proteins. Interacts with Piwi proteins and directly binds piRNAs, a
CC       class of 24 to 30 nucleotide RNAs that are generated by a Dicer-
CC       independent mechanism and are primarily derived from transposons and
CC       other repeated sequence elements. Also during spermatogenesis,
CC       promotes, with UBR2, SPO11-dependent recombination foci to accumulate
CC       and drive robust homologous chromosome synapsis. Interacts with LINE-1
CC       retrotransposon encoded LIRE1, stimulates LIRE1 polyubiquitination,
CC       mediated by UBR2, and degradation, inhibiting LINE-1 retrotransposon
CC       mobilization. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q99MV2}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts with UBR2; does not lead to Tex19.1 degradation and
CC       stabilizes it. Interacts with piRNA-associated proteins DDX4, EDC4,
CC       MAEL, PIWIL1, PIWIL2, RANBP9 and TDRD6. Interacts with L1RE1.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q99MV2}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q99MV2}.
CC       Note=Was initially reported to localize in the nucleus. However, it was
CC       later shown to localize in cytoplasm only. Cytoplasmic localization is
CC       distinct from the meiotic nuage, also named P granule, a germ-cell-
CC       specific organelle required to repress transposon activity during
CC       meiosis. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q99MV2}.
