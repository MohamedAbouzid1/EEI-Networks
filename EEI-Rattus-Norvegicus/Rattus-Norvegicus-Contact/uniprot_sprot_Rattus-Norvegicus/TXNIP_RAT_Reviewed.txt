CC   -!- FUNCTION: May act as an oxidative stress mediator by inhibiting
CC       thioredoxin activity or by limiting its bioavailability (By
CC       similarity). Interacts with COPS5 and restores COPS5-induced
CC       suppression of CDKN1B stability, blocking the COPS5-mediated
CC       translocation of CDKN1B from the nucleus to the cytoplasm (By
CC       similarity). Functions as a transcriptional repressor, possibly by
CC       acting as a bridge molecule between transcription factors and
CC       corepressor complexes, and over-expression will induce G0/G1 cell cycle
CC       arrest (By similarity). Required for the maturation of natural killer
CC       cells (By similarity). Acts as a suppressor of tumor cell growth.
CC       Inhibits the proteasomal degradation of DDIT4, and thereby contributes
CC       to the inhibition of the mammalian target of rapamycin complex 1
CC       (mTORC1) (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8BG60,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9H3M7}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer; disulfide-linked. Interacts with TXN/thioredoxin
CC       through its redox-active site. Interacts with transcriptional
CC       repressors ZBTB16, ZBTB32 and HDAC1. Interacts with DDIT4.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9H3M7}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8BG60}.
CC   -!- PTM: Ubiquitinated; undergoes heterotypic 'Lys-48'-/'Lys-63'-branched
CC       polyubiquitination catalyzed by ITCH and UBR5 resulting in proteasomal
CC       degradation. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9H3M7}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the arrestin family. {ECO:0000305}.
