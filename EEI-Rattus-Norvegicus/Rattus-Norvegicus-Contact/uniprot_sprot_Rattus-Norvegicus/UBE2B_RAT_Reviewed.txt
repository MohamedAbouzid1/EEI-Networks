CC   -!- FUNCTION: E2 ubiquitin-conjugating enzyme that accepts ubiquitin from
CC       the ubiquitin-activating enzyme E1 and transfers it to a E3 ubiquitin-
CC       protein ligase (PubMed:1313008). In vitro catalyzes 'Lys-11'-, as well
CC       as 'Lys-48'- and 'Lys-63'-linked polyubiquitination (By similarity).
CC       Together with the E3 enzyme BRE1 (RNF20 and/or RNF40), plays a role in
CC       transcription regulation by catalyzing the monoubiquitination of
CC       histone H2B at 'Lys-120' to form H2BK120ub1 (By similarity). H2BK120ub1
CC       gives a specific tag for epigenetic transcriptional activation,
CC       elongation by RNA polymerase II, telomeric silencing, and is also a
CC       prerequisite for H3K4me and H3K79me formation (By similarity). May play
CC       a role in DNA repair (By similarity). Associates to the E3 ligase RAD18
CC       to form the UBE2B-RAD18 ubiquitin ligase complex involved in mono-
CC       ubiquitination of DNA-associated PCNA on 'Lys-164' (By similarity). In
CC       association with the E3 enzyme UBR4, is involved in N-end rule-
CC       dependent protein degradation (By similarity). May be involved in
CC       neurite outgrowth (PubMed:15946855). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P63146,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:1313008, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15946855}.
