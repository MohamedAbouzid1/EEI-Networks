CC   -!- FUNCTION: Hydrolase that can remove conjugated ubiquitin from target
CC       proteins such as p53/TP53, RPS2/us5, RPS3/us3, RPS10/eS10, BECN1, SNX3
CC       and CFTR. Acts as an essential regulator of p53/TP53 stability: in
CC       unstressed cells, specifically deubiquitinates p53/TP53 in the
CC       cytoplasm, leading to counteract MDM2 action and stabilize p53/TP53.
CC       Following DNA damage, translocates to the nucleus and deubiquitinates
CC       p53/TP53, leading to regulate the p53/TP53-dependent DNA damage
CC       response. Component of a regulatory loop that controls autophagy and
CC       p53/TP53 levels: mediates deubiquitination of BECN1, a key regulator of
CC       autophagy, leading to stabilize the PIK3C3/VPS34-containing complexes.
CC       In turn, PIK3C3/VPS34-containing complexes regulate USP10 stability,
CC       suggesting the existence of a regulatory system by which PIK3C3/VPS34-
CC       containing complexes regulate p53/TP53 protein levels via USP10 and
CC       USP13. Does not deubiquitinate MDM2. Plays a key role in 40S ribosome
CC       subunit recycling when a ribosome has stalled during translation: acts
CC       both by inhibiting formation of stress granules, which store stalled
CC       translation pre-initiation complexes, and mediating deubiquitination of
CC       40S ribosome subunits. Acts as a negative regulator of stress granules
CC       formation by lowering G3BP1 and G3BP2 valence, thereby preventing G3BP1
CC       and G3BP2 ability to undergo liquid-liquid phase separation (LLPS) and
CC       assembly of stress granules. Promotes 40S ribosome subunit recycling
CC       following ribosome dissociation in response to ribosome stalling by
CC       mediating deubiquitination of 40S ribosomal proteins RPS2/us5, RPS3/us3
CC       and RPS10/eS10, thereby preventing their degradation by the proteasome.
CC       Part of a ribosome quality control that takes place when ribosomes have
CC       stalled during translation initiation (iRQC): USP10 acts by removing
CC       monoubiquitination of RPS2/us5 and RPS3/us3, promoting 40S ribosomal
CC       subunit recycling. Deubiquitinates CFTR in early endosomes, enhancing
CC       its endocytic recycling. Involved in a TANK-dependent negative feedback
CC       response to attenuate NF-kappa-B activation via deubiquitinating IKBKG
CC       or TRAF6 in response to interleukin-1-beta (IL1B) stimulation or upon
CC       DNA damage. Deubiquitinates TBX21 leading to its stabilization. Plays a
CC       negative role in the RLR signaling pathway upon RNA virus infection by
CC       blocking the RIGI-mediated MAVS activation. Mechanistically, removes
CC       the unanchored 'Lys-63'-linked polyubiquitin chains of MAVS to inhibit
CC       its aggregation, essential for its activation.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q14694}.

CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: Specifically inhibited by spautin-1 (specific and
CC       potent autophagy inhibitor-1), a derivative of MBCQ that binds to USP10
CC       and inhibits deubiquitinase activity. Regulated by PIK3C3/VPS34-
CC       containing complexes (By similarity). {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Found in a deubiquitination complex with TANK, USP10 and
CC       ZC3H12A; this complex inhibits genotoxic stress- or interleukin-1-beta
CC       (IL1B)-mediated NF-kappa-B activation by promoting IKBKG or TRAF6
CC       deubiquitination. Interacts with IKBKG; this interaction increases in
CC       response to DNA damage. Interacts with TANK; this interaction increases
CC       in response to DNA damage. Interacts with TRAF6; this interaction
CC       increases in response to DNA damage. Interacts with ZC3H12A; this
CC       interaction increases in response to DNA damage. Interacts with G3BP1
CC       (via NTF2 domain) and G3BP2 (via NTF2 domain); inhibiting stress
CC       granule formation. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q14694}.

CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q14694}. Early endosome
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q14694}. Note=Cytoplasmic in normal conditions
CC       (By similarity). After DNA damage, translocates to the nucleus
CC       following phosphorylation by ATM (By similarity).
