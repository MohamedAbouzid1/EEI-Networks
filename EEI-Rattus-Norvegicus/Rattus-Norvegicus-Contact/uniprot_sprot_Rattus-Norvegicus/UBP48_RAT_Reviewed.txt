CC   -!- FUNCTION: Deubiquitinase that recognizes and hydrolyzes the peptide
CC       bond at the C-terminal Gly of ubiquitin. Involved in the processing of
CC       polyubiquitin precursors as well as that of ubiquitinated proteins.
CC       Plays a role in the regulation of NF-kappa-B activation by TNF receptor
CC       superfamily via its interactions with RELA and TRAF2. May also play a
CC       regulatory role at postsynaptic sites (PubMed:14535949). Plays an
CC       important role in cell cycle progression by deubiquitinating Aurora
CC       B/AURKB and thereby extending its stability. In the context of H.
CC       pylori infection, stabilizes nuclear RELA through deubiquitination,
CC       thereby promoting the transcriptional activity of RELA to prolong
CC       TNFAIP3 de novo synthesis. Consequently, TNFAIP3 suppresses caspase
CC       activity and apoptotic cell death. Functions also in the modulation of
CC       the ciliary and synaptic transport as well as cytoskeleton
CC       organization, which are key for photoreceptor function and homeostasis.
CC       To achieve this, stabilizes the levels of the retinal degeneration-
CC       associated proteins ARL3 and UNC119 using distinct mechanisms. Plays a
CC       positive role in pyroptosis by stabilizing gasdermin E/GSDME through
CC       removal of its 'Lys-48'-linked ubiquitination.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q86UV5, ECO:0000269|PubMed:14535949}.

CC         residue protein attached to proteins as an intracellular targeting
CC         signal).; EC=3.4.19.12; Evidence={ECO:0000269|PubMed:14535949};
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts with TRAF2 and RELA. Interacts with GPS1.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q86UV5}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q86UV5}. Nucleus

CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q86UV5}. Note=In neuronal cells, it localizes to
CC       dendrites, as well as somas. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:14535949}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Present in the brain, in particular in the
CC       postsynaptic density and the dendritic lipid raft fractions (at protein
CC       level). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:14535949}.
