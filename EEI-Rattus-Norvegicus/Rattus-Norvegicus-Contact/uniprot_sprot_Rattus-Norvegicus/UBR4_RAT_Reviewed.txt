CC   -!- FUNCTION: E3 ubiquitin-protein ligase involved in different protein
CC       quality control pathways in the cytoplasm. Component of the N-end rule
CC       pathway: ubiquitinates proteins bearing specific N-terminal residues
CC       that are destabilizing according to the N-end rule, leading to their
CC       degradation. Recognizes both type-1 and type-2 N-degrons, containing
CC       positively charged amino acids (Arg, Lys and His) and bulky and
CC       hydrophobic amino acids, respectively. Does not ubiquitinate proteins
CC       that are acetylated at the N-terminus. Together with UBR5, part of a
CC       cytoplasm protein quality control pathway that prevents protein
CC       aggregation by catalyzing assembly of heterotypic 'Lys-11'-/'Lys-48'-
CC       linked branched ubiquitin chains on aggregated proteins, leading to
CC       substrate recognition by the segregase p97/VCP and degradation by the
CC       proteasome: UBR4 probably synthesizes mixed chains containing multiple
CC       linkages, while UBR5 is likely branching multiple 'Lys-48'-linked
CC       chains of substrates initially modified. Together with KCMF1, part of a
CC       protein quality control pathway that catalyzes ubiquitination and
CC       degradation of proteins that have been oxidized in response to reactive
CC       oxygen species (ROS): recognizes proteins with an Arg-CysO3(H) degron
CC       at the N-terminus, and mediates assembly of heterotypic 'Lys-63'-/'Lys-
CC       27'-linked branched ubiquitin chains on oxidized proteins, leading to
CC       their degradation by autophagy. Catalytic component of the SIFI
CC       complex, a multiprotein complex required to inhibit the mitochondrial
CC       stress response after a specific stress event has been resolved:
CC       ubiquitinates and degrades (1) components of the HRI-mediated signaling
CC       of the integrated stress response, such as DELE1 and EIF2AK1/HRI, as
CC       well as (2) unimported mitochondrial precursors. Within the SIFI
CC       complex, UBR4 initiates ubiquitin chain that are further elongated or
CC       branched by KCMF1. Mediates ubiquitination of ACLY, leading to its
CC       subsequent degradation. Together with clathrin, forms meshwork
CC       structures involved in membrane morphogenesis and cytoskeletal
CC       organization. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q5T4S7}.

CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Component of the SIFI complex, composed of KCMF1, UBR4 and
CC       calmodulin (CALM1, CALM2 or CALM3). Interacts with E2 conjugating
CC       enzymes UBE2A and UBE2B. Interacts with RB1.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q5T4S7}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q5T4S7}.
CC       Cytoplasm, cytoskeleton {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q5T4S7}. Nucleus
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:A2AN08}. Note=Localizes to endosomes via its
CC       association with calcium-bound calmodulin (By similarity). Concentrates
CC       at the leading edge of membrane structures involved in actin motility
CC       (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:A2AN08,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q5T4S7}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The UBR-type zinc finger forms a pocket that mediates
CC       recognition of type 1 N-degrons. It can also recognize type-2 N-degrons
CC       via two phenylalanines, Phe-1672 and Phe-1714, on its hydrophobic
CC       surface. In addition to substrate-binding, the UBR-type zinc finger
CC       probably positions substrate proteins in the proximity of the bound and
CC       activated E2-ubiquitin conjugate to enable their ubiquitination.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q5T4S7}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: Constitutes an atypical E3 ubiquitin-protein ligase composed of
CC       a hemiRING-type zinc finger, a UBR-type zinc-finger interacting
CC       subdomain (UZI) and an N-terminal region that can serve as an affinity
CC       factor for the E2 conjugating enzymes UBE2A and UBE2B. Compared to
CC       classical RING-type, the hemiRING-type only binds a single zinc ion: a
CC       hydrogen bonding network is present instead of a second zinc ion. The
CC       hemiRING-type zinc finger maintains specificity for E2 conjugating
CC       enzymes UBE2A and UBE2B, which have high intrinsic lysine reactivity,
CC       obviating the need for the robust thioester activation of classical
CC       RING-type E3 ubiquitin-protein ligases. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q5T4S7}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the UBR4 family. {ECO:0000305}.
