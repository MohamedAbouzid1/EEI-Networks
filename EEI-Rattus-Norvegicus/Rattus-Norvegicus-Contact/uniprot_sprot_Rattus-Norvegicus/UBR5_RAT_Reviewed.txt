CC   -!- FUNCTION: E3 ubiquitin-protein ligase involved in different protein
CC       quality control pathways in the cytoplasm and nucleus (By similarity).
CC       Mainly acts as a ubiquitin chain elongator that extends pre-
CC       ubiquitinated substrates (By similarity). Component of the N-end rule
CC       pathway: ubiquitinates proteins bearing specific N-terminal residues
CC       that are destabilizing according to the N-end rule, leading to their
CC       degradation (By similarity). Recognizes type-1 N-degrons, containing
CC       positively charged amino acids (Arg, Lys and His) (By similarity).
CC       Together with UBR4, part of a cytoplasm protein quality control pathway
CC       that prevents protein aggregation by catalyzing assembly of heterotypic
CC       'Lys-11'-/'Lys-48'-linked branched ubiquitin chains on aggregated
CC       proteins, leading to substrate recognition by the segregase p97/VCP and
CC       degradation by the proteasome: UBR5 is probably branching multiple
CC       'Lys-48'-linked chains of substrates initially modified with mixed
CC       conjugates by UBR4 (By similarity). Together with ITCH, catalyzes 'Lys-
CC       48'-/'Lys-63'-branched ubiquitination of TXNIP, leading to its
CC       degradation: UBR5 mediates branching of 'Lys-48'-linked chains of
CC       substrates initially modified with 'Lys-63'-linked conjugates by ITCH
CC       (By similarity). Catalytic component of a nuclear protein quality
CC       control pathway that mediates ubiquitination and degradation of
CC       unpaired transcription factors (i.e. transcription factors that are not
CC       assembled into functional multiprotein complexes): specifically
CC       recognizes and binds degrons that are not accessible when transcription
CC       regulators are associated with their coactivators (By similarity).
CC       Ubiquitinates various unpaired transcription regulator (MYC, SUPT4H1,
CC       SUPT5H, CDC20 and MCRS1), as well as ligand-bound nuclear receptors
CC       (ESR1, NR1H3, NR3C1, PGR, RARA, RXRA AND VDR) that are not associated
CC       with their nuclear receptor coactivators (NCOAs) (By similarity).
CC       Involved in maturation and/or transcriptional regulation of mRNA by
CC       mediating polyubiquitination and activation of CDK9 (By similarity).
CC       Also acts as a regulator of DNA damage response by acting as a
CC       suppressor of RNF168, an E3 ubiquitin-protein ligase that promotes
CC       accumulation of 'Lys-63'-linked histone H2A and H2AX at DNA damage
CC       sites, thereby acting as a guard against excessive spreading of
CC       ubiquitinated chromatin at damaged chromosomes (By similarity).
CC       Regulates DNA topoisomerase II binding protein (TopBP1) in the DNA
CC       damage response (By similarity). Ubiquitinates acetylated PCK1 (By
CC       similarity). Acts as a positive regulator of the canonical Wnt
CC       signaling pathway by mediating (1) ubiquitination and stabilization of
CC       CTNNB1, and (2) 'Lys-48'-linked ubiquitination and degradation of TLE3
CC       (By similarity). Promotes disassembly of the mitotic checkpoint complex
CC       (MCC) from the APC/C complex by catalyzing ubiquitination of BUB1B,
CC       BUB3 and CDC20 (By similarity). Plays an essential role in
CC       extraembryonic development (By similarity). Required for the
CC       maintenance of skeletal tissue homeostasis by acting as an inhibitor of
CC       hedgehog (HH) signaling (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O95071,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q80TP3}.

CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homotetramer; composed of a dimer of dimers. Associates with
CC       CDK9 and TFIIS/TCEA1 and forms a transcription regulatory complex made
CC       of CDK9, RNAP II, UBR5 and TFIIS/TCEA1 that can stimulate target gene
CC       transcription (e.g. gamma fibrinogen/FGG) by recruiting their
CC       promoters. Associates with the E3 ligase complex containing DYRK2,
CC       EDD/UBR5, DDB1 and DCAF1 proteins (EDVP complex). Binds TOPBP1.
CC       Interacts with PIH1D1. Interacts with CIB1.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O95071}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Nucleus {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O95071}. Cytoplasm
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O95071}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Highest levels found in testis. Also present in
CC       liver, kidney, lung and brain. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12239083}.
CC   -!- DEVELOPMENTAL STAGE: In early postnatal life, expression in the testis
CC       increases to reach a maximum around day 28.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12239083}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The UBR-type zinc finger forms a pocket that mediates
CC       recognition of type 1 N-degrons. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O95071}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The UBA domain recognizes and binds ubiquitin. It acts as an
CC       ubiquitin acceptor required to place 'Lys-48' of ubiquitin into the
CC       HECT domain activie site, thereby potentiating the E3 ubiquitin-protein
CC       ligase activity. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O95071}.
