CC   -!- FUNCTION: Putative transmembrane transporter that plays a role in
CC       mitochondrial metabolism via an as yet unclear mechanism. Originally,
CC       this mitochondrial protein was thought to act as a proton transmembrane
CC       transporter from the mitochondrial intermembrane space into the matrix,
CC       causing proton leaks through the inner mitochondrial membrane, thereby
CC       uncoupling mitochondrial membrane potential generation from ATP
CC       synthesis. However, this function is controversial and uncoupling may
CC       not be the function, or at least not the main function, but rather a
CC       consequence of more conventional metabolite transporter activity.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P55916}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts with HAX1; the interaction is direct and calcium-
CC       dependent. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P55916}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Mitochondrion inner membrane
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P56501}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000255}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the mitochondrial carrier (TC 2.A.29) family.
CC       {ECO:0000305}.
