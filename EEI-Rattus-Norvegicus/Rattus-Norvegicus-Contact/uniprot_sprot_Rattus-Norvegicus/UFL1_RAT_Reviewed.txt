CC   -!- FUNCTION: E3 protein ligase that mediates ufmylation, the covalent
CC       attachment of the ubiquitin-like modifier UFM1 to lysine residues on
CC       target proteins, and which plays a key role in various processes, such
CC       as ribosome recycling, response to DNA damage, interferon response or
CC       reticulophagy (also called ER-phagy) (By similarity). Catalyzes
CC       ufmylation of many protein, such as CD274/PD-L1, CDK5RAP3, CYB5R3,
CC       DDRGK1, EIF6, histone H4, MRE11, P4HB, PDCD1/PD-1, TRIP4, RPN1,
CC       RPS20/uS10, RPL10/uL16, RPL26/uL24, SYVN1/HRD1 and TP53/p53 (By
CC       similarity). As part of the UREL complex, plays a key role in ribosome
CC       recycling by catalyzing mono-ufmylation of RPL26/uL24 subunit of the
CC       60S ribosome (By similarity). Ufmylation of RPL26/uL24 occurs on free
CC       60S ribosomes following ribosome dissociation: it weakens the junction
CC       between post-termination 60S subunits and SEC61 translocons, promoting
CC       release and recycling of the large ribosomal subunit from the
CC       endoplasmic reticulum membrane (By similarity). Ufmylation of
CC       RPL26/uL24 and subsequent 60S ribosome recycling either take place
CC       after normal termination of translation or after ribosome stalling
CC       during cotranslational translocation at the endoplasmic reticulum (By
CC       similarity). Involved in reticulophagy in response to endoplasmic
CC       reticulum stress by mediating ufmylation of proteins such as CYB5R3 and
CC       RPN1, thereby promoting lysosomal degradation of ufmylated proteins (By
CC       similarity). Ufmylation in response to endoplasmic reticulum stress is
CC       essential for processes such as hematopoiesis, blood vessel
CC       morphogenesis or inflammatory response (By similarity). Mediates
CC       ufmylation of DDRGK1 and CDK5RAP3; the role of these modifications is
CC       however unclear: as both DDRGK1 and CDK5RAP3 act as substrate adapters
CC       for ufmylation, it is uncertain whether ufmylation of these proteins
CC       is, a collateral effect or is required for ufmylation (By similarity).
CC       Acts as a negative regulator of T-cell activation by mediating
CC       ufmylation and stabilization of PDCD1/PD-1 (By similarity). Also
CC       involved in the response to DNA damage: recruited to double-strand
CC       break sites following DNA damage and mediates monoufmylation of histone
CC       H4 and ufmylation of MRE11 (By similarity). Mediates ufmylation of
CC       TP53/p53, promoting its stability (By similarity). Catalyzes ufmylation
CC       of TRIP4, thereby playing a role in nuclear receptor-mediated
CC       transcription (By similarity). Required for hematopoietic stem cell
CC       function and hematopoiesis (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O94874, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8CCJ3}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Catalytic component of the UFM1 ribosome E3 ligase (UREL)
CC       complex, composed of UFL1, DDRGK1 and CDK5RAP3 (PubMed:20531390).
CC       Interacts with E2-like enzyme UFC1 (By similarity). Interacts with RELA
CC       (By similarity). Interacts with NBN; promoting recruitment to double-
CC       strand breaks following DNA damage (By similarity). Interacts (when
CC       phosphorylated) with YWHAG/14-3-3-gamma; sequestering UFL1 and
CC       preventing its association with PDCD1/PD-1 substrate (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O94874, ECO:0000269|PubMed:20531390}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Endoplasmic reticulum membrane
CC       {ECO:0000305|PubMed:20531390}. Cytoplasm, cytosol
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O94874}. Nucleus {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O94874}.
CC       Chromosome {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O94874}. Note=Recruited to double-
CC       strand breaks by the MRE11-RAD50-NBN (MRN) complex following DNA
CC       damage. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O94874}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Ubiquitously expressed with expression detected in
CC       brain, skeletal muscle, lung, heart, gall bladder, liver, small
CC       intestine, pancreas, spleen and kidney (at protein level)
CC       (PubMed:20228063). At 8 weeks after birth, high expression in the
CC       Purkinje cell layer of the cerebellum (PubMed:20531390).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:20228063, ECO:0000269|PubMed:20531390}.
CC   -!- PTM: Ubiquitinated, leading to its degradation by the proteasome.
CC       Interaction with CDK5RAP3 protects both proteins against ubiquitination
CC       and degradation via the proteasome. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O94874}.
CC   -!- PTM: Phosphorylated at Ser-462 by ATM, enhancing protein ligase
CC       activity and promoting ATM activation in a positive feedback loop.
CC       Phosphorylation at Thr-535 by AMPK promotes its interaction with
CC       YWHAG/14-3-3-gamma, thereby preventing UFL1 association with PDCD1/PD-1
CC       substrate. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O94874}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the UFL1 family. {ECO:0000305}.
