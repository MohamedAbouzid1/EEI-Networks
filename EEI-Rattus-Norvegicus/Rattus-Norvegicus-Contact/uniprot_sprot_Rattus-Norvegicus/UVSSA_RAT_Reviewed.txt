CC   -!- FUNCTION: Factor involved in transcription-coupled nucleotide excision
CC       repair (TC-NER), a mechanism that rapidly removes RNA polymerase II-
CC       blocking lesions from the transcribed strand of active genes.
CC       Facilitates the ubiquitination of the elongating form of RNA polymerase
CC       II (RNA pol IIo) at DNA damage sites, thereby promoting RNA pol IIo
CC       backtracking and access by the TC-NER machinery to lesion sites. Acts
CC       by promoting stabilization of ERCC6 by recruiting deubiquitinating
CC       enzyme USP7 to TC-NER complexes, preventing UV-induced degradation of
CC       ERCC6 by the proteasome. Also facilitates transfer of TFIIH to RNA
CC       polymerase II. Not involved in processing oxidative damage.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q2YD98}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts with the elongating form of RNA polymerase II (RNA
CC       pol IIo) during transcription stress. Interacts with the TFIIH complex
CC       during transcription stress. Interacts with ERCC6. Interacts with
CC       ERCC8. Interacts with USP7. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q2YD98}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Chromosome {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q2YD98}.
CC       Note=Accumulates at UV DNA damage sites.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q2YD98}.
CC   -!- PTM: Monoubiquitinated at Lys-419 in response to transcription stress;
CC       this promotes efficient transfer of TFIIH to stalled RNA polymerase II.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q2YD98}.
