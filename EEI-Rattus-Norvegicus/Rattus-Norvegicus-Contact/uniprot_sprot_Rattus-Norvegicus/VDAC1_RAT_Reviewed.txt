CC   -!- FUNCTION: Non-selective voltage-gated ion channel that mediates the
CC       transport of anions and cations through the mitochondrion outer
CC       membrane and plasma membrane (PubMed:11485562). The channel at the
CC       outer mitochondrial membrane allows diffusion of small hydrophilic
CC       molecules; in the plasma membrane it is involved in cell volume
CC       regulation and apoptosis (By similarity). It adopts an open
CC       conformation at low or zero membrane potential and a closed
CC       conformation at potentials above 30-40 mV (PubMed:450112). The open
CC       state has a weak anion selectivity whereas the closed state is cation-
CC       selective (PubMed:25628567). Binds various signaling molecules,
CC       including the sphingolipid ceramide, the phospholipid
CC       phosphatidylcholine, and the sterols cholesterol and oxysterol. In
CC       depolarized mitochondria, acts downstream of PRKN and PINK1 to promote
CC       mitophagy or prevent apoptosis; polyubiquitination by PRKN promotes
CC       mitophagy, while monoubiquitination by PRKN decreases mitochondrial
CC       calcium influx which ultimately inhibits apoptosis. May participate in
CC       the formation of the permeability transition pore complex (PTPC)
CC       responsible for the release of mitochondrial products that triggers
CC       apoptosis. May mediate ATP export from cells (By similarity). Part of a
CC       complex composed of HSPA9, ITPR1 and VDAC1 that regulates mitochondrial
CC       calcium-dependent apoptosis by facilitating calcium transport from the
CC       ER lumen to the mitochondria intermembrane space thus providing calcium
CC       for the downstream calcium channel MCU that directly releases it into
CC       mitochondria matrix (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P21796,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q60932, ECO:0000269|PubMed:11485562,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:25628567, ECO:0000269|PubMed:450112}.
CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalyzes the scrambling of phospholipids across the outer
CC       mitochondrial membrane; the mechanism is unrelated to channel activity
CC       and is capable of translocating both anionic and zwitterionic
CC       phospholipids. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P21796}.

CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: Inhibited by nitric oxide. Voltage-gated ion
CC       channel activity is inhibited by lanthanum(3+) and ruthenium red
CC       (PubMed:11485562). Mitochondrial calcium transport is inhibited by
CC       lanthanum(3+), ruthenium red and Ru360 (PubMed:11485562).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P21796, ECO:0000269|PubMed:11485562}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer and homotrimer; in response to cyclic AMP or
CC       calcium; oligomerization is required for scramblase activity. Component
CC       of the mitochondrial permeability transition pore complex (mPTPC), at
CC       least composed of SPG7, VDAC1 and PPIF. Interacts with SPG7, NIPSNAP2
CC       and SLC25A30. Interacts with hexokinases including HK1. The HK1-VDAC1
CC       complex interacts with ATF2. Interacts with BCL2L1. Interacts with
CC       BAK1. Interacts with RTL10/BOP (via BH3 domain). Interacts with
CC       amyloid-beta and APP; induces VDAC1 dephosphorylation. Interacts with
CC       TMEM41B. Interacts with BCAP31. Interacts with HSPA9; this interaction
CC       couples ITPR1 to VDAC1 (PubMed:17178908).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P21796, ECO:0000269|PubMed:17178908}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Mitochondrion outer membrane
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P21796, ECO:0000269|PubMed:17178908}; Multi-pass

CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P21796}; Multi-pass membrane protein

CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17178908}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Widely expressed. High levels in heart and kidney
CC       with lower levels in brain and ascitic tumor. Very low levels in liver.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10998068}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: Consists mainly of a membrane-spanning beta-barrel formed by 19
CC       beta-strands. The helical N-terminus folds back into the pore opening
CC       and plays a role in voltage-gated channel activity.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P21796}.
CC   -!- PTM: Phosphorylation at Ser-193 by NEK1 promotes the closed
CC       conformational state preventing excessive mitochondrial membrane
CC       permeability and subsequent apoptotic cell death after injury.
CC       Phosphorylation by the AKT-GSK3B axis stabilizes the protein probably
CC       by preventing ubiquitin-mediated proteasomal degradation.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P21796}.
CC   -!- PTM: Ubiquitinated. Undergoes monoubiquitination and polyubiquitination
CC       by PRKN; monoubiquitination at Lys-274 inhibits apoptosis, whereas
CC       polyubiquitination leads to its degradation and promotes mitophagy.
CC       Deubiquitinated by USP30. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P21796}.
