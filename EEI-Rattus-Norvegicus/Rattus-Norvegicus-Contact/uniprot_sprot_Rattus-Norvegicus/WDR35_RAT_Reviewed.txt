CC   -!- FUNCTION: As a component of the IFT complex A (IFT-A), a complex
CC       required for retrograde ciliary transport and entry into cilia of G
CC       protein-coupled receptors (GPCRs), it is involved in ciliogenesis and
CC       ciliary protein trafficking (By similarity). May promote CASP3
CC       activation and TNF-stimulated apoptosis (PubMed:20193664).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9P2L0, ECO:0000269|PubMed:20193664}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Component of the IFT complex A (IFT-A) complex. IFT-A complex
CC       is divided into a core subcomplex composed of IFT122:IFT140:WDR19 which
CC       is associated with TULP3 and a peripheral subcomplex composed of
CC       IFT43:WDR35:TTC21B. Interacts directy with IFT122, ITF43 and TTC21B.
CC       Interacts with IFT43. Interacts with CFAP61 (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8BND3, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9P2L0}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm, cytoskeleton, microtubule organizing
CC       center, centrosome {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8BND3}. Cytoplasm,
CC       cytoskeleton, cilium axoneme {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8BND3}. Cytoplasm,
CC       cytoskeleton, cilium basal body {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8BND3}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed at high levels in testis and at lower
CC       levels in the brain (at protein level). Also present in other tissues,
CC       including heart, uterus, spinal cord, ovary, liver, kidney, lung,
CC       pancreas and stomach. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:20193664}.
