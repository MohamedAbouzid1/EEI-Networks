CC   -!- FUNCTION: Multi-functional protein which regulates not only caspases
CC       and apoptosis, but also modulates inflammatory signaling and immunity,
CC       copper homeostasis, mitogenic kinase signaling, cell proliferation, as
CC       well as cell invasion and metastasis. Acts as a direct caspase
CC       inhibitor. Directly bind to the active site pocket of CASP3 and CASP7
CC       and obstructs substrate entry. Inactivates CASP9 by keeping it in a
CC       monomeric, inactive state. Acts as an E3 ubiquitin-protein ligase
CC       regulating NF-kappa-B signaling and the target proteins for its E3
CC       ubiquitin-protein ligase activity include: RIPK1, RIPK2, MAP3K2/MEKK2,
CC       DIABLO/SMAC, AIFM1, CCS, PTEN and BIRC5/survivin. Acts as an important
CC       regulator of innate immunity by mediating 'Lys-63'-linked
CC       polyubiquitination of RIPK2 downstream of NOD1 and NOD2, thereby
CC       transforming RIPK2 into a scaffolding protein for downstream effectors,
CC       ultimately leading to activation of the NF-kappa-B and MAP kinases
CC       signaling. 'Lys-63'-linked polyubiquitination of RIPK2 also promotes
CC       recruitment of the LUBAC complex to RIPK2. Regulates the BMP signaling
CC       pathway and the SMAD and MAP3K7/TAK1 dependent pathways leading to NF-
CC       kappa-B and JNK activation. Ubiquitination of CCS leads to enhancement
CC       of its chaperone activity toward its physiologic target, SOD1, rather
CC       than proteasomal degradation. Ubiquitination of MAP3K2/MEKK2 and AIFM1
CC       does not lead to proteasomal degradation. Plays a role in copper
CC       homeostasis by ubiquitinating COMMD1 and promoting its proteasomal
CC       degradation. Can also function as E3 ubiquitin-protein ligase of the
CC       NEDD8 conjugation pathway, targeting effector caspases for neddylation
CC       and inactivation. Ubiquitinates and therefore mediates the proteasomal
CC       degradation of BCL2 in response to apoptosis. Protects cells from
CC       spontaneous formation of the ripoptosome, a large multi-protein complex
CC       that has the capability to kill cancer cells in a caspase-dependent and
CC       caspase-independent manner. Suppresses ripoptosome formation by
CC       ubiquitinating RIPK1 and CASP8. Acts as a positive regulator of Wnt
CC       signaling and ubiquitinates TLE1, TLE2, TLE3, TLE4 and AES.
CC       Ubiquitination of TLE3 results in inhibition of its interaction with
CC       TCF7L2/TCF4 thereby allowing efficient recruitment and binding of the
CC       transcriptional coactivator beta-catenin to TCF7L2/TCF4 that is
CC       required to initiate a Wnt-specific transcriptional program.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P98170}.

CC       similarity). Interacts with TLE3 and TCF7L2/TCF4 (By similarity).
