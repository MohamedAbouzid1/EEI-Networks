CC   -!- FUNCTION: ATP-dependent metalloprotease that catalyzes the degradation
CC       of folded and unfolded proteins with a suitable degron sequence in the
CC       mitochondrial intermembrane region. Plays an important role in
CC       regulating mitochondrial morphology and function by cleaving OPA1 at
CC       position S2, giving rise to a form of OPA1 that promotes maintenance of
CC       normal mitochondrial structure and mitochondrial protein metabolism (By
CC       similarity). Ensures cell proliferation, maintains normal cristae
CC       morphology and complex I respiration activity, promotes antiapoptotic
CC       activity and protects mitochondria from the accumulation of oxidatively
CC       damaged membrane proteins. Required to control the accumulation of
CC       nonassembled respiratory chain subunits (NDUFB6, OX4 and ND1) (By
CC       similarity). Involved in the mitochondrial adaptation in response to
CC       various signals, such as stress or developmental cues, by mediating
CC       degradation of mitochondrial proteins to rewire the mitochondrial
CC       proteome (By similarity). Catalyzes degradation of mitochondrial
CC       proteins, such as translocases, lipid transfer proteins and metabolic
CC       enzymes in response to nutrient starvation in order to limit
CC       mitochondrial biogenesis: mechanistically, YME1L is activated by
CC       decreased phosphatidylethanolamine levels caused by LPIN1 activity in
CC       response to mTORC1 inhibition (By similarity). Acts as a regulator of
CC       adult neural stem cell self-renewal by promoting mitochondrial proteome
CC       rewiring, preserving neural stem and progenitor cells self-renewal.
CC       Required for normal, constitutive degradation of PRELID1 (By
CC       similarity). Catalyzes the degradation of OMA1 in response to membrane
CC       depolarization. Mediates degradation of TIMM17A downstream of the
CC       integrated stress response (ISR) (By similarity). Catalyzes degradation
CC       of MICU1 when MICU1 is not assembled via an interchain disulfide (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O88967,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q96TA2}.
