CC   -!- FUNCTION: Transcription regulator required to maintain maternal and
CC       paternal gene imprinting, a process by which gene expression is
CC       restricted in a parent of origin-specific manner by epigenetic
CC       modification of genomic DNA and chromatin, including DNA methylation.
CC       Acts by controlling DNA methylation during the earliest multicellular
CC       stages of development at multiple imprinting control regions (ICRs).
CC       Acts together with ZNF445. Required for the establishment of maternal
CC       methylation imprints at SNRPN locus. Acts as a transcriptional
CC       repressor in Schwann cells. Binds to a 5'-TGCCGC-3' consensus sequence
CC       and recognizes the methylated CpG within this element.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8C6P8}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Nucleus {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8C6P8}.
CC       Note=Binds various differentially methylated regions (DMR), including
CC       the Snrpn DMR. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8C6P8}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed in oligodendrocytes and at lower levels
CC       in astrocytes. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15070898}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The KRAB domain is required for function as transcriptional
CC       repressor. {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: Zinc fingers 2 and 3 mediate recognition of the target element,
CC       ZF2 interacting with the 5' half (TGC) and ZF3 interacting with the 3'
CC       half (CGC). {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the krueppel C2H2-type zinc-finger protein
