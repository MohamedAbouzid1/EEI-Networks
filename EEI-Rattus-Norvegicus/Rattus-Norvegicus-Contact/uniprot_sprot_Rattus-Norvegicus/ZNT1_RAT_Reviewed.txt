CC   -!- FUNCTION: Zinc ion:proton antiporter that could function at the plasma
CC       membrane mediating zinc efflux from cells against its electrochemical
CC       gradient protecting them from intracellular zinc accumulation and
CC       toxicity (PubMed:19095042, PubMed:24951051, PubMed:7882967).
CC       Alternatively, could prevent the transport to the plasma membrane of
CC       CACNB2, the L-type calcium channels regulatory subunit, through a yet
CC       to be defined mechanism. By modulating the expression of these channels
CC       at the plasma membrane, could prevent calcium and zinc influx into
CC       cells. By the same mechanism, could also prevent L-type calcium
CC       channels-mediated heavy metal influx into cells (PubMed:15378655,
CC       PubMed:16741752, PubMed:19767393). In some cells, could also function
CC       as a zinc ion:proton antiporter mediating zinc entry into the lumen of
CC       cytoplasmic vesicles. In macrophages, can increase zinc ions
CC       concentration into the lumen of cytoplasmic vesicles containing
CC       engulfed bacteria and could help inactivate them (By similarity). Forms
CC       a complex with TMC6/EVER1 and TMC8/EVER2 at the ER membrane of
CC       keratynocytes which facilitates zinc uptake into the ER (By
CC       similarity). Down-regulates the activity of transcription factors
CC       induced by zinc and cytokines (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Y6M5, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15378655,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:16741752, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19095042,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19767393, ECO:0000269|PubMed:24951051,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:7882967}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY:
CC       Reaction=2 H(+)(out) + Zn(2+)(in) = 2 H(+)(in) + Zn(2+)(out);
CC         Xref=Rhea:RHEA:72627, ChEBI:CHEBI:15378, ChEBI:CHEBI:29105;
CC         Evidence={ECO:0000269|PubMed:24951051};
CC   -!- ACTIVITY REGULATION: Calcium-dependent. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:24951051}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer. Interacts with TMEM163 (By similarity). Interacts
CC       and forms a complex with TMC6 and TMC8; the interaction regulates zinc
CC       transport into the ER (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Y6M5,
CC       ECO:0000305|PubMed:7882967}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000269|PubMed:24951051,

CC       pass membrane protein {ECO:0000255}. Note=Localization to the plasma
CC       membrane is regulated by cellular zinc status. Recruitment to the
CC       plasma membrane from an internal pool is stimulated by zinc while in
CC       absence of zinc the plasma membrane pool is endocytosed and degraded
CC       (By similarity). Localizes to the basolateral surface of enterocytes
CC       (PubMed:24951051). Localizes to zinc-containing intracellular vesicles
CC       in macrophages (By similarity). Localizes in the cytoplasm and to the
CC       ER, Golgi and nucleus membranes in keratinocytes (By similarity).

CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:7882967, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9560190}.
